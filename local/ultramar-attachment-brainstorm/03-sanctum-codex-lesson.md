# 03 — Sanctum (Varro and the *Codex* lesson)

**Status:** local brainstorm only  
**Theater:** Ultramarine Reclusiam annex / sanctum side-chapel  
**Who:** Varro; Ultramarine Chaplains (incl. Leandros-adjacent voices or peers); overheard distrust of the Ones in grey  
**Note:** Foundation Wars lesson — blind *Codex* adherence nearly kills Chapter + rogue trader dynasty + Mechanicus Explorator. No Versibar. No curse-stage taxonomy.

---

The sanctum is colder than the hangars.

Incense here is Ultramarine: clean, ranked, convinced of its own arithmetic. Wolf-skull incense is not invited, so Varro brings his own weather in the seams of his plate and stands where a guest Chaplain may stand — not at the altar spine, not in command, exactly on the hinge between hospitality and judgment.

He does not announce himself.

He listens.

Two Ultramarine Chaplains speak in the side aisle as if grey enamel cannot hear Latin when it is whispered like doctrine.

— The Ones in grey hold hinges well enough — the first says. — That is not the same as trust. They do not wear the *Codex*. They wear a frontier excuse. Non-compliance always begins as necessity and ends as pride.

— Victrix-pretenders — the second answers, sharper. — Nine seals, no honest company spine. They help serfs for theater. They stand irregular on purpose. When the swarm presses, irregular becomes heresy with good timing. Leandros is not wrong to watch foreign geometry near restored Captains.

A pause. A censor’s chain ticks.

— Guilliman returned with the *Codex* living in his shadow — the first continues. — Chapters that rewrite it for comfort are how the Imperium learns new ways to die. Mark them. Do not love them.

Varro steps out of the column shadow.

He does not raise his voice. Judex work rarely needs volume. It needs placement.

— You mark us for not worshipping a book — Varro says. — Good. Listen, then, to what worshipping it almost cost.

Both Chaplains turn. One reaches for posture. The other reaches for authority. Neither reaches far enough to own the aisle.

— This sanctum is Ultramarines’ — the sharper one begins.

— And you filled it with my Chapter’s name — Varro answers. — So you invited the lesson.

He walks to the midpoint between them — not crowding, not deferring — and sets the Executor skull where both must see the fire-orbs.

— Foundation Wars — he says. — Early wet paint. Batavi still counting themselves with Terra’s furniture because Terra’s furniture was the only language we shared. The *Codex Astartes* was our template. Templates are not gods. We learned that with other people’s blood.

The sharper Chaplain’s vox clicks — objection loading.

Varro does not grant the slot.

— There was a rogue trader dynasty on our rim — he continues. — Legitimate warrant. Useful lanes. They asked for Astartes cover the way merchants ask for weather: urgently, and with too much confidence. Our captains of that hour opened the *Codex* and found the neat answer. Detachment size. Command tree. Compliance theater. They followed it as if the page could see the void better than the men drowning in it.

A thinner thread of incense leaves Varro’s jaw-vents — not theater, throttle.

— The dynasty died on schedule — he says. — Not because the *Codex* is false. Because the *Codex* is general, and the frontier was specific. While we were arranging ourselves into a diagram a dead man would approve, the true threat finished its meal. Ships. Houses. Names. Gone. We arrived correct and late.

The first Chaplain’s silence becomes attentive despite itself.

— There was also a Mechanicus Explorator — Varro goes on. — Not your forge-politics. A void-ark chasing dark data into places maps call unclean. It requested Batavi teeth for boarding geometry. Again we opened the book. Again we found purity of structure. Again we refused the ugly lane because the ugly lane was not written in the preferred tense.

He lets that hang until it becomes weight.

— The Explorator did not return — Varro says. — Its last scream was not heresy. It was mathematics: a Chapter that would rather be *Codex*-pretty than alive beside allies. We heard it. We filed it. We buried what pieces the void gave back. And in the same wars, when our own brothers began treating orders like optional poetry under the curse’s idiocy, the book still told us to correct them like Ultramarines correct failure — by ceremony, by rank theater, by assuming the material holds shape.

His skull tips a fraction toward the aisle’s Ultramarine banners.

— The material did not hold shape — Varro says. — So we bent the rulebook around the wound. We kept your spine. We cut your romance. We wrote shorter law: hold. Keep the idiot in the formation. Do not let him invent a private war. PRIMA through NONA learned different killing dialects so the Wall could speak more than one sentence. DECIMA stayed the anchor. None of that was contempt for Guilliman’s gift. It was refusal to kill dynasties and Explorators for the comfort of looking familiar to men who will never walk our mesh.

The sharper Chaplain finds his edge again.

— You confess deviation and call it wisdom.

— I confess corpses — Varro answers. — Wisdom is what we bought with them. You distrust the Ones in grey because we do not genuflect to neatness. Distrust us for the right reason, then: we will not burn a hinge to satisfy your paperwork. We will not arrive correct and late while your serfs die in oil and your allies die on unanswered vox. If that is untrustworthy, mark it in your litany. Just do not pretend the mark is purity. It is fear of a Chapter that survived by editing.

The first Chaplain — the one who spoke of necessity becoming pride — speaks quieter now.

— And your nine? Victrix-pretenders who carry serf grease on their gauntlets?

— Grease is honest — Varro says. — Sermons about our untrustworthiness, spoken where you thought grey could not hear, are less honest. My brothers help without ceremony because ceremony was one of the luxuries that made us late in the Foundation Wars. If you want trust, fight beside them. If you want the *Codex* as idol, keep your aisle. The swarm does not grade your theology.

He turns to leave, then adds one last placement strike — for Leandros’s shadow as much as for the two bodies in the sanctum:

— Blind adherence almost killed my Chapter, a warrant-house of traders, and an Explorator that deserved better than our politeness. We still carry the *Codex* as template. We do not carry it as a blindfold. Confuse those two again, and you will repeat our early math with Ultramar’s remaining worlds as the remainder.

The sharper Chaplain starts a rebuttal.

Varro is already at the hatch.

Incense thins. The sanctum tries to become Ultramarine again.

It does not fully succeed.

In the side aisle, two Chaplains are left with a foreign lesson stuck between their teeth: that the Ones in grey are not untrustworthy because they lack a book —

— but because they remember what the book costs when men love it more than the living line.
