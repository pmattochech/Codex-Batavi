# 02 — Corridors / hangar (unasked help)

**Status:** local brainstorm only  
**Theater:** Fortress monastery annex / recovery hangars — same Ultramarine company umbrella  
**Who:** Separated glimpses of the nine; Ultramarine witnesses (Gadriel, Chairon, sergeants, serfs); Varro absent from most beats  
**Note:** No ceremony. No speeches. Help, then walk on.

---

They do not arrive as a parade after the strategium.

They dissolve into the fortress the way water finds cracks — PRIMA through NONA given lanes, billets, and the kind of free minutes between briefings that proud Chapters waste on polish.

Ultramarines notice them anyway.

Not because the Batavi seek notice.

Because the work does.

In a service corridor behind the hangar spine, Lieutenant Gadriel turns a corner expecting empty logistics and finds Hadrian Voss of PRIMA already there. A munitorum sled has thrown a track. Two serfs and a stuttering loader-servitor are losing a quiet war against jammed teeth and shame. Voss has one knee down in oil that will never love grey enamel. He seats the track with a forge-law shove, taps the servitor’s receipt rune once — correction, not kindness theater — and stands.

The serfs begin to thank him.

Voss is already walking.

Gadriel almost speaks. The moment does not ask for command. It asks for filing. He files it.

Two decks up, Chairon hears chain-rattle and follows it into a void-lock antechamber. Kael Rheden of OCTAVA has a trapped maintenance serf out of a partial seal-collapse by the time Chairon arrives — black-pauldron economy, knife used as pry, body used as hinge. The serf gasps doctrine-thanks. Kael checks the lock geometry, finds it acceptable, and leaves without offering a name.

Chairon watches the empty hatch a second longer than *Codex* requires.

— Doorstop — he mutters, and cannot decide if it is insult or respect.

In the hangar proper, under the hard light of recovery gantries, Cassian Holt of SECVNDA walks a civilian overflow queue as if he is reading weather. A cult-scared clerk is too perfect in his calm — posture rehearsed, blinks on a timer, gratitude phrases cut from a manual. Holt stops. Does not draw. Asks one soft question about shift-rot and soap brand. The clerk’s answer is flawless.

Holt’s helm tilts.

An Ultramarine sergeant moves to intervene — jurisdiction, papers, pride.

Holt is already guiding the clerk toward a side alcove with two fingers on a shoulder, ivory-quiet, no spectacle. Later the sergeant will learn the clerk’s papers were better than his scent. Later still, he will pretend he knew.

Holt does not stay for the arrest theater. Help completed. Pattern closed. Walking resumes.

Near a fuel spine, Torven Ash of TERTIA finds a serf-team fighting a chemical seep with the wrong foam. He does not lecture. He takes the nozzle, burns the seep’s edge into obedience with a controlled kiss of flame, hands the tool back, and points once at the correct canister stripe.

— That one — he says.

Then he is gone, leaving the serfs staring at a problem that has become a lesson without a sermon.

Bram Halder of QVARTA becomes a rumor before he becomes a sighting: a collapsed crate-stack in a high-G transfer bay, three mortals pinned under mass that should have waited for a Magos. Ultramarines arrive prepared for tragedy and find Halder already planted beneath the worst edge — Stasis made flesh — holding what gravity wanted to finish. Serfs scramble braces into place around him. When the stack is locked, Halder steps out as if polite men always spend minutes as architecture.

He nods to the nearest Ultramarine.

No speech.

Walking.

Rurik Fenndrift of QVINTA almost ruins the pattern by being Rurik. In a high vault he finds servitors tangled in cable like idiots praying. He cuts them free mid-hover, jump-pack hissing soft blasphemies, and for one dangerous second his vox opens toward an empty catwalk where Kael is not.

Then he remembers a skull turning one degree in a strategium.

The joke dies.

He sets a servitor upright, pats its cranial plate like a misbehaving pup, and flies on in disciplined silence that costs him visibly.

Helmut Eisen of SEXTA does not fit through every mortal door. The hangar notices him the way cities notice earthquakes. A recovery Thunderhawk’s landing claw has bitten wrong into deck teeth; Techmarines argue ritual; serfs wait to be told they are in the way. Eisen walks into the argument without joining it. Terminator mass becomes lever. The claw yields. A Magos emits static that might be offense or gratitude.

Eisen is already leaving, each step a closed debate.

Orin Vitrael of SEPTIMA is found in a lens-bay where a plasma conduit has begun to sing the wrong note. Ultramarine crew form a beautiful perimeter of hesitation. Vitrael vents, reseats, locks, and checks the heat bloom with the boredom of a man correcting a brother’s bad habit. A serf flinches when the conduit settles.

— Still alive — Vitrael says, almost kind. — Keep it that way.

He walks.

Severin Marck of NONA appears where panic thickens: a shrine-spur where wounded ratings swear they hear whispers in the coolant. An Ultramarine Chaplain-aspirant is already reaching for litany volume. Marck does not compete. Nullity work is quieter. He sets a ward-bead, stands in the wrong quiet until the room’s fear has nowhere to breed, and guides two shaking serfs back to their pallet line with a palm between shoulder blades — not blessing, ballast.

When the whispers fail to return, Marck is gone down the corridor, threshold manners intact.

By the third watch, the company has a new unofficial report that never quite makes it onto a slate:

The Ones in grey do not wait to be asked.

They do not pose for thanks.

They help a servitor the way they hold a hinge — because the work is in front of them — and then they keep walking, as if ceremony were a civilian disease.

Gadriel, later, says it to Chairon in a hangar shadow without looking at him:

— They treat our serfs like shared sweat.

Chairon snorts.

— They treat our pride like optional cargo — he answers. — I am starting to like that less than I should.

Somewhere deeper in the fortress, nine different wars continue not announcing themselves.

The swarm is still at the doorsteps.

The Batavi, between killings, keep picking up what the Imperium drops — and refuse to turn it into a sermon.
