# 01 — Strategium (Acheran)

**Status:** local brainstorm only  
**Theater:** Ultramar recovery — company strategium  
**Who:** Varro (detachment command); Hadrian Voss (PRIMA); Cassian Holt (SECVNDA); Torven Ash (TERTIA); Bram Halder (QVARTA); Rurik Fenndrift (QVINTA); Helmut Eisen (SEXTA, TDA); Orin Vitrael (SEPTIMA); Kael Rheden (OCTAVA); Severin Marck (NONA); Drusus-appointed Apothecary; Otho-appointed Haruspex; Captain Acheran and company officers  
**Note:** Titus and Calgar already met the Batavi off-stage before this official company reception.

---

The strategium smells of ozone, disinfectant, and old triumph struggling to stay relevant. Hololith light cuts the air into blue geometry. Outside the sealed shutters, the world still argues with Tyranid residue and traitor ash; inside, Ultramarines stand as if posture alone could enforce the *Codex*.

Captain Acheran occupies the command mark without theatrics. Around him, officers of his company fill the secondary arcs — Lieutenants among them, sergeants with clean enamel and unclean fatigue. No Titus. No Calgar. Those meetings have already happened elsewhere, in rooms that measured hinges and load. This room measures **protocol**.

Opposite them, the Batavi arrive as a diagram.

Varro takes the front. Executor wolf-skull. Incense thin as a drawn line. Judex first, diplomat never. Behind him, twelve become readable only if one knows how to count foreign: nine grey apexes in a shallow arc, then the Apothecary Drusus sealed for flesh, then the Haruspex Otho sealed for the unseen.

The nine do not shuffle.

Hadrian Voss of PRIMA. Cassian Holt of SECVNDA. Torven Ash of TERTIA. Bram Halder of QVARTA. Rurik Fenndrift of QVINTA, jump-pack still ticking residual heat like a suppressed laugh. Helmut Eisen of SEXTA, Terminator silhouette turning the arc into a fortress line by existing. Orin Vitrael of SEPTIMA. Kael Rheden of OCTAVA. Severin Marck of NONA.

Acheran’s gaze travels the arc once and stops on the math.

— Twelve — he says. — Nine line. Three specialists. Your Chapter Master’s office sent word you would reinforce this theater. The swarm is not finished dying. Traitors still test the doorsteps. And Ultramar does not rebuild itself with polite numbers. Why nine fighting blades, Chaplain? Why not a company?

A sergeant’s vox clicks — pride clearing its throat. Varro does not look at the sergeant. He looks at Acheran, because geometry answers the man who owns the room.

— Because a company is cheap — Varro says. — These nine are not.

Rurik, half a step behind Kael, tips his helm a fraction and murmurs — soft enough, he believes, for OCTAVA alone:

— Tell him we also came for the coffee. Ultramarine coffee. I hear it is cowardly.

Kael does not answer.

Varro’s skull turns a single degree. One glance. No words. The incense does not change; the air does.

Rurik’s jump-pack ticks once more, then seems to understand fear. His shoulders square. His humor dies mid-birth. For the rest of the briefing he becomes what QVINTA pretends it can never be: still.

Varro faces Acheran again as if nothing happened — which, for Judex work, is the same as punishment completed.

— You do not know our seals — Varro continues. — You need not. Learn only this: each of these brothers is the foremost of his vexilla — PRIMA through NONA. In our house they stand next for DECIMA, the Castellan’s anchor guard. In your tongue, so the *Codex* may sleep at night: they are the next to be shaped into what you would call Victrix Guard.

That lands. Even pride understands Victrix.

Acheran’s jaw sets behind the helm’s discipline.

— You claim nine Victrix-grade killers as an attachment.

— I claim nine men each Legatus hated to release — Varro says. — One from every fighting seal. DECIMA itself does not tour; it anchors. Sending its incoming blood is the highest spend short of our Castellan walking your decks. If we meant courtesy, we would have sent ninety line brothers and kept these at home.

Cassian Holt of SECVNDA stands ivory-quiet, Silence habit even without the lecture. Helmut Eisen of SEXTA makes the hololith feel smaller by occupying space like a verdict. Severin Marck of NONA watches the room’s psychic weather without moving his hands. Kael of OCTAVA remains a doorstop with Watch-black under parade paint. Rurik of QVINTA remains miraculously mute.

Acheran steps into the hololith’s throat-map — swarm vectors, traitor scrap-signs, civilian spurs still coughing.

— Calgar already placed you on a hinge behind our glory-face — Acheran says. Not a question. Inventory. — Titus accepted that geometry. I am the Captain who must bleed with it. So speak plain: are nine enough when the door is this wide?

Varro’s answer is Iron Protocol without needing the motto aloud.

— Nine of these are not a squad. They are a sample of a Wall. Each fights a different war under one Chapter. Your sergeants will call it irregular. Call it whatever keeps your paperwork neat. On the line, they will hold what a larger, prouder attachment would debate first.

Acheran studies them again — not as guests now, as ammunition with opinions.

— Then you are under my company’s operational umbrella for the duration of this attachment — he says. — You will not rewrite Ultramarine command. You will not collect old Watch debts in blue enamel. You will hold the hinges we name. If your Victrix-pretenders are half what you claim, the swarm will notice before my sergeants finish complaining.

Varro inclines the skull a fraction — acceptance, not submission.

— We hold — he says. — Assign lanes. My brothers already met your Chapter’s heavier names. They do not need ceremony. They need work.

Behind Varro, Rurik’s silence continues — absolute, terrified, almost devout.

Acheran cuts the hololith to the first ugly throat and begins issuing orders like a man who has decided the argument can wait until after the killing.

The Batavi adjust half a step into the named geometry — PRIMA to NONA in one arc, Judex at the tip — and for the first time since they entered, the strategium looks less like a courtroom and more like a wall agreeing where the tide is allowed to hit.
