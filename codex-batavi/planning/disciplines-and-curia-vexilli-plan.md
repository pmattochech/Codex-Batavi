## 1. Two layers: fuel and form

Post–**Valerian Heresy** doctrine splits what chimeric architecture **offers** from what the Chapter **chooses**.

| Layer | What it is | Failure mode |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Fuel** (biology) | **Predisposition** in **Strand α** / **Strand β** registers. Near matching threat signatures, endocrine and limbic pressure rise — Waaagh resonance, **Shadow in the Warp** weight, traitor gene-seed recognition. | Unmanaged fuel + stress → **Wolf’s Curse** ratchet ([`projection-aurea-wolfs-curse.md`](../biological-encyclopedia-bestiary/projection-aurea-wolfs-curse.md) §B). |
| **Form** (culture) | Three **disciplines** — trained rites that **harness** fuel into bounded combat psychology. | **Vanity** (refuse rotation, glory hunts) or **clearance breach** (depth without medic sign-off). |

**Wolf’s Curse** is not a fourth discipline. It is **degeneration** when the fire is not held on the wall but allowed to eat the marrow.

**Historical note:** Foundation War and early Ork/Tyranid contacts record **uncontrolled** mass responses — biology ahead of rite, before ***Curia Vexilli*** harness. Post–***ca.* 051.M42** field speech uses only the three discipline names below.

## 2. The three disciplines

### 2.1 Wild Hunt (Ork / brute-force geometry)

* **Fuel:** Waaagh pressure, primal psychic noise, green-war mass. Scales with **presence size**, not a single skirmish.  
* **Form:** Predatory assault discipline — closure speed, melee dominance, pack tempo. **Bolter-anvil** remains default; deep Hunt authorizes descent into blade geometry.  
* **Cultural home:** **V** *Quinta* (storm assault, drop clearance) — **most frequent** deep Hunt; other vexes train **edge** Hunt as mission requires.  
* **Rebound (post-harness norm):** Tremor, elevated **Furor** watch, pharmakon — **not** chapter-wide unconscious collapse. Mass hypovolemic “deck drop” is filed as **pre-*Curia*** hazard or **unauthorized** depth.

### 2.2 March of the Machine (Tyranid fleet / Shadow geometry)

* **Fuel:** **Shadow in the Warp** and aggregate hive hunger drying local Immaterium — **not** “a Tyranid on the scope.” **Genestealer cults** and larval nets without fleet-scale shadow **do not** call deep March; **Silence Cohort**, *Lex Imperialis*, and allied intel cover that gap.  
* **Form:** Analytical silence, pattern slaughter, dampened affect — **march-edge** for coalition void/boarding; **march-deep** only with logged Shadow threshold and clearance.  
* **Cultural home:** **IV** *Quarta* (static grav-locked holds; registered Tyranid defense).  
* **Rebound:** Mental exhaustion, repetitive task fixation — managed by **rotation**, not public catatonia on allied decks.

### 2.3 The Cull (traitor Astartes / treason imprint)

* **Fuel:** **Strand β** treason-recognition — corrupted Astartes sharing lineage root; intensity scales on **corruption depth** and **proximity to Horus Lupercal’s line**, not headcount.  
* **Form:** Ice rectification — precise execution, not berserk release.  
* **Cultural home:** **I** *Prima* (Valerian shame lattice; obligation fourth field on *Alabarda*).  
* **Depth tiers:**

| Tier | Trigger (summary) | Rebound risk |
|------|-------------------|--------------|
| **I** | Traitor Astartes (any Traitor Legion) | Moderate purpose drain; days–weeks |
| **II** | Horus-line depth / extreme corruption | Significant purpose drain; lasting scars |
| **III** | Fallen Batavi / *Lupercal* claim at Chapter scale | Purpose collapse — potentially permanent |

*Daemon princes* and entities that erased brotherhood imprint read as **alien**, not treason — **amplified *Axiom of Reason***, not the Cull.

**Generic Chaos** (cultists, daemons without gene-seed signature): no discipline axis; elevated *Axiom* only.

## 3. Depth bands and rotation

**Depth bands** (edge / deep per discipline) and **rotation tables** are co-signed by **Genetor Primus** and **Confessor Primus** — one register, two fenced domains (clinical caps + conduct caps).

| Ledger | Owner lane | Tracks |
|--------|------------|--------|
| **Fuel load** | Genetor | Exposure hours, Shadow/Waaagh proximity, pharmakon, Furor/Exanimus markers |
| **Harness debt** | Genetor | Authorized depth per sortie |
| **Conduct** | Confessor | Vanity, shame-glory, refusal of relief, penance status |

**Rotation law:** The same battle-brother or lance cycled into **deep** discipline repeatedly without stand-down **raises** Wolf’s Curse risk even when each sortie is **cleared**. Greedy command is a **logged sin** — medic warning ignored, chaplain vanity file open, Legatus override on the *Alabarda* relay.

**Sustained theaters** (e.g. Armageddon-scale Ork war): *Quinta* may assault often, but **lances rotate**; cross-vex relief (*Sexta* hold, line vexillatio swap) is *Strategium* expectation, not hero preference.

## 4. The *Curia Vexilli* (banner court)

Each **Vexillatio** I–IX maintains a **banner court** — informal Low Gothic: **“the three at the staff.”** ***Decima*** does **not** duplicate the lattice; **Triumvirate** authority applies directly ([`military-hierarchy.md`](../personae-command-index/doctrine-and-organs/military-hierarchy.md) §3).

| Seat | Title | Domain (**does not cross**) |
|------|--------|-----------------------------|
| War geometry | **Legatus** | Schedule, theater, coalition face, deployment after hearing risk |
| *Medicinae* | **Genetor Vexilli** | Clearance, fuel, depth, rotation biology, pharmakon |
| *Reclusiam* | **Confessor Vexilli** | Conduct, vanity, penance, marks, relief refused |

### 4.1 Parallel review

When a brother is nominated for line duty or **deep** discipline, **Genetor Vexilli** and **Confessor Vexilli** review **in parallel** — separate charts, **no** sequential gate, **no** comment on the partner’s lane.

| Genetor | Confessor | Legatus |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Fit | Fit | Schedules |
| Not fit (clinical) | Fit | Hears **clinical** risk brief only |
| Fit | Not fit (conduct) | Hears **conduct** risk brief only |
| Not fit | Not fit | Hears both; override needs **strong cause** or Triumvirate exception |

**Legatus** may deploy after brief; **logged override** if deploying against one or both flags. Pattern overrides escalate to **Genetor Primus** / **Confessor Primus**.

### 4.2 Authority summary

- **Genetor Vexilli:** **Hard veto on clearance** (depth and fitness for harness).  
- **Confessor Vexilli:** **Hard power on penance and marks** (vanity, shame-glory).  
- **Legatus:** **Owns the calendar**; cannot invent clearance or erase penance.

## 5. Chapter organ spine (redundancy — not Legati)

**Only** the nine Phalanx governors bear the title **Legatus**. Chapter-wide *Medicinae* and *Reclusiam* delegates use **Primus** ranks below.

| Post | Title | Origin | Role |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| Apex *Medicinae* | Chief Apothecary **Drusus** | Triumvirate | Law, *Vermilion*, appeal |
| Organ delegate | **Genetor Primus** | Former **Genetor Vexilli** | Co-signs depth/rotation tables; audits nine vex Genetors; Watch disclosure policy under Drusus |
| Apex *Reclusiam* | Master Chaplain **Varro** | Triumvirate | Law, *Axiom*, appeal |
| Organ delegate | **Confessor Primus** | Former **Confessor Vexilli** | Co-signs conduct caps; audits nine vex Confessors; penance jurisprudence under Varro |

**Liaison-Praefect** (Deathwatch correspondence, e.g. **Soren Riis**) serves under **Genetor Primus** for *Pactum Clavis Sanguinis* — not the organ head.

Adjutant Chaplains and sub-cohort Apothecaries remain in the **500** table; **Genetor Vexilli** / **Confessor Vexilli** are the **senior** vex voices on the *Alabarda* relay (often drawn from those pools).

## 7. Coalition and Watch (operative summary)

- Allies may find Batavi **strange**; doctrine targets **unreliable sustainment** — winning the hour then **vanishing** from the next beat.  
- **Deathwatch** Grey tier: stage, pharmakon, **harness depth**, rotation flags — not “brother will auto-enter deep March.”  
- **Certified *Watch cycle*** proves mesh under alien tempo without **dissolving** into unmanaged fuel.
