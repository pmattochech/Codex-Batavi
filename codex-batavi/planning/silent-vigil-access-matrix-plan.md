# SILENT VIGIL ACCESS MATRIX PLAN

**Status:** draft  
**Priority:** `[P0]`  
**Purpose:** define who knows what about the anti-inversion contingency framework.  
**Scope:** planning access model; this file is not the sealed canon matrix itself.

---

## 1) Design Principles

- Compartmentalize by duty, not rank prestige alone.
- Maintain operational sufficiency at each access level.
- Prevent unnecessary detail spread that could weaken the protocol.
- Keep Alaric informed on existence and purpose, without exposing full kill-path architecture.

---

## 2) Access Tiers (Planning)

## Tier A — Full Strategic Access

Entities:

- Varro
- Drusus
- Tyvar
- Senior Legatus high command set
- Radulf (as required by strategic intervention role)
- Guilliman (request-level sovereign access)

Contains:

- full trigger ladder
- full cohort sequencing
- command firewall details
- cull authorization thresholds
- fail-deadly contingencies and fallback command chain

## Tier B — Limited Operational Access

Entities:

- Captain Kadmos
- Otho

Contains:

- execution geometry
- activation signals and handshake protocols
- immediate objectives for each escalation stage
- minimum legal frame required for compliance

Excludes:

- full strategic rationale tree
- full cross-tier political access map
- non-essential long-horizon contingency branches

## Tier C — Objective-Only Awareness

Entities:

- Alaric

Contains:

- protocol exists
- purpose: protect chapter and Imperium if inversion occurs
- authority basis and reason for compartmentalization

Excludes:

- detailed kill routes
- specialized cohort choreography
- matrix internals and failover architecture

---

## 3) Operational Rules

- Need-to-know distribution is mandatory at every update cycle.
- Tier B can execute with limited context, but cannot self-expand scope.
- Tier A retains version authority and rewrite authority.
- Any matrix expansion for Tier B requires dual confirmation from Varro and Drusus.
- Any disclosure to Alaric beyond objective-level requires exceptional joint authorization.

---

## 4) Access Events And Governance

## Planned Event Types

- **Initial lock event:** establish baseline holders and archive hash
- **Escalation event:** temporary elevation of limited holders when trigger group B/C activates
- **Post-event rollback:** revoke temporary elevations after stabilization
- **Audit event:** verify who accessed which segment and why

## Governance Notes

- Keep access logs sealed and cross-signed.
- Ensure continuity if Triumvirate members are unavailable.
- Preserve role-based replacement chain for each tier.
