Noviomagus did not fear rebellion the way softer worlds feared it.

Fear on Noviomagus was procedural. It had inventory numbers, route maps, waste quotas, and processing tables. The world had been built on one assumption: every structure fails eventually, every oath cracks under enough hunger, and every district can become a furnace if discipline slips one notch too low.

Hive Vespera learned what that assumption meant when it stopped speaking the Imperium’s name in public and started speaking Alaric’s.

At first the refusal looked like municipal friction. Tithe records delayed by one cycle. Administratum seals returned unsigned. Shrine attendance dropped in lower decks while unauthorized assemblies filled old ventilation vaults. Then came the language shift: petitions no longer addressed to Terra or Throne, but to the Wall itself.

Not "for the Emperor."
For Alaric.

The city’s noble houses moved last, which meant they had moved first in secret. They funded the quiet assemblies, redirected grain chits, and paid guild foremen to repeat one sentence until it sounded like reason:

Noviomagus does not need the Imperium if it already has its own king in iron.

By the time the phrase reached high command, Vespera had already stopped being a hive and become a thesis.

The first answer did not come from Astartes guns.
It came from law.

High Lords emergency authority moved down through Lex Imperialis channels into an Arbites mandate packet delivered under sealed convoy. Marshal Acastian Vhal arrived on Noviomagus with two Judge-Militant teams and enough writ metal to turn any operation into legal architecture.

The mandate chain was read in the black chamber without ornament:

High Lords.
Lex Imperialis.
Arbites supervisory authority.
Batavi force projection.

No one in that chamber mistook the arrangement for subordination. The Batavi did not operate under the Arbites. They operated with them: law set sentence architecture, Astartes set kill geometry, and each side held privileges the other did not own.

Alaric did not debate the need for punishment. He debated one line only.

One Arbites draft model proposed punitive fines and estate seizure for compliant noble houses, preserving bloodlines under supervision.

Alaric read the clause once and his answer came with visible disgust, cold enough to deaden the room:

— They used my name against Terra. Fines are bookkeeping for treason.

Marshal Vhal watched him for one measured second, then drew a black strike through the fine model and pushed the amended page to Judge-Militant Serix for countersign.

Only then did Alaric sign the purge order in thirteen words and send no amendment:

— Secession doctrine confirmed. No clemency channel. Break and reset the city.

No redemption clauses were attached.
No staged surrender terms.
No gradual compliance ladder.

Line command received a sequence cold enough to read like machine law: isolate, enter, sever command caste, erase symbols, process survivors to penal labor, strip nobility to servitor stock. Arbites clerks and shock cadres were attached at each sentence node for warrant verification, custody logging, and legal handoff.

The geometry split at signature:

**Corbec Ardens** took **all of PRIMA** — line battle companies and every specialized cohort under PRIMA’s lattice — plus Arbites shock cadres for the **lower hive**: rabble, stewards, guild militias, barricade war, labor capture, and the red work in manufactoria and hab trenches.

**Alaric** took a narrow column — Arbites Judge-Militant teams, Mechanicus conversion liaison, custody clerks — for the **upper spire tiers and house compounds** alone: negotiation theater, house heads, minor nobility, familiars, and household servants who had failed to denounce their masters.

The briefing included evidence bundles recovered from Vespera’s own data vaults: noble account ledgers with hidden transfers, prayer sheets altered by local clerks to replace Imperial liturgy with civic devotion formulas, district maps annotated with choke points where rebel stewards intended to starve loyal sectors into compliance. A **Shadows** overlay — matte-black wax seals on duct schematics — marked **kill-box** candidates: chem-pit arenas, maintenance spines above the magistrate feed, and a noble-only vent shaft into the old tribunal annex that did not appear on the public hive roll. Corbec circled that shaft in orange chalk and assigned it to black pauldrons **before** dawn.

A **Stasis** binder — gunmetal tabs on tram and choke maps — rated **hold geometry**: which barricade faces would fold in twelve seconds, which freight mouths could take a mutant tide, which bridge spans could not flex without snapping a labor lane. Corbec assigned **forty** gunmetal brothers to the horizontal war before the first barricade burned.

A **Nullity** folio — cobalt ribbon, *Librarium* seal beside each page — charted **witch-signatures** in Vespera’s shrine warrens: unsanctioned psykers hidden from Tithe inspection, latent choirs fed by house chaplains, plaster dust that had begun to crawl on auspex stills, and a primary spike the Haruspices labeled **Kethmar-bled** until the house name was struck from public law. Corbec assigned **twenty** cobalt brothers and **five** embedded **Batavian Haruspices** under PRIMA’s Nullity–*Haruspicium* liaison channel. No bolt without clearance.

A separate **Silence** annex — ivory-tabbed, *Medicinae*-adjacent handwriting on the cover — catalogued **pattern debt**: ventilation-vault assembly times matched to pay-cycle drops; foremen whose gait signatures appeared in six districts under different names; tutors whose lesson-card revisions shared one ink-chemistry trace with house archivists. None of it was chaotic. That made it worse. Corbec read the annex twice and assigned **fifty** ivory brothers before the first gate sealed.

The rebellion had not been desperate.
It had been administrative.

It had used the language of guardianship while preparing to split a forge world off from Terra and call the split love.

In underhive canteens the propaganda came as folk wisdom, not manifesto. Foremen spoke about local dignity and frontier burden. Charter-clerks repeated that Terra extracted and never understood. School-slates replaced imperial civic catechisms with production myths in which Noviomagus had forged itself alone, as if no tithe fleets had ever carried ore and blood outward in the same holds.

In upper levels the language changed again. Nobles hosted private dinners where they raised sealed cups to "pragmatic sovereignty," then sent polite letters to each other describing the Emperor as a distant legal abstraction and Alaric as immediate providence. Every letter carried deniable phrasing. Every sentence was designed to be read in two directions until guns chose which meaning survived.

By the sixth week, the city had developed two calendars: the official one, still stamped by imperial shift law, and the whispered one, counting toward a "Declaration Night" that no one wrote down in full. Workers who never saw a noble in their lives began repeating house slogans as if they had authored them. The slogans were distributed in pay envelopes, scratched under canteen tables, and stitched into children’s lesson cards by tutors who had learned exactly how far propaganda could bend before it snapped into sedition.

The rabble believed what the nobles had paid to teach them.

In sump canteens and tram mouths, foremen swore the Wall would **come home** when Declaration Night broke — that grey armor on the avenue would not be punishment but **recognition**, the Castellan finally shedding Terra’s leash to stand with the people who had kept his name alive. Mothers told children to wave white cloth, not because surrender was virtue, but because **Alaric** was supposed to see kin, not enemies. Guild hands painted his frontal-wolf echo on barricade cloth and meant it as welcome, not defiance. When shift sirens changed tone the night before the assault, thousands still thought the sound meant a prince’s arrival, not a noose closing.

The noble houses believed the same thing, but they priced it.

They did not wait for shock. They prepared **ledgers**: sealed tithe abatements drafted in Vespera’s name, a crown commission scroll already countersigned by three houses, promissory veins of ore and penal labor pledged for six decades if the Castellan would **accept** the title they had hung on him in public — **Castellan-King of Noviomagus**, Wall-sovereign, throne below the spines and above the Administratum clerks who had never bled in the mesh. Bribery packets rode with the envoys in null-field cases: gene-wrought reliquaries, forge-right deeds, a marriage clause offering house daughters as dynastic seal. Their private letters assumed he **wanted** what they offered and only needed law dressed as love to take it.

In the black chamber, if anyone on the Batavi side expected the rebels’ faith to buy mercy, they kept that expectation buried where it could not be heard.

On the Astartes side, purge geometry was authored jointly and executed separately. **Corbec** drew the lower-hive kill grid; **Alaric** drew the noble severance law. Both men shared the same legal alignment — loyalty to the Imperium first, command personae second, sentiment never — and neither treated Vespera as mistaken. They treated it as infected governance.

They had all survived too many campaigns to confuse personal devotion with lawful loyalty.

---

The operation began before dawn-cycle under forge-cloud, when Vespera’s upper towers were still lit in dim maintenance amber and the lower transit arteries ran on skeleton schedules.

From orbit, the assault looked less like war than like maintenance. Traffic lanes blinked from green to locked red in concentric rings. Elevator trunks stopped at synchronized heights. Heat signatures in key plazas were boxed by moving walls of disciplined steel while the rest of the hive still believed it had a full morning left before consequence — or, in the lower decks, believed the morning belonged to **him**, and that the locks were ceremonial until the Castellan showed his face and claimed what they had prepared in his name.

Corbec ran the **full PRIMA** command lattice from the rear synchronization node — line companies and specialized cohorts unmasked for internal purge, Arbites shock cadres slaved to each breach wave. Alaric did not walk the magistrate avenue. While Corbec’s steel descended, the Castellan climbed by secured lift trunk toward the house spires with Judge-Militant Serix’s writ teams and conversion liaison already moving on separate schedules.

PRIMA sealed arterial gates, cut outbound cargo, and severed relay spires with heavy precision fire. Line battle companies entered the civic core where unofficial councils had met under the painted faces of old machine saints. Cohort detachments peeled off by task, not by vanity:

**Silence** — the **throat**: ration markets, tram mouths, ventilation vaults, any choke where speech could become crowd.

**Shadows** — the **vertical**: false-flag stewards, chem pits, duct war, hatches above the line — **under Silence lattice**, **blade-first** (combat knife, power gladius); stalker bolt and melta only where the geometry could not be closed quiet.

**Stasis** — the **horizontal**: barricades, riot funnels, tram mass, bridge spans — hold, do not chase.

**Nullity** — the **poisoned air**: shrine warrens, witch-signatures, latent choirs — no bolt without Haruspic clearance.

Arbites shock columns advanced in the wake of each PRIMA breach line, black armor under Lex banners, sealing each combat gain into enforceable sentence territory. Where ivory had already tagged a mouth, the shock troopers arrived to **sign** what Silence had read.

The chronicle files what follows by **phase** and **cohort**, because Vespera’s purge was one lattice with four specialized teeth and a line that only fired what the slate had already named.

## Phase I — Opening breach

### Corbec and the line

Corbec walked the central magistrate avenue without haste, grey armor carrying PRIMA’s orange lens wash through ash and alarm light. He did not issue speeches through open vox. He issued vectors.

— Third lane, lock.
— Atrium six, burn the archive.
— Upper bridge, no passage.

Every command arrived like a gate closing.

Line officers and cohort sergeants repeated those vectors without embellishment. In Batavi channels, words were not used to inspire. They were used to remove uncertainty. By the time the third relay wave cleared, every sub-unit in the kill grid knew exactly which lanes were for neutralization, which were for extraction, and which were for labor capture.

The first civic vox tower that attempted a "citizen regency declaration" survived long enough to transmit nine words before PRIMA siege fire cut the mast at the joint and dropped six stories of relay steel into the crowd cheering below.

They had been cheering **for** Alaric.

When grey armor appeared on the magistrate feed in the same hour — PRIMA marks, not a Castellan’s solitary spear — the sound in the plaza changed. Not battle cry. Not yet surrender. The raw disbelief of people who had been told the Wall would **side** with Vespera against Terra and had instead met a full Vexilla’s kill geometry. Some threw themselves flat with painted wolf cloth still in their hands. Others stood frozen long enough to be logged as auxiliaries. A woman in apron-steel was seen beating her fists against a tram pillar, screaming Alaric’s name as if volume could rewind the bolts Corbec’s lattice had already issued. The crowd ran red through dust and sparks, and whatever speech had been planned for that hour dissolved into static and the first honest understanding that **no** king had come home — only law, in steel.

### Silence

In the hab trenches below that tower, the same static kept crackling for nearly an hour, blending emergency channel code with fragments of the failed declaration. Residents later said they could still hear "rightful ruler" buried in the hiss long after the mast had cooled. Whether that was memory or signal residue did not matter. Both forms were dangerous — especially the memory of a welcome that had never been returned.

Silence did not fire into the static. They **listened**. Emergency code mixed with fragments of the failed declaration; beneath it, a steward’s voice coaching a child’s chorus to sing Alaric’s name on shift change. Silence triangulated the harmonic — not sorcery, **routing** — and sent Arbites to the maintenance closet behind Hab Tier Six. Inside: a portable vox rig, three prayer sheets with civic devotion formulas, and a tutor who had stitched secession into lesson cards. He was taken alive because alive still had ledger names on his tongue. The children were routed to processing queues with labor indices, not to mercy speeches.

At the civic statues, stewards tried to route civilians into symbolic mass gatherings, still betting that mass adoration would stay Astartes hands. Ivory was already there in service plate where the crowd expected only grey — pauldrons dulled under soot cloaks, auspex wands hidden in tool belts, brothers posing as shift inspectors long enough to mark which faces were **local** and which were **paid**. When PRIMA broke the gatherings by corridor segmentation and non-negotiable dispersal timing, the tags were already on the stewards’ collars. Line companies closed the pockets; Silence took the tagged mouths alive or dead before the crowd could turn grief into a second sermon. Those who moved lived into processing queues. Those who held banners instead of obeying route orders were logged as active auxiliaries to secession and removed with the same efficiency as armed cadres.

A woman in shift-leader blues had guided civilians into a ring, voice warm with practiced mercy — eyes that never tracked the statue’s face, always the crowd’s reaction. Silence had watched her for eleven seconds: gait too symmetrical, pauses too evenly spaced, the micro-sway of a body that had rehearsed sympathy in a mirror. A concussion grenade dialed to **stun** geometry shattered the ring. Webbing took her at the ankle; the carbine stock took her teeth when she tried to bite through the filament. An Arbites clerk read the charge — *incitement, secession register* — while she gurgled through broken enamel.

### Stasis

Vespera tried to answer with barricades, with stolen industrial cutters, with guild militias wearing plated aprons and noble bodyguards in inherited carapace polished for ceremony and useless for real war.

Gunmetal shields took the arterial mouth at Magistrate Lane Three — **scuta** three ranks deep, storm shields braced on portable barrier pins, grav-boots locked to deck plate. Guild militia threw themselves at the wall twice; power gladii took the ones who reached the rim; the rest learned that ceramite did not negotiate. The first barricade held twelve seconds against the **line** behind Stasis; the second held nine; the third did not complete construction before melta wash turned it into running metal **through gaps Stasis opened one second at a time** — semi-auto through the slot, then the slot closed again. Barricade war became arithmetic: seconds purchased by flesh, subtracted by geometry.

When stewards tried to turn the magistrate plaza crowd into a **human shield**, Stasis did not shoot into the mass. They **segmented** it — shock-lines, shield walls, tram-gate drops — herding panic into filing channels where Silence could read faces and line brothers could shoot what the slate named. A woman with wolf cloth on her shoulders hit the shield rim until her collarbone cracked; she was logged collateral and lifted anyway because the channel could not stop. That was Stasis cruelty: the hold was never gentle. It was **exact**.

## Phase II — Corbec’s synchronization node

Corbec did not leave the tribunal annex. He had planted the ***Alabarda* + Vexilla** in the deck plates — halberd head down, banner furled against the haft, relay spine lit with PRIMA’s orange wash. Arbites clerks stood at the warrant table beside him. The *Alabarda* merged auspex from a dozen breach points into one lattice only his Vexilla could read: heat blooms, vox hash, grav-boot signatures, Nullity witch-markers flashing cobalt on the overlay.

His orders did not rise in volume. They **partitioned**.

— Kill-team **Alpha**, manufactorum spine — semi-auto, labor capture lanes intact.
— **Silence** on guild mouthpieces, vault assemblies, and lesson-card nodes — pattern validation first; custody if the mouth still has names; bolt if the mouth reaches for a weapon or a vox.
— **Shadows** on upper duct map — chem cells first, then tribunal annex noble-shaft; seal or kill; no independent melta without line clearance below.
— **Stasis** on tram four and all mass blocks — hold until cleared; bridge spans locked until Nullity clears witch-sign; no pursuit through a choke that is not green on the overlay.
— **Nullity** on shrine warrens and Kethmar-bled spike — Haruspic lead; Oblivion cells on rods; no bolt, no flame on primary mark without body intact for *Medicinae* filing.

Line brothers answered in **kill teams** of five: one sergeant, two riflemen, one support, one cohort liaison where the threat demanded it. They moved on Corbec’s relay ticks, not shouts — the orange lens pulse on the *Alabarda* haft the only visible heartbeat of the whole purge grid.

The noble houses had priced coronation. They had also priced **one** dead Legatus.

### Shadows

**Shadows** had been in the annex rafters since the third relay tick — two overwatch pairs, suppressors hooded, stalker barrels trained on the vent mouths the briefing had flagged. The **noble-shaft** above the warrant table had read **cold** on auspex until it did not. House assassins used a cut grav-chute and a seal the public roll had hidden; black pauldrons saw the heat bloom too late to stop the drop, not too late to **map** where the shaft breathed afterward.

### Corbec

Vent grates coughed dust. A kill-team of house **ambush assassins** — sump-trained, grav-chute slings cut — fell through the dark in a spread pattern meant to land on the warrant table and the man beside it. The lead carrier wore a shaped charge on his chest like a lover’s medal.

Corbec did not step away from the planted haft.

He lifted the ***Alabarda*** one hand-width clear of the deck and turned the **hammer face** of the head in a single horizontal sweep — not a flourish, a gauge line. The bomber’s trajectory met steel and **reversed**: the assassin flew end-over-end across the annex, hit the far bulkhead, and detonated in a wet orange bloom that scorched plaster and killed nothing that mattered. The relay spine on the haft never flickered off.

The second fell onto the **hook** and was gone before his monofilament cleared its sheath. The third took the **blade** through the sternum and was pitched into his brothers mid-drop. A fourth tried to slide under the sweep; Corbec’s boot pinned the line, the halberd came down through collar and deck plate together, and the fifth died trying to feint left where the haft was already waiting.

The last one had training enough to **miss** the first arc.

He came down from the rafters alone, blades crossed, aiming for the throat behind the banner. Corbec let the *Alabarda* plant again and caught him **mid-air** with his right gauntlet closed around the skull through cropped hair and sweat-slick skin. The assassin kicked once against ceramite. Corbec closed his fist. The sound was small: a fruit pit in a foundry press. The body twitched, went limp, and was dropped for Arbites to tag.

Marshal Vhal’s clerk did not stop writing. Corbec wiped a smear of blood from his gauntlet on thigh plate, checked the orange wash on the relay, and resumed the lattice as if the annex had only exhaled.

### Shadows

When the Legatus hand closed, black pauldrons moved. They did not congratulate. They **sealed**: melta on the noble-shaft collar, smoke canisters down the branch lines, a stalker bolt through the throat of a second-cut survivor trying to crawl back into the vent — then a **power gladius** through the spine when his fingers still twitched on the lip. The annex above the warrant table became a dead lung. Corbec logged *Legatus threat closed* and never spoke of the half-second late auspex; black pauldrons logged *shaft geometry corrected* and carried the correction into the rest of the hive.

## Phase III — House counterattack

Then the houses spent what they had been saving for **coronation**.

Sump gangs and forge-militias opened the underhive pens. **Mutants** with too many arms and not enough bone came up through freight lifts, driven by foremen who still believed the Castellan would call them **kin** if they bled loudly enough. Contract **Ogryn** in house livery — bought loyalty with protein and brass — took tram avenues in shoulder-to-shoulder blocks, slab shields scavenged from foundry molds, voices reduced to one syllable: **push**. **Chem-altered** pit fighters from illegal arenas moved faster than human, pupils blown white, skin lacquered with combat stim until hearts would rupture an hour later; they did not need an hour. In the shrine warrens, **unsanctioned psykers** the noble chaplains had hidden from Tithe inspection woke the air wrong — nausea in the gut, blood taste on the tongue, plaster dust crawling up walls as if gravity had become a suggestion.

On Corbec’s overlay, cobalt markers flared along the ward spine. The *Alabarda* relay carried one Haruspic line in flat telemetry, not prayer:

— Shrine ward live. Nullity forward. Stasis hold bridge until cobalt clears.

The rabble had thought PRIMA’s advance was the end of hope. They mistook it for the **beginning of their proof**.

### Shadows

Illegal arenas had bled into the manufactoria years before the purge — house coin, guild pride, bodies altered until they were consumables. When Vespera’s counterattack opened the pits, **chem-altered** fighters spilled upward through maintenance spines. Overwatch pairs bracketed each spine mouth: stalker bolts at the neck seam, no mag-dump, no warning shout.

One pit champion, veins black under the skin, made it to a coolant alcove with a monomolecular shiv. A Shadows brother descended on rappel line, smoke canister first, **power gladius** second — point finding the spine through stim-rigid muscle behind the collar seam, boot driving the corpse into the sump without ceremony.

A steward in **servitor coveralls** — correct grease, wrong gait — tried to reach a magistrate pict-blind after the plaza feed showed PRIMA instead of a crown. Shadows **appeared** one junction ahead: stalker bolt through the wrist; **power gladius** through the ribs when he turned to run. The coveralls were house issue under the grease.

A “medicae” team pushed through a riot pocket toward wounded civilians. Overwatch saw the kit — no blood on the whites, plasma packs on the wrong side for Noviomagus triage doctrine. Shadows dropped the lead “medic” from a gantry with a heavy bolt pistol shot dialed to **quiet**, then melta-sealed the hatch so the pocket could not receive a second wave. Line medicae arrived afterward and did the actual triage.

Shadows mapped three promethium cache points in the first hour — shrine door, tram locker, school-slate storage bin. Melta charges kissed each seal in **small** burns timed to Corbec’s lattice. When a gang hand tried to trigger a manual fuse from a ladder, a black pauldron **sawed** the ladder rungs out from under him with short **combat-knife** strokes through the welds and let him fall into his own cache’s warmth.

In the **manufactorum fork**, Sergeant Voss held kill-team **Alpha** in the open while ivory marked a steward amber on the overlay. The steward slipped into a side duct. He came out the other junction into a kill-box Shadows had **already** measured: suppressed bolt through the knee; second bolt through the mouth when he tried to scream Alaric’s name into the dark. Arbites tagged the body; Voss moved east. On Corbec’s slate the fork ticked **green**. **Priced**, not pursued.

### Stasis

At **Freight Mouth K-7**, when the underhive pens vomited **mutants** up the lifts, Stasis sealed the mouth with **scuta** while line brothers cut lift power from the flank. The first abhuman wave hit the shields and recoiled. Stasis opened a **kill slot** for three seconds: power mauls, gladius thrusts, bolt pistols mag-locked to shield rims firing semi-auto into kneecaps and spines. The slot closed. The wave broke into runoff that **Shadows** hunted in side ducts — **combat knives** in the dark, **power gladii** when a mutant turned in the squeeze and ceramite needed a thrust, not a scream. Corbec logged *abhuman auxiliaries contained* and kept the labor capture lane south **green**.

On **Track Four**, black pauldrons killed stewards in the upper gallery first — stalker bolts through las-designators and incendiaries, **power gladius** through the house officer’s throat before the coded rod could flash — so the Ogryn would charge blind. Corbec’s relay marked the gallery **clear** six seconds before the block hit the floor.

Gunmetal shields locked into **scuta** across the tram geometry before the first slab shield touched ceramite. Three Ogryn, house brass on their brow bands, bellowing a lord’s name that was not Terra’s. Stasis did not give ground. Gravitic boots bit deck plate until the tram rails sang; power mauls answered mass at knee, ankle, hip. A gladius found the lead Ogryn’s throat when the slab shield dipped one finger-width too low. Line riflemen fired semi-auto into gaps the shields opened for one second at a time. Corbec’s relay marked the block **cleared** in forty-one seconds.

One Stasis brother died when a slab edge caught his vambrace seam and cracked the arm beneath. His partner stepped into the gap before the body finished falling. The squad logged the loss on the *Alabarda* tally before the tram lights changed.

### Nullity

**Nullity** took the shrine ward because it had to be taken **right** — not last in importance, last in **permission**. Bolts before clearance could scatter witch-signatures into the mesh. Flame could turn a body into evidence ash.

Five **Batavian Haruspices** advanced in the lattice’s wake — force staves grounded, third eyes shuttered behind *Axioma Rationis* discipline. One tasted the spike on the secure band:

— Primary mark, female, house-trained, attempting corridor unmake. Secondary choir, latent, behind the icon wall. Do not give them a name on open vox.

The shrine warrens had been built for Imperial liturgy once. Vespera’s noble chaplains had **repurposed** them — forge soot in bronze bowls, prayer cards with Alaric’s wolf echo where the Aquila should sit, plaster dust crawling in the corner of mortal sight.

Cobalt brothers planted **cognitive hazard beacons** at the ward mouth. Civilians who followed witch-panic surged toward the bridge; gunmetal turned them back. Inside, Nullity moved.

The **secondary choir** — twelve lay worshippers humming the lullaby wolf-banner hymn — broke when a Haruspic ran **Blindgate occlusion** across the entry arch. Brothers webbed the latents one by one; two who seized were sedated; the rest went to Arbites transfer. The ring was **fuel**, not the fire.

She waited in the inner corridor — woman in house silk, Kethmar’s colors under soot, eyes milk-white, blood running from her nose as she tried to **unmake** the obedience of anyone who entered. Nullity cells drove **null rods** into the choke points; the air went flat. She screamed without sound. The Haruspic spoke one line of **Blindgate**: *You do not have purchase here.* Her knees broke posture first, then her mind. Nullity finished with **gladius** — not bolt, not flame. An Arbites clerk began the witch-mark custody chain before the body cooled.

Behind the icon wall: reliquary wax, gene-wrought thread, a lesson card soaked in promethium scent. Haruspices filed anomaly class **Kethmar-bled**.

**Residual wash** took longer than the primary mark. Nullity brothers walked the tiles with auspex wands and null rods, hunting after-images. When the ward read clean on three consecutive Haruspic passes, the relay ticked cobalt **green**. Corbec logged *psy-threat suppressed — shrine warrens*.

### Stasis

Stasis had held the **shrine bridge** since cobalt first flared. Corbec severed detonation leads from the lattice; gunmetal refused the crowd that tried to surge across “to escape the witch.” When Nullity finished in the ward beyond, Stasis marched over the bridge without looking at what remained on the shrine tiles. The hold had done its work.

### Silence

In Sump Concourse Nine, a guild mouthpiece preached that the Wall would **stop** if enough faithful died **visible**. A Silence brother broke his jaw with the carbine stock before the Arbites clerk finished the custody chit. A second mouthpiece prayed in High Gothic **too perfect** — tagged, webbed, one bolt through the left eye when he spasmed against restraints. Corbec’s relay ticked: *Sump Nine — voice nodes down.*

Silence switched vox-scrambler pouches on the local relay spire. Open channels thinned until only work-order grammar remained. A foreman whispering *rightful ruler* into a dead handset had been tagged by ivory; Shadows found him in a coolant niche twenty minutes later — handset fused to his palm by melta discipline, throat opened with a **combat knife** when he tried to bite through the burn.

In Manufactorum Band C, a pay-clerk wore correct forge overalls and wrong hands — no calluses, smile held one half-second too long. Behind a false panel: house ledgers, wolf-banner stencils still wet. He died reaching for a stub-pistol in the ledger spine. Six minutes of quiet that let Voss’s **Alpha** keep the manufactorum spine open one junction east.

In Vault Cluster **M-12**, Silence **mapped** fled assemblies: chalk on pipes, sample bags, a voice recorder on a hidden vox relay cycling the wolf-banner hymn. An adolescent straggler was webbed alive as *witness*, not *mouthpiece*.

At the **lower docks**, ivory seized a lithograph press stamping wolf echo beside the Aquila, impounded the plate, broke a distributor’s hand when he called them *traitor to the Wall*, and returned to the queue before panic became choreography.

## Phase IV — Line purge and manufactoria sweep

### Line kill teams

Where the threats were merely human — gang clusters with las-cutters, militia with stolen autoguns — **line kill teams** did the work Shadows and Silence had priced.

Sergeant **Voss** led **Alpha** through the manufactorum spine in interlocking kill geometry Corbec had copied from SECVNDA’s measured-line registers. Corbec’s voice on the relay:

— Alpha, left fork — two shooters, partial cover. Semi-auto. Hands and weapons.
— Alpha, hold — Silence tags moving through your rear.
— Alpha, clear fork — labor queue east.

Voss’s team took eleven armed stewards in nine minutes and did not fire once into a crowd that had thrown itself flat with wolf cloth still in fist. When a mutant tide surged a side corridor — four bodies fused at the ribcage — Voss burned one with melta at the choke, dropped two with bolter pairs, let the fourth reach arm’s length so the support brother drove a gladius through the primary spine and pinned it to the deck for Arbites logging.

### Stasis

In the lower manufactorum tier, civilians flooded side corridors trying to outrun the sweep. Corbec’s lattice drove movement into containment channels. Where stampede outran sorting crews, **Stasis** sergeants ran shock-lines, shield walls, and tram-gate drops until panic learned the wall’s direction.

### Line companies

Where armed clusters refused disarm order, line brothers cut them down where they stood; Stasis did not pursue past a choke that had not turned green on the overlay.

In Boiler Ward Seventeen, a rebel steward detonated his own ration queue when asked to surrender records. Fifty-seven civilians died under collapsing grain hoppers and burning promethium mist. The steward was recovered in pieces and catalogued as "self-expended command material."

In Hab Court Twelve, a rebel catechist preached that the Castellan would **vindicate** them at the last hour. He spoke for four minutes before a PRIMA marksman removed him mid-sentence. His followers surged the lane shouting Alaric’s name as plea until Corbec’s choke geometry answered with bolter discipline. Thirty-two died at the choke gate still believing recognition had been one breath away.

By the time the counterattack broke, Vespera’s gangs had learned: the Castellan was not coming to crown them; *PRIMA* was not one squad but a **machine**; and the orange pulse on a distant relay meant Corbec had already assigned their deaths before they finished screaming Alaric’s name.

## Phase V — Shrine district: final bridge

The last organized resistance in the lower hive ended where rebel stewards had chained refinery workers to process columns and threatened to vent promethium lines if PRIMA crossed the final bridge.

### Stasis

**Stasis** had held the near abutment since the manifold first showed heat. Gunmetal shields turned the bridge approach into a funnel — no civilians across, no stewards back, no second surge once the witch-ward began to taste wrong. Corbec held detonation leads from the synchronization lattice; the bridge deck did not flex.

### Nullity

**Nullity** held the ward mouth behind the abutment. Cobalt brothers in **Oblivion** cells maintained null-rod geometry along the promethium spine — witch-signatures **liked** martyrdom, and chained civilians were fuse wire. A Haruspic walked the manifold in sealed warding. When he nodded once, Corbec knew the fuse was **mechanical** again, not poisoned.

### Silence

**Silence** had been on the district for an hour. Spotters crouched in gantries, reading **hands** not faces — which palm rested on a dead-man stud, which “medic” carried kit weight wrong for medicae. They fed Corbec numbers; they did not shoot until the center-axis team moved.

A PRIMA brother took the left steward at the wrist before the palm could close. A second took the false medic’s elbow until the detonator key fell. The right steward tried to scream Alaric’s name into the speaker loop; an ivory spotter drove a power gladius through the speaker housing, then through his throat.

The bridge did not vent.

### Nullity and Silence — aftermath

Workers were cut free, triaged, and sorted by labor index. Nullity screened clusters for **residual wash** and flagged eleven for *Medicinae* observation. Silence swept the crowd for the **too calm** face — three tagged for penal review. The shrine speaker was removed as evidence; a cobalt brother burned the housing with a melta kiss **small** enough not to trip the manifold.

Haruspices logged the district **clean** at shift end. Corbec closed the shrine file.

Some cried from relief. Some cursed the Astartes anyway. Some stood in shock, staring at the corpses of men who had called themselves liberators and had used chained civilians as fuse wire.

## The red hour

Above the red decks, the house spires heard the same sirens and mistook them for the prelude to coronation.

The avenue drains clogged in the first hour. By the second, blood ran down the hive’s service slopes in thin red sheets. By the third hour, Vespera looked red from a distance — not metaphor, not poetry, red because enough people had bled into its channels that color became infrastructure.

Night-cycle maintenance crews worked through it with filter masks. Pict-captures from municipal towers were reclassified from civic archive to punitive evidence. No footage was kept for mourning. It was kept for doctrine: how fast language becomes insurgency, how quickly adoration mutates into treason when obedience to the Imperium is replaced by obedience to a person.

By second hour-cycle, the lower decks had learned what each pauldron meant: ivory removed your voice from the mesh; black owned the ceiling; gunmetal chose which way your body moved; cobalt made the air **flat**. The brutality was procedure that happened to leave bodies — and, where the lattice allowed, **names** written down before the bodies cooled.

## Alaric’s column — spire tiers (parallel track)

While Corbec’s lattice ran the lower hive, the Castellan climbed toward the house compounds. Two **Nullity** brothers screened the spire chapels — reliquaries, private shrines where wolf echo had been pressed into gold leaf. They found no second primary mark after the warrens were cleared; they found **latent tools**: censers used to focus choir breath, ledgers of “prayer attendance” that were attendance for sedition. The tools were impounded. Alaric did not pause his march. Nullity filed *spire seals intact* and descended when the noble work was done.

---

The noble houses attempted negotiation when Corbec’s outer districts were already drowning in red.

They were not shocked that he had come. They were shocked that he had come **without accepting** — and that the screaming below was not theater.

House envoys met him first in the plaza of broken statute under white signal cloth, Arbites clerks flanking the Castellan’s column in black Lex order. The envoys brought bribe cases open on the stones before any weapon was drawn: ore ledgers, forge-right seals, the crown commission scroll with **Castellan-King of Noviomagus** already inked in house calligraphy, waiting only his thumb. The senior envoy — House Kethmar’s mouthpiece, not the patriarch — spoke as if closing a contract, not begging for lives.

— Lord Castellan, we have cleared your path. Terra’s clerks are the corruption. Take the title we forged in your honor and Vespera stands loyal under one Wall.

A second envoy unrolled a tithe abatement worth three decades of production and laid it beside a gene-wrought reliquary in a null-field case.

— Accept sovereignty and the purge ends before noon. Refuse, and you waste blood you do not need to spend.

They had believed, to the last, that Corbec’s slaughter was **performance** — rabble spent to satisfy Arbites writ while the houses purchased the throne Alaric had already been given in street song. Shock moved through the envoys as inventory when the Castellan did not reach for the scroll: the wrong line item in a ledger that had never contemplated refusal.

Alaric looked at the crown commission once. He did not touch the reliquary.

— You have confused idolatry with obedience.

He gave no second line.

The envoys were turned back with their guards disarmed and logged. Judge-Militant Serix read the warrant for **house-head judgment** over hard vox in full Lex form — no extrajudicial claim, no hidden tribunal. Then the patriarchs were summoned: not negotiators, but the men who had signed the sedition ledgers.

Seven house heads answered the summons in ceremonial carapace they had worn to weddings. They came still believing the audience was **coronation**.

Alaric met them in full plate — gauntlets closed, helm on, Visage protocol unbroken.

Arbites recorded the act as deliberate exhibit: not a blade, not a bolt, but the Castellan’s own armored hands doing what law asked in witness sight. He did not speak during the killings. He broke necks through ceremonial gorgets, crushed throats under ceramite knuckles, drove skulls into marble with gauntlet blows until the plaza stones were slick and the remaining patriarchs could not pretend this was negotiation. It took less time than the envoys’ bribery speech. When the last head of house stopped moving, Alaric had a clerk wipe his gauntlets and signed the seizure docket without ornament.

Below, Corbec’s vox traffic never paused. Above, the message was complete: the Castellan was not their king. He was the man who would unmake them with his fingers if law asked it.

Minor nobles — cousins, cadet branches, oath-bound knights of house retinues — were not given the plaza. Arbites chains and *Medicinae* sedation moved them straight to Mechanicus conversion rigs in the former judicial annex. Every rig had a queue column. Every queue column had a house identifier. No banner heir left the spire tiers with a name that could be spoken in public.

**Familiars** were not spared.

Adult kin of noble blood — aunts, uncles, cousins who had eaten at house tables and worn house colors — went to the same servitor queues as minor nobles. The mandate was explicit: no parallel bloodline left to romanticize treason.

**Children** were sorted by viability. Those young enough for reallocation and not flagged as latent command risk were taken under Arbites transfer writ to off-world Imperial institutions — Schola, orphanage manufactoria, Administratum feeder houses — after **hypno-doctrination** that burned one lesson into sleep architecture: their bloodline meant **shame and treason**, their house names were dead law, loyalty existed only through Terra’s chain. The treatment was not mercy. It was disposal with future utility.

**Servants and household staff** — cooks, valets, bodyguards who had eaten house salt — were judged on denunciation. Those who had filed no warning to Arbites or loyalist channels before Declaration Night were stripped to penal labor cohorts and **penal battalions**: they should have denounced their masters and had not. Those who had — rare, and usually coerced — were retained under new seals at reduced function, always watched.

No exemptions for bloodline.
No exemptions for age among adults.
No exemptions for familiars who called themselves innocent by proximity.

In Batavi law, a noble who turns a city into a doctrine weapon does not remain a citizen. He becomes material — and everyone who ate at his table becomes evidence.

Mechanicus liaison crews had arrived with portable conversion rigs before nightfall. Some minor nobles attempted last-minute religious appeal once shackled to the conveyor rails, shouting Imperial prayers they had mocked in private chambers three cycles earlier. The adepts did not answer. The saw-arms did not pause. A prayer timed to machinery is still only sound.

Every noble title in Vespera was voided by noon-cycle.
Every noble estate was seized by evening.

No interpretation was permitted after the shrine bridge closed. The city would be told what happened in numbers.

The numbers were posted at every cargo elevator by next shift: executed in resistance, killed in collateral collapse, transferred to penal labor, designated for servitor conversion, retained for immediate industrial continuity. A second slate — ivory-sealed, smaller font — listed **cells named**, **mouths removed**, **vaults mapped**, and **lesson nodes burned**; a third — black-tabbed, almost never posted in public — counted **ducts sealed**, **chem nodes burned**, and **false flags removed**; a fourth — gunmetal-tabbed, filed behind Arbites counters — listed **chokes held**, **mass blocks broken**, and **bridge spans cleared without flex**; a fifth — cobalt-tabbed, *Librarium*-sealed, almost never shown to civilians — listed **primary marks dead**, **choirs dispersed**, **wards read clean**, and **residual wash cases observed**. Most citizens did not understand those ledgers, but they understood the pauldrons still walking the queues afterward, and they understood why maintenance hatches began to arrive welded shut. Citizens read the public totals in silence while line marines watched from gantries above, orange lenses tracking crowd behavior for precursors of second-wave agitation — and **Silence** walked the same gantries without orange wash, listening for the hymn that tried to start again under breath.

Below the totals sat a final row in smaller script:

UNCOUNTED: BODIES UNRECOVERABLE FROM MELT ZONES, CHOKED DRAINS, OR STRUCTURAL COLLAPSE.

No one asked how large that number was. In Vespera, if a number could not be produced, it meant someone had become process waste before accounting could catch up.

Some families returned to those postings for nine cycles, searching lines and totals for missing names that never appeared. By cycle ten they stopped coming. The board remained anyway, because punishment in Noviomagus did not end when fear peaked. It ended when memory was forced into administrative shape.

---

Punitive normalization began before smoke had cleared.

Cadastre teams entered with armed escort and rebuilt Vespera’s population map by district, function, and utility. Surviving civilians were assigned in three bands:

retained labor,
penal labor,
or removal to out-hive reclamation works.

Every resident was marked.
Every ration line was rerouted.
Every transit permit was reissued under new seals.

The old district names were retained only where production value justified historical continuity. Three sectors were redesignated by output class alone: Furnace-Labor Band, Penal Reclamation Band, and Corrective Habitation Band. Civic identity was replaced by function index. The city map became a balance sheet.

Three million paper records were pulped in the first week. Family registries were reduced to labor capability tags. Marriage permissions were frozen pending output review. Travel between sectors required stamped work justification and biometric verification. In place of neighborhood councils, each block received a quota marshal and two armed clerks. Governance was no longer discussion. It was throughput.

Curfew bells were recalibrated to shift rhythm rather than civic custom. Old feast days were deleted from factory calendars and replaced with maintenance overhauls. Shrine offerings were rationed by production compliance, and any district that failed output for two consecutive cycles lost incense allocation and heating supplements until quotas recovered.

In corrective schools, instructors replaced civic rhetoric exams with compliance mathematics: route timing, ration arithmetic, failure penalties, and legal chain-of-command logic. Essays were removed from curriculum for three years on the grounds that interpretive language had recently proven hostile to stability. Children learned that ambiguity was a luxury districts with blood in their drains no longer had. **Silence** maintained rotating presence in those schools — not as punishers, as **listeners**: scanning slates for wolf-echo doodles, comparing handwriting drift to pre-purge lesson cards, plucking tutors who tried to smuggle devotion back in through mathematics word problems. The cohort filed those catches under *recurrence prevention*. Vespera would not be allowed to teach sedition twice in the same room.

The servitorization convoys left by night-cycle under blackout banners.

Minor nobles and adult familiars who had spent decades consuming labor became labor machinery by dawn. The seven house heads never entered the queues — they were pulp on plaza stone. Process logs listed conversion yields, interface stability rates, and projected output gains for six fiscal cycles. No log recorded the old names except in strikeout columns under legal archive.

The converted units were reassigned with deliberate symbolism. Cadet knights and council jurists of reduced rank were mounted in ore presses and refuse compactors at the same docks where house banners had once been paraded. House archivists were processed into index servitors and chained to records bays where they spent eighteen-hour cycles labeling the destruction of their own dynastic files. The Chapter did not advertise those assignments. It did not need to. Word moved faster than any sanctioned bulletin.

Children taken under hypno-doctrination left on Administratum transports without house names on their manifests — only viability codes and destination seals. Servants condemned to penal battalions marched in the same week under different banners: no crest, only labor index and Arbites overseer.

Vespera’s unofficial councils were erased from map rolls.
Their halls became storage.
Their printing rooms became penal intake.
Their icon walls were pressure-washed until stone showed through.

The **shrine warrens** were not reopened for worship. Nullity and *Mechanicus* fume crews sealed the ward mouths with cog-hash welds; Haruspices left passive beacons in the walls that would scream on the *Haruspicium* net if witch-signatures ever climbed again. House chaplains who had fed the choirs were already servitor stock or dead. The hive learned that some doors stayed closed not from grief, but from **procedure**.

At central square, the old civic motto was cut away and replaced with a forged plate carrying one sentence in standard command script:

ASSETS ARE TO BE MAINTAINED, DISCIPLINED, AND EXPENDED BY LAW.

That sentence was read over loudhailers to every district at shift change for thirteen cycles.

On cycle fourteen it was no longer read. It was assumed.

No one in command pretended it was kindness.

It was care in the Batavi sense:
food issued to keep output stable,
medical intervention to prevent waste,
discipline applied without illusion that affection cancels ownership.

The population was not loved less because it was treated as assets.
It was loved as assets: counted, repaired, protected, and broken when it threatened the whole.

On Noviomagus, that distinction was not contradiction.
It was policy.

And policy had custodians. In the weeks after the purge, audit teams from outside the hive arrived to verify quotas, mortality baselines, and conversion efficiencies. They did not carry swords in Vespera’s streets. They carried seals and legal authority, and they confirmed that no local commander had exceeded punitive mandate or diluted it into sentimental fraud. The purge had no heroes, only compliance reports.

Marshal Vhal’s office signed the final supervisory addendum with a line that entered Noviomagus legal teaching cycles: "Personal devotion to command authority is not legal loyalty." In practical terms, the city had tried to enthrone a protector and, by that act, declared itself outside the Imperium’s chain.

Children **born in Vespera after** the purge learned the city in this order: block number, ration tier, work assignment lineage, prohibited phrases. They did not inherit house names because there were none left with legal standing on-world. They inherited output obligations and medical maintenance schedules.

Children **of the houses** who survived infancy long enough for transfer carried a different sentence off-world: hypno-burned shame, no recoverable crest, usefulness to the Imperium only after their bloodline had been administratively killed. In Batavi jurisprudence both outcomes were stability — one local, one exported — and stability was mercy in a universe where most hives received only famine and fire.

Adults who remembered the old order adapted in quieter ways. They stopped speaking full sentences in tram queues. They used hand taps for dissent and coughed over forbidden names in market rows. By the second year, even those habits had thinned. Going quiet proved cheaper than being heard by ivory.

The **Silence Cohort** did not leave when the red washed off the drains. Fifty brothers became twenty, then twelve — enough to keep the mesh honest. They were the reason a forbidden phrase died in a throat when an ivory pauldron entered a tram car, even when no bolt followed.

The habits that remained were mechanical: checking stamp dates twice before crossing sectors, lowering voices near ventilation grilles, pausing instinctively when sirens changed tone. These behaviors were not taught in manuals. They were learned the way scar tissue learns weather.

---

When the purge closed, Alaric filed the final summary with no ornament. Corbec filed the lower-hive casualty ledger separately; the two documents were stitched by Arbites into one legal record.

Secession cell dismantled.
Hive command caste replaced — house heads dead by Castellan judgment; minor nobility and adult familiars servitorized; house children reallocated under hypno-seal; household servants to penal labor or battalions.
Lower population purged under PRIMA geometry.
**Silence** annex: **cells named**, **vaults mapped**, **lesson nodes burned** — recurrence watch ongoing.  
**Shadows** annex: **duct geometry corrected**, **chem pits scorched**, **noble-shaft sealed** — upper mesh patrol reduced but not withdrawn.  
**Stasis** annex: **Track Four cleared**, **K-7 mouth sealed**, **shrine bridge held** — one brother KIA, choke pins replaced across six districts.  
**Nullity** annex: **Kethmar-bled mark terminated**, **latent choir dispersed**, **shrine warrens sealed**, **spire chapel tools impounded** — residual watch twelve cycles.
Penal outputs integrated.
City function restored.

No victory declaration followed.
No remembrance liturgy was offered for the rebel dead.

There was, however, one public recital at shift bell in every surviving district. Not a eulogy: a reading of the revised civic articles - obligation to Terra, obligation to tithe, obligation to work, prohibition on private cults of command personae, and automatic penal transfer for repeat civic-theological agitation. The recital lasted six minutes and repeated for ninety cycles.

At the end of each recital, one additional line was read in Vespera only, added by direct command seal:

LOYALTY TO THE WALL EXISTS ONLY THROUGH LOYALTY TO THE THRONE.

By the thirtieth cycle, most residents mouthed the words before the loudhailers finished. By the ninetieth, children could recite the article chain by memory while sorting scrap wire at school benches. Language had been retooled. This, too, was a form of reconstruction.

After ninety cycles the loudhailer recitals ceased, but the phrase architecture stayed in speech. Petition clerks reported that even complaints now arrived in compliance syntax: citizens no longer wrote "this is unfair." They wrote "output risk increases under current ration distribution." Suffering translated itself into usable grammar.

Vespera resumed production three days later at reduced capacity, then climbed toward prior quotas under armed supervision and hard ration law.

From orbit, the hive looked darker where tower glass had blackened, and redder where fresh anti-corrosion coatings had been laid over blood-stained metal.

The city lived.

Not because it had been forgiven.
Because it had been dismantled and rebuilt in place, with every surviving body assigned value and every disloyal structure converted into a tool.

That was the Batavi answer to rebellion in the mature post-Vigil era:

not argument,
not reconciliation,
not theater of mercy,
but a purge so complete the hive turned red, then an accounting so exact the red became another line item in the cost of keeping the Wall loyal to Terra.

Vespera learned the lesson and kept breathing.

It breathed through filters controlled by others, through schedules written by men it feared, and through labor lines that left little room for romance about freedom or kingship.

That, too, was part of the sentence.

Years later, inspectors would call Vespera a model of recovered productivity. They would praise its low sabotage rates and high compliance metrics, noting with bureaucratic satisfaction that cult-devotion incidents had dropped below neighboring hive averages.

No report could quantify the silence that settled over its plazas at dusk, when shift sirens died and people looked at the red-streaked metal skin of their city and remembered, without saying it aloud, the day it had been made to relearn what law meant.

In the tenth year after Red Harvest, Strategium analysts logged Vespera at twelve percent above pre-secession output while medicae annexes still reported elevated panic signatures during siren shifts and security drills. The city functioned better than before and slept worse than before. Both outcomes were accepted as efficient.

There were no monuments for Red Harvest in Vespera. The closest thing to memorial was an old drain gate in Lower Magistrate Lane that still rusted dark no matter how often crews repainted it. Workers passed it on shift change without pausing. In a city rebuilt as an asset ledger, remembrance that interrupted throughput had no sanctioned place.
