# Alabarda — weapon to relay (post-heresy conversion)

The problem after Malakor V was not that the Chapter lacked symbols.

It had too many.

Every corpse becomes a symbol if you allow the living to stare at it long enough. Every name becomes a litany if you repeat it in formation. Every victory becomes a hymn if you let relief turn into devotion.

The Strategium understood this before the rest of us did. Not as wisdom. As fear with a filing system.

PRIMA had to carry the shame register, because PRIMA had been the vector. But PRIMA could not be allowed to carry it like a trophy skin. If shame becomes an emblem you show off, it turns into a different kind of pride.

Pride was what opened the door.

So the Chapter did what it always does when the human mind starts building dangerous myths.

It built a tool instead.

The halberd I carried on Malakor V was not a relic then. It was an officer weapon pattern, kept in the rack because reach and hook geometry solve problems bolters cannot solve cleanly. It had no relay. No banner holder. No ceremony weight.

It was honest steel.

After the heresy was broken — after the sentence had been delivered and the theater’s blood-price filed — Armourium took the halberd away with paperwork that made it look like routine maintenance.

It was not routine.

They stripped the haft down to its core, rebuilt it around a relay spine, and carved a housing that could carry signal through the same shaft that could still break bone. They added the banner holder not as decoration, but as an enforced reminder that PRIMA does not get to move through the mesh as if Malakor V were only a story that ended.

The conversion was a doctrine act.

The same tool that killed the butcher became the tool that signaled the debt.

Not by speech. By physics.

When the relay is active, the shaft’s vibration changes. You feel it through your gauntlet. The weapon becomes heavier in a way that is not material weight — an insistence. A reminder that the Fourth Field is not a triumph panel. It is an obligation panel, and the obligation does not belong to the bearer alone. It belongs to the lattice.

I saw the finished weapon once, when it was first logged.

Not in a parade. Not in a ceremony. In an Armourium bay with oil on the deck and light too bright for comfort. A tech-serf held it like an instrument that could kill him by accident if he breathed wrong.

The relay spine ran up the haft like a surgical implant.

The banner holder sat on the upper third, a quiet clamp waiting for cloth that would never be allowed to read like a slogan.

The halberd head remained functional — edge maintained, hook geometry intact.

Weapon and signal. Sentence and warning.

That is what PRIMA became after Valerius.

Not forgiven.

Not redeemed.

Regulated.

Later, when I carried it as *Legatus*, I did not think of it as mine.

Tools do not belong to men.

They belong to the work that needs doing.
