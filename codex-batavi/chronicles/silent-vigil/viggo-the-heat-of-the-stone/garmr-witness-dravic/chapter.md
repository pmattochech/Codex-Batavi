# [CHRONICLE] GARMR'S SHELF — DRAVIC BEFORE THE JUDEX

**Classification:** *Reclusiam* sealed — Triumvirate access; DECIMA line need-to-know  
**When:** **~055.M42** (same engagement as [`viggo-saga.md`](../seed-in-iron/chapter.md))  
**Where:** Noviomagus Prime — *Reclusiam* annex chamber (after-action confession on Garmr’s Shelf operation)  
**Who:** **Brother-Sergeant Cael Dravic** (DECIMA); **Varro** (Judex)  
**Companion (Judex response):** [`varro-anima-speculum.md`](varro-anima-speculum.md)

---

The chamber had no icons and no warmth. Bare steel, one lumen strip, one black table never used as a table. Dravic stood at attention with his gauntlets unclasped behind his back because clasped hands had started to shake.

Varro did not sit.

— Speak — the Judex said.

Dravic swallowed once. His mouth was dry enough that the motion hurt.

— Captain Graile held the redoubt. Overlap lanes. Stable shelf. Lord Alaric ordered hold and took the ravine alone.

— Your order.

— To hold position, my lord.

Varro’s helm remained still.

— You disobeyed.

— Yes.

No rebuke came. Worse than rebuke: permission to continue.

— I took ridge overwatch with scope. I saw first-tier brood rush, then larger bodies, then the Patriarch line. I saw the Lord Castellan accelerate instead of attrit. Shield and combi-melta were stripped off him under pressure. He kept only the sword. Then he changed gait.

Dravic’s right hand twitched against his own vambrace. He forced it still.

— Four-point movement. Too fast for his mass. Plates flushing red outside heraldry limits. No cadence calls. No fire discipline. Just impact. Tear. Repeat.

Varro took one step closer, a small correction in distance.

— And then.

— Locus bracket. Patriarch push. One strike took the helm.

Dravic stopped. His throat closed for a second.

— Say it — Varro said.

— His face.

The word stayed in the air like a shell that had not detonated yet.

— I saw it for less than a full breath. Enough.

Dravic’s voice dropped lower, not dramatic, just tight.

— Contorted. Not pain. Rage, but not a man’s rage. Jaw overextended, fangs bared, mouth set like it meant to tear through anything that moved. Eyes red-lit enough to leave streaks when he turned. Skin and scar geometry were still his, but everything in that face was bent around kill intent. No speech. No command. Only predation.

Varro did not move.

— Continue.

— Patriarch died almost immediately after. That was the point where my stomach dropped. Not because it died. Because of how easy it was.

Dravic breathed through his teeth.

— Then he howled. My vision narrowed. Hearing flattened. I had sweat running under neck seals in subzero wind. Hand already on gladius, half-draw tension in wrist, no conscious order from me.

— You did not draw.

— No.

— Why.

— Because if I drew, I would die and teach the line the wrong lesson.

For the first time, something like approval entered Varro’s silence.

— And the pup.

— I saw him move toward a white runt under the dead mother. Still in Red Wolf geometry. Left gauntlet came up to strike. He hesitated at commit point and drove the blow into the ground beside it instead.

Dravic’s eyes fixed on a spot over Varro’s shoulder, replaying.

— He stayed over the animal. Did not move. Heat came off his plates hard enough to shift frost. Pup tremor changed. Breath steadied. He looked at the dead pack. Knelt. Dug under the runt with claws still red-lit, lifted it on a chunk of snow and soil so he would not burn it. Recovered shield. Opened heat-routing vanes. Cooled. Then direct lift. Then helm recovery. Sword recovery. Pup into collar slot under helm. Only then did he come to my position.

Varro gave a small nod.

— He gave you an order.

— Return redoubt. Hold. Shift Delta-Seven on six-minute mark.

— You complied.

— Yes.

— Delta-Seven.

— Three stragglers came for collar seam. I took center lane with two pairs. We cut them before contact.

Dravic finally looked straight into the red lenses of the Executor helm.

— After that I stepped in front of him and said DECIMA jargon.

— Say it now.

— We hold, for those who can't.

The chamber went still again.

— His response.

— No words. But he looked at me. Gratitude was there. So was... apology.

Varro’s voice came out flat enough to be law.

— Correct read.

Dravic’s shoulders dropped by millimeters, the first sign of release.

— My lord, request instruction.

— On what.

— How to carry this and stay line-useful.

Varro did not answer at once. When he did, each sentence landed like a measured hammer stroke.

— You carry it by naming it correctly. Not miracle. Not curse-spectacle. Geometry failure and recapture.

— Yes, my lord.

— You carry it by not feeding it to the mess as fear theater.

— Yes.

— You carry it by holding your post when ordered, even after seeing what should not be seen.

Dravic stood a little straighter.

— Yes.

Varro took one final step back, distance restored.

— Record sealed. You remain combat-effective. Tremor is not disqualification. It is memory.

Dravic’s jaw worked once before he could speak.

— Understood.

— Dismissed, Sergeant.

He turned to leave, then stopped.

— My lord.

— Speak.

— I thought I was about to see the Wall fail.

Varro’s lenses held him.

— You saw the cost of keeping it standing.

Dravic left with his hand no longer shaking, though the scar remained where Varro had placed it: not removed, only filed into duty.
