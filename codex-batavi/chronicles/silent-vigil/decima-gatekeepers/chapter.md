# [CHRONICLE] DECIMA GATEKEEPERS

**Classification:** sealed implementation session — limited-custody execution design  
**When:** **~055.M42** (immediately after [`post-voices-legatus-ring/chapter.md`](post-voices-legatus-ring/chapter.md))  
**Where:** Noviomagus Prime — DECIMA drill vault / command lattice annex  
**Who:** **Kadmos**, **Otho**, **Tyvar**, DECIMA execution cadres (restricted), oversight feeds from **Varro** and **Drusus**  
**Cross-references:** [`post-voices-legatus-ring/chapter.md`](post-voices-legatus-ring/chapter.md) · [`old-voices-council/chapter.md`](old-voices-council/chapter.md) · [`../../../personae-command-index/doctrine-and-organs/protocol-access-matrix.md`](../../../personae-command-index/doctrine-and-organs/protocol-access-matrix.md)

The DECIMA drill vault had no audience galleries and no ornament. It was a functional room built for mistakes that could not be afforded in real corridors. Steel partitions formed a rotating maze; ceiling rails carried dummy targets weighted to Astartes mass; floor nodes tracked pressure signatures with enough sensitivity to expose hesitation by half a step.

Kadmos stood at the central console with his bronze arm docked into the command plate. Data streamed in straight bars down the lens in his right eye. Beside him, Otho kept one gauntlet resting on his force stave, not as threat and not as ceremony, only as stable contact with reality while the psychic telemetry feed ran in the edge of his cognition.

Tyvar watched from a side mark, arms behind his back, saying nothing.

DECIMA cadre leaders entered in two files and stopped at the painted line.

Kadmos began first.

— This session is limited custody. You are receiving execution geometry only.

No one moved.

— You are not receiving full doctrine rationale. You do not need it to execute correctly.

One of the cadre sergeants gave a short nod.

— Understood.

Otho stepped forward half a pace.

— You will treat trigger language as machine language. Not prophecy. Not debate. If the signal says tier shift, you move to assigned state. If ambiguity remains, you escalate upward, not sideways.

Tyvar finally spoke.

— Repeat that.

The sergeant answered immediately.

— Escalate upward, not sideways.

— Again — Tyvar said.

— Escalate upward, not sideways.

Tyvar gave one short nod.

Kadmos keyed the first simulation lane. The partitions shifted with a grinding roar, opening a corridor pattern copied from known palace sectors. Amber runes lit on the floor.

TIER II MOCK STATE.

— We are not practicing heroics — Kadmos said. — We are practicing lock discipline.

He fed assignment slices to each squad:

Route denial.
Mag-seal authority.
Power choke points.
Fallback relay.

No one received the whole map.

That was deliberate.

The first run failed in thirty-six seconds.

A squad leader deviated to “assist” an adjacent lane under the assumption that one successful breach would speed aggregate containment. The move opened a blind corner and produced a simulated command leak to the center axis.

Kadmos killed the run and reset the vault.

— That is how chapters die — he said. — Not from insufficient courage. From unauthorized help.

Otho added, voice flat:

— Under stress, minds invent stories to justify disobedience. Do not narrate. Execute.

Second run.

Better.

Third run.

Clean.

On the fourth, Otho injected psychic-noise overlays into the signal stream: false panic bursts, command-code echoes, ghost vox clips with near-valid signatures.

One squad flinched, paused, and asked for confirmation.

Tyvar answered before Kadmos could.

— Delay is acceptable. Drift is not. You asked up-chain. Correct.

The squad resumed and closed its gate line with only four seconds lost.

Kadmos marked the lane green and let it stand.

— Keep that behavior — he said. — Controlled delay is recoverable. Freelance adaptation is not.

By the sixth run, fatigue had started to show in small ways: narrower breathing, longer blink intervals, shoulders tightening before turns. Kadmos reduced chamber heat two degrees and increased signal density instead of calling halt.

— If this ever triggers live — he said — you will be tired already.

The seventh run introduced a moving target weighted to Castellan mass profile. It did not attack. It advanced and issued contradictory command strings through layered vox signatures.

Half the room felt the same reflex at once: obey the voice that sounded closest to old certainty.

Otho cut power to one lane and flooded it with white noise.

— There — he said. — That instinct. File it and cage it.

Tyvar looked at the paused squads.

— Who do you obey when command itself is the hazard.

No one answered.

He answered for them.

— You obey chain law, not memory.

The room went very still.

Kadmos resumed the simulation without commentary.

This time the squads held position, ignored contradictory strings, and executed route denial exactly as assigned. The target reached the locked line and stopped with no open lane available.

Kadmos ended the run.

No applause.
No speeches.

He disconnected his bronze arm from the console and faced the cadre leaders.

— You are gatekeepers, not hunters. If this protocol moves, your first duty is to prevent expansion.

Otho gave the final correction.

— And if you mistake speed for clarity, you will open the path you were built to close.

Tyvar stepped to the center mark and delivered the session’s closing sentence in a tone that left no room for interpretation.

— Your loyalty is measured by whether you can disobey the wrong voice at the right second.

The cadre leaders saluted and withdrew in silence.

When the door sealed, Kadmos and Otho remained at the console while Tyvar reviewed the run logs.

Kadmos spoke first.

— They can hold gates.

Otho did not look up from his telemetry slate.

— Under drills.

Tyvar closed the file.

— Then we continue until it is not drills.

He paused before leaving.

— Next step is codification.

Kadmos inclined his head.

— Varro and Drusus?

— And the Castellan for boundary disclosure — Tyvar said. — Objective only.

Otho gave a thin, unreadable expression and shut down the psychic feed.

— He will understand the reason.

Tyvar answered at the hatch.

— Understanding is not the same as acceptance.

He left.

Kadmos and Otho stayed in the vault with the chamber lights still amber, reviewing gate timings line by line, building a doctrine that hoped never to be used and refused to be unreadable if it was.
