# The Audit of Logic

# Tyvar’s Vigil: Stasis and Nullity in the Cohors Batavorum

**Audit file:** Behavioral observation, task force.  
**Location:** Primary deployment yard, Noviomagus fortress-monastery.  
**Subject:** Shield-Captain Tyvar auditing structural readiness of the Batavi.

For Shield-Captain Tyvar, the loyalty of Adeptus Astartes had always been an unstable variable. Gold is constant; transhuman chimeric flesh is biologically flawed. Common Chapters exhaled pride, dogmatic zeal, or a thirst for glory that invariably verged on lethal insubordination. Yet watching the deployment yard on Noviomagus, the Throne’s emissary found no martial zeal. He found absolute nullity.

Before him, a shock company waited to board assault transports. There were no war-hymns. No clashing gauntlets on shields, no oaths shouted aloud, no prayers to the Emperor. There was only the respiratory stasis of biological machines running at minimum caloric draw. The silence was so dense the only sounds across the vast yard were acid rain dripping on grey ceramite and the synchronized hum of dorsal reactors held at idle march.

Tyvar adjusted his helm’s optical sensors, hunting biometric fluctuation. The **Wolf’s Curse** — the visceral biological degradation that condemned the lineage to monstrosity and irrational predation — should have boiled under those plates. Biology demanded fury. Genetics screamed for blood. Yet the relentless doctrine of the *Axioma Rationis* crushed it in a logical compactor.

The Custodian understood that cutting silence was neither blind devotion nor spiritual peace. It was the structural tension of a spring locked by a magnetic catch. Every Batavi formed there was a vessel of cataclysmic violence, forced to stillness by the mathematics of military discipline and terror of inefficiency. They were not resting; they were denying organic existence until the function of killing was assigned.

Tyvar walked slowly between the ranks. No helm turned to admire his auramite armor. No micro-gesture of reverence or intimidation was sketched by the Batavi. They were implements waiting for the hand that wields the tool.

To an Inquisitor, that absence of religious fervor might smell of deviation or heresy. But to Tyvar, the perfect sentinel, it was the apex of Imperial function. Gold needs no scandalous prayer; gold needs barriers that do not yield. In that necrotic silence, Tyvar audited the Cohors Batavorum and declared it structurally sound. Stasis was their greatest weapon.
