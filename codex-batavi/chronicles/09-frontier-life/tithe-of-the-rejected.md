# Life on the Frontier

# \[CHRONICLE\] THE TITHE OF THE REJECTED

## ACT I: THE ORBIT OF SHAME

### Chapter 1: The Dregs’ Manifesto

The space above Noviomagus Prime was not a void; it was a graveyard of industrial by-products held in suspension.

The upper atmosphere of the Forge World, saturated for millennia with promethium burn and microparticles of iron, acted like a sick prism. The local star’s light, passing through that toxic haze, stained the orbital horizon with the **Yellow Scar**—a halo of chromatic pollution that recalled bile and oxidized metal. Nothing entered that sky without being christened by Noviomagus’s soot.

At the summit of the orbital citadel **Castrum Ferrum**, the Hangar Primus Observation Platform thrust into nothing like the point of an adamant spear. There silence was absolute, held by sonic containment fields that walled off the roar of the macroforges kilometers below.

Lord Castellan **Alaric von Helis** stood motionless beside two meters of armored glass.

He was the embodiment of Batavi doctrine: *Iron within, iron without*. Alaric was fused to the **Castra-Lupus armor**, the apex of Master of the Forge Kadmos’s brutalist genius. The chassis—a colossal hybrid between a Dreadnought and Terminator plate—had no pistons that cracked or servos that groaned under load. Kadmos had designed the Castra-Lupus reinforcement systems for one purpose: to keep the suit from flying apart under the raw force of Alaric himself.

The Lord Castellan’s physiology, refined by *Das Erbe*, gave him agility and physical power that made *Tacticus* armor look like a slow shackle. Inside that mountain of industrial gray (*Mechanicus Standard Grey*), Alaric moved with a heretical lightness, as if he wore only a linen tunic and silk trousers. The armor did not carry him; it tried, desperately, to keep up.

The **Crimson** helm, wolf-snouted, gleamed with yellow lenses that scanned the emptiness. In his hands, the sharp claws were extensions of his own will. Behind him, the red cloak made from the hide of a **Batav Wolf** and his white *Thunderwolf* lay across the crimson shoulder plates where the Wolf with Gladius and the Imperial Eagle marked his lineage.

Through the visor, Alaric watched the tear in reality open ten thousand kilometers out. The **Warp** spat out the fleet *Mercy of Terra*. They were *Bovine-Mass* and *Bulk-Heavers* haulers, rusted hulls leaking radiation and despair.

To Alaric, that fleet was not reinforcement; it was the by-product of a logistical heresy committed by High Command. Waste of assets was a flaw his blood could not ignore. He dwelt in the **Axiom of Reason**, but the predatory instinct in his tendons already thrummed against the inefficiency coming from Malakor.

— **Docking report,** — Alaric’s voice reverberated, modulated through the vox to sound like tectonic plates grinding stone.

Beside him, a logistics servitor trembled under the data flood. — *Identification: Mercy of Terra. Sender: Lord-General Draxus. Cargo classification: “Low-priority biological reinforcement / authorized discard.”*

Alaric did not move, but the temperature on the platform seemed to drop. — **“Discard.”**

He watched the first ship, the *Iron Belly*, begin its approach vector. To General Draxus, it was trash. To Alaric, it was ore to be forged.

Behind him, the hiss of pneumatic doors announced Legate Kestros. The envoy’s synthetic perfume began to foul the sterile air, a dissonant note in the iron symphony of the **Cohors Batavorum**.

Alaric did not turn. He kept his eyes on the Yellow Scar. The Iron Wolf was ready to claim what the Imperium had dared call scrap.

The sound of Legate Kestros’s steps was a rhythmic insult to the platform’s austerity. He walked with the affectation of one who breathed the filtered air of Malakor’s command spires, wrapped in purple silks and a white *grox*-hide cloak that swept the adamant floor, gathering the industrial dust he deemed beneath him. Behind him, two Astra Militarum honor guards stood rigid, but their eyes betrayed instinctive dread: they did not look to the orbital immensity, but to the crimson helm at Alaric’s neck. To them, the Lord Castellan was not an ally; he was a force of nature barely contained by a shell of industrial gray.

Kestros stopped three meters away. The contrast was grotesque. The Legate was a monument to bureaucratic vanity; Alaric was a monolith of lethal efficiency.

— Lord Alaric von Helis — Kestros’s voice was thin, loaded with the polished condescension of the courts. — Lord-General Draxus sends his regards. He regrets the… rusticity of this fleet, but the sector war effort demands every first-grade bolt stay on the front line. We considered that Noviomagus Prime, as a bastion of recycling and forgecraft, could extract value even from what others would call… detritus.

Alaric turned. The motion was so fluid and silent that Kestros stepped back, instinctively. There was none of the hydraulic groan expected of armor that size; only the displacement of air as colossal mass moved with feline precision.

— **Lord-General Draxus confuses logistics with discard, Legate,** — Alaric’s voice, amplified by the Castra-Lupus vox array, thrummed in Kestros’s bones like distant thunder. — **These ships are not transports. They are vectors of failure. The General is not sending reinforcements; he is dumping his administrative incapacity into my hangar.**

Kestros forced a nervous smile, extending a brass *datapad* whose cryptography seals gleamed under the Yellow Scar’s light.

— Let us be pragmatic, my Lord. The Imperium is vast and flesh is cheap. Malakor is being purged of inefficient biomes. The General saw a chance to lighten his ration stores and, at the same time, give you what he described as “**Friction Geometry** materiel” for your most dangerous industrial zones.

Alaric took the device. His armored fingers, ending in short, sharp claws, handled the data-crystal with unsettling delicacy. On his yellow lenses, the cargo manifest unfolded like a litany of misery:

* **Lot A-1 (Ogryns):** 4,000 units. Note: *Unstable aggression. Suggestion: Suicidal shock charge.*  
* **Lot B-4 (Beastmen):** 8,000 units. Note: *Level 3 genetic deviation. Uncontrollable animal instinct.*  
* **Lot C-12 (Nightsiders):** 3,000 units. Note: *Total ocular atrophy. Useless for any duty above the planetary crust.*  
* **Lot D-9 (Longshanks):** 2,000 units. Note: *Bone degeneration. Structural failure at 1G.*  
* **Overall cargo status:** *12% estimated mortality during offload. Biological scabies and systemic malnutrition.*

Alaric read each line, his intellect processing the statistics through the prism of the **Axiom of Reason**. Where the General saw “abominations,” Alaric saw biological components wasted by small minds.

— **General Draxus classifies thirty thousand souls as “biological surplus,”** — said Alaric, returning the *datapad* with a dry motion that nearly tore it from Kestros’s hands. — **He looks at a Nightsider and sees a cripple who fears the light. I look and see an operator who can keep Noviomagus’s deep thermal generators in absolute dark, without spending a single watt on lighting for anyone’s comfort.**

— But they are mutants, Lord Alaric! — Kestros exclaimed, his voice rising an octave. — Dregs of the worst kind! The tithe the General grants you is permission to use them until they expire. You may throw them into minefields or process them as biomass for your servitors. The General does not require survival reports.

Alaric stepped forward. The bootfall did not shake the floor, but the aura of physical threat radiating from the Castra-Lupus made the honor guards move hands to weapons. Alaric tilted the crimson helm, the wolf’s metal muzzle centimeters from the bureaucrat’s pale face. Ozone and cold metal overwhelmed Kestros’s perfume.

— **Tell your General that in Noviomagus the only dregs we recognize is his inability to see an asset’s function,** — Alaric hissed. — **He sent me what he thinks is scrap. I accept the cargo. But from this moment, sovereignty over these lives belongs to the Cohors Batavorum. If a single overseer from your fleet touches any of these individuals with a shock-whip inside my hangars, I will treat that act as sabotage of the Imperium’s war infrastructure. And you know how I deal with saboteurs.**

Kestros paled, his arrogance melting under the yellow glow of Alaric’s lenses. — You… you will give them *rights*? — he stammered.

— **I will give you *Purpose*, Legate. Something your bureaucratic bloodline forgot long ago.**

Alaric turned away, his red cloak cracking the air with the sudden motion. The dialogue was closed. The Iron Wolf had no time left for words; wounded flesh was coming, and iron had to be ready.

He keyed the command vox, tuning the Triumvirate frequency. — **Drusus. Varro. Stand by. Draxus’s fleet brings what they call trash. We will prove it is the iron missing from our Wall.**

Kestros still stammered protests about “purity of human lineage” and “risk of spiritual contamination” as Alaric ignored him, walking toward the platform’s central console with the heretical fluidity of an apex predator. The Chapter Master opened the private Noosphere, a high-bandwidth encrypted data channel that linked the Triumvirate’s minds in a communion of iron and logic.

In milliseconds, Malakor’s cargo manifest was projected onto **Drusus** and **Varro**’s retinas, together with Alaric’s cold, processed verdict through the **Axiom of Reason**.

— **Triumvirate, analyze the asset,** — Alaric commanded. — **The Astra Militarum sent us thirty thousand units of “trash.” I want potential conversion now. Noviomagus does not accept waste.**

The answer came first as biostatistical data and heat flows. Chief Apothecary **Drusus**, in the *Castrum Medicae* laboratory, overlaid medical records on the Chapter’s maintenance and defense needs.

— *Lord Castellan,* — Drusus’s voice on the private channel was like a scalpel through surgical silk. — *General Draxus operates under an aesthetic fallacy. He classifies Longshanks as “degenerates” because their femurs snap at 1G. He does not see that these abhumans’ elongated structure and nerve density, once integrated with Kadmos’s Exo-Spines, will let them reach the macroforge cooling cores where an armored Astartes cannot maneuver. They are not cripples; they are extended-reach laborers. Initiating chemical triage protocols.*

Then the spiritual channel filled with **Varro**’s dark resonance. The High Chaplain was in the Reclusiam, watching through external feeds the latent fury of Beastmen confined in the cargo holds.

— *I feel the dissonance through the ships’ hulls, Alaric,* — Varro transmitted, his voice weighted with Sacred Dread. — *The Beastmen are in confinement pre-psychosis. Draxus sees animals for the slaughter; I see a vanguard that needs no doctrine to hate the enemy, only an anchor so it does not lose itself in the beast. I will give them the discipline of Useful Fear. They will be the herd dog with wolf teeth.*

Alaric turned back to the armored glass. The ships, now only kilometers away, looked like floating wounds in the yellowed firmament. He saw dirty ice plumes—oxygen leaks and waste—gleaming under Noviomagus’s toxic light.

— **The decision is made,** — Alaric declared, sealing the protocol on the console. — **Isolate Hangar 12 and Hangar 14. Activate the Logistical Silence Protocol. No one outside the Chapter gets visual or audio access to this offload. If the Imperium wants to believe we destroyed the scrap, so be it. But what they threw away as dregs, we will forge as the foundation of our next millennium.**

He entered a final command, engaging magnetic docking locks across the sector.

— **Kadmos, ready the Armory. Drusus, saturate the nutrient tanks. Varro, prepare to welcome the beast. Noviomagus Prime is not a discard port. It is the crucible where what was broken becomes iron again.**

Alaric turned to Kestros, still standing there like a confused parasite. The **Castra-Lupus** breastplate gleamed under the window’s amber spectrum. — **Leave my platform, Legate. Tell your General the tithe is accepted and the blood receipt is signed. And pray I never need to audit the utility of your lungs to the Throne.**

Under the crimson helm’s yellow lenses, Kestros beat a retreat, fleeing to his corvette. Meanwhile the first hauler, the *Iron Belly*, began docking procedure. The clang of ship metal against the station’s magnetic clamps echoed like the first hammer stroke on a colossal anvil.

The Iron Contract was being written.

### Chapter 2: The Arks of Mercy

Inside the *Bovine-Mass* hauler designated **“Iron Belly,”** time had been obliterated by the monotony of stagnation and the constant vibration of engines on the edge of collapse. The ship, originally built to haul *grox* cattle in stasis, had been criminally refitted to carry three times its permitted intelligent biomass.

The air in the cargo decks was not merely recycled oxygen; it was a thick, acidic soup of carbon dioxide, acrid sweat, and the metallic sweetness of gangrenous wounds. Emergency lighting—an anemic red strobing in erratic cycles—threw long, trembling shadows on peeling metal walls where the condensation of thousands of breaths ran like tears of soot and rust.

In the center of Cargo Bay 09, the Ogryn called **Nork** sat on the diamond-plate floor.

Nork was a mountain of flesh and muscle, an exemplar of *Homo sapiens gigantus* who, in any other circumstance, would have been the pride of an Astra Militarum shock regiment. Now he was only a hungry, confused giant. His hands, thick as tree trunks, cradled his left arm, which hung at a grotesque angle. The fracture, weeks ago during a ration riot on Malakor, had been “treated” by an Imperial guard with a rifle butt and a dirty canvas bandage now fused to the skin.

— Nork… Nork feels the deck shake — the giant murmured, his voice like an internal combustion engine with sand in the cylinders.

Around him, others of his kind and bands of Beastmen shared the same claustrophobic space. The hold was a barrel of biological powder. The Beastmen, driven by territorial aggression worsened by starvation, snarled in the deep dark, horns clashing on support beams as they fought for every centimeter of space.

— Shut it, big’un — hissed a nearby Beastman, goat-faced, horns filed to fit the hauler’s low ceilings. — If you whine, the white-coats hear. And when they hear, the shock-whip sings.

Nork hunched his massive shoulders, trying to merge with the cold metal wall. He vaguely remembered the “Gold General” who had visited before embarkation. The man had said they would go somewhere they would be “useful.” Nork liked being useful. He liked lifting heavy things and hearing he was a “good lad.” But what he felt now was not usefulness; it was the weight of forgotten scrap in a storeroom.

Suddenly a new sound cut through the whine of failing air scrubbers.

Not the crack of the whip, nor an officer’s shout. It was a deep hydraulic boom from outside the hull, followed by the keening of metal claws tearing vacuum. The *Iron Belly* jerked violently as **Castrum Ferrum**’s magnetic clamps bit the ship with braking force that hurled hundreds of abhumans into bulkheads.

The bay’s red lights died for a second, replaced by cold amber gleam from the seams of the main hatch beginning to depressurize.

— They’re here — the Beastman whispered, sharpening a torn metal plate against the floor. — We die here, or we die in the forge. Ready claws.

Nork rose with difficulty, his broken arm throbbing in time with the engines as they finally fell silent. He stared at the colossal hatch. He did not feel the Beastmen’s hate, only childlike, terrified curiosity. He wanted to know whether, in the new place, the water would be clean. And whether the new master would be big enough not to need a whip to be heard.

What Nork could not see—but Alaric had already decided—was that the giant’s arm would be the first piece repaired in Noviomagus’s new design.

Decompression was not a sigh; it was a scream of tortured metal.

When Hangar 12’s pneumatic seals mated with the *Iron Belly*’s maw, pressure and temperature differential whipped a violent atmospheric phenomenon through the holds. The ship’s foul, hot air was sucked into the station’s vast vacuum chambers, replaced by a blast of sterile, cold air heavy with ozone and weapon oil.

For the **Nightsiders** (*Homo sapiens tenebris*), confined on the ship’s deepest, darkest levels, the moment was sensory apocalypse. Without skin pigment and with bulbous eyeballs evolved to catch bioluminescent fungus-glow, they felt the station’s vibration before they heard it. To them Noviomagus was not a planet; it was a rhythmic, crushing pulse through the soles of bare feet.

— The mountain is talking — whispered **Vren**, a young Nightsider whose skin was so pale blue veins looked like electrical tattoos under the epidermis.

He pressed his hands to his ears but could not block the main hatch opening. Noviomagus’s gear-scream was a bass note that set teeth humming.

The hatch finally gave.

A white condensation fog flooded the hold, hiding what lay beyond. At first, only the heavy silence of expectation. Then the first shock. The hangar’s work lights—powerful industrial lumens—cut the fog like spears of white fire.

The Nightsiders screamed as one. To them that light was not illumination; it was raw radiation burning unprotected corneas. They fell to their knees, covering their faces with filthy rags, skin searing under Noviomagus’s clinical glare.

Amid the panic, Beastmen read the light as a battle signal. They leaped from shadows, snarling, hooves hammering metal, ready to charge anything that crossed the fog.

— They come to burn us! — roared a Beastman, yellow eyes bloodshot with terror.

Nork the Ogryn stayed frozen. His small brown eyes tried to process the hangar’s immensity. He saw support beams like iron gods’ legs, felt the station’s cold wind dry the sweat on his skin. For the first time in months, the air did not taste of death—it tasted of… nothing. A clean emptiness that frightened him more than familiar filth.

Then, from the fog’s heart, a new sound ruled the hangar. Not an officer’s shout or a guard dog’s bark. The rhythmic, heavy, unquestionable tread of ceramite boots on adamant.

The Triumvirate entered the beast’s belly. They brought no chains; they brought the Verdict.

Condensation fog from the hatch began to disperse under deliberate atmospheric pressure. From Hangar 12’s heart, three colossal silhouettes emerged, cutting white vapor like war monuments given life.

Panic inside the *Iron Belly* died at once, replaced by biological shock silence. Beastmen who seconds before had readied a suicidal charge locked in place, predatory instinct screaming a warning their minds could not parse: *The top of the food chain had just walked into the room.*

At the **column’s** center, **Alaric von Helis** advanced. The **Castra-Lupus armor** shone under industrial lumens, industrial gray against the vibrant **Crimson** of his helm and pauldrons. He did not walk like a soldier; he moved with the economy of contained natural disaster. Each step was a declaration of sovereignty. The wolf-hide cloak swung heavy; his gauntlet claws gleamed, ready to tear or rebuild.

To his left, Chief Apothecary Drusus. His chassis kept the cohort’s industrial-gray thermal camouflage, surgical white reserved for his multi-lens tactical helm, heavy med-pack, and right arm mounting the *Narthecium*, marked with the *Apothecarion*’s red heraldry. He reeked of chemical antiseptic that cut the ship’s stench. The *Narthecium* on his arm buzzed, blue-light scanners already sweeping the crowd, cataloging fractures, infections, and vitamin deficiencies in real time.

To the right, High Chaplain Varro. A tower of ebony and static dread. Though armor and heavy cowl were sunk in absolute black, the Executor mask dominating his face was a stripped wolf skull, textured in aged bone and ivory, blood-red lenses glowing in the gloom. His *Crozius Arcanum* radiated latent energy, pulsing like the heart of a dead star. He was the Anchor, Guardian of Reason who imposed order by the brutality of his presence alone.

Alaric stopped at the foot of the embarkation ramp. He did not draw weapons. He simply looked at the mass of wounded flesh and terrified eyes.

— **I AM LORD CASTELLAN ALARIC VON HELIS, MASTER OF NOVIOMAGUS PRIME,** — his voice, projected through the Castra-Lupus amplifiers, was not a shout but a frequency that made the hull itself vibrate. — **YOU WERE SENT HERE TO BE DISCARDED. THE IMPERIUM DECIDED YOUR USEFULNESS HAD EXPIRED.**

He stepped forward, entering the sight line of Nork the Ogryn, trembling on his knees.

— **THE IMPERIUM WAS WRONG.**

Alaric pointed to Hangar 12, where rows of Medicae servitors and triage tables waited under amber light.

— **FROM THIS MOMENT, DRAXUS’S WHIP IS BROKEN. NOVIOMAGUS DOES NOT TAKE SLAVES; NOVIOMAGUS FORGES COMPONENTS. YOU WILL COME DOWN. YOU WILL BE FED. YOU WILL BE REPAIRED.** — His yellow lenses fixed on the Beastman leader still clutching a metal plate. — **BUT UNDERSTAND THIS TRUTH: MY PROTECTION HAS A PRICE. THE PRICE IS IRON IN YOUR BLOOD AND SWEAT ON YOUR BROW. NO ONE DIES IN THIS WORLD WITHOUT MY LEAVE. AND I DO NOT PERMIT WASTE.**

Varro stepped forward, striking the *Crozius*’s base against the deck. The sound was like a hammer on a divine anvil. — ***AXIOMA RATIONIS*: THE TOOL HAS VALUE ONLY IF IT IS KEEN. DESCEND TO THE FORGE. SILENCE IS YOUR FIRST LESSON.**

Nork, feeling terror mixed with a hope he could not name, looked at Alaric’s red helm. For the first time in his life, no giant had called him animal; he had been called *component*. And in the Ogryn’s simple mind, being a component of something as vast as that warrior seemed the noblest thing in the universe.

Slowly, the thirty thousand units began to descend. The tithe was being collected.
## ACT II: THE MAINTENANCE OF FLESH

### Chapter 3: Wave 1 – Taming the Fury

Hangar 12 was not a welcome hall; it was a high-pressure industrial processing zone. As the *Iron Belly*’s ramp touched adamant deck, the mass of flesh and desperation began to spill.

Smell struck first: the rancid musk of Beastmen and ammonia-heavy Ogryn sweat slammed the station’s air scrubbers into a chemical fog of organic stink and ozone. Draxus’s escort guards, still on the ship’s upper levels, tried to hold control the only way they knew.

— **Hold formation! Fall back, you animals!** — a Guard sergeant screamed, his thin, hysterical voice lost in the mob’s roar.

He triggered his shock-goad, arcing blue voltaics into the chest of a hesitating Beastman leader on the ramp. The abhuman roared—not in submission, but in pain and pent fury. The shock’s crack was the trigger. Animal instinct, smothered for weeks in the hold, detonated.

Beastmen surged like a tide of horns and claws. Behind them, Ogryns frightened by noise and sudden violence began to shove, turning the ramp into a crush zone. Seconds remained before chaos became biological massacre that would stain Noviomagus’s hangar.

Alaric stayed motionless, watching Imperial discipline collapse. He did not draw his weapon. Instead he tilted the **Crimson** helm toward Varro.

Noosphere traffic was a maximum-priority binary command: **\[EXECUTE: ANCHORING PROTOCOL\]**.

Varro stepped forward. He did not run; he marched like judgment inevitable. The **Cohors Batavorum**’s High Chaplain was not merely a weapons master; he wielded **Sacred Dread**. When he reached the ramp’s foot, his *Crozius Arcanum*’s energy field roared, throwing pulsing red light and nightmare shadows across the hangar walls.

— **SILENCE.**

The word was not a shout; it was a psychic-acoustic detonation. Varro keyed his black wolf-helm vox to a sub-harmonic frequency shaped to strike panic’s center in the abhuman brain.

The sound-wave hit Beastmen like a physical wall. The charging leader froze mid-stride, yellow eyes dilating as instinctive fear of an apex predator replaced rage. Varro drove his *Crozius* base into the deck; the impact cracked adamant.

— **THE BEAST DOES NOT COMMAND HERE,** — Varro declared, his voice reverberating in every lung present. — **YOU STAND BEFORE THE LAW OF IRON. NOISE IS WASTE. PANIC IS INEFFICIENCY. WHOEVER SNARLS AT THE WALL WILL BE CRUSHED BY IT.**

The silence that followed was absolute and terrifying. Thousands of abhumans, moments ago in frenzy, now stood paralyzed. Varro rotated his helm slowly, red lenses pinning each Beastman, measuring will to live against will to fight.

Fury had been tamed. Now flesh had to be assessed.

With Varro’s silence laid like a shroud over the hangar, organic chaos gave way to clinical order. Chief Apothecary **Drusus** advanced, followed by a **line of** Medicae servitors whose metal wheels whispered against the deck.

Drusus did not see souls; he saw biological diagrams of efficiency and failure. To him the mass of Ogryns and Beastmen was an inventory of damaged assets demanding systemic reboot. The *Narthecium* on his right arm projected scanning lasers that cut the hangar’s amber gloom, lighting infected tissue and misaligned bone in sterile blue glare.

He stopped before **Nork**. The Ogryn was a trembling mountain of flesh, cradling his left arm like a dead fetus.

— **Unit identification: *Homo sapiens gigantus*. Status: active, permanent maintenance,** — Drusus declared. His voice held no human compassion, but absolute clarity the Ogryn read as safety.

Drusus took Nork’s arm. The giant flinched instinctively, a grunt of pain escaping thick lips. — Hold still, — the Apothecary commanded. The tone was not aggression but machine instruction. — Resistance increases repair time. Time is a finite resource.

With the precision of a jeweler running a hydraulic press, Drusus cut the filthy bandage with a monomolecular blade on his arm. Pus and necrotic flesh stank up; Drusus’s armor filters did not even flicker. He studied the exposed humeral fracture, poorly calcified into a twisted mass of bone and scar.

— **Diagnosis: irregular callus. 22% muscular atrophy. Secondary-grade systemic infection,** — Drusus logged to the Noosphere for Kadmos’s records. — **Structure is misaligned. The drive assembly will not function if the chassis is warped.**

Nork looked into the medical giant’s white lenses, tears cutting clean tracks through grime on his blunt face. — Nork… Nork can carry? Nork want be good lad…

— **You will carry what I assign, Unit Nork,** — Drusus replied. He activated a pneumatic injector, slamming a massive dose of analgesics and chemical suppressors into the Ogryn’s neck base. — **But first I will break what was set wrong so Noviomagus’s iron can redo what is right.**

Drusus signaled two cargo servitors. — **Take him to Osteological Reconstruction. Total anesthesia. I want that arm realigned and pinned with titanium fiber in two hours.**

Nork was led away—not by fear of the whip, but by the promise his “chassis” would be fixed. As Drusus moved to the next Beastman, he saw Draxus’s biological tithe had higher regenerative potential than projected. They were not trash; they were resilient organisms that only needed management unafraid to bloody its hands for iron’s perfection.

While Drusus’s medical processing advanced with assembly-line precision, Alaric von Helis remained on his observation platform, a crimson-and-gray sentinel. He watched not only bodies being repaired but souls in transition. The **Axiom of Reason** held that a hungry tool was an unpredictable tool.

Under Alaric’s command, the hangar’s side hatches opened—not to weapons, but to civil logistics distribution tanks. The smell of dense nutrient soup—purified protein compound, Noviomagus grain, mineral additives—began to replace ozone and fear.

— **BEGIN BIOLOGICAL REPLENISHMENT CYCLE,** — Alaric’s voice rang, authoritative and final.

Industrial servitors, guided by civilian logistics workers under **Magda**’s supervision, advanced with great brushed-metal basins. To abhumans used to Draxus ships’ moldy, rationed protein paste, what they saw was an industrial miracle. It was not merely food; it was high-grade fuel.

Beastmen and Ogryns hesitated. Instincts forged in centuries of scarcity and punishment waited for the “price.” They watched human civilians with hunger and paranoia mixed.

— **Eat,** — said Varro, walking the lines, *Crozius* now inactive but still imposing. — **Noviomagus does not invest in empty engines. Every mouthful is a contract of service. By taking this food, you accept the duty that follows.**

A young Beastman, ribs visible through sparse fur, plunged hands into a basin and brought hot nutrient to his mouth. The heat seemed to reboot his nervous system. He stopped, stared at his own hands, then at the black giant before him. No whip. Only the offer continuing.

From the platform, Alaric watched the bio-social data. Aggression levels yielded to chemical satisfaction lethargy. The first barrier—biological distrust—had been breached through the stomach.

— **They eat like animals, but they look like men,** — Alaric commented on the Triumvirate private channel. — **Varro, ensure the Throne gratitude litany is sung during digestion. Drusus, monitor allergic reactions to Noviomagus additives.**

Alaric descended from the platform. His **Castra-Lupus** moved with that disturbing agility, the **Crimson** helm reflecting orange gleam from the soup vats. He stopped before a group of Ogryns eating bread in silence.

— **Noviomagus iron tastes bitter at first, but it braces the spine,** — the Lord Castellan declared. — **Today you are fed as guests. Tomorrow you will eat bread your own sweat helped make.**

Wave 1 was consolidated. Malakor’s biological “trash” now had full stomachs and bodies under repair. But the Wall knew full-belly peace was brief; the next challenge would demand more than soup—it would demand silence and speed.

### Chapter 4: Wave 2 – The Precision Hunt

Wave 1’s processing still echoed through Hangar 12 when the second ship group—light *Haste-Hauler* haulers—mated to the suction rings. If Wave 1 was mass and impact, Wave 2 was instinct and flight.

As soon as the *Shadow of Fortune*’s hatches opened, there was no Beastman roar—only thousands of light feet and claws scraping metal at frantic frequency. **Ratlings** (*Homo sapiens minimus*) and **Felinids** (*Homo sapiens hirsutus*) did not wait for Alaric’s verdict. To them, any authority in armor was a potential executioner.

The ramp had barely touched adamant when the crowd exploded outward. It was perfect kinetic dispersal. Ratlings, masters of concealment, seemed to dissolve into air the moment they touched column shadows. Felinids, with predatory reflexes and superior agility, leaped cargo crates and climbed overhead supports, hunting open ventilation ducts.

General Draxus’s guards, posted on the ship’s upper parapets, panicked.

— **Contain the cargo! Fire to stun!** — an Imperial officer shouted, drawing his laspistol and snapping a crimson beam that ricocheted harmlessly off the station’s armored ceiling.

Alaric, motionless at the hangar center, did not raise his voice. He only tilted the **Crimson** helm slightly. On the tactical Noosphere, a low-latency order went to the shadows around him: **\[PROTOCOL: SILENT ENCIRCLEMENT\]**.

— **Draxus’s ignorance is infinite,** — Alaric said on the private channel. — **He tries to net the wind. He does not understand that in Noviomagus, even shadows have teeth.**

## ACT III: THE DIGNITY OF STEEL

### Chapter 9: Home Behind the Wall

Hangar 12’s sound was progress cacophony—but as abhumans crossed the threshold into the new hab complex, acoustics changed. Kadmos had designed walls with sonic and thermal insulation layers, ensuring Noviomagus forge-roar became distant, comforting heartbeat-thud instead of oppressive noise.

Bast lowered eyes to her food, strange heat in cheeks not from soup steam. She was not a “monster” to him. She was just another Wall resident taking breakfast. In that moment, soup tasted like something she had never known: peace.

### Chapter 10: The Baptism of Water and Rediscovering the Image

— **Those seeking civilian life found harbor,** — Varro’s voice roared without amplification yet thrummed chests. — **But you… you look at the Wall and see not only protection. You see debt. You see duty demanding blood and vigil. Noviomagus does not ask sacrifice from fear—but from honor.**

A colossal **Ogryn**, face cut by deep Malakor bayonet scar, stepped forward. He wore crude steel breastplate, held tower shield heavier than ordinary human.

— Malakor treated us slaughter animals — Ogryn said, voice rough but decided. — **Lord in black**… here we got bed. Food. Name. I don’t wanna be animal no more. I wanna be Wall. I want nobody suffer what we suffered.

Varro set armored hand on giant’s shoulder. — **Then you will be the Rock, brother. Noviomagus accepts your shield.**

Nearby, human veterans mutilated and “discarded” as invalids on Malakor queued before 10th Company scouts. With Kadmos’s new legs and arms they felt stronger than youth. Civilian life was rest they did not yet want. They wanted right to fight under banner of wolf who saved them.

Division was clear, harmonious:

* **20,000** chose civilian life: technicians, artisans, growers, scribes. They would be fortress economic soul and life support.  
* **10,000** chose Iron Legion: shock Ogryns, recon Ratlings, heavy infantry. They would be Chapter’s first human defense line.

Day’s end, Bast watched from distance as new legionaries swore oath. Heart tightened—but Elian beside her set mechanical hand on her shoulder.

— Everyone guards Wall their way, Bast — he whispered. — They guard with shield. We guard making sure shield never fails—and city behind it worth defending.

Bast looked at small wood wolf in hand. She understood now. Noviomagus Prime was not only fortress—it was pact. Draxus’s tithe had died; in its place rose society where steel and heart beat same rhythm.

### Chapter 13: Iron Marking – Birth of a People

Acclimation cycle had ended. What had been thirty thousand hungry, traumatized survivors now spread across **Ascension Yard** like living extension of fortress itself. Sound was no longer weeping or chain-drag but metallic unison of boots and prosthetics striking adamant.

Yard center: monumental brazier burned blue flames fed by refined promethium. Around it waited Triumvirate. **Alaric von Helis** foremost, crimson armor reflecting fire while **Varro** held Chapter standard and **Kadmos** ran celebration lights.

— **People of Noviomagus!** — Varro’s voice, amplified by citadel vox-casters, reverberated to deepest substructures. — **Today shame’s tithe is void. Today Malakor chains melt to forge tomorrow. You are no longer guests. You are the Wall.**

One by one, each group’s representatives advanced for **Iron Marking** ritual.

Bast was first from civilian division. She walked head high, olive-leaf clasps shining under blue light. Before Varro she did not kneel from fear—she inclined head in respect. Varro dipped steel-bristle brush in paint made from iron powder and blue-gray polymer—exact shade of Batavi armor plates.

With firm ritual stroke he painted vertical stripe on Bast’s robe left shoulder.

— **Bast of Noviomagus,** — Varro sentenced. — **May your work sustain life metal protects. You are iron that supports.**

Next, Ogryn who chose Iron Legion advanced. Varro applied same mark—this time on heavy armor pauldron.

— **Gorg of Noviomagus. You are iron that strikes back.**

Ceremony lasted hours. Each of thirty thousand received mark. For civilians, symbol of citizenship and protection; for military, seal of new brotherhood. Iron-gray now united all, regardless of species or origin.

Finally Alaric stepped forward. He drew power sword—not for combat, but salute. Blade force field hissed, throwing sparks like falling stars across dark yard.

— **Look at your comrades,** — Alaric ordered. — **On Malakor you were numbers on a cargo manifest. Here you are names defending humanity’s frontier. If enemy comes, he will find no slaves. He will find a pack that knows its own soul’s worth.**

Roar erupted from crowd—not mob shout but **Wall Cry**—rhythmic sound, fists on chest, tools on shields.

Bast, hand in hand with Elian—both showing gray shoulder mark—looked up. Far off, on distant parapet, she saw Alaric’s silhouette watching horizon. She clutched small wood wolf in pocket.

Noviomagus Prime was no longer only a rock in space. It was home. And for the first time, the rejected knew: if universe tried to take it, they would have thirty thousand iron reasons to say: **“No.”**
