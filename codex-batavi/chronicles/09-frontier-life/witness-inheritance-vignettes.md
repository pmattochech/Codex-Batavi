## I. The forge knows your name *(ca. 046.M42 · Noviomagus Prime · lower tiers)*

The Batavi did not attend the **Saint of the Overflow** procession. Patrol windows and forge quotas forbade spectacle. What they attended was **heat**.

When the procession passed the manufactoria apron, civilians pressed palms to rattling coolant pipes — a superstition older than the Chapter’s oath — and whispered names of the drowned year. Auxilia overseers did the same. Halfway through Third Shift, Sergeant **Martol** found his squad doing it without orders: ceramite palms on blue-stained iron, mouths moving under sealed helms.

Martol almost broke the habit with *vox* discipline. Then he remembered **Shared Sweat**: shared hazard teaches faster than pamphlets.

He logged it as nothing. In the margins of his slate he wrote **thermal coupling check** — the lie friction doctrine allows — and filed no annex.

Within a decade, Third Shift knuckle-drummed the pipe **once** before crossing the apron, same as the workers’ children did when they ran errands through grease smoke. No Chaplain consecrated it. The forge had already decided.

## II. Salt at the lip *(ca. 072.M42 · Selene-Black anchorage · auxilia pier)*

The Needles families still spoke **salt-debt** when they loaded tithe crates: not religion on parchment, **contract** you could taste.

A **Quinta** detachment waited on the pier while Munitorum tallies fought over tonnage. A girl — fifteen, maybe — broke rank from the labor gang and pressed a flake of **hardbread** into the nearest Batavi’s hand. Her eyes dared him to refuse. “Eat,” she said. “So the tide knows you’re not stealing weight.”

The Marine — **Brother Caeros**, elevation **068.M42** — hesitated one heartbeat. Then he placed the crumb on his tongue through the lower helm seal, locked it, and swallowed dry.

His sergeant did not reprimand him. The Judex had not yet filed **Dead’s Salt** as formal rite; Caeros had performed it **anyway**, six years early, because the girl’s hands shook like someone who had watched uncles vanish into the void for less than a misplaced crate.

Later, when **friction-rites** canonized salt at the shield wall, veterans said the pier had been a rehearsal. Caeros never claimed credit. He only said: *She fed me fear with manners.*

## III. The recruit who renamed the bell *(ca. 091.M42 · Noviomagus · recruitment annex)*

Neophyte **Arlen** came from **District Nine**, where shift-change bells were called **widow-clappers** — ugly joke, accurate physics. During drill, when the fortress bell tolled, Arlen murmured the District phrase under breath.

The cadre chief almost corrected him to High Gothic.

Drusus — passing through on inspection — stopped the correction with one raised gauntlet. “Repeat what you said,” he ordered.

Arlen repeated it. The phrase was crude. It was also **exactly** the cadence cadets used when they feared their hearts would sprint into **Furor**.

Drusus did not smile. He recorded **acceptable cadence drift** and moved on.

Within two cohorts, half the Annex murmured District Nine slang when bells struck — not because doctrine demanded it, because **biology** responded to the same rhythm the district had used for three centuries of quota grief.

Varro did not sanctify the phrase. He permitted it **until** it invited chorus euphoria. Then he burned only the chorus — not the whisper.

## IV. Judex at the threshold *(ca. 118.M42 · *Castrum Ferrum* · transit spine)*

Two Batavi stalled at a blast door because a civilian tech drew **three chalk ticks** on the lintel — old shipyard ward against “bad closure.” The Marines waited while she finished.

A junior Chaplain hissed that superstition slowed deployment.

Varro — passing as a wall of bone-skull shadow — halted beside him. “Measure the delay,” he said.

The delay was **four seconds**.

Varro stared through the Chaplain’s lenses until the priest looked down. “Four seconds buys twelve minutes of trust when this door jams for real,” Varro said. “She thinks we respect her grammar. She will not sabotage seals out of spite. Record **threshold tolerance** — not indulgence.”

Nothing entered **friction-rites.md** that week. Something entered **Reclusiam** marginalia: *lintel ticks permitted on transit class doors where auxilia maintain primary seal authority.*

No parade. No pilgrimage. Only a Chapter learning that **culture**, here, meant **whose hands own the hinges.**

## Closing note (*Strategium* filing stub)

Witness inheritance does not produce manifestos. It produces **habit that survives audit**. When allied priests ask what the Batavi “believe,” the honest answer is uncomfortable:

They believe **what kept the last shift alive long enough to clock out.**
