# Ash: Fenris’s Silence at Noviomagus

**Location:** Martha’s Kitchen (Absolute Decompression Zone).  
**Event:** Resupply of Logan Grimnar’s Great Company (return from the Ghoul Stars campaign).  
**Present:** The Noviomagus Triumvirate, the Great Wolf and his guard, the Solis family.

War has a specific smell. For the Space Wolves it smells like blood, sweat, and frozen ozone. For the Batavians it smells like oil, hot iron, and chemical exhaustion. But in Martha’s Kitchen, on that storm night on Noviomagus, war smelled like rosemary roast meat and rye bread fresh from the oven.

The service door, reinforced with adamantium to withstand bombardment, opened with a hydraulic groan.

— **Come in and clean your boots!** — Martha’s voice cut through the rain outside before the first giant even entered.

Logan Grimnar, the Great Wolf of Fenris, came in. His Terminator armor was dented, coated in black soot from some dead world in the Ghoul Stars. He looked tired—a mountain of skin and ceramite carrying the galaxy’s weight. Behind him, Ragnar Blackmane carried a barrel of *mjod* on one shoulder and a heavy reinforced wooden cargo box on the other.

— Old Mother — Grimnar greeted, his voice a hoarse thunder. He obeyed, scraping magnetic boots on the industrial metal mat Jonas had installed. — We bring hunger and stories. Does Noviomagus have bread to satisfy the first?

— For hunger, we have stew — Martha answered, wiping her hands on her apron. — For stories, the children have not slept for two hours waiting for you.

The table was set. On one side, Fenris’s noble savagery; on the other, Noviomagus’s stoic wall. Alaric von Helis, helmless, ate with mechanical precision, limiting micro-gestures to the strictly necessary for caloric intake, while little Leo tried to climb his armored arm.

Ragnar, mouth full of bread and mead, narrated the saga of the “Seven-Headed Beast of Xoth.” — …And then I jumped on the central throat! The creature tried to bite me with the other six, but my frost sword spun like a hurricane! *Vosh!* Three heads flew before I touched the ground!

Drusus, seated at the table’s end, emitted static through his surgical helm’s vox. He lowered his fork.

— **Correction.** — The Apothecary’s voice was clinical. — **Xoth xeno anatomy suggests the rotation needed to decapitate three thick-chitin necks in a single arc would require roughly 4,000 newtons of torque. Given planetary gravity and fall angle, Jarl Blackmane would have dislocated his shoulder. Simultaneous decapitation probability is twelve percent. Most likely outcome: severe lacerations and death by hemorrhage.**

Ragnar stopped, fork halfway. The Jarl of Fenris grinned, showing fangs. — You’re as fun as a toothache, Drusus. Hemorrhage doesn’t make a good song.

— **The song is irrelevant,** — Varro cut in, skull mask turned to the young Jarl. — **What matters is that the enemy ceased vital function. Ragnar’s method was… inefficient, but effective.**

When dinner ended its nutritional utility and *mjod* began to circulate, Logan Grimnar grew serious. He signaled Ragnar, who brought the reinforced wooden box. There were breathing holes in the box. A low sound came from inside—not a growl, but heavy breathing.

— Alaric — said Grimnar. — Fenris pays its debts. Your kitchen feeds us, your yards repair our ships. I brought something for you. And for the girl.

He looked at Elara Solis, the Admiral, seated beside Alaric, peeling an apple with a combat knife. Grimnar opened the box.

What came out was not a furious monster. It was a Thunderwolf pup. But by Fenris standards it was a biological defect. Its coat was not glorious gray or white but matte black, smudged with ash-gray. It was smaller than normal, with shoulders too broad and short, dense legs. And, most disturbing to Fenrisians: it did not howl.

The pup left the box, scanned the room full of giants and humans, and made no sound. It walked to the center of the rug, sniffed the air, and sat, watching Alaric with fixed yellow eyes.

— His pack name was *Silence* — Grimnar explained, heavy with regret. — He is a pure Thunderwolf, but… he was born voiceless. He does not howl at the moon. He does not growl at challenge. On Fenris, a wolf that does not sing its saga is considered cursed. My Iron Priests wanted to sacrifice him. They said he would bring ill luck on the hunt.

Alaric von Helis rose with piston precision. The Chapter Master crouched before the “defective” pup. The Fenris wolf did not flinch. It looked at Alaric’s scars, recognized machine, man, and monster, and did not blink.

— **He has no defect, cousin,** — said Alaric, extending the gauntlet. The wolf licked the cold metal of Alaric’s finger, then bit, testing hardness. — **He just runs on a different frequency.**

Elara approached. Without biological hesitation, she set her hand on the beast’s broad dark head. — On Fenris silence is a flaw because you fight for glory — she said, meeting Grimnar’s gaze analytically. — But here on Noviomagus… silence is function.

Alaric took the pup by the scruff. The wolf locked its body, accepting gravity.

— **Drusus,** — called Alaric.

— **Scanning,** — the Apothecary answered at once. — **Bone structure fifteen percent denser than Fenrisian average. Vocal cords atrophied, but hearing amplified forty percent. He spends no energy on noise. All metabolic capacity goes to perception and bite.**

A sub-millimeter contraction locked Alaric’s jaw—the closest he would get to a smile. — **You were going to sacrifice the best scout in the litter because he could not sing, Logan. That is resource waste.**

Alaric set the wolf on the floor. The animal walked straight to Elara Solis’s side and sat on the Admiral’s boots, mounting guard facing the door.

— What do we call him? — asked Ragnar. — *Mute-Claws*? *Ghost*?

Elara cut meat from her plate and offered it to the wolf. The beast ate in one dry, economical bite. *Clack.*

— **Ash,** — said Elara. — **Because he was born from flame, but he is what remains when fire dies. And ash smothers more than fire.**

— **Ash,** — repeated Alaric, ratifying the naming. — *Cinere.*

That night, while Fenrisians slept dreaming of loud battles, Alaric von Helis stayed awake in the kitchen, processing tithe reports. At the Admiral’s feet, Fenris’s exiled pup slept in absolute silence. He was the only wolf in the whole galaxy who truly understood Batavian doctrine: you do not need to howl to be dangerous. You only need to be there when the door opens.
