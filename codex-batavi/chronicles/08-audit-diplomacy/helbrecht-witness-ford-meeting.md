# The High Marshal at Witness Ford: First Meeting with the Wall

**When:** 096.M42 (Silent Vigil)  
**Where:** **Witness Ford** outward choke — **Threshold** mesh patrol bastion; **Tempest Marsh Channel** transit authorization in effect  
**Geography lock:** [`castra-vetera-galactic-lock.md`](../../atlas-and-topography/castra-vetera-galactic-lock.md). Retired in new prose: *Porta Nihili*, *Loken Passage*.

---

## I. The Ford

**Witness Ford** was not a wide embrace harbor; it was the **physical choke** where convoys earned receipt before inward routing — pylons, cordon auspex, and *Strategium* math the Batavi treated as sacred trenchwork. Beyond it, the **Threshold** trench amplified translation hazard; only **Insular chart-houses** filed to Castra Vetera made the approach statistically survivable. Whoever crossed without authorization was not a visitor: they were ordnance.

That week the *Segmentum* authorized passage for a **crusade fist** of Black Templars — three gunships, frigate escorts, and at the center the ancient standard of **High Marshal Helbrecht**. The Imperial mission was generic enough to fit half a scroll line: *faith reinforcement in the Pacificus shadow*; the real mission, everyone knew, was to **show the fist** where the Astronomican failed.

Alaric von Helis did not board the Templar ship. He received Helbrecht in the **transit chamber** of the patrolling Vexillatio’s anchor orbital bastion — an adamantium box without windows, lit by hololiths that drew the ford and marsh approach like a longitudinal artery cut. The Lord Castellan stood, *Castra-Lupus* sealed, wolf helm fixed on the map. There was no crusade-cousin greeting. There was **mutual armor recognition**.

— The Ford does not open from pity — said Alaric. The voice ran so low the room’s sensors boosted a decibel for human attendants. — It opens from geometry. You bring fire. I bring the wall. Do not confuse the two.

Helbrecht did not smile. To smile would be to yield ground.

— Fire is geometry too, Castellan. Ours measures guilt.

Varro leaned on the wall, hood low, said nothing. Drusus, beside the field med-console — a useless ritual in that room, but habit — logged only the two leaders’ **basal heart rate** and filed it as *diplomatic baseline*.

---

## II. The Interruption

Warning did not come as declared heresy. It came as **mass pressure**: edge sensors caught a cloud of small craft — Drukhari raiders mixed with smugglers who had learned to cling to larger predators to slip through holes unworthy of the name *route*. It was not system invasion; it was **opportunistic bleeding** at the choke — the kind of thing that happens when the Imperium announces movement too large to pass unnoticed.

Alaric did not move his fist to a weapon.

— We hold the ford — he said to the Vexillatio’s open *vox* and, by extension, to Helbrecht’s channel. — *We hold.*

The Black Templars needed no invitation to kill.

What followed was not a narrative battle — it was **violent accounting**. The Batavi closed prism faces with suppression fire and interdiction fields; the Templars launched boarding torpedoes where geometry still allowed *cut* instead of *friction*. Helbrecht led the third assault in person — not from vanity, but because his faith had **physical weight**: where he landed, the line stopped being doubt and became obligation.

Alaric stayed in the bastion until the map showed **breach**: one raider that had slipped the blockade, collision vector with the civilian orbital population of secondary platform *Wall-Four*.

Then the Castellan **moved**.

Witnesses on the Templar cruiser’s bridge described afterward — in servitor language, without epiphany — a **three-and-a-half-meter object** crossing assisted void on Terminator thrusters, intercepting the xeno craft like a human round. There was no duel of honor. There was **mechanical termination**: the raider split under torque its original design never assumed.

When the channel opened, the first voice was not praise.

— Civilians on *Wall-Four* intact — said Alaric, breathing steady on the *vox*. — Continue the blockade.

Helbrecht, another man’s blood on black armor — not his; his men’s — answered in a hoarse tone:

— That is how it is done.

---

## III. Two Faithful, Two Readings

Later, in the same transit chamber, hololith still dripping wreckage like data rain, Helbrecht faced the wolf helm.

— You did not pray a word out there — he said. It was not accusation; it was **faith audit**.

— I fulfilled — Alaric answered. — The Throne does not pay for this mesh for sermons. It pays for it to exist tomorrow.

A long silence. The kind Varro respected.

Helbrecht nodded once, heavy as a hammer.

— I will remember the wall when I hear wolf — he said. — And I will remember the wall did not ask permission to hold the line.

Alaric did not thank. To thank would admit emotional debt.

— When you return — he said only — bring coordinates. Do not bring a sermon.

Helbrecht almost laughed. Almost.

---

## IV. Echo (165.M42 — reader’s note)

Years later, in *Castrum Ferrum*’s yard, when Guilliman asked whether Alaric’s humanity was flaw or resource, the High Marshal set hand on sword pommel — **reflex**, not decision. Alaric stepped forward, **as before** at a choke where each had already measured the other.

On that first day at **Witness Ford**, neither had sworn friendship.

They had traded **the only coin that survives in the *Nihilus***: proof each knew how to hold what the other called duty.
