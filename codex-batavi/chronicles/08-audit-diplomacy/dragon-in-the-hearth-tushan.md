# The Dragon in the Hearth: Tu’Shan’s Warmth at Noviomagus

**Location:** Martha’s Kitchen (Absolute Decompression Zone), *Castrum Ferrum*.  
**Visitor:** Tu’Shan, Regent of Prometheus, Chapter Master of the Salamanders.  
**Date:** 580.M42 (post-Apotheosis).  
**Present:** The Noviomagus Triumvirate, the Solis family, and Tu’Shan.

If Logan Grimnar’s arrival had brought storm pressure and Guilliman’s had imposed tribunal stillness, Tu’Shan’s presence registered in Noviomagus as a thermodynamic anomaly. Not the radioactive heat that chars blood at the forge, but the contained thermal radiation of a hearth in the grip of winter.

When the service door hissed open, the oppressive humidity of acid rain vanished at once. The giant who filled the frame radiated a temperature that cleared the window glass in microseconds. Tu’Shan wore no helmet. His skin had the texture and color of polished charcoal, and his eyes were two scarlet embers. In any other transhuman that physiology would read as destruction; in Tu’Shan it radiated forge stability.

— **With your leave, Lady Solis,** — the Lord of the Salamanders rumbled. His vocal projection was tectonic but deliberately modulated to safe decibels so as not to harm mortal eardrums.

Martha, hands dusted with flour from freshly worked dough, did not even interrupt the kinetics of her movements.

— Clean your boots, Tu’Shan. And if you scorch my rug with that drake-scale cloak, the penalty is weaving a new one by hand.

The Chapter Master of one of the First Founding’s most revered orders—the warrior who had imposed physical punishment on the *Marines Malevolent* captain for unnecessary cruelty—showed a lip contraction that revealed perfectly white teeth. A smile.

— **Nocturne’s ash stays outside, Mother Martha,** — he answered, scraping heavy boots on the industrial mat with metric care.

Tu’Shan brought no weapons or organic xenos trophies. He carried an object wrapped in thick, refractory leather. Advancing to Noviomagus’s industrial stove, he set the bundle on the reinforced counter and unfolded it.

— **Alaric informed me, in our logistics reports, that this room’s primary cauldron had a structural fault,** — Tu’Shan declared.

The revealed piece was a pot. Yet in essence it was an artificer relic forged in Nocturne’s depths. Made of fire-tempered *adamantium*, it bore micro-engravings where flames and wolves intertwined along the rim. The metal was dark but behaved like a perfect heat sink.

— **This alloy’s conductivity distributes heat on a millimeter scale in 0.001 seconds,** — Tu’Shan explained with a Forge Master’s precision. — **It holds food temperature for three planetary cycles without a continuous flame.**

Martha stopped kneading. She wiped her hands on her apron and touched the metal. It was perfectly warm. — You used a star-forge’s time and resources to make a root-soup pot?

— **Soup feeds the Throne’s protectors, ma’am,** — the Dragon answered, inclining his formidable face. — **Therefore soup is a high-priority strategic asset.**  
The matriarch blinked, holding any emotional slip, and jerked her chin toward the table. — Sit down, you sentimental giant. Before I test your armor’s density with a ladle.

The room’s biological dynamic went under hard test when the children approached. Little Leo, seven, and Sara’s baby stayed on the central rug. To a standard citizen, an unmasked Salamander is the visual incarnation of an incandescent demon. Tu’Shan, however, recalibrated his mass. With a deep servo hum he knelt, reducing his terrifying height and leveling his presence.

He opened his gauntlet. On the ceramite palm he generated a micro chemical flame—perfectly controlled. Sara’s baby crawled toward the glow. The child’s mother tensed, ready to intervene, but Admiral Elara Solis touched her arm to hold her in place.

*— Watch,* — Elara ordered in a practical whisper.

When the baby reached the fire, Tu’Shan did not pull back his hand; he closed armored fingers *around* the flame, snuffing it for lack of oxygen in a nanosecond, and let the child grip his warm thumb.

— **Fire only consumes what is impure and inefficient, little one,** — Tu’Shan murmured.  
Leo, less cautious, climbed the giant’s calf. — Have you killed real dragons?  
— **Countless,** — the Chapter Master confirmed, lifting the boy with one arm’s strength and setting him on his heavy pauldron. — **But the universe’s logistics state a truth, Leo: merit is not in putting out the dragon’s flame, but in making sure that flame never reaches your city’s foundations.**

On the table’s far side, Alaric von Helis watched the scene’s geometry. He and Chaplain Varro exchanged a silent look—a biometric read confirming zero friction. *Furor* threat was null.

Dinner proceeded, served in the new culinary relic that met its thermodynamic specs perfectly. Verbal interaction escalated into doctrine audit.

— **You categorize yourselves as tools,** — Tu’Shan broke dense Noviomagus bread with bare hands, ember eyes fixed on the Lord Castellan. — **I studied Noviomagus dogma. *Instrumentum Solum.* It is formidable cognitive armor. But iron, in essence, is a cold conductor.**

— **Absence of heat preserves molecular stability, Tu’Shan,** — Alaric countered. His rough voice was like two stones being sanded. — **Excess temperature melts the core. Fire consumes the bearer. The Wall does not fall because we refuse to burn.**

Tu’Shan nodded, a heavy motion. He pointed first to Elara, slicing xenological data with Drusus, then to Martha.

— **Look at your rear. Iron is what builds the house’s walls and roof, Alaric. But it is fire contained in a hearth that turns shelter into a viable home. A tool does not love what it builds.** — Tu’Shan leaned forward, layering his warmth over the table’s cold. — **I read your spacing. How you place your mass to block any draft that might reach the Admiral. You are not a blind tool, Iron Wolf. You are the artisan. And your masterwork is not this *castrum*… it is preserving this family.**

Alaric stopped. He lowered his food. Trained by the Silent Vigil—acid rain outside, grief in the corridors—not to yield a millimeter of humanity, he was boxed in by Nocturne’s irrefutable logic. A sub-millimeter contraction locked his jaw.

— **The theory is statistically… viable,** — the Lord Castellan conceded in a tone that barely broke the room’s static. — **But if you report this inference to Grimnar, error margin on our tactical alliance with Fenris may drift.**

Tu’Shan let out a deep laugh, a rumble that shook dust from the ceiling. — **Logan Grimnar is a veteran mastiff who growls loud only to hide his own need for closeness. His secret is anchored in fire, brother.**

Tu’Shan’s departure was pragmatic. On the adamant threshold Martha handed him a package wrapped in coarse cloth.

— Noviomagus Ash Bread, — the matriarch defined it. — It is a dense composite. Functional. One block’s intake supports a laborer’s caloric spend for a full solar cycle. Give it to your smiths.

Tu’Shan took the rustic loaf as if holding a sacred plasma core. He executed his Chapter’s martial salute—fist to left breastplate, head bowed.

— **On Nocturne our axiom is: ‘In battle’s fire, on war’s anvil, we are tested.’ But you proved forge efficiency is decided at the dinner table’s maintenance.** — Tu’Shan raised his head. — **Vulkan lives.**

— **The Emperor protects,** — Martha answered dryly. — **Now spin up your exhausts and leave before this rain eats your armor’s polish.**

While Salamander transport burned the night sky, Elara Solis rested a hand on Alaric’s cold gunmetal forearm.

*— Their biology is… different from Fenris’s,* — the Admiral assessed.

— **The vector is complementary,** — Alaric confirmed, eyes on the departing ship’s fire trail. — **We are the static Wall that denies the beast passage. They are the active Fire that consumes darkness. The equation works.**
