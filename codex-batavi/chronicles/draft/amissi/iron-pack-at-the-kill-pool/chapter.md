# Iron pack at the kill-pool

**Status:** **Draft** — not on read chain.  
**Theater:** joint purge (unnamed world), ~**090–120.M42** band (Einar alive; pre–Sigma Moon).  
**Forces:** *Amissi* (Einar Dreadwolf) · **V** *Quinta* escort Chaplain Ordin · Space Wolves (Champion Skalf; **Ragnar Blackmane**) · Legatus **Armin Sturmwahl** · Lord Castellan **Alaric von Helis**.  
**Locks:** strange allies / **never us** — no Chapter brotherhood; Fenris **wrong wolves → iron pack** filing only.

---

The promethium had not finished burning when the last heretic stopped moving.

Einar Dreadwolf stood in the center of the kill-pool, Dreadwolf Claws still extended, energized talons shedding orange static into air that already tasted of iron and burned hair. Eighteen bodies lay in the geometry he had cut — cult militia, three bearing stolen Aquila plate, two in black ceramite that had once belonged to the Long War. None of them had died quickly enough to become a problem for anyone else. That had been the only metric.

His helm was gone. It had taken a chainaxe through the bowl at the height of the second wave; the Mk V shell lay ten meters back, visor stripe cracked, useless. Without it, there was nothing between the world and what Moraine-Nine had made of him.

Exanimus did not announce itself politely. The jaw had outgrown the face that Aethelgard shore had once owned — maxilla wide enough to crush a human throat without effort, canines that no longer fit behind the lip, a mane of cable and nail-loom tissue merged along the nape where foreign architecture had ridden in on Strand pressure and ship steel. Flame-pool irises, slit pupils. Red vambraces and greaves like open wounds on grey plate. He breathed through it with the flat discipline of a century held at Furor only, then forced across a threshold he could never uncross.

He looked, to an untrained eye, like the stories Fenris told about the edge of the curse.

He looked, to anyone who knew Noviomagus filing, like a brother under sentence.

Behind him, ten paces back, Chaplain Ordin of the Fifth *Quinta* held the leash without touching it. Varro’s written ***licentia lupina*** rested in his gorget seal — delegation, not promotion. The wolf-skull helm on his shoulders matched the Judex profile in bone geometry: stripped lupine muzzle, no lower jaw in the upper piece, mandible on rails for the feeding law Ordin obeyed as any other *Reclusiam* priest. The lenses were **not** ember-red. Standard orange read. Troll Slayer Orange. The skull was sentence borrowed from Varro’s office, not a second Executor pretending to be the Wall’s shadow.

Ordin watched Einar’s shoulders rise and fall and counted breath intervals the way Drusus had taught every *Amissi* escort to count them.

— Hold — he said, voice filtered but stripped of theater. — File them Imperial until the slate clears.

Einar did not answer. The Nails sang under the cowl. His hands wanted the next arc. There was no next arc. The heretics were finished.

That was when the wind changed.

They came out of the smoke on the left flank — six grey hunters in pelts and rune-work, pack spread, not bolt-line. Space Wolves. The lead figure was broad even for his kind, frost axe low, wolf-tail talismans clicking on his belt. A Champion. Skalf, if the local *vox* gossip had names; a jarl’s fist in the field, if it did not.

Skalf stopped when he saw the kill-pool.

He saw the bodies first — traitor colors, cult sigils, the right kind of dead. His nostrils would have read that before his eyes finished. Then he saw what had made them.

The thing in the center.

Not Wulfen. **Wrong.** Too much iron in the posture. Too much **stillness** after slaughter. The red on the limbs was paint law, not blood frenzy. The cables along the jaw were not Fenrisian gift. The claws were open-hand assault geometry, not kraken fury.

And behind it — a priest in a wolf’s dead face.

Skalf’s axe came up a finger’s width. Two of his pack mirrored him without being told.

— Ordin — Einar said. Not a question. A pressure valve.

— I hear them — Ordin replied. — You will not.

Einar locked his knees. Every tendon in his Exanimus frame screamed to treat six new targets as closure for the Nail song. He made his cortex file them the way Varro’s sentence had been drilled into him: **Imperial. Not quarry. Not yet.**

Skalf walked forward until the heat from the promethium touched his knee guards.

— Who marks this ground? — he asked. Fenris low Gothic, guttural, not quite a challenge and not quite courtesy.

Ordin stepped into the line between Skalf and Einar. Not wide. Precise. The borrowed wolf-skull caught the firelight; orange lenses, not red. Skalf’s gaze snagged on it like a hook.

— Chaplain Ordin — the priest said. — Fifth *Quinta*. *Amissi* escort. This kill-zone is under Judex sentence.

— Judex — Skalf repeated, as if tasting a false word. — You wear a Wolf Priest’s dead and walk a **wulfen** in open sight. You call that Imperial?

Einar’s claws twitched. Ordin did not look back.

— I call it **held**. He is under watch. You are not the warrant.

— He has the stink of the Anvil on him — one of Skalf’s pack said from the flank. — Nails. Look at the jaw. That is not your curse. That is **betrayal** wearing muscle.

— He killed what you came to kill — Ordin said. — Before you arrived. Check the plate. Check the sigils. Then check your aim.

Skalf’s eyes never left Einar’s face.

— Does it speak? Or only butcher?

Einar’s throat worked. Speech cost him more than killing had. When the words came, they were tide-flat, scraped raw.

— This ground is closed — he said. — Withdraw. While I still file you Imperial.

Not a howl. Not a plea. A boundary drawn in the only language his leash allowed.

Skalf’s pack went still in the way wolves went still when another predator claimed a carcass.

— You threaten **us**? — Skalf asked.

— I warn you — Einar said. — You stand in my arc.

Ordin raised one gauntlet, palm out — not surrender, **stay**.

— He is not yours to judge — the Chaplain said. — Varro holds the file. I hold the interval. If you want doctrine, ask the Judex on the channel. If you want blood, you are standing in the wrong pack’s kill.

— **False priest** — Skalf said, and the axe rose fully now. — Fenris does not leash abominations and call them brother. You dishonor the skull you stole.

— **Stolen** — Ordin echoed, flat. — **Licensed.** Read the seal or read the air wrong again.

Two of Skalf’s wolves moved. Ordin did not retreat. Einar’s claws fully extended — and he **did not** step forward. That cost more than any witness would ever measure.

The sky answered before the axe could.

A shadow crossed the promethium smoke — not a Stormbird, something faster, steeper. Jump pack flare. A figure hit the ferrocrete between the packs with the sound of a siege plate dropped from height, *Alabarda* head first, bannerless, grey iron and crimson trim. Legatus geometry. Armin Sturmwahl landed in a crouch, rose without ceremony, and put the halberd’s reach between Skalf’s chest and Ordin’s skull.

— You will not name my brother without my blade in the conversation — Armin said.

His voice was open channel. No theater. No oath for Fenris to mishear as kinship.

Skalf looked at the Legatus, then at Einar, then at the borrowed wolf-skull again. The math was ugly and getting uglier.

— Another iron colossus — he said. — Another priest playing Fenris. What **is** this Chapter?

— **Predecessors** — Armin said. — You are in our joint axis. Act like it.

From the north, heavy footsteps — not sprint, **march**. Servomotors in unison. The grey tide of a Batavi spearhead entering the edge of the kill-pool, and at its front a silhouette that made the fire shrink back from habit.

Alaric von Helis did not run. He did not raise *Breakwater*. He walked into the geometry as if the ground had always belonged to load-bearing mass.

Behind the Castellan’s left shoulder, lower and faster, a Space Wolf in rune-work plate peeled off from a second pack — young king stride, no howl, wolf at heel. Ragnar Blackmane checked his pace when he saw Skalf’s axe raised, saw Ordin’s licensed skull, saw Einar bare-faced and **held**.

— Skalf — Ragnar said. — Stand down.

— Lord — Skalf turned, snarl half-formed. — That is **wulfen**. That priest wears our dead—

— That is **their** law — Ragnar said. — Not ours to rewrite on their soil.

Alaric stopped three meters from Skalf’s axe. Orange lenses under the industrial lupine helm. Viggo’s pelt and the Batav Wolf’s on his shoulders, motionless.

— Castellan — Skalf said, axe still up. — Do you leash monsters?

— I **sentence** them — Alaric said. — And they kill what you came to kill. You arrived late. You will not arrive **proud**.

Skalf’s jaw worked.

— He threatened my pack.

— He warned you off his arc — Alaric said. — There is a difference. Learn it or pay for the mistake.

Ragnar stepped between Skalf and the *Alabarda*, one hand on the Champion’s shoulder, not friendly — **pack discipline**.

— We saw the dead — Ragnar said, quieter, to Alaric, not for Skalf’s glory. — Traitor plate. Cult sigils. Clean cuts. Your **Lost** did the work before we spent bolt shells.

Alaric’s lenses fixed on Einar. The *Amissi* brother did not look away. Did not bow. Did not soften.

— He is mine to judge — Alaric said. — Not yours. Not Fenris’s. **Mine.**

The words were not warmth. They were load-bearing fact.

Ordin inclined his skull a millimeter — acknowledgment, not gratitude.

— The interval holds — he said. — Withdraw your axe, Champion. The Judex seal is live.

Skalf looked at Ragnar. Ragnar did not blink.

Slowly, the axe lowered.

The promethium crackled. Somewhere, a heretic’s ammo pouch cooked off — pop, hiss, nothing that mattered.

Einar retracted his claws one finger at a time. The Nail song did not stop. It never stopped. He filed the six Space Wolves as **Imperial** again, and again, until his hands stopped shaking.

— Wrong wolves — Skalf muttered, not quite to anyone. Fenris under his breath, not vox.

Ragnar heard it anyway.

— Aye — he said. — Wrong wolves. **Iron** pack. Not ours.

He did not say *brother*. He did not offer *mjod*. He nodded once to Alaric — receipt, not embrace.

— Joint axis holds — Ragnar said. — Pull your fist back, Skalf. We have more heretics to burn upstream.

Skalf spat into the ash.

— They walk like a pack — he said to Ragnar, low. — I will give them that.

— They walk like **themselves** — Ragnar said. — That is enough.

Armin did not lower the *Alabarda* until Skalf’s pack had cleared ten meters. Ordin counted Einar’s breaths again.

— Hold — he said.

— I held — Einar answered.

Alaric turned without ceremony, grey iron and pelts following inertia, and marched back toward the main line. The Wall did not celebrate. The Wall moved on.

Above the kill-pool, the smoke thinned enough to show a sliver of toxic sky — indifferent, the same color for every pack that died under it.
