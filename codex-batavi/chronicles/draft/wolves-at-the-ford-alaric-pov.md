# [DRAFT] Wolves at the Ford — Alaric POV

**Classification:** Working draft — dual-POV set (see [INDEX.md](INDEX.md))  
**POV:** Lord Castellan **Alaric von Helis** (first person)  
**When:** Post–Viggo frontier friction; **~055.M42** *approx* (CV cross-file TBD)  
**Where:** Witness Ford approach theater — Ork Waaagh! fragment  
**Who:** **100 Batavi** (Alaric **50**, Armin **50**); Space Wolves Great Company on the ridge (predecessors)  
**Locks:** Alaric kills Warboss; **Alabarda** Armin only; **Castra-Lupus**; Batavi do **not** know Fenris **wolves with swords in their mouths** coinage here; Viggo — aftermath weight only.

---

The ridge behind us held the **predecessors** — sons of Russ, grey plate, Fenris heat — and they had not granted the line.

That was the arithmetic I cared about.

Fifty of my elites already marched on my left block; fifty of Armin’s on the jump spine to port. One hundred Batavi against a Waaagh! fragment that the Administratum had politely called *manageable* until it wasn’t. The Space Wolves had come to **witness** our place in the kill-order. They would witness something else.

*Castra-Lupus* moved when I moved. The plates did not complain. Viggo’s white forepaws crossed my gorget the way they always did — weight, heat, the small blue glow of taxidermy eyes that had stopped being a wolf and become **proof** that a good soldier could die of age and still leave a geometry on your shoulder. I did not think about the mead-hall word **trophy**. I thought about the last week: the smirk in a Wolf Priest’s vox, the courtesy that was not courtesy, the pack laughter that filed us as **muzzled** things — wolves who would not sing for Fenris.

I was tired of waiting for respect.

Professional politeness from a **predecessor** Chapter costs nothing on the ledger. They withheld even that. After Viggo, I had enough.

Armin’s countdown clicked on the private channel — three, two, one — and I answered without poetry.

— We hold.

We had argued before the march. That was also arithmetic.

Armin walked beside me until the blocks split, pack housings singing their low grav hum. His helm carried the black wolf crown; mine carried the Iron Head. He sounded amused, which for *Quinta* always meant **danger dressed as sport**.

— Bold move, Castellan — he said. — Stealing the kill from the Wolves of Russ. Idiotic move. Same sentence. For a man who keeps saying we must be **allied** and **polite** to every **predecessor** who spits on our vox.

I did not look at him. I looked at the green wall.

— Politeness is a receipt — I said. — They stopped issuing ours.

— So you decided to issue **theirs** in Ork blood.

— I decided they would stop filing us as tame.

A pause — the kind Armin uses when he is **measuring** whether I am performing doctrine or leaking something human.

— Emotion from you — he said, lightly. — Rare. Like neophyte days. You used to brood in the grav-bay instead of sleeping.

— I used to be smaller.

— You used to **talk** more. Brooding counted.

The corner of my mouth almost moved. Almost.

— Viggo was a good soldier — Armin said, and the lightness went out of his voice for one tick. — Wolf. Not marine. Still good. You did not have to prove that to Fenris.

— Fenris was not the audience — I said. — **We** were.

He clicked acknowledgment — *Quinta* shorthand for *understood, still think you’re an idiot*.

— When we breach — he said — try not to emote on my descent window. I have a schedule.

— When you land — I said — try not to admire your own boots.

— Impossible. They’re excellent.

That was the joke between us: timing and vanity, nothing softer on the channel where fifty brothers could hear.

Then the blocks split, and jokes ended where geometry began.

The Orks came happy.

Good. Happiness makes a charge **predictable**.

My fifty locked *scuta* along the seam. Magnets found magnets. I took the center with **Breakwater** — the slab proportional to what *Castra-Lupus* demands of a Castellan’s frame; a wall sized for a wall-sized man. The first impact shivered through my forearms and into the stake ports; waste heat bled where it was supposed to bleed. I did not howl. I **invoiced** the pressure back into the green line.

Bolter discipline: assigned bursts. A body fell; the line advanced one permitted step. Another body; another step. The rhythm was forge rhythm — not rage, **throughput**. Furor stayed where Varro’s ledger wanted it: filed, not spent.

On the channel, Armin’s window opened.

— Descent — he said.

I heard the packs behind the Ork crest — not as prayer, as **industry**. Fifty impacts in the same second. Spike sabatons meeting mud and bone. The sound was Noviomagus grammar: **done**, not **announced**.

Only Armin carried an **Alabarda** — Legatus metal, not line issue. I did not watch the geometry of his circle; I watched **my** seam. That was the contract between us: I hold the teeth; he eats the throat.

The green line bent.

When bent becomes break, you feel it in the soles before the auspex confirms — a loosening, like a conveyor missing a tooth. Armin’s block had punched a hole where the Waaagh! thought density meant courage. My fifty still held the press so the hole did not **close**.

I moved.

*Castra-Lupus* did not lumber. It **permitted** speed because Kadmos had built the marriage for torque, not pageantry. The predecessors on the ridge would file the plate wrong — Cataphractii in their mouths, **Castra-Lupus** in ours. Let them spit that we rename what we touch. They were not wearing it.

Armin appeared at the breach edge like a countdown made flesh — **Alabarda** planted once, lifted once, green falling in arcs that had **numbers** behind them. He saw me and did not salute.

— You’re late — he said.

— You’re early — I said.

— Standard difference.

We fought **side by side** then — not brotherhood theater, **closure**. He took the left arc; I took the center mass. Breakwater caught a power klaw that would have removed his pack; he did not thank me. He opened the Nob behind it in the half-second I manufactured.

Good soldier habits.

The Warboss rose from the trampled dead — bigger, louder, the Ork answer to a Castellan’s scale. It roared as if volume could rewrite physics. I let it spend the breath.

Geometry: shield edge high, left foot planted, **Sentence** finding the gap under the lifted chin guard where green arrogance always forgets to plate. One thrust, torque from the hips through *Castra-Lupus*’s marriage of Cataphractii and **Dreadnought** bone. The Warboss stopped roaring.

I did not celebrate.

— Receipt — I said on open vox, for the line.

Armin vented his pack once, amused again.

— You emoted — he said. — On the thrust. I saw it.

— That was physics.

— That was **satisfaction**. Neophyte tell.

— File it as combat data.

— I will file it as **Castellan petulance** and love you anyway.

He would not say *love* on a wide channel in those words; he said it the Batavi way — insult as affection, timing as trust.

After, the field had the quiet of a shift change.

Brothers tagged ammunition and sorted wounded Orks from those that would stand. The chain under Viggo’s pelt did not chime much; I had not walked far enough for music. The white head’s blue lenses caught the low sun. Good soldier. Not marine. Still **witness**.

On the ridge, grey wolves stood in a line that had not descended. I did not know what name they would give us in their mead hall. I did not care yet. Care would come when they needed our axes at the next ford.

Armin wiped his **Alabarda** on a dead greenskin’s harness — practical, not ritual.

— The predecessors are watching — he said.

— Let them watch — I said. — We did not sing.

— No — he said. — We **held**. Then we **cut**. They can translate.

— Alliance — I said.

— Politeness — he answered, mocking my earlier word, but gentle. — You stole their lesson and gave them a better one. Bold. Idiotic. **Effective**.

I looked at the ridge. A Wolf Lord’s silhouette — still, measuring.

— Next time — Armin said — ask me before you spend my reputation on your pride.

— Next time — I said — ask me before you call my pride **neophyte**.

He clicked laugh.

— There it is — he said. — The brooding grav-bay boy. Welcome back.

I turned toward the mud road and the hundred still standing in two fifties, as if the battle had been a ledger entry completed on time.

— We hold — I said.

Not for Fenris.

For us.
