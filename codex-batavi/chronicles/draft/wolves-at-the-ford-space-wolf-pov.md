# [DRAFT] Wolves at the Ford — Space Wolf POV

**Classification:** Working draft — dual-POV set (see [INDEX.md](INDEX.md))  
**POV:** Wolf Lord **Gunnar Grey-Mane** — full Great Company on the ridge  
**When:** Post–Viggo frontier friction; **~055.M42** *approx* (CV cross-file TBD)  
**Where:** Witness Ford approach theater — Ork Waaagh! fragment  
**Who (witness):** Space Wolves Great Company; **Batavi** — Alaric von Helis, Armin Sturmwahl, **100** marines  
**Locks:** **Muzzled wolves** → **wolves with swords in their mouths** (Fenris coinage, end of draft); Cataphractii read vs Batavi **Castra-Lupus** spite; no Wolf Priest **anima** read on Alaric.

---

We had come to break the green tide and teach the grey wolves their place in the kill-order.

That was the plan.

The grey wolves had other ideas.

They went **first** — without our leave, without our vox blessing, without the courtesy a pack owes a pack when two Chapters share one throat of land. Our Jarl had not yet given the word. Our Blood Claws had not yet tasted the wind. And there they were, a hundred Batavi in industrial grey, marching into the Ork front like the Emperor had signed **their** name on the contract alone.

Until that hour we called them what they were: **muzzled wolves**. Wolf-painted. Wolf-marked. **No howl.** No saga worth the drinking. A Chapter the Administratum tolerated and every son of Russ **shunned** — too many bodies, too little truth on the scroll, too much **silence** where a warrior should make noise.

I stood on the ridge with my company at my back — all of us watching, which is a kind of punishment in itself when your pride is Fenris-made.

The Orks came on in their idiot joy. Tractors welded to faith. Axes bigger than men. A wall of green that laughed at geometry.

The Batavi answered with **work**.

Fifty of them hit the earth in front of the charge and **locked**.

I had seen Terminator plate before. Cataphractii, the scribes say — old Legiones bulk, a walking vault. The grey wolves loved their **private names** for everything; their scribes filed **Castra-Lupus** on the Castellan as if he had invented a new terror. **Spite** in our camp said they **erase honest names** and paste their own on any slab of metal they touch. Cataphractii is **Cataphractii**. We knew the shape.

We did **not** know the **speed**.

The Castellan — Alaric, the file called him — moved in that plate like a lie told to physics. His shield came up first. Not a buckler. Not a storm shield in the sense our Terminators carry. **The Breakwater** — a slab so wide and tall that, edge to edge, it read like a **Primaris brother** made into **rectangle and doctrine**: a wall you could hide a man behind, if the man were not already a wall himself.

Fifty elites interlocked smaller *scuta* along his line. Magnets sang. Ceramite kissed ceramite. The Ork charge hit that seam and **stopped** as if the world had found a rule it did not like.

No howl from the Castellan.

No prayer shouted for our benefit.

Only **impact** — methodical, rhythmic — the way a forge hammer falls when a smith is **not** angry, only **employed**.

Bolters spoke in **assigned** bursts. Not mag-dump glory. **Receipts.** A greenskin fell; the line did not break stride to cheer. Another fell; the shield wall **inched** forward one calculated step, boots grinding Ork dead into the mud like **pressed scrap** on a foundry floor.

That was the first thing that turned my stomach: their brutality was **not** missing. It was **on schedule**.

On the ridge, Brother Hrolf muttered,

— They fight like a **mill**.

I did not answer. I was watching the sky.

The other fifty had climbed — jump packs, assault spine, the Fifth’s way. We had heard the grey wolves were **jump** mad on their storm moons. Hearing is not **seeing**.

**Storm descent.**

They did not land like angels. They **punched** the ground.

Reinforced sabatons — **spike rows** on the boot lip, industrial talons — met mud and bone together. The sound was **wrong**: not thunder, **machinery**. Fifty impacts in one window, a **ground-pound** chorus that made the Orks look up at the wrong moment.

And the Legatus of the Fifth — **Armin**, the slate said — was already **spinning**.

His weapon was not a chainsword. Not in this rite. An **Alabarda** — three-faced halberd, chain-bearded cheek, **Type Descensus** the Batavi registers called it, as if naming the bite made it civil. He planted the butt spike, pivoted, and the head became a **circle of death** — not frenzy, **radius**. Every Ork that entered the arc paid **piece-rate**: opened, dropped, shoved aside for the next invoice.

His men copied the geometry. Jump pack vent. Spike plant. **Cut.** Bolt pistol finish. **Move.** No warrior preening. A **production line** with blood instead of oil.

Green gushed. Limbs flew. The Orks **loved** it at first — finally, a good fight — and then understood, too late, that the fight was **already** a factory and they were **raw stock**.

I felt my Canis Helix stir in my blood — the old yes, **kill** — and something colder behind it: **respect** trying to be born where **contempt** had lived for years.

The green line **bent**.

Not broke — Orks do not break so easy — but **bent**, the way a conveyor belt buckles when the teeth catch.

On the ridge, our Jarl had said nothing. His jaw was iron. The whole company watched — **every** pack, **every** claw — because this was the hour we were meant to send **our** young into the meat, and the grey wolves had **stolen** the first teaching.

We watched anyway.

Because a wolf who will not look at another wolf’s kill is **afraid** of what he might learn.

The breach opened where bent becomes **broken**.

Armin’s circle had **eaten** the Ork center until the charge lost its **face**. Alaric’s fifty **held** the teeth of the press — the Castellan’s slab taking a **Dreadnought-class** blow that would have pulp a lesser Terminator and giving back **one** gladius thrust through a gap no Ork saw until its soul left.

Then the two leaders **moved toward each other** — not running for glory, **closing the equation**.

I saw it clear from the ridge.

Alaric: immovable. Each step a **permit** the ground must sign. Shield edge catching a power klaw, shedding sparks like **cut steel on the lathe**. He did not roar. The Orks roared **for** him and still died.

Armin: teeth. The **Alabarda** rose and fell in **counted** arcs — one-two, plant, **sweep** — jump pack used not to fly but to **correct angle**, a predator adjusting **without** losing tempo.

They met in the broken middle **side by side**.

For the first time in my life I understood why their men followed them without songs.

Not because they were **muzzled**.

Because the **sword** was **in the mouth** — held there by **discipline**, released only where the **line** allowed blood.

Alaric blocked a Nob’s swing that would have taken Armin’s pack-housing off. Armin **opened** the Nob’s throat in the half-second the shield created. No thanks. No glance. **Next** target.

Two wolves.

One kill-zone.

Different bite.

When the Waaagh! fragment **splintered**, the field stank of burned promethium and sweet rotting greenskin.

Silence came — not peace, **post-production** quiet.

The Batavi did not cheer. They **tagged** ammo on their forearms and began **sorting** the wounded Orks from the ones that would stand again.

On the ridge, our Jarl spoke at last. His voice was low, for the packs only.

— I did not give them the line.

— No, Jarl — I said.

— And I will not pretend they asked.

— No.

He watched the Castellan lower the Breakwater one inch, as if setting down a tool too heavy for mortal hands. Watched Armin plant the **Alabarda** spike and vent his pack.

— We called them **muzzled wolves** — the Jarl said.

The word tasted old in my mouth. Wrong-shaped for what we had seen.

— Aye — I said. — Because they would not howl for us.

— We were wrong — he said, and Russ help me, I knew he was right. — Not about the silence. About **what** the silence **was**.

Brother Hrolf, young still in the fang, frowned.

— What then, Jarl?

Gunnar looked down at the field — at the layered dead, the gouged mud, the grey crimson moving through it like **oil through a machine that had never once apologized for being sharp**.

— **Wolves with swords in their mouths** — he said. — Not tame. **Aimed.**

The phrase went through the company like a new blade being drawn.

Not friendship. Not pack.

**Recognition.**

I walked down after the Jarl gave the nod — not to embrace them. Fenris does not embrace strangers who steal the first kill.

I walked because a Wolf Lord must **measure** what he will fight beside.

The Castellan stood among his fifty. Armin among his. Ninety-seven Batavi still standing, filed in **two** blocks of fifty as if the battle had been **planned** on a slate before the first Ork saw them.

Up close, the shield was worse than from the ridge: **Breakwater** scarred, Ork blood in the grooves, still **perfectly** aligned to his pauldron angle. Cataphractii plates — **not** whatever name their scribes sold — and the man inside moved like **hunger** that had learned **calendar**.

— You took the line — I said.

Orange lenses found me. No warmth. No insult.

— The line was **taken** by the Orks — the Castellan said. — We **held** it. You may still fight when your Jarl says.

Armin vented the last heat from his pack. The **Alabarda** head dripped green.

— Tell your packs — he said — we do not need your **saga**. We need your **axes** when the green returns.

I almost laughed. Fenris laughter, bitter.

— We will not call you **muzzled** again — I said. — My Jarl has a new name for you.

— We do not need names from Fenris — Alaric said. — We need **witness**.

He said it the way our priests say *Emperor* — flat, final.

I looked at the white-black weight on his shoulder. I did not call it trophy. Not today. Today I had seen what **iron winter** could mean when it **chose** a wall instead of a grave.

— **Wolves with swords in your mouths** — I said. — That is what we will say in the mead hall. Whether you like it or not.

For a long second, nothing.

Then the Castellan gave one dip of the slab — not salute. **Acknowledgment**.

— Say it — he answered. — Then **hold** beside us when the tide turns.
