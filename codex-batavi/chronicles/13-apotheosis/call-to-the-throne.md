# THE CALL TO THE THRONE

*(175.M42 — Noviomagus Prime, Castra Vetera Subsector)*

---

## I. THE ARRIVAL

The fleet did not announce approach. It was simply there.

Seven ships of auramite — the paint unmistakable even at distances that made hulls indistinguishable to the human eye — emerged from the Loken Passage with the precision of someone who knew the coordinates by heart. No identification signal. No landing request. The open *vox* channel carried a single transmission:

*"Captain-General Trajann Valoris requests audience with the Lord Castellan of Noviomagus."*

In the *Strategium*, the duty officer looked at the panel for two seconds. Then he relayed to the Triumvirate.

Alaric received the news, stood still a moment, and said:

— Open the main hangar. Maximum honor protocol. — A pause. — And wake Drusus.

---

Trajann Valoris came down the ramp with six Custodes in close formation — polished auramite, spears at march position, helms without raised visors. Each measured between two and two and a half meters. In the context of Noviomagus, where corridor standard was built for armored Astartes Terminator, they seemed to fit perfectly, which was a quiet reminder that Custodes were not Astartes.

They were something else.

Trajann was the largest of them — not obviously, but in a way you noticed afterward. The Captain-General’s auramite did not flaunt the excess iconography one might expect from the man closest to the Throne. It was functional, engraved with the minimum necessary meaning, and extremely well kept for ten millennia of use.

He stopped three meters from Alaric.

The two stood a moment in silence — mutual assessment of two organisms whose categories were close enough to compare and far enough that comparison was never direct.

Alaric was larger. Trajann was older. Both knew it.

— Lord Castellan — said Trajann. No courtesy inflection, no chill of threat. Inventory tone.

— Captain-General — said Alaric. Same tone.

Tyvar stood four meters to Alaric’s right, motionless, spear vertical. Something in the Custodes’ posture differed from standard — not tension, but attention of a different degree. He had guarded Alaric for **~129 years** — from **051.M42** (*Limes Lupus*) to this corridor — spanning the **Silent Vigil** and what came after. It was the first time his direct superior stood in the same corridor.

Trajann looked at Tyvar for two seconds.

— Tyvar.

— Captain-General — said Tyvar. The voice was as always — no warmth, no ceremony.

— Your report was… informative — said Trajann.

Tyvar did not answer. That was the correct answer.

---

## II. THE STRATEGIUM

The Noviomagus Prime *Strategium* was a room designed to be functional, not impressive — hololithic maps, cogitator terminals, the iron table without ornament Alaric had used for six centuries. The only aesthetic concession was the window to the outer forge yard, where Noviomagus industrial chimneys produced the permanent iron haze locals called “the second sky.”

Trajann entered, assessed the space once, and stood beside the table. He did not sit. The six Custodes of his escort lined the walls. The Triumvirate stood before him — Alaric center, Drusus left with surgical helm and multi-lenses, Varro right in ***Executor* wolf-skull helm** — same brutal cast as Fenrisian **Wolf Priests**, consecrated to **Judex** law rather than hearth-counsel. Under ***Reclusiam* helm law**, subordinate Chaplains still wore ***Codex* death-skull** unless **Varro** had issued ***licentia lupina***.

Tyvar remained behind Alaric, slightly to the right.

— You came to audit — said Alaric. Not a question.

— I came to calibrate — said Trajann. — There is a difference. An audit seeks faults. A calibration seeks the exact function of the instrument. — He looked at Alaric with eyes that had weighed ten thousand years of loyalties and treacheries. — What are you, Castellan?

— An instrument of the Emperor on the *Nihilus* frontier — said Alaric. — It works while biology allows and fails when biology fails.

— That is the right answer — said Trajann. — It is also what every Chapter Master would give if they knew I was in the room.

— Then let me show you what most Chapter Masters would not — said Alaric. — The part where biology fails.

---

## III. THE DESCENT

The Black Cells lay on sublevel forty-eight of Noviomagus Prime — below the foundries, below the fuel tanks, below the military archives. The cargo elevator that descended there was wide enough for three Astartes in Terminator armor side by side.

None of Trajann’s escort Custodes went down. The Captain-General ordered them to remain on the upper level. Only he, the Triumvirate, and Tyvar descended.

The access corridor was lit by red lumens — the standard color for environments that required reduced visibility for biological, not tactical, reasons. The air smelled of containment discharge and something harder to identify: a **pheromonal marker** a feral **Versibar** released continuously, a chemical trace of territory any predator biology registered as warning.

Drusus walked ahead. Cell records were on his panel.

— How many? — asked Trajann as they descended.

— Seven — said Drusus. — Four chose *The Iron Prison* in the Testament. Three were contained during progression crises before protocol was established — cases prior to formal Testament, kept by the Castellan’s decision.

— Operational history?

— Two took part in *Adversarii at Portas* in the last hundred twenty years. Both survived the incidents. Protocol is no recovery after release — but in those two cases, circumstances allowed post-action containment. It is an exception, not the rule.

Trajann processed that in silence.

— *Adversarii at Portas* — he repeated. — You release battle-brothers as area weapons.

— We release instruments who chose to be area weapons — said Varro, without inflection. — The Testament is irrevocable because it was signed while the mind still worked. We honor the choice the man made before he became the beast.

Trajann did not answer at once. Then he said:

— That is either the most disciplined or the coldest thing I have heard from an Astartes Chapter.

— The two are not mutually exclusive — said Varro.

---

The first cell was lit by containment force-field grilles — ceramite alone would not have been enough, and the engineer who designed the cells had calculated every component for the strength of a *Castraferrum* Dreadnought without limiters. The occupant was at the back, against the wall, and it took a moment for eyes to adjust to red light and scale.

4.4 meters. Armor had been destroyed years ago — what remained was the body, and the body was enough. A dense dark **coat** covered most of the torso and limbs. The head was too large for human scale, the jaw thrust forward, canines long enough to show even with the mouth closed. The eyes were the most disturbing — not because they were animal, but because they were not wholly animal. Something inside blinked intermittently, like a weak radio signal through interference.

The lapse of consciousness. The fragment Drusus had identified as the condition for remaining in the cells.

Trajann stood before the grille a long moment.

— Tell me what he is now — he said. — Clinically.

Drusus turned to the panel.

---

## IV. THE BRIEFING

— Body mass: between three hundred thirty and three hundred eighty kilograms without armor. Bone density is roughly double that of an Astartes in Exanimus stage — which is already double standard Astartes. Upper-limb strike kinetic impact exceeds that of a Dreadnought power fist without hydraulic limiting. — Drusus paged data with the same tone he would use for mechanical component specs. — Sustained sprint speed in open space: fifty to fifty-six miles per hour. In a closed corridor, mass and agility together create a containment geometry that essentially rules out interdiction by standard infantry.

— Armament?

— Claws. Canines. Skull impact structure functions as ram, with documented penetration capacity of three centimeters standard ceramite per direct strike. No artificial armament — the organism dispensed with the need.

— Gene-seed — said Trajann. — Status.

— Biologically compromised in phenotypic expression. Structurally intact. — A pause with clinical weight. — No contamination from **the Warp.**

Trajann looked at him.

— Confirm that.

— Confirmed. — Drusus did not hesitate. — Execratio is a genetic expression failure, not daemonic corruption. **Chimeric** architecture — **Strand α** primary scaffold, **Strand β** secondary — produces, under unmanaged extreme biological stress, a phenotypic expression cascade that forces the organism beyond containment parameters. There is no Warp influence in that process. A **Versibar** cannot be possessed by a daemon. It cannot be corrupted by Chaos beyond what it already is. It is a broken biological machine, not an Immaterium aberration.

— What makes it different from a fallen Astartes? — asked Trajann.

— A fallen Astartes chooses treason — said Varro; the words came through his ***Executor* wolf-skull helm** — the same brutal cast as a Fenrisian **Wolf Priest’s**, but consecrated to **Judex** law, not hearth-counsel. — A **Versibar** chooses nothing. Biology stripped choice before the soul could betray. — A pause. — That is simultaneously less dangerous and more tragic.

Trajann turned to the cell again. The occupant had moved — now closer to the grille, eyes on the group, nose working. Classifying scents.

— How you kill one efficiently — said Trajann. Not a question. The next item on the list.

— Spine — said Alaric, for the first time since the descent. The voice was low and absolutely direct. — Total destruction of upper cervical segments, or force-blade section at the articulation between C2 and C3. Same as would kill me. — A two-second pause. — Full-charge plasma held against the brain stem also works. Standard bolter to the torso is noise.

— And you — said Trajann. — You can kill one.

— I can command one, to a point — said Alaric. — And if command fails, I can kill one. Same skill applied in two directions.

— Alpha Frequency — said Trajann. Clearly from Tyvar’s report.

— Yes.

— And if you fall?

Silence for three seconds.

— For that — said Alaric — I have Tyvar.

---

## V. THE QUESTION NO ONE ASKS

They rose back to the *Strategium*. Trajann’s six escort Custodes stood exactly where they had been left — motionless, spears vertical, helms sealed.

Trajann stood before the window to the forge yard. Watched the chimneys a moment.

— Why did you show me that? — he said, without turning.

— Because you would find out anyway — said Alaric. — And I prefer you find out from me than from an Inquisition report drafted by someone who does not understand the difference between what we are and what a fallen Chapter is.

— Most Chapters would hide the cells.

— Most Chapters do not have a Custodes living in the fortress for **one hundred twenty-seven years** — said Alaric. — Tyvar already knows everything. What I am showing you is that I have nothing to hide from whoever has duty to kill me if I fail.

Trajann turned to him.

— You believe you can fail.

— I know I can fail — said Alaric. — Every Astartes can. The difference between me and most is that I built containment architecture before I needed it. — He looked at Tyvar. — Tyvar is part of that architecture. Always was.

Trajann looked at the Castellan a moment. Then at Tyvar.

— **One hundred twenty-seven years** — he said.

— **One hundred twenty-eight** in three months — said Tyvar.

— And your judgment? — said Trajann. The question was direct, meant for the Custodes, not the Castellan. — After **one hundred twenty-eight years**. Does the instrument still work?

Tyvar was silent two seconds. For him, that was long reflection.

— The instrument has flaws it documents voluntarily and monitors actively — said Tyvar. — It requests supervision instead of avoiding it. It maintains containment protocols most Chapters would deny having. — A pause. — That is not a guarantee of reliability. It is evidence the mind still works.

— Is that enough for a Custodes? — said Trajann.

— No Astartes is enough for a Custodes — said Tyvar. — But this one is conscious enough of what it is to be usefully watched.

Trajann nodded once.

---

## VI. THE NIHILI PROTOCOL

— I will leave more Custodes — said Trajann. — Not because you asked. Because what I saw in the cells justifies expanded permanent presence, not reduced. — He looked at Alaric. — Four additional guardians, semiannual rotation. Tyvar retains permanent command.

— I accept — said Alaric.

— And there is a second matter.

Trajann opened a sealed compartment on his armor forearm — a gesture Custodes rarely made in third-party presence — and withdrew a dark gray metal cylinder, roughly fist-sized, with no visible markings except a black wax sigil Alaric recognized as the Captain-General’s personal seal.

Trajann walked to Tyvar and placed the cylinder in the Custodes’ hand.

— *Nihili Protocol* — he said. — Activation requires physical breakage of the sigil followed by a six-digit code you will receive on a separate channel. Effect: cascading overload of Blackstone Pylons on the border network. The overload destabilizes the subsector’s temporal anchor in a centripetal collapse. — A pause. — Nothing biological inside the containment radius survives. No physical structure remains intact. No gene-seed. No data. No **Versibar**.

The silence in the room had a different quality after that.

— Conditions of activation — said Alaric. The voice was absolutely calm.

— Tyvar’s exclusive judgment — said Trajann. — If the Castellan falls and containment fails. If the Chapter is compromised irreversibly. If the instrument becomes a greater threat than asset. — He looked at Alaric. — You have no veto on this.

— I know — said Alaric.

— And you agree.

— I built this fortress to be the last wall — said Alaric. — If the wall falls, it must not leave a passage. — A pause. — The *Nihili Protocol* is the logical conclusion of everything we built here.

Trajann looked at him a moment.

— You are the most unusual instrument I have met in ten thousand years — he said. — And I have met many instruments.

— Is that praise? — said Drusus, so clinically it was impossible to tell if it was a genuine question.

— It is an observation — said Trajann.

---

## VII. DEPARTURE AND THE NAME

Trajann remained on Noviomagus three days. He met Kadmos to inspect the forges. He met Otho to assess the *Librarium*. He visited the Black Cells a second time, alone, without the Triumvirate.

No one present knew what he thought on that second visit.

When he left, the outbound transmission was a single line, without formal protocol:

*"The Administratum will log Castra Vetera Subsector and the Loken Passage as Porta Nihili. Strategic coordinates of permanent Throne interest. Geographic designation, not honorific."*

Alaric read the transmission in the *Strategium*. Then he looked at Tyvar, in standard position beside the forge window.

— *Porta Nihili* — said Alaric. — The gate of nothing.

— If it falls — said Tyvar — the void consumes it.

— That is what the name means.

— Yes — said Tyvar.

Alaric looked at the Noviomagus chimneys a moment.

— It is the right name — he said.

Tyvar did not answer. But there was no dissent in the silence.

The four additional Custodes arrived the following cycle. Noviomagus Prime now had five golden guardians in its corridors.

None of them sat. None ate. None removed the helm.

The Throne had increased its attention. For the Batavi, who had asked for vigilance from the beginning, that was the only form of recognition that made sense.
