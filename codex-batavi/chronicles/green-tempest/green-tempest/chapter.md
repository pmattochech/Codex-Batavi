## The First Day: The Shadow on the Sensors

The war was not announced with blood, but with cold mathematics.

Seven days before the first scrap-meteor Ork tore Noviomagus forge-atmosphere, the deep-listening cogitators at **Witness Ford** screamed in binary — then the **Threshold** trench arrays echoed the same verdict in a harmonics band that made Navigator liaison officers leave the room without explanation. Gravitational anomaly. Biological density. Psychic effervescence so colossal it bent starlight the way a thumb bends wax.

Lord Castellan Alaric von Helis stood at the *Strategium* holo-table when confirmation sealed: *Waaagh! Skarjaw*.

The initial response followed *Codex* doctrine with the indifference of a man inventorying bolts. Millions of civilians drained into hive-root bunkers. Permacrete on the **South Wall** received ablative plating in shifts that did not stop for prayer. **Cohors Batavorum** positioned itself to be what Castra Vetera had forged it to be: the anvil on which the enemy would shatter.

Chief Apothecary Drusus did not watch the map. He watched the line.

In the *Castrum Medicae*, hormone charts for every **Vexillatio** on the wall trembled before auspex could resolve fleet silhouettes. Cortisol spiked in patterns that did not match fear. Growth hormone rose as if the body had already answered a question no officer had asked aloud. Drusus logged the read as *pre-stimulus hypertrophy* and injected suppressors into his own carotid with a hand that did not shake because shaking would have been data.

Executor Chaplain Varro stood at the table’s far edge, cowled wolf-skull a matte absence in the holo-glow. He had not spoken since the Ford screamed. Silence, for Varro, was not empty; it was load-bearing.

— Millions — a Munitorum liaison said, voice too bright for the room. — They will want the wall to look like a wall.

Alaric did not look at him.

— It is a wall — he said. — Reinforce the South sector. Do not retreat.

The liaison heard *Codex*. The *Strategium* heard *geometry*. Drusus heard marrow voting.

## The Fifth Day: The Pack’s Shiver

As the Ork scrap fleet crossed the Threshold trench and began to foul Noviomagus approach lanes, the forge-world’s upper atmosphere grew suffocating — saturated with latent psychic pressure. The *Waaagh!* field projected radiating violence: a primal terror that made mortal noses bleed in bunker dark and turned children’s dreams into wet, soundless falling.

For the Nine Phalanx garrison, the effect inverted.

Controlled lethargy evaporated from fortress corridors. Radio silence fractured into electric tension. Battle-brothers abandoned maintenance rites on bolters and honed melee blades with manic, almost liturgical obsession. Ozone, coolant, and acidic transhuman sweat fouled barracks until serfs opened vents that had not been opened since the Foundation War’s last repayment clause was signed.

That was not tactical anxiety. It was hunger.

Drusus walked the inner galleries with a med-slate and a private loathing of glassware. Every beaker looked fragile enough to crush. He filed the urge as *Furor-adjacent irritability* and doubled suppressor intervals for himself alone — the Eye that watches cannot become the claw that smashes.

On the *Castrum Ferrum* parapet, Alaric stood motionless against a sky bruising green at the edges. Optical monitors showed his pupils losing their roundness: iris consumed by an orange halo, pupil narrowing toward a vertical slit. He did not pace. He did not speak. He faced the burning atmosphere the way a dam faces a reservoir — not in denial of weight, but in refusal to move the datum.

Varro found him there at hour nineteen.

— The Reclusiam hears the same pressure — Varro said, vox low. — Brothers are praying with their hands on knives.

— Let them hold steel — Alaric said. — Steel is honest.

— And the Axiom?

A beat. Wind carried forge-smoke and distant civilian sirens — throughput theology expressed as *descend now, count later*.

— The Axiom is the collar — Alaric said. — Not the leash.

Varro inclined his skull a fraction — assent without comfort. Below them, Noviomagus continued to file itself: bunkers sealed, tithe roads cleared, Administratum stamps pressed into manifests as if paper could outrank biology. The Chapter had learned that trick in the crucible. Paper had survived. Flesh had learned to read paper as warning.

## The Sixth Day: Cellular Ignition

Twenty-four hours before orbital impact, the chimeric line — **two predator architectures** pulling the same marrow — collided with Ork effervescence along the entire South Wall.

There was no command on the open *vox*. No strategium order. No Castellan gesture. The transition was a unanimous cellular detonation: proximity of millions of xenos hungry for war fracturing chemical inhibitors that kept the Beast caged in gene-code. The **Wild Hunt exponent** had begun.

Sergeant **Kael**, patrolling a delayed evacuation corridor, stopped dead.

Musculature hypertrophied inside ceramite; sealed plates groaned against sudden mass expansion. Beneath tactical helm lenses his physiology regressed — iris dilated, orange halo, pupil shrinking to a predatory slit. Irritability became pain. A *servitor* stumbling, a child’s distant cry, a fragile human heart in a sealed stairwell — all echoed like artillery in hyper-developed auditory channels.

Kael let out a low growl that made permacrete tremble and civilians flatten against ablative siding. He did not strike the weak. The **Axiom of Reason** worked like iron on his mind: he watched humans not as prey but as helpless pups of the pack. Overflowing aggression turned entirely toward the celestial vault. He *wanted* the ships to fall. Holding violence back lacerated nerve.

Along the wall, gray giants paced in circles, scraping barricades with gauntlets, snarling at stars.

Drusus saw it on the slate before he saw it on the stone.

— Not a tactic — he told Varro on a private channel. — A metabolic vote.

— Can you veto it?

— Not without killing the voter.

Varro’s answer was silence again — the kind that admitted the rift could hear careless certainty. On the triage mezzanine, mortal medicae had begun to understand the paradigm shift without vocabulary for it: humanity’s guardian angels had become chained abominations, and only milliseconds remained before the chains snapped.

## The Seventh Day: The Wall of Blades

When hollow Ork landing asteroids punched atmosphere, the skies bled plasma and toxic smoke.

The green tide poured from impact craters by the thousands — roaring, expecting the Imperial answer: disciplined firing trenches, bolter storms, saturation artillery, slow strategic withdrawal. Orks had come to break shields.

What they found was predatory silence.

*Batavian Iron-Guard* were not crouched in trenches. They stood exposed on barricade parapets. Bolters mag-locked at waists. In their hands, chainswords, power axes, and heavy trench knives hummed, thirsty.

When the xeno wave hit gray ceramite, the *Codex Astartes* tore like wet parchment. This was not defense. It was industrial slaughter.

Muscular strength, supersaturated by endocrine storm, nullified Ork mass advantage. Strategy gave way to visceral instinct. A field scribe recorded a 3rd Company veteran — weapon smashed — seizing an Ork *Nob* with bare hands and, with a roar that obliterated jet-engine noise, ripping the alien thorax in half by brute traction alone.

They did not fight like line infantry. They fought like apex predators defending the threshold of their own nest.

Kael sheathed nothing now. He moved in the pack’s language: short leaps, impact before thought, blade seeking throat and not honor. Drusus walked behind the line injecting coagulation stimulants — not analgesics — calculating terminal cost while brothers howled inward. He was a cursed gardener watching iron trees burn a thousand years of sap in one day. Magnificent. Absolutely terrifying.

## The Climax: The Duel at the Gate

At the meat grinder’s epicenter, the green storm made room for its avatar.

Warboss **Skarjaw** rose from wreckage like a leviathan of scar tissue, irregular tusks, and hydraulic pistons — cackling amid carnage, power klaw raised to decapitate the gray *humie* who dared stand in his tide.

Alaric von Helis did not dodge. He did not fall back on Castra Vetera fencing rehearsed in cleaner wars.

Alpha frequency — not sorcery, not litany; bio-telepathic sub-vocal resonance locked to chimeric marrow — shattered the air around him. He deactivated his power gladius and let it drop. Wild Hunt tolerated no intermediaries; it demanded intimacy of contact.

He took Skarjaw’s full charge head-on.

*Terminator* servos screamed under tons of kinetic force. Adamantine boots did not yield a centimeter on blood-stained ferrocrete. Ferrocrete under the Castellan’s soles fractured into a three-meter crater; Alaric’s geometry did not.

Both hands free, he drove fingers into the Warboss’s lower jaw — riveted metal, thick bone, Ork arrogance made flesh.

For a fraction of a second the war’s cacophony went mute.

Skarjaw stared into helm lenses and, perhaps for the first time in a life of massacres, found an atrocity older and wilder than his species. Leverage driven by Wild Hunt aberrant biomechanics shattered cranial reinforcements. Mandible tore free with a wet, mechanical rip.

The leviathan collapsed, choking on liters of black blood and oil.

Alaric raised the dripping trophy and loosed a thunderous roar — not military victory cry but absolute proclamation of territorial dominance.

The *Waaagh!* energy cracked. Orks — organisms wired never to know fear — felt atavistic terror of an undeniable superpredator. The green tide fell back. They fled.

On the *vox*, someone began to chant *We hold.* Alaric did not repeat it. The wall had already spoken.

## Epilogue: The Price of Flesh

Eradication completed. Noviomagus remained under the Aquila. Forges would burn again.

When the last Ork escape engine vanished over the horizon and silence returned to the plateau, the bill came due.

Wild Hunt creates no energy from nothing; it is a violent loan against the organism’s future. When the adrenaline peak crashed, Batavi biology collapsed in domino. No victory chants. No banner raisings. Only the hollow sound of hundreds of tons of ceramite hitting blood-stained ground as battle-brothers fell unconscious.

Alaric — still drenched in enemy ichor — took two stumbling steps toward the Imperial triage line before collapsing like a decapitated statue.

Drusus’s subsequent reports revealed the scale: acute multi-organ failure — tissue self-calcined by hyper-accelerated metabolism. They had beaten the green storm because they let themselves be monsters for one solar cycle. Transhuman flesh was not built to host a hurricane that long without coming apart.

In stasis chambers beneath the *Castrum Ferrum*, while **all Vexillationes** present sank into regenerative coma filed as ***Exanimus* stage**, one observation outlived the immediate casualty lists: the Lord Castellan’s nearly destroyed left arm did not atrophy into keloid. Cells entered febrile mitotic boil — weaving bone and nerve from ash in weeks, not months. Wild Hunt burned the body in a chemical forge; what remained chose to mold into harder alloy than the steel it replaced.

The Chapter’s doctrine hardened its harshest lesson:

*The Beast is the most terrible of tools. Slip its chains, and the sector may live. But let it breathe too long, and the conduit that carries it will be reduced to ash.*

Drusus closed the record with a margin note the *Strategium* would not circulate for years: *We are not degenerating. We are overclocking. The pendulum has not yet earned its name.*

— End of Record
