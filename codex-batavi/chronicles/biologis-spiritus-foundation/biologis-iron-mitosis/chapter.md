## I. Dogma and Heresy

The Adeptus Mechanicus worships gear with ignorant devotion. Terran medical doctrine states a simple rule: flesh torn away in battle must be replaced with metal. A lost leg becomes a piston; a severed arm, an augmetic claw. The Belisarian furnace was designed to patch soldiers and return them to the trench.

After **Skarjaw** fell and the South Wall went quiet, I walked rows of amniotic tanks until the *Apothecarion* sounded like a greenhouse for nightmares.

Wild Hunt is not merely stimulant flood. That is the surface. In the cubes along the outer ring, amputated brothers were not healing — they were **refusing** to accept amputation as final state. Humeral stumps wove bone from lipid ash. Nerves crawled like solder. Chronic organ failures **reset** as if the body had decided Guilliman’s patterns were suggestions written by men who had never met an Ork tide.

I filed the phenomenon **predatory reconstruction** in a margin no Inquisitor was meant to read without Triumvirate clearance.

That alone would earn expurgation. What happened in the **central tank** — the Alpha cocoon — challenged transhuman anatomy itself.

## II. Death Certificate

They brought me what remained of Alaric von Helis hours after he tore the *Warboss*’s jaw free.

My slate wrote the verdict before my mouth did:

* Massive cranial fractures; frontal-lobe hemorrhage saturating subdural space.  
* Double ventricular rupture; primary and secondary hearts burst under Wild Hunt hydraulic overload.  
* Three lungs collapsed; xenos toxin drowning in viscous filtrate.  
* Left arm absent from mid-humerus — crushed, pulped, discarded on the plateau with *Terminator* plating that had failed geometry, not courage.

Clinical death. By *Codex* protocol I ordered Tech-Priests to warm a *Dreadnought* chassis reactor to entomb what remained of his mind before rot became theology.

— The chassis is for men who stay dead — I told the enginseer.

— Then he is the wrong category of corpse — the priest answered, voice filtered through incense and offended logic. — Your auspex still graphs intent.

He was correct. Alpha frequency — that damned balance between cold command and caged fury — pulsed on my screen like a second heartbeat arguing with the first. The Castellan’s biology rejected my decree the way a wall rejects apology.

I submerged him in maximum-density bacta and sealed the cocoon.

Varro stood at the viewport once, wolf-skull reflecting boiling fluid.

— If he wakes wrong — Varro said — the Reclusiam will know before your charts.

— If he wakes at all — I said — the charts will not be enough.

## III. The Alpha Cocoon (Four Weeks)

**Week one**

Thermal alarms screamed. Bacta in the central tank **boiled** — not metaphor; near-radiant temperature climb that fogged armored glass and made servitors step back as if heat had moral weight.

Through the viewport I watched the left stump **explode** with growth. Flesh was not knitting; it was **carved** by genetic fury, bone extruding in boiling synthetic blood like a smith pulling steel from a white furnace. I logged cell division rates that would have been comedy if they were not killing the man they were saving.

**Week two**

I stopped pretending the skull would reassemble like a puzzle. The frontal lobe did not restore old pathways — it **deleted** them. Memory and personality traits filed as caloric waste, traded for connections optimized to tactical processing and survival instinct.

Myocardium regenerated spontaneously. The primary heart that formed was **wrong** in the only way medicine respects: denser, louder, built to pump blood thick enough for Alpha demand. I felt analytical ice slide down my own spine and hated that I could admire the engineering while mourning what it ate.

**Week three**

Varro returned when the tank’s hum changed pitch — the sound of a body negotiating with its own blueprint.

— He is not becoming Versibar — I said before the Judex could ask. — Do not file it that way.

— I file what the soul sounds like — Varro answered. — Not what you wish the slate said.

We watched silence together. Load-bearing, as always.

**Week four — day thirty**

Fluids drained. Decompression hiss. The glass unlocked.

Alaric tore from coma and stood naked on the *Apothecarion* grate, soaked in synthetic blood that steamed in the cold recycled air.

He did not sway. He did not ask where he was. His eyes found the viewport, then me, then the door — geometry first, names later.

---

## IV. Examination

I examined him in silence because speech would have been vanity.

Motricity in the left arm: one hundred percent. Virgin skin on newborn limb — pale, unscarred, obscene against a torso that was a topographic map of centuries. Fingers closed on my *Narthecium* with pressure that made the tool complain.

— Report — he said. One word. Low. Open channel habit even here.

— You were dead — I answered. — You disagreed.

A beat. Not humor. Calibration.

— The wall — he said.

— Holds — I said. — Noviomagus holds. The 3rd Company is in induced coma on the lower slabs. You will sign nothing today.

He looked at the new arm as if it were issued kit — not gift, not curse, **equipment**.

— Skarjaw — he said.

— Dead — I said. — Jaw in the field morgue if you want a trophy. The *Waaagh!* broke.

He nodded once. The nod cost him nothing visible. That was the terror: the bill had been paid somewhere I could not auspex.

I ran the mandatory neural sweep. Frontal lobe: reduced autobiographical density; elevated threat-mapping bands. Heart: nonstandard mass, acceptable output. Marrow: febrile mitotic residue — **iron mitosis**, I wrote in the margin, because the Chapter needed a name that sounded like metallurgy and not prayer.

— You traded memory for function — I told him. — I cannot quantify how much. You will discover absences the way men discover missing teeth — with the tongue, not the chart.

He accepted the sentence the way he accepted siege: as weather.

— Cuirass — he said.

I handed him *Castra-Lupus* segments from the sterile rack. He dressed without assistance. Each magnetic lock sang a small industrial prayer.

When the last plate sealed, he was again the Lord Castellan — not miracle, not Emperor’s mercy, but **aberration** that obeyed military function’s tyranny.

Yet when he turned to leave, his gaze caught mine through my surgical helm lenses.

Something archaic looked out. Not Versibar hunger. Not Furor heat. A predator that had learned to file reports.

---

## V. The Price Equation

Our commander’s recovery was not miracle. Where common Astartes scar only enough not to bleed out, the Alpha rebuilds from absolute zero to dominate the environment that wounded him. The Beast caged in his cells does not allow the chassis to stop.

Each reconstruction at this magnitude, I now believe, spends humanity as coin. The math is simple; spare parts are finite. The day will come when nothing human remains to pay the beast’s price.

Until then, he commands — and I watch the margins.

I sealed a Triumvirate copy and burned the draft that used the word *miracle*. Miracles invite Inquisitors. **Overclock** invites pharmacology.

Alaric von Helis walked out of my laboratory on his own feet. The forge-world’s forges burned again above us. Below, brothers slept off a storm the Chapter had not yet learned to harness.

I am the Brake. If I fail, Wild Hunt stops being weather and becomes climate.

*Instrumentum Solum.*

— Drusus.
