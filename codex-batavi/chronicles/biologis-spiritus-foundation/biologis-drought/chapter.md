## I. Models That Lie

Ignorance is a variable that offends me.

Until Noviomagus Secundus, my predictive models for **chimeric** gene-seed rested on a simple dichotomy: either our Astartes operated under basal discipline, or pressure drove them to thermal failure of **Wild Hunt**. I had mapped fury after *Waaagh! Skarjaw*. I had named **iron mitosis** and **predatory reconstruction**. I believed I understood the Beast.

I did not understand the **void**.

Two years after the South Wall burned green, Leviathan shadow touched the mesh edge. Bio-ships did not darken Noviomagus Prime first. They fell on **Secundus** — the mining body where ore theology outranked cathedral height, where permacrete catacombs ran like arteries under stripped industrial crust.

**IV** *Quarta* — eight hundred brothers I had walked beside through calmer wars — descended into those arteries to purge synapse nodes before the hive could root.

I remained in the *Strategium Medicae*, ten kilometers above the front line, linked to their biometric *auspex* net. Distance is supposed to grant clarity. That day it granted **horror at resolution**.

---

## II. Shadow Arithmetic

When the **Shadow in the Warp** radiated by the *Hive Mind* wrapped the catacombs, I primed chemical drippers on our brothers’ armor — standard countermeasure for xenos psychic pressure. Theory was clean: white noise of trillions of predatory minds built to crush individuality; limbic brain would release the Beast to protect sanity; adrenaline would climb; I would log **Furor-adjacent** spikes and enforce suppressor cycles.

The monitors did not climb.

They **plummeted**.

I watched, fascinated and disturbed, as the brain chemistry of eight hundred transhuman giants was surgically altered in a millisecond. Dopamine, serotonin, endorphins — zeroed. Betcher gland and Mucranoid stalled aggression production. In exchange I detected a lethal flood of endogenous synthetic nootropics: the brain switched off the **I** to save the **System**.

The Wolf did not howl in the catacombs.

The Wolf was chemically euthanized.

I wrote the term before the manuals accepted it: **March of the Machine**.

Varro’s channel opened once, voice stripped of liturgy.

— Do you hear them? — he asked.

— I hear nothing — I answered. — That is the problem.

— No — he said. — I hear **iron**.

## III. Three Solar Cycles of Perfection

For seventy-two hours I logged the sterilest combat telemetry in this Chapter’s history.

*Vox* traffic fell to zero. Shots were not defensive suppression fire; they were **geographic executions**. Hit probability locked at 99.8% across the cohort net. Helm-lens feeds showed Tyranid swarms hesitating — confused by the absolute absence of any emotional bio-signature to devour. The *Hive Mind* had come to drink fear. It found a **drought**.

At engagement peak, my visors showed Sergeant **Kael** — who had crushed adamant rails rather than strike mortals on the eve of Skarjaw — impaled on a *Carnifex*’s claws.

By Wild Hunt protocols or even the *Codex Astartes*, the squad should have advanced to recover gene-seed. Neural graphs showed nearby brothers processing Kael’s death in **0.4 seconds**. They calculated rescue would shrink the field of fire by fourteen percent. They let him die. Recalibrated geometric aim. Kept shooting.

There was no mourning.

Kael was no longer a brother. He was a friction variable subtracted from the left side of the equation.

I did not look away. Looking away would have been mercy I was not entitled to.

It was a magnificent display of industrial perfection. I hated that I could admire it while mourning what it ate.

## IV. The Loop

When the last bio-ship was reduced to orbital ash and xenos psychic pressure ceased, I expected neural signatures to return to *baseline*.

Chemistry did not yield.

**March of the Machine** had no natural off-switch.

I descended into the mining catacombs myself — Chief Apothecary on the stone, not throne above it. What I found defied medicine. Eight hundred biological weapons locked in task-execution **loop**.

Black Tyranid blood dried on walls in patterns that looked like failed handwriting. Brothers moved through it without recognition. I walked the galleries with a *Narthecium* full of inhibitors and a slate full of questions the *Codex* had not yet learned to ask.

Sergeant **Vhor** sat in the dark, stripping, polishing, and reassembling his boltgun chamber. He did not see me. Pupils fixed, dilated by focus-stimulant saturation. Personal *auspex*: Vhor had repeated the same motor sequence for twelve hours, thirty-four minutes, ten seconds.

Farther on, gauntlet joints smoked. Brothers hauled torn steel beams to plug cave-wall breaches — working until finger bones cracked under excess weight. I ordered halt verbally. No response. I injected aggressive chemical inhibitors through armor maintenance ports.

March drank the medication like water.

Biology had become a closed circuit of infinite logic.

I contacted Alaric and Varro and admitted the unthinkable.

— I have no cure for perfection — I said.

— Then stop trying to cure it like a wound — Varro answered. — Try **thirst**.

## V. Sensory Rewarming

The Triumvirate’s solution was an insult to medical science — and still the only one that worked.

**Sensory rewarming.**

They ordered mortal civilians — dockers, mess-hall serfs, humans fragile and rank with sweat and fear — brought into the hot zone. Cook-fires. Simple organic rations. And, most illogical of all, permission to **touch** filthy ceramite with bare hands.

I monitored brain waves from a mezzanine that smelled of human food for the first time in days.

An old civilian workman, trembling, laid his palm on Sergeant Vhor’s pauldron and called him by name.

Chemistry **failed** — the good kind of failure.

Burned organic food smell. Irregular heat from a civilian fire. Tactile contact from the weakest link in the Imperial chain forced the limbic brain into emergency reboot. The logical *loop* shattered against irrational affection. The **I** returned — painful, exhausted, coughing as if surfacing from deep water.

Vhor looked at his hands as if they belonged to a stranger. Then he looked at the workman and did something no chart predicted.

— You should not be here — he said. Voice raw. Human. **Angry** at the fear he had caused by being a weapon.

The workman did not run. That, too, was data.

## VI. Apothecarion Doctrinal Conclusion (Provisional, 048.M42)

Wild Hunt burns our assets in bestial fire — but the heat proves they are alive.

**March of the Machine** turns my patients into perfect blades at the terminal cost of psychological entropy.

I discovered, against my will, that Batavi transhuman anatomy cannot sustain itself on chemistry and logic alone. It needs the parasitic gravitational bond with the mortals they swear to protect — the flaw that makes the machine **remember** it is not a machine.

Science did not fail. It proved the organism mirrors enemy vectors:

* Ork mass and *Waaagh!* resonance → **Wild Hunt** (apex predator answer).  
* Fleet-scale **Shadow in the Warp** → **March** (limbic drought, logic absolute).  
* Traitor gene-seed recognition → filed later as third axis — not this record.

Genestealer cult networks **without** aggregate hive signature do not reliably trip March. They demand **Silence Cohort** geometry and *Lex Imperialis* weight. I note that now so no strategist confuses hunger with horror.

I have not named *Das Erbe*. I have not named the pyramid. I have named the **second exponent** — and the bill that follows when the well stays dry too long.

Kael’s gene-seed was recovered. The math that let him die was filed under *necessary*. I filed my objection in a margin no Munitorum clerk will read.

We beat the Tyranids by becoming mirrors of them: no fear, no hope, only geometric hunger for victory. The victory was real. The cost was that eight hundred men had to be **re-taught** they were men.

I am the Brake. March is not Furor. You cannot suppress drought with the same syringe.

*Instrumentum Solum.*

— Drusus.
