## I. The Seven-Day Vigil (Pre-Impact)

**Day −6**

Deep-listening cogitators at **Witness Ford** confirmed the *Waaagh!* before the **Threshold** trench arrays agreed in a harmonics band that made Navigator liaison officers leave briefing rooms without courtesy. I did not leave. I stayed with the charts.

Disordered biological mass. Chaotic psychic effervescence — an affront to symmetry if you still believe the universe owes symmetry to surgeons. What disturbed my diagnostics was not fleet tonnage. It was the read on my own brothers’ biometric *auspex*: testosterone, endorphins, growth hormone in the 3rd Company rising two hundred percent from the millisecond the Orks’ shadow touched the mesh edge.

There are no tactical orders for this. The *Codex Astartes* does not foresee marrow voting in advance of command.

I logged *pre-stimulus hypertrophy* and filed a suppressor cycle for the wall **Vexillationes**. I did not file hope.

**[Medical self-analysis]**

Electric buzz at the base of my skull, near cortical implants. Nameless irritation at the fragility of glass test tubes in the *Apothecarion* — each one a small planet that could shatter if I closed my fist wrong. I injected five milligrams nootropic suppressors into my own carotid ten minutes after the Ford screamed. Shaking would have been data. I am Chief Apothecary. I must be the Eye that watches and the Scalpel that cuts — not the Claw that smashes.

Varro found me at the slab array when the third chart spiked.

— Your hands are steady — he said on private channel. — That is not always mercy.

— It is procedure — I answered. — If I become the specimen, who files the specimen?

Silence on his end — load-bearing, the way Reclusiam silence is architecture, not absence.

**Day −4**

Civilian bunkers swallowed millions. Permacrete crews worked through black rain without singing. On the South Wall, fire discipline held by habit alone while muscular tension **of the line** became visible to unaugmented eyes: servos groaning, gauntlets clenched, throats working on nothing. My brothers were not afraid of rocks falling from the sky. They were *hungry* — and hunger, in a transhuman, is never only metaphor.

I walked the inner galleries with a med-slate and hated glassware anew. Every beaker looked fragile enough to crush. I doubled my suppressor interval and told no one, because telling would have been a confession that the Brake could slip.

**Day −2**

Lord Castellan Alaric abandoned the holo-map. He spent hours on the *Castrum Ferrum* parapet, face to a burning atmosphere that had not yet admitted the first scrap-meteor. Through optical monitors I watched his pupils lose roundness: orange halo devouring iris, pupil narrowing toward a vertical slit.

Our **chimeric** architecture — **two predator threads** pulling the same marrow — answered physical threat with acute hypertrophy, as if gene-seed had scented the Orks’ biological challenge (*Who is strongest in this system?*) and chosen to answer in the exact guttural tongue the xenos understood.

I sent a sealed note to the *Strategium*: *Recommend elevated Furor watch on Castellan telemetry. Do not interrupt his vigil with maps.*

They filed it under *morale*.

**Day −1**

Executor Chaplain Varro and I stood in the *Castrum Medicae* overlook while forge-sirens and distant civilian descent alarms braided into a single pressure on the ears.

— The Reclusiam hears knives in prayer — Varro said.

— I hear hormones — I said. — Prayer is cheaper.

He inclined his wolf-skull a fraction — assent without comfort.

— And the Axiom?

— The Axiom is a collar — I said. — Not a cure. We are days from learning whether the collar holds when the pack smells blood at scale.

Below us, Noviomagus filed itself: manifests, stamps, throughput theology expressed as *descend now, count later*. The Chapter had learned that trick in the crucible. Paper survived. Flesh was only now admitting paper had been warning it.

**Day 0 — The Fall**

Landing rocks split the ground. Fetid xenos spores and burned engine oil flooded trench geometry. Logical protocol dictated siege defense and friction. My **Vexillatio** brothers magnet-locked bolters to their waists as if firearms had become shame.

Through cranial servo-lenses I saw Sergeant **Kael** sheathe his firearm and draw his heavy trench knife. No *vox* order from command. No Castellan gesture on open channel. Pure chemical consensus along the South Wall — unanimous cellular detonation.

I wrote the term the manuals would later fight over: **Wild Hunt exponent**. At this date we had no *Curia* clearance bands, no edge/deep ledger, no Genetor sign-off. We had only the Beast answering the *Waaagh!* before doctrine learned its name.

## II. Geometry of Flesh (Field Observation)

Terran military theory states an iron rule: never engage Orkoid organisms in melee if fire superiority can avoid it. Ork physiology is geometrically superior in mass, organ redundancy, blunt trauma resistance. On Noviomagus plains that day, *Cohors Batavorum* refuted the rule through industrial slaughter — and I refuted my own peace of mind by walking inside it.

Under Wild Hunt’s endocrine storm, strength, traction, and impact rose in geometric progression. I watched brothers ignore wounds that should have incapacitated — limbs pierced by shrapnel, ceramite torn — not from stoicism but because central nervous systems shut pain receptors so motor adrenaline would not stutter.

I did not administer analgesics. I administered coagulation stimulants and logged terminal cost on graphs that would horrify any Mechanicus auditor who believed flesh was gear.

**Blade Wall**

We did not fight as Imperial soldiers. We fought as superpredators defending spawning territory. I visually logged a 2nd Company brother whose visor had shattered using his own tactical helm as a club to beat an Ork *Nob* to cranial failure. Motor efficiency was brutal, filthy, devoid of Castra Vetera fencing elegance. It aimed exclusively at traumatic, irreversible destruction.

Mortals on the triage mezzanine learned a paradigm shift without vocabulary: guardian angels had become chained abominations, and only milliseconds remained before the chains snapped. The **Axiom of Reason** held — I saw it hold in Kael’s geometry when he turned aggression toward the vault and not toward the weak — but holding cost laceration you could hear in growls that made permacrete tremble.

**Alpha duel — telemetry**

Lord Castellan Alaric von Helis met *Warboss* Skarjaw at the meat grinder’s epicenter. My sensors warned his *Terminator* armor was compromised before he chose compromise. He deactivated his gladius and accepted the charge head-on — intimacy of contact, no intermediaries.

Alpha frequency spiked on my slate: not sorcery, not litany; bio-telepathic sub-vocal resonance locked to chimeric marrow. Ferrocrete under his boots fractured into a three-meter crater. Servos screamed. Adamantine did not yield.

When he drove fingers into Skarjaw’s jaw and tore mandible free with hydraulic leverage, the *Waaagh!* field cracked. The horde fled not from tactical defeat but from atavistic terror — the primitive mind meeting something older and wilder than Ork arrogance.

I stood in the trench line with a *Narthecium* full of coagulant and a mind artificially anchored in function’s lethargy.

**[Apothecary’s perception]**

I felt displaced from my own lineage. While my brothers howled inward, lost to slaughter, I only calculated. Muscles tearing beyond bone limit. Micro-infarcts blooming like bad stars. Future renal and hepatic collapse written in advance on charts that would become doctrine if we survived long enough to be ashamed of them.

I was a cursed gardener watching a forest of iron trees burn a thousand years of sap in one day. Magnificent. Absolutely terrifying.

## III. Cost of Victory (Rebound and Mitosis)

The exact moment external stimulus — Ork biological presence — cleared the perimeter, Wild Hunt collected its caloric debt.

General systemic collapse. The entire 3rd Company collapsed to the deck: hypovolemic shock, simultaneous multi-organ failure. Bodies had devoured calcium, lipid, and vital protein reserves to sustain an abomination of strength no *Codex* chapter was built to host for a full solar cycle without coming apart.

Hundreds of tons of ceramite hit blood-stained ground. No victory chants. Only the hollow sound of rebound — **biological**, not temporal; a loan against the organism’s future, not a dome rewinding cells.

Alaric — drenched in enemy ichor — took two stumbling steps toward triage before collapsing like a decapitated statue. I caught his telemetry on the way down: hearts that had burst under pressure now stuttering back toward rhythm as if biology refused the death certificate I had already drafted.

**Iron mitosis — first naming**

On surgical slabs I opened comatose brothers and found cellular regeneration refusing Guilliman’s patterns. *Batavi* tissue was not merely healing; it was reconstructing denser.

The Lord Castellan’s nearly destroyed left arm did not atrophy into keloid. Cells entered febrile mitotic boil — weaving bone matrices and nerve cords from ash in weeks, not months. Wild Hunt burns the body in a chemical forge; what remains chooses to mold into harder alloy than the steel it replaced.

I submerged him in maximum-density bacta and watched the stump explode in violent growth through viewport glass that fogged with heat. First week: thermal alarms. Second week: myocardium regenerating denser than before. Fourth week: he stood naked on the grate, newborn limb pale against a torso map of centuries, gaze archaic beneath heavy brows.

Recovery was not Emperor’s miracle. It was genetic engineering aberration filed under *overclock*.

**Apothecarion doctrinal conclusion (provisional, 046.M42)**

We are not suffering a degenerative disease. We are operating under reactive paramilitary biology.

Our architecture identifies brute-force chaos (Orks) and uncages the Beast — **Wild Hunt** — to fight on its terms. If it identifies cosmic horror and **mass psychic suppression** from the *Hive Mind* — the **Shadow in the Warp** that accompanies fleet-scale swarm — it will shut down limbic noise and surface the **Machine** (**March of the Machine**). **Genestealer cult networks without that aggregate signature are not a reliable trigger;** they demand another geometry (Silence Cohort, *Lex Imperialis*).

The secret of secular survival is not stopping transformation. It is surviving **rebound** when the storm passes — and, eventually, learning to harness fuel before it votes without us. That harness does not exist yet on this date. I am writing this the week the wall went quiet and the slabs filled.

I have not named the pendulum. I have not named *Das Erbe*. I have only named the bill.

In this triad we were forged: I am the Brake. Varro is the Anchor. Alaric is the Blade.

If I fail to monitor containment doses at these hormonal levels, Wild Hunt will cease to be a passing combat storm and become permanent natural state. An army that lives forever inside Wild Hunt is not an Adeptus Astartes Chapter; it is a horde of maddened beasts.

We are not Orks. We are the iron anvil that shatters them — provided the anvil remembers it is not the hammer.

*Instrumentum Solum.*

— Drusus.
