# LIBER BIOLOGIS: FORGE OF FLESH (IRON MITOSIS)

*(Personal record: 046.M42 | Post-op South Wall / Waaagh! Skarjaw invasion)*

**Classification:** *Vermilion* / Triumvirate eyes.  
**Reporter:** Chief Apothecary Drusus, Lord of the *Castrum Medicae*.  
**Object of study:** Lord Castellan Alaric von Helis and “Iron Mitosis.”

The Adeptus Mechanicus worships the gear with ignorant devotion, and Terran medical doctrine states a simple rule: flesh torn away in battle must be replaced with metal. A lost leg becomes a piston; a severed arm, an augmetic claw. The Belisarian furnace was designed to patch soldiers and return them to the trench.

Yet as I walked the rows of amniotic tanks in my *Apothecarion* after the South Wall broke, I saw how limited Imperial dogma is before our gene-seed.

*Jagd* is not merely a stimulant flood driving slaughter. That is the surface of the equation. What I saw in those hibernation cubes brushed biological heresy. My amputated brothers were not merely healing; they were entering aggressive hypermetabolism. *Jagd* triggered “Predatory Reconstruction.” I saw humeral stumps not form keloids but weave new bone matrices, nerves, and muscle from zero. They cannibalized their own lipid reserves, forging the missing part from the inside out as if flesh refused to accept defeat. Even chronic secondary organ failures were being “reset” and cured. The storm incinerates the body, yes — but the ash left behind chooses to shape a harder alloy than the original steel.

That alone would earn expurgation from less enlightened Inquisitors. Yet what happened in the central tank — Alpha cocoon — challenged everything I know about transhuman anatomy.

They brought me what remained of Alaric von Helis hours after he tore the *Warboss* Skarjaw’s jaw free.

The diagnosis was a death certificate. Massive cranial fractures with severe frontal-lobe hemorrhage. Double ventricular rupture; primary and secondary hearts had burst under *Jagd* pressure. Three lungs collapsed and drowned in xenos toxins. Finally, the left arm did not exist from the humerus down, crushed by the Ork’s hydraulic claws.

He was clinically dead. By *Codex* protocols I ordered Tech-Priests to warm a *Dreadnought* chassis reactor to entomb what remained of his mind.

But the Lord Castellan’s biology rejected my decree. His stabilization at Alpha frequency — that damned balance between *Immanitas* cold and *Versibar* fury — said no to systemic failure.

I submerged him in maximum-density bacta.

**First week:** my laboratory’s thermal alarms screamed. Fluid in Alaric’s tank boiled. Temperature climbed to near-radiant levels. No peaceful cure occurred in there. Through thick viewports I watched the stump of his left arm explode in violent, grotesque cell growth. Flesh was not growing; it was being carved by force by his own genetic code’s fury, stretching bone in boiling water.

**Second week:** I focused *auspex* on his internal organs. Myocardium regenerated spontaneously, but the primary heart that formed was different. Denser. Super-adapted to pump the thick blood his Alpha frequency demanded. But it was his brain that sent analytical ice down my spine. The frontal lobe rewired itself. It was not rebuilding neural pathways to restore old memories. It was deleting them. Alaric’s brain sacrificed memory and personality traits to forge new connections aimed solely at tactical processing and instinctive survival.

**Fourth week:** exactly thirty days after he had been pronounced dead, the cycle completed.

Fluids drained. The tank glass unlocked with a decompression hiss. Alaric tore from coma and stood naked, soaked in synthetic blood, on the *Apothecarion* metal grate.

I examined him in silence. Motricity in his left arm was one hundred percent. The newly forged limb wore virgin skin, pale and without a scratch, macabre contrast to his massive torso — a topographic map of centuries of war scars.

Our commander’s recovery was no miracle of the Emperor; it was genetic engineering aberration. Where a common Astartes scars only enough not to bleed out, the Alpha rebuilds from absolute zero to dominate the environment that wounded him. The “Beast” caged in his cells simply did not allow the chassis to stop. His biology does not obey human anatomy; it obeys military function’s tyranny.

Yet meeting his gaze before I handed him his cuirass, I logged the terminal side effect of that transaction. Each time the body performs reconstruction at that magnitude, humanity retracts a little further. The left-arm tissue was newborn, but the gaze beneath heavy brows was archaic, inhuman — something that lurks in the void’s depths.

Alaric von Helis pays for new flesh with fragments of his own soul. The math is simple, and spare parts are finite. The day will come when nothing human remains to pay the beast’s price.

Until then, he commands.

*Instrumentum Solum.* — Drusus.
