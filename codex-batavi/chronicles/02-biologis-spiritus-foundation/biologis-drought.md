# LIBER BIOLOGIS: DEATH OF THE “I” (THE DROUGHT)

*(Personal record: 048.M42 | Campaign: Purge of Noviomagus Secundus)*

**Classification:** *Vermilion* / Triumvirate eyes.  
**Reporter:** Chief Apothecary Drusus, Lord of the *Castrum Medicae*.  
**Object of study:** 4th Cohort and limbic collapse.

Ignorance is a variable that offends me. Until the incident on Noviomagus Secundus, my predictive models for the **chimeric** gene-seed rested on a simple dichotomy: either our Astartes operated under basal discipline, or pressure drove them to thermal failure of *Jagd*. I had mapped fury. I believed I understood the Beast.

Then the Tyranids fell on the mining zones, and the mathematics failed.

I was isolated in the *Strategium Medicae*, ten kilometers above the front line, linked to the 4th Cohort’s biometric *auspex* net. When the **Shadow in the Warp** radiated by the *Hive Mind* wrapped the permacrete catacombs, I primed the chemical drippers on our brothers’ armor. Xenos psychic pressure was absurd — white noise of trillions of predatory minds built to crush individuality. Theory said a *Batavi*’s limbic brain would release the Beast to protect sanity. I waited for adrenaline spikes, tachycardia of rage, frenzy.

But the monitors did not climb. They plummeted.

I watched, fascinated and disturbed, as the brain chemistry of eight hundred transhuman giants was surgically altered in a millisecond. Dopamine, serotonin, and endorphins zeroed. The Betcher gland and Mucranoid stalled aggression production. In exchange I detected a lethal flood of endogenous synthetic nootropics. The brain switched off the “I” to save the “System.”

The Wolf did not howl in the catacombs. The Wolf was chemically euthanized. *Ruhe* was born there.

For the next three solar cycles I watched the sterilest combat telemetry ever logged in this Chapter’s history. *Vox* traffic fell to zero. Shots were not defensive suppression fire; they were geographic executions. 4th Cohort hit probability locked at 99.8%. Visual telemetry from helm lenses showed Tyranid swarms hesitating, confused by the absolute absence of any emotional bio-signature to devour.

At engagement peak, my visors showed Brother Kael impaled on a *Carnifex*’s claws. By *Jagd* protocols or even the *Codex Astartes*, the squad should have advanced to recover the gene-seed. But I saw on the neural graphs that nearby brothers processed Kael’s death in 0.4 seconds. They calculated rescue would shrink the field of fire by 14%. They let him die. Recalibrated geometric aim and kept shooting. There was no mourning. Kael was no longer a brother; he was a friction variable subtracted from the left side of the equation.

It was a magnificent display of industrial perfection. Until the war ended.

When the last bio-ship was reduced to orbital ash and xenos psychic pressure ceased, I expected the 4th Cohort’s neural signatures to return to *baseline*. Chemistry did not yield. *Ruhe* had no natural off-switch.

I went down into the mining catacombs myself. What I found defied medicine. Eight hundred biological weapons locked in a task-execution *loop*.

I walked through black blood drying on the walls and found Sergeant Vhor. He sat in the dark, stripping, polishing, and reassembling his boltgun’s chamber. He did not see me. His pupils were fixed, dilated by focus-stimulant saturation. I checked his personal *auspex*: Vhor had been repeating that same motor sequence for twelve hours, thirty-four minutes, and ten seconds.

Farther on, I saw brothers whose gauntlet joints smoked, hauling torn steel beams to plug holes in the cave wall. They worked until finger bones cracked under excess weight. I ordered halt verbally. No response. I injected aggressive chemical inhibitors straight into their bloodstreams through armor maintenance ports. *Ruhe* drank the medication like water. Biology had become a closed circuit of infinite logic.

I was forced to contact Alaric and Varro and admit the unthinkable: I had no cure for perfection.

The Triumvirate’s solution was an insult to medical science — and still the only one that worked. “Sensory rewarming.” They brought the weak into the slaughterhouse.

They ordered mortal civilians — dockers, mess-hall serfs, humans fragile and rank with sweat and fear — brought into the hot zone. They were told to light cook-fires, cook simple organic rations, and, most illogical of all, physically touch the filthy ceramite of catatonic Astartes.

I monitored brain waves. When an old civilian workman, trembling, laid his bare hand on Sergeant Vhor’s pauldron and called him by name, chemistry failed. The smell of burned organic food, irregular heat from a civilian fire, and tactile contact from the weakest link in the Imperial chain forced the limbic brain into emergency reboot. The logical *loop* shattered against irrational affection. The “I” returned, painful and exhausted.

**Archive note:** *Jagd* burns our assets in bestial fire, but the heat proves they are alive. *Ruhe* turns my patients into perfect blades — at the terminal cost of psychological entropy. I discovered, against my will, that Batavi transhuman anatomy cannot sustain itself on chemistry and logic alone. It needs the parasitic gravitational bond with the mortals they swear to protect.

Science did not fail; it only proved the machine needs flaws to stay human.

*Instrumentum Solum.* — Drusus
