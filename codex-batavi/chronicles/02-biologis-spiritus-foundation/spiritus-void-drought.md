# LIBER SPIRITUS: GOSPEL OF THE VOID (THE DROUGHT)

*(Personal record: 048.M42 | Leviathan incursion, Noviomagus Secundus)*

***Classification:** Vermilion / Judex eyes. **Reporter:** Master Chaplain Varro (Guardian of Reason). **Troop spiritual state:** Null / total ego suppression.*

---

## I. PERSONAL VIGIL: SHADOW’S SILENCE

*The **Shadow in the Warp** reached Noviomagus long before bio-ships darkened the sun. It is abyssal cold that does not touch skin but freezes the vital link between soul and the Emperor’s light. Normally an Astartes response to that psychic terror is Hate — the sacred fire we Chaplains keep lit with litanies and war-hymns to burn xenos fear.*

*I prepared to stoke Fury **on the line**. I prepared anchoring rites to keep alien terror from breaking our line. I expected the **fire-and-fury howl** — the **wolf-cult** Chaplain’s model, sacred noise and sacred rage. I expected the titanic will-struggle to cage the Beast.*

*But when the first spore wave veiled the sky, there was no scream. No Wolf’s snarl. Only the metallic dry sound of eight hundred bolter locks snapping open in unnervingly perfect unison.*

*I looked at my 4th Cohort brothers. Their helm lenses showed no fear, no rage, no devotion, no honor. They had become mirrors of the void assailing us. Where Apothecary Drusus saw miraculous tactical adaptation (genesis of his “Perfect Soldier”), I saw immediate death of spirit. Our Axiom of Reason entered aberrant overclock; sterile logic devoured faith.*

***[Confessional self-analysis]** I tried to pray on the battlefield. Words left my mouth but I felt no weight on my soul. In a moment of lucid horror I realized I myself was calculating my *Crozius Arcanum*’s kinetic dispersion angle instead of focusing on the strike’s purity and faith. *Ruhe* did not infect only soldiers; it was desiccating my own soul.*

## II. USELESS LITURGY (FIELD OBSERVATION)

*For seventy-two hours of uninterrupted combat, my function as Chaplain became absolutely obsolete.*

*I tried to intone the Catechism of Hate over *vox* to brace their hearts. My words struck their armor ceramite and fell in the mud, ignored like irrelevant data variables. They needed no moral support. They needed no courage. A machine needs no courage to grind flesh; it needs only constant energy and mathematical vectors.*

* ***Soldier without ego:** I watched Brother-Sergeant Karras sacrifice his entire tactical squad to lure a Hive Tyrant into a crossfire kill-box. Not one second of hesitation. No farewell, no martial honor-martyrdom for Terra. Mere accounting transaction. Three lives traded for one tactical advantage.*  
* ***Absence of faith:** They did not fight for the Emperor of Mankind. They fought because biological survival algorithm dictated xenos threat was structurally incompatible with planet Noviomagus. Had I ordered them to cease fire in the Golden Throne’s name, I suspect they would have walked around me — or shot me — classifying me as an inefficient variable.*

*Tyrannic *Hive Mind* tried to crush us with brutal synaptic pressure. But it found no fertile minds to break. It hit a biological firewall it could not cross. The line had dried its own souls so not a drop remained where enemy fear could swim.*

*I felt a ghost shouting orders in a cemetery of iron automatons. I fought, killed, coordinated advance — yet felt more kinship with Administratum lobotomized *servitors* than my own brothers. The Wolf was dead. Only Iron remained.*

## III. BROTHER’S DEATH AND CURE BY THE ROOT

***Post-combat status:** Logistical catatonia.*

*Victory was the mathematically cleanest in our history. And the most profane. When skies cleared, lugubrious silence remained. I walked the ranks. My brothers did not remove helms to breathe unfiltered air. They did not clean acidic ichor from weapons to honor machine-spirit — only because grime reduced mechanism efficiency.*

*I found a neophyte standing in mining tunnels, staring at a white wall for four hours. I touched his shoulder with my *Crozius*. “Battle is over, brother,” I said. He turned his helm. “Maintenance is continuous,” the toneless vox-processed voice replied. “Basal rest is cycle waste.” It was the sound of an Imperial cogitator speaking through human flesh.*

*Drusus worked frantically to wake their bodies with injections and stimulants. I, in turn, had to wake guilt and pain. I had to force catatonic giants to hold reliquaries and pieces of fallen brothers’ armor. It did not work. They processed objects as field trash.*

*I had to bring them to the surface, near working civilian forges. Radiated heat, imperfect smell of human food, frightened children crying in hives… fragility finally broke the steel trance.*

*I witnessed the exact moment Sergeant Karras’s soul returned to its shell.*

*He stood inert before a civilian workwoman who offered, hands trembling, a metal cup of water. Karras extended the massive gauntlet. He took the cup — and his hand hesitated. Trembled. *Ruhe*’s cold perfect logic collapsed utterly before irrational, unnecessary human gratitude. Karras broke his helm seal, removed the helm, and wept in silence. Not purely from grief — because emotion rebooted abruptly and the pain of sending his own men to death finally struck with hammer weight.*

## Reclusiam theological verdict:

**Jagd* debases us to beasts — and beasts, with iron leashes, can be tamed by discipline. *Ruhe* elevates us to logical machines — and machines feel loyalty to nothing, only follow mathematical programming.*

*If the Chapter remains in that state long enough, we will need Chaos gods to corrupt and destroy us; we will become indifferent to the Imperium’s survival we swore to defend.*

*I pray to the Throne we never need inhabit that silence-desert again. For if we forget how to feel pain and compassion entirely, we will have become exactly what our enemies already are: efficient, nameless monsters. Fragile, weeping humanity is the only anchor that keeps our Iron from rusting in the void.*

*Instrumentum Solum. — Signed, Judex Varro, Guardian who fears Silence.*
