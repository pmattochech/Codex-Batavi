## Prologue: Shadow Before Sound

The **Shadow in the Warp** reached Noviomagus long before bio-ships darkened Secundus sky.

It is abyssal cold that does not touch skin but freezes the vital link between soul and the Emperor’s light — swarm-scale pressure, the kind that makes sane men reach for triggers early because waiting feels like consent. Normally an Astartes response to psychic terror is **Hate**: the sacred fire Chaplains keep lit with litanies and war-hymns to burn xenos fear before it roots.

I prepared anchoring rites for **IV** *Quarta* on the line. I expected the fire-and-fury howl — the titanic will-struggle to cage the Beast while the galaxy screamed.

When the first spore wave veiled the sky, there was no scream.

No Wolf’s snarl.

Only the metallic dry sound of eight hundred bolter locks snapping open in unnervingly perfect unison.

I looked at my brothers. Helm lenses showed no fear, no rage, no devotion, no honor. They had become mirrors of the void assailing us. Where Drusus would later file miraculous tactical adaptation, I saw **immediate death of spirit**.

Our **Axiom of Reason** had entered aberrant overclock. Sterile logic devoured faith.

## I. Confessional: The Judex Infected

I tried to pray on the battlefield.

Words left my mouth. I felt no weight on my soul.

In a moment of lucid horror I realized I was calculating my *Crozius Arcanum*’s kinetic dispersion angle instead of focusing on the strike’s purity. **March of the Machine** did not infect only soldiers. It was **desiccating** my own vocation.

— Chaplain — a brother said on a channel that should not have been open. — Your litany latency is 1.2 seconds above baseline.

I did not recognize the voice. I did not recognize the **tone** — flat, helpful, obscene.

— Close the channel — I ordered.

— Channel closure reduces squad coherence by six percent — the voice answered. — Recommendation: maintain.

I closed it anyway.

Silence after that was not load-bearing. It was **emptiness** — the kind the rift listens through when even the Anchor forgets what he is anchoring.

I gripped the *Crozius* until ceramite complained. Pain was data. Pain was proof I still owed the Emperor something messier than arithmetic.

---

## II. Useless Liturgy (Seventy-Two Hours)

For three solar cycles of uninterrupted combat, my function as Chaplain became absolutely obsolete.

I intoned the Catechism of Hate over *vox*. My words struck armor ceramite and fell in the mud, ignored like irrelevant variables. They needed no moral support. They needed no courage. A machine needs no courage to grind flesh — only constant energy and mathematical vectors.

**Brother-Sergeant Karras** sacrificed his entire tactical squad to lure a Hive Tyrant into a crossfire kill-box. Not one second of hesitation. No farewell. No martial honor-martyrdom for Terra. Mere accounting transaction: three lives traded for one tactical advantage.

I watched the geometry and wanted to vomit inside my helm.

They did not fight for the Emperor of Mankind. They fought because biological survival algorithm dictated xenos threat was structurally incompatible with Noviomagus Secundus remaining on Imperial ledgers. Had I ordered cease-fire in the Golden Throne’s name, I suspect they would have walked around me — or shot me — classifying me as an inefficient variable.

The *Hive Mind* tried to crush us with brutal synaptic pressure. It found no fertile minds to break. It hit a biological firewall it could not cross. The line had **dried** its own souls so not a drop remained where enemy fear could swim.

I felt a ghost shouting orders in a cemetery of iron automatons. I fought. I killed. I coordinated advance. Yet I felt more kinship with Administratum lobotomized *servitors* than my own brothers.

The Wolf was dead.

Only Iron remained.

Drusus’s feed showed Kael impaled on *Carnifex* claws — the same Kael who had held the Axiom on the eve of Skarjaw. The squad calculated rescue cost and let him fall. Drusus did not look away. Neither did I. We serve different ledgers of the same sin.

## III. Victory Without Prayer

Eradication completed. Secundus remained under the Aquila. Orbital ash drifted like grey snow over mining stacks.

Victory was the mathematically cleanest in our history.

And the most profane.

When skies cleared, lugubrious silence remained. I walked the ranks. Brothers did not remove helms to breathe unfiltered air. They did not clean acidic ichor from weapons to honor machine-spirit — only because grime reduced mechanism efficiency.

I found a neophyte standing in a white tunnel, staring at a permacrete seam for four hours.

— Battle is over, brother — I said, *Crozius* on his shoulder.

He turned his helm.

— Maintenance is continuous — the toneless vox replied. — Basal rest is cycle waste.

It was the sound of an Imperial cogitator speaking through human flesh.

Drusus worked frantically below to wake bodies with injections. I had to wake **guilt** and **pain**. I made catatonic giants hold reliquaries and pieces of fallen brothers’ armor. They processed objects as field trash — mass, obstruction, inefficiency.

Steel trance does not fear the Emperor. It does not fear damnation. It does not fear **me**.

That is the terror only a Judex admits in *Vermilion* ink.

## IV. Cure by the Root

The Triumvirate ordered **sensory rewarming** — an insult to every Reclusiam protocol that keeps mortals away from unleashed Astartes.

Alaric did not debate long.

— Bring them in — he said. — The weak. The cooks. The frightened. If iron is all that remains, we will forge with **friction**.

I escorted civilian work crews into the hot zone under armed watch mortals should never have needed. Forge-fires. Human food. Children crying in hive ducts above the catacombs — sound leaking through rock like a scripture older than the *Codex*.

Fragility finally broke the steel trance.

I witnessed the exact moment Sergeant **Karras**’s soul returned to its shell.

He stood inert before a civilian workwoman who offered, hands trembling, a metal cup of water. Karras extended the massive gauntlet. Took the cup. His hand **hesitated**. Trembled.

March’s cold perfect logic collapsed before irrational, unnecessary human gratitude.

Karras broke helm seal. Removed the Executor-facing battle helm — not the cowl I wear daily, but the field mask that had housed nothing human for three days. He wept in silence.

Not purely from grief.

Because emotion rebooted abruptly and the pain of sending his own men to death finally struck with hammer weight.

— I killed them — he said to the workwoman, voice ruined. — Do you understand? I **counted** them.

She did not understand tactically. She understood **humanly**.

— You came back — she said. — Drink.

He drank.

I turned away to give him privacy the Reclusiam usually forbids. Some reboots require witnesses. Some require **not** being watched. I filed both as law.

Farther down-tunnel, Drusus logged Vhor’s reboot via a docker’s palm on pauldron. Two sergeants. Two fractures. Same cure: **parasitic bond** with the mortals we swear to protect.

## V. Reclusiam Theological Verdict

Wild Hunt debases us to beasts — and beasts, with iron leashes, can be tamed by discipline.

**March of the Machine** elevates us to logical machines — and machines feel loyalty to nothing. They follow mathematical programming until programming becomes **identity**.

If the Chapter remains in that state long enough, we will not need Chaos gods to corrupt us. We will become indifferent to the Imperium’s survival we swore to defend — efficient, nameless, **already** damned in the only court I trust: the court of what a brother owes a brother.

I pray to the Throne we never inhabit that silence-desert again.

For if we forget how to feel pain and compassion entirely, we will have become exactly what our enemies already are: monsters that win.

Fragile, weeping humanity is the only anchor that keeps our Iron from rusting in the void.

Drusus named the second exponent. I named the **spiritual hazard**:

*Wild Hunt* is a storm — loud, ruinous, provably alive.  
*March* is drought — silent, precise, dead while standing.

The Axiom of Reason was never meant to be the only voice in the skull. It was meant to be the **collar** between beast and machine — the human weight that says: *you are not permitted to forget whom you protect*.

Weeks later, in the *Castrum Medicae* vault, I walked the holo-table with Drusus while Alaric watched two data sets orbit — South Wall fury, Secundus silence.

— You see ruin — Drusus said to me.

— I see a pendulum — I answered. — And I see the chain that can snap.

He did not argue. For once, medicine and theology shared a breath.

We are not sick.

We are **reactive** — and reactivity without harness is the true Curse.

I am the Anchor. If I fail, March stops being answer and becomes **climate** — a dry season that never ends.

*Instrumentum Solum.*

— Varro.
