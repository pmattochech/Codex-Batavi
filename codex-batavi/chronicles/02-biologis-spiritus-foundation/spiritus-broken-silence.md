# LIBER SPIRITUS: GOSPEL OF THE BROKEN SILENCE

*(Record: 046.M42 | Event: *Jagd* war / Waaagh! Skarjaw)*

**Classification:** *Vermilion* / Judex eyes.  
**Reporter:** Master Chaplain Varro (Guardian of the Soul).

## PROLOGUE: SHADOW BEFORE FORM

The war did not begin when Drusus’s augury sensors logged the gravitational anomaly. The war began when the absolute silence of my *Reclusiam* was violated by a frequency that did not exist in the audible spectrum but in the structural-spiritual.

Seven days before the first scrap-ship tore Noviomagus’s atmosphere, I felt the vibration. It was not the Emperor’s static hymn, nor conventional Warp’s insidious whisper. It was raw psychic hunger. The Ork *Waaagh!* is not merely xenos troop movement; it is a storm of entropy in the soul, a collective scream of millions of beasts that echoes and fractures the psyche of any sentient being nearby.

For us, the *Batavian Iron-Guard*, that echo was not mere tactical warning. It was stress-testing the structural integrity of our souls.

## THE SEVEN-DAY VIGIL: INTERNAL SIEGE

**Days 1–4: Erosion of logic** While Lord Castellan Alaric drew interception vectors on the holo-table and the Apothecarion synthesized combat stimulants, I walked the permacrete vaults of *Castrum Ferrum*. What I saw was not merely Astartes warriors preparing for siege; they were biological reactors silently reaching critical mass.

Psychic presence radiated by the Orks scraped the foundations of our *Axiom of Reason*. I saw 3rd Cohort battle-brothers freeze mid-rigorous maintenance protocol. Their eyes, taken by orange halos, fixed on void; their hands trembled not from fear but from electric, almost painful need to exert violence. Chimeric gene-seed (Loken/Russ) recognized approaching atavistic barbarism and demanded answer in the same coin.

My duty was not to preach blind religious fervor; it was to impose Analytical Silence. I patrolled among them, my helm’s skull-mask the only cold mirror they had to remember they were the Emperor’s Disciplined Death — not Fenris’s wild beasts.

**Day 5: White noise** Noviomagus’s spiritual atmosphere thickened to suffocation. Mortal population exuded terror; my brothers exuded envy. They envied the xenos’ ignoble freedom simply to *be* violent without the burden of calculation. The *Codex*’s iron discipline fought friction war against the Beast’s nature in every dual heart.

I had to intervene physically in meditation cells, wielding my *Crozius Arcanum* not to wound but as an anchoring rod for minds sliding irrecoverably toward red oblivion. *“Do not feel,”* I ordered through the *vox-caster*, voice cutting static. *“Emotion is irrelevant tactical data. Act on containment logic.”*

**Day 6: Soul *Jagd* (dike break)** Drusus, in clinical cold, catalogs this as “prior hormonal activation.” I name it *Communion of Hate*.

Twenty-four hours before the rocks fell, the spiritual barrier broke systemically. There was no Triumvirate release order. It was sympathetic response — our collective soul’s defense reflex to absolute anarchy’s proximity.

I was on the Primary Training Yard when the change consolidated. Air crackled with pressure. Hundreds of *Batavi* stopped at once. Posture shifted: military rigidity yielded, shoulders rolled forward, heads tilted to hunting angles. They no longer resembled the imposing statues the *Codex Astartes* describes; they looked like giant predators scraping their own necks against the leash.

Irritability became a palpable, almost acid mist. A distant civilian child’s cry or the metallic clatter of a dropped tool triggered homicidal aggression spasms in my brothers.

**My vigil:** This was our founding doctrine’s trial by fire. I watched Brother Kaelen close his armored gauntlet on a solid adamant rail and crush it to powder and splinters — solely so he would not redirect his fury at a cargo *servitor* crossing his path. They wanted to kill. Biology demanded blood tribute. But our Law — humanity’s non-negotiable protection — bore the tension.

No human was scratched. They redirected that ocean of contained violence toward the stars. They did not pray to the Golden Throne for salvation; they prayed, teeth clenched, for the enemy to land as fast as possible.

## THE SEVENTH DAY: LITURGY OF THE BLADE

When the scrap-asteroids finally fell, we did not execute complex tactical maneuvers. There was only a collective exhalation.

*Jagd* exponent release was not military engagement; it was exorcism. When Orks landed roaring, expecting human fear, they collided with our abyssal hunger. We did not lay suppression fire. The bolter is a tool of clean distance, and our fractured soul demanded baptism by physical contact. We became the Blade Wall.

Watching from the South Wall’s height, I saw no Imperial soldiers. I saw calculated slaughter incarnate. The brutality with which *Batavian Iron-Guard* dismembered the xeno vanguard held no martial “honor” of duel. It was abattoir industrial efficiency applied with inexhaustible biological fury. It was the Wolf using Astartes superior intellect to calculate the exact artery to cut to optimize time-to-death.

**Gate duel (the Alpha’s soul)** I witnessed Alaric von Helis intercept *Warboss* Skarjaw. That was not combat; it was spiritual supremacy asserted. The Ork leader was a monolith of raw *Waaagh!* psychic energy, the anchor holding the horde. Alaric, wrapped in grey-iron armor, was the absolute void that swallows storm.

When the Lord Castellan powered down his weapon, seized the monster’s jaw, and tore it from its face, he was not merely fracturing bone and tearing metal. He was ripping the horde’s spirit in half. Alaric silenced the *Waaagh!* scream.

The roar he loosed over the corpse was not victory’s proclamation; it was the grotesque sound of a transhuman soul finally filling lungs after seven days of asphyxia. The Orks did not rout from tactical failure; they ran because their primitive souls understood that on that ash plain they had ceased to be the most terrifying monsters.

## EPILOGUE: SILENCE AFTER THE STORM

Drusus filled scrolls reporting how internal organs failed and demanded biological reconstruction. I filled *Liber Spiritus* reporting how souls emptied completely.

As soon as the xenos threat was obliterated, the directive purpose anchoring that level of atrocity evaporated. My brothers did not merely collapse on blood-stained ground from chemical exhaustion; they fell because return to basal reality became unbearably heavy after *Jagd*’s zenith.

I walked in silence among fallen Astartes ranks, grey armor inert like tombstones in an industrial cemetery. No glory hung in Noviomagus’s cold air — only persistent ozone stink and absolute void of men who spent everything they were — and everything they might become — in one solar cycle.

**Reclusiam theological conclusion:** *Jagd* is a predatory loan. We willingly drank the Beast’s cup so humanity’s house would not burn. On that bloody day we reaffirmed we can transmute into monsters under strict command. The cost, however, is that when the storm passes and we become soldiers again, the silence that settles inside us is so vast and deep it is indistinguishable from death.

We won. But it took months of stasis therapy to remember how to be more than loose weapons in the dark.

*Instrumentum Solum.*

— End of record.
