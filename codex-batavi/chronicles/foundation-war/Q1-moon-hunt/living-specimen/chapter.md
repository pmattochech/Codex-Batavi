## I. Interrogating Biology

By the fifth year of the Castra Vetera crucible, void traffic had begun to learn a new dishonesty: routes that looked straight on charts acquired polite curves, and convoy masters spoke of *mirror hygiene* the way sane men speak of washing hands — ritual without theology, because theology would have admitted the mirrors were not washing anything, only returning a face you were not sure belonged to you.

Forty-six names from **Promise of Tithing** still sat in **Valerius**’s ledger like teeth the Chapter could not stop tonguing. **Pryce**’s last three words — *not us; repeat* — had become a superstition officers repeated when auspex returned green too eagerly.

Aethelgard, meanwhile, remained stubbornly literal. Its mud did not lie. It kept what it caught until the tide decided to arbitrate, and even then it often gave back only enough of a body to teach the living that retrieval is not the same as return. Drusus had stopped expecting the planet to cooperate with his filing system; he had not stopped expecting his filing system to save brothers who were still breathing.

What he needed now was not another tableau of absent Marines — boots aligned like grades, gauntlets folded as if sleep could be administrative — but a pulse that persisted long enough to answer questions strategy had been screaming into the wrong language ever since Torven’s squad went quiet on the needle-bridge and left twenty-one empty spaces arranged with an enemy’s idea of neatness.

A live hunter was a sensor you could not buy from Mars. It was also an admission that the Chapter had moved from hunting an opponent to interrogating a biology, and that the Apothecary’s art, at its root, was the same as the stalker’s: decide what still counts as a person when the teeth come out.

## II. Glass Rills — Keep the Nervous System Talking

They had taken this one along the **Glass Rills**, where quartz grit and tidal shallows turned movement into light and light into accusation — the same wound-geometry **Iria** had walked as bait two years prior, though Drusus did not say that aloud on the pier because saying it would have filed shame as **procedure**. Kadmos’s ambush had worked well enough to wound without finishing — an ugly precision Drusus had requested in terms that made even veteran sergeants flinch, because *keep the nervous system talking* sounds like mercy only until you watch a xenos limb hang by filament and realize mercy was never the verb in the sentence.

The creature still wore mimic-mesh in places, the fabric half sloughed like shed skin, leaving patches of slick hide and the obscene suggestion of a Guard silhouette at the wrong distance. Its mandible-mask — part armor, part cartoon of a human jaw — caught rain and held it in beads until the beads became a second mouth, jeweled and stupid and cruel. When it breathed, the sound was too controlled for an animal in shock; when it watched the line of Astartes that dragged it toward the sea, its eyes behind biomatrix glass tracked motion the way a schola instructor tracks a student who has not yet earned the right to fail.

Drusus hated that comparison the moment it formed. Hate, for him, was not heat; it was a margin note that read *verify*, because if the enemy could teach, then every Batavi hesitation became curriculum, and Torven’s empty bridge had been a classroom the Chapter had attended too late.

The Chapter filed the quarry under a label the *Lex Ignis* catalogs would later try to rebrand as their own discovery — **Type A** — as if a letter could cage what the Needles named only in whispers tied to tide and hunger. Naming was never neutral on Aethelgard. Iria had said as much on the burned bridge, and Drusus had written her words into an annex without telling her, because annexes were where the Apothecarion kept truths that were not yet safe for Strategium tables.

The capture kinship that bound this specimen to earlier horrors was not myth; it was doctrine written in absence. Boots in pairs at Sallow Morrow. Gear arranged like offerings. **Rhylan**’s bolt shell standing upright in mud too regular for wind. The sealed tissue from the first clean hunter-kill still hummed in null-storage with a harmonic rhyme Drusus had logged as *unverified cross-theater coupling* after the Silica Field — quartz scream and void reflection attending the same school. The live hunter was a thread pulled from the same weave, and Drusus intended to follow it until the weave tore or confessed.

## III. Pier Nine — If It Sleeps, We Learn Sleep

**Pier Nine** had been a fish-gut staging ground before the war made it a sacrament of inconvenience. Now it smelled of ozone from null-fields, copper from old blood, and the sweet rot that clung to nets no one had time to mend. The cage rose where cranes once swung catches — an insult to hospitality, as the Needles muttered, because hospitality on Aethelgard meant sharing salt, not displaying a neighbor’s predator like a lesson.

Null-fields hummed at a frequency that worked into teeth and made molars ache with the sensation of being filed. Sedation mist drifted in bands calibrated to insult xenos chemistry without granting the clean exit of unconsciousness; Drusus had argued for that cruelty in a tone so flat the Strategium mistook it for dispassion until Alaric translated it for them afterward: *If it sleeps, we learn sleep.*

Bone rails lined the approach, etched with prayers Varro insisted would not help and refused to omit, because omission would have conceded that help was not being solicited from heaven but demanded from discipline — and discipline, on that pier, meant witness. Every witness was complicit. Complicity was the Chapter’s crude substitute for consent, and consent, Drusus knew, was a luxury the Imperium rarely paid for in full.

Rain came in thin needles off the shingle chop. Oil-slick runoff made the planks a ledger no scribe would thank you for: every boot-print a line item, every smear a revision. Alaric took position where the deck met the landward stairs — not parade rest, not a pose for artists who liked to paint *the Castellan* as if he were a statue with a nameplate. His presence was mass without swagger, the romance of command expressed as something too heavy to perform. He did not need to move to occupy space; space learned him the way a room learns a loaded weapon set on a table.

Kadmos held the inner geometry of Marines along the railings, a second spine for the pier, but the Castellan’s stillness was the hinge mortals orbited without knowing they were orbiting.

**Sergeant Kessler** had asked for the outer cordon with a request that sounded like an order and was granted like a favor, because Alaric had learned on the shingle — seventy-two names delivered by a dishonest sea — that some mortals needed to spend their anger where it could see the horizon. She stood in flak scoured pale by mud in patterns that resembled accidental heraldry, hands locked behind her back in the posture of a woman trying not to strangle anyone.

**Iria** moved inside the cordon with water-skins and the quiet authority of someone who had traded shell-cloak for waxed leathers that would not sing when wet; Administratum stamps on her sleeves looked like jokes told by a clerk who had never knelt in vein-mud.

— Anger is ballast — Kessler told her without turning.

Iria answered only with a nod, because some translations did not need Gothic.

Drusus had banned nonessential personnel from the pier. Bans on Aethelgard were suggestions the tide enjoyed ignoring. A boy — twelve, perhaps thirteen, carrying water for the workers who tightened bolts on the cage — slipped through because curiosity on that world outranked law the way hunger outranked etiquette. Drusus saw him in peripheral vision and calculated trajectories: if the child stayed behind the bone rail, sightlines remained tolerable; if he drifted, the pier’s geometry would give him too many angles on the cage, and angles became memories, and memories became politics.

— Behind the rail — Drusus said, *vox* stripped to the tone he used for brothers who were about to do something stupid.

The boy hesitated with the exquisite honesty of youth, caught between obedience and the need to *see*. Iria caught him once by the shoulder, murmured something in Needles salt-speech that made him flush, and lost him again when a surge of bodies — porters, a Munitorum tally-clerk, two Chapter serfs hauling cable — pretended to be order. Adults were worse than children. Children at least knew they were stealing a glance.

The water-bearer learned a lesson Pier Nine would tattoo into him: **witness** was not the same as *audience*. He stayed behind the rail after Iria’s grip released, fingers white on the skin straps, eyes on the cage mesh until rain beaded the biomatrix glass and made the hunter look like a constellation you could not trust. He would carry that constellation into the boy’s sentence later — *you keep cages too* — not as accusation alone, but as **geometry**: cages visible, cages admitted, cages still cages even when the keepers wore ceramite.

**Kessler** saw him hold the line and did not praise him. Praise turned discipline into theater. She only adjusted his strap once, rough as a sister, and returned to the outer cordon where anger still needed horizon.

## IV. Auditioning the Courtroom

When the cage sealed and the null-field settled into its steady ache, Drusus allowed himself three seconds with eyes closed — not prayer, calibration. He listened to the specimen’s respiration through the pickup woven into the mesh. Fourteen breaths per minute. Too slow for true panic. Heart rate banded in a narrow window that spoke of training or of biology that did not bother with adrenaline spikes humans could read. On his helm display he called up the truncated baseline from Torven’s site — thermal ghosts, particulate traces, useless for a live chart — and felt the old shame of incomplete data like grit under a seal.

**Genetor Threx** had arrived before dawn in the sense that Mechanicus officers arrived: first as a hum on auspex, then as light on a mezzanine rig bolted to a warehouse flank, aug-eyes recording everything with the patience of a creditor who enjoyed watching interest accrue. His Skitarii waited in the rain like red saints who had mistaken corrosion for virtue. Drusus noted their stance, their pack configurations, the way their thumbs rested near cut-down galvanic weapons — not threat yet, *preparedness*, the religion of the cog.

— Salvage window opens at seal — Threx sent on a channel Drusus had not blessed. — Compliance is efficiency.

Drusus did not answer. Answering would have been a mirror.

When the examination began in earnest, the pier narrowed to the width of Drusus’s attention. Rain needled the null-field until the mesh sang a thin complaint — not music, *pressure* — and every witness on the pier learned that **live capture** meant living with a sound you could not argue into silence.

He let his auspex kiss the air through null-field weave, logging pulses that stayed too patient for animal fear. Muscle recruitment patterns assumed four dimensions of threat where human tacticians budgeted three: not merely front, flank, rear, but *above* in the sense of hierarchy — who in the crowd read as predator, who as herd, who as irrelevant.

Through slits in the mesh he watched pupil response track motion behind glass. The enforcers were not enforcers yet, only shapes in rain; the boy was not yet a mistake, only a blur of motion near Iria’s elbow. The specimen’s gaze lingered where lingered attention would matter in a fight — Alaric’s center mass, Kadmos’s hands, the Skitarii’s advancing line — then returned to Drusus’s lenses with something that was not challenge and not pleading.

*Verdict* — Drusus thought — and hated the word because it sounded like Varro’s vocabulary bleeding into his charts.

Varro stood at his shoulder without speaking, censers cold, *Codex* death-skull helm held steady as bone enamel. He watched the specimen watch *them* with the focused absence of appetite that meant a mind still choosing targets. When Drusus subvocalized a reading — *oculomotor micro-saccade suppressed; predictive tracking engaged* — Varro’s reply was not a word but a fractional tilt of the skull, acknowledgment without collaboration. Chaplains guarded thresholds. Apothecaries measured them. On Pier Nine both jobs looked like staring.

**Kadmos** watched the outer cordon the way he would watch a kill-box — not for drama, for **bunching** — because mortals under stress learned to cluster where cluster became target geometry. He corrected spacing with hand signals older than *vox* protocol, two fingers *move*, three *hold*, one *do not look at the mesh like it is a mirror*. Vitreus had not happened yet; the habit was already forming.

**Threx**’s aug-eyes recorded from the mezzanine with creditor patience — *compliance is efficiency* still unspoken on the channel Drusus had not blessed — and Drusus felt the pier narrow further: not cage and specimen alone, but **three courts** auditioning one another — Apothecarion, Mechanicus, Chaplaincy — each filing restraint as data it could invoice later.

Alaric did not lean in. He did not need to. His stillness did the work walls do when rooms forget how to lie.

Drusus extended the reading beat one minute longer than protocol required — not cruelty, **hunger** for data the Chapter could not afford to buy twice. He watched the specimen’s eyes follow **Kessler** when she shifted weight, follow **Iria** when she passed between rain and lumen, follow the boy when the boy forgot himself and leaned past the bone rail by half a handspan. The hunter did not track prey. It tracked **rank** — who would intervene, who would mourn, who would file.

That was when Drusus understood, with the cold nausea of a man who has just recognized his own face in an enemy mirror: the creature was not afraid of the cage. It was **auditioning** the courtroom.

The specimen blinked once — slow, deliberate — as if testing whether null-field weave could be **negotiated** the way rank could be negotiated on a pier where every witness wore armor and called it duty. Drusus logged *blink latency; non-panic* and hated the elegance of the number because elegance was how institutions learned to sleep while live alphabets died.

The boy behind the bone rail did not know he was being graded. He only knew rain needled his scalp and the mesh sang and the hunter’s eyes found him anyway — not as prey, as **variable** — and Drusus felt his hand twitch toward the rail not to restrain the child but to restrain himself from filing the moment as **data** before the moment became a person. Apothecaries were dangerous on piers. They turned children into columns when columns were the only language the Imperium paid for in full.

**Iria** saw Drusus’s hand twitch and stepped half a pace — not between them, **beside** — because translation sometimes meant standing where officers could not pretend witness was throughput. She did not speak. Speaking would have turned the pier into **courtroom theater** before Type **B** arrived to sanitize shame with military economy.

**Valerius** watched the three-court audition from embarkation lip without stamping yet — Marine continuity beside Mechanicus yield beside Chaplain threshold — because parallel filing was sometimes the only mercy institutions could afford before the pier spent the cord in integers that opened ash.

**Kessler** saw the gaze land and stepped half a pace into the sightline — not shield, **witness** — because some lessons had to be seen by someone who would still shoot the ground first if laughter came bolt-deep without humor. The specimen’s pupils tracked the movement, reassessed, returned to Alaric. *Rank*, Drusus wrote in the margin. *Intervention hierarchy. Courtroom, not hunt.*

## V. Type B — Sanitizing Shame

Then the soldiers came, and the pier learned that Type **B** does not debate custody.

They hit the cordon like a second tide — no warning shout, no challenge the *Codex* would have recognized as kin. Faunal assets snapped low at knees, all sinew and trained trajectory; enforcers rose behind them with the bored efficiency of men who had been told to clean a mess before it became language. Their weapons chewed cover shaped for duels rather than clearance, because their war was not knightly; it was **courtroom violence** dressed as pack instinct. They did not roar. They removed obstacles the way housekeepers remove guests who have embarrassed the clan — firm, practiced, offended in a way that did not require facial expression.

**Valerius** — arrived on the pier with stamps before dawn, because stamps were how staff officers learned to hold truth when truth arrived as two columns — watched Type **B** erase Type **A**’s alphabet and wrote *Mechanicus yield dispute* beside *Marine restraint* before the smoke thinned, parallel filing begun while bodies still steamed.

Alaric met them at the hinge where pier met staging because geometry demanded weight there, not because he mistook their intent. He understood before the first bolter coughed that they were not reclaiming a brother in any sense the Chapter could file without lying; they were sanitizing shame, erasing a living indictment before it could be translated into Strategium grammar. He did not yield the first meter; mass at the hinge spent their momentum before it could unspool the cordon. Chain-teeth answered galvanic crack; the sound braided across wet wood until the noise became a texture you felt in your sternum.

The pier — built for fish and salt — learned it could host tribunals larger than any Needles elder had imagined.

The Skitarii tried to advance on the cage — Mechanicus prerogative, Threx’s voice scraping *vox* with treaty language like shrapnel.

Drusus did not shout. He stepped into the narrow space between red chrome and mesh until ocular arrays had to tilt to keep him in frame.

— Breach containment — he said. — And I will file you as hazard waste and tell the Castellan you **slipped**.

The halt that followed was not obedience; it was the twitch of limbs hunting a rule that outranked shame and failing to find it. Threx watched from above, recording restraint as data he could invoice later, the way creditors note politeness so interest arrives with better manners.

Mortals went down under Marine shoves brutal enough to save — ribs bruised, jaws rattled, pride sacrificed on the altar of *not dead*. Kessler found herself spitting orders she did not know she still believed could matter, voice borrowed from some cleaner war where sergeants thought ranks could argue with physics. Iria dragged the water-bearer back by his collar a heartbeat too late and took a grazed calf from a ricochet or a blade — later she would not swear which; salt and copper singed the air the way tide singed faith.

Drusus kept his eyes on the specimen once, because a healer’s worst habit is hope, and saw the hunter’s biomatrix glass track the enforcers with something older than fear — not pleading, not loyalty, *verdict* again — the look of a creature recognizing its own courthouse. For a fraction of a second Drusus understood what the Chapter had caught: not a beast, not a knight’s opponent, but a cell in a body whose politics they did not yet speak.

When the killing blow fell, it fell with military economy, clean almost to the point of mercy if mercy means refusing to prolong what honor had already sentenced. Alaric’s blow or another brother’s — on the pier afterward, witnesses would disagree, because memory likes to hand violence to the largest silhouette — but the result did not bargain. The body that had been their chance at a live alphabet became meat the tide would argue over later, and the silence that followed was not peace but the hollow after a door slams and the house pretends nothing changed.

**Threx**’s aug-eyes recorded restraint from mezzanine — absence as sample before absence existed — and Drusus felt creditor patience measuring whether compliance was efficiency or appetite dressed as treaty language.

The boy still watched from behind the bone rail — fingers white on skin straps — and Drusus felt the pier’s geometry shift: not victory, **receipt** written in two ledgers at once. Marines had stopped a courthouse from finishing its sentence; Type **B** had finished it anyway, and the child had seen both monsters perform efficiency without asking his consent to witness.

**Merit** tried once to clip a recorder to mezzanine hololith until **Valerius** stamped **NON-COMPLIANT** — witness routes only, no throughput dressed as capture packages when Type **B** was erasing live alphabet in real time.

**Kadmos** posted pier cordon one meter deeper — geometry as insurance — because courthouse violence dressed as pack instinct still required hinge weight before mesh learned what *repeat capture required* meant as hope dressed as procedure.

Rain tried to wash the pier clean and failed the way rain always failed on Aethelgard — not from lack of effort, from **wrong scale**. **Kessler** ordered tarp over what remained because tarp was how mortals told the sea *not yet*; the sea answered with patience older than chapters.

**Varro** moved along the bone rail once more, censers cold, and did not bless the dead hunter. Blessing would have implied the courthouse had been **holy**. It had only been **efficient**.

## VI. You Keep Cages Too

The cry that broke it belonged to the boy who had carried skins and thirst and the fatal habit of looking. He dropped his burdens and stared at Alaric through rain-smear and smoke, not as savior but as second monster in a world that had standardized monstrosity until rescue itself wore the silhouette of appetite.

Iria caught the sob when it came — translation not as language alone but as verdict, the way she always did when the Needles chose blunt water over High Gothic.

— He says you keep cages too.

The words landed on the pier like a second tide — smaller, colder, more honest than the first. Mortals who had cheered the cage an hour earlier found their throats empty. **Kessler** felt the sentence hook behind her sternum; she had held outer cordon so anger could see horizon, and horizon had returned the favor by showing her a child who still knew how to name hypocrisy without needing High Gothic.

Alaric did not answer. Some answers would have been comfort, and comfort would have been a lie that cost them tomorrow’s wall. He looked at the child once, helm lamps dimmed to a courtesy that was almost obscene, and then returned his attention to the dead hunter as if attention could be a form of respect.

Kessler swallowed whatever she would have said to the boy; she had learned on the shingle that some sentences only tightened the noose.

The boy did not look away from Alaric — not challenge, **audit** — as if he had learned on the shingle that monsters came in pairs and courtesy was sometimes the sharper cage. **Iria** did not soften the translation. Softening would have turned verdict into comfort, and comfort would have cost them tomorrow’s wall.

**Kaell** — not yet chosen by Furor, still brother with auspex marriage scheduled for morning that would never arrive on schedule — watched the boy’s fingers white on skin straps and felt something in his own throat tighten that was not fear and not pity, only **recognition** of a variable the mesh had not budgeted. He did not step forward. Stepping would have turned recognition into **intervention hierarchy** the specimen had already graded.

**Kadmos** posted one extra meter on landward cordon without order — geometry as apology the Chapter did not know how to speak — because children who named cages correctly were sometimes the only witnesses worth spacing for when Type **B** cadence marched inland like punctuation without mercy.

**Varro** passed the boy without censer smoke — *Codex* death-skull catching lamplight wrong — because smoke would have turned the child's sentence into **liturgy**, and liturgy was how institutions learned to sleep while witnesses still counted teeth.

Somewhere inland, Type **B** cadence kept marching — not in answer to the child, not in mercy, in **persistence** — and the sound reached the pier as rhythm without lyrics, soldier grammar the Chapter had not yet learned to read. **Drusus** heard it through auspex static and did not look toward the tree line. Looking would have turned inland marching into **prophecy**. He wrote *repeat capture required* instead, because procedure was sometimes the only wall you could build when a live alphabet died and a child still knew how to name cages.

The pier had been a courthouse tonight — null-field for bench, bone rails for bar, Astartes for jury that did not ask the boy to testify. Efficiency had passed sentence on the hunter and on the Chapter’s pride in one stroke. That was the cruelty **Iria** translated without softening: not that the kill was wrong, that the kill had been **visible** while the cages stayed.

Threx’s channels hissed static that wanted to be an objection and settled for being a receipt. The *Lex Ignis* would claim spoilage and lost yield in the same breath, because Mechanicus grief wore numbers when it could not wear shame.

**Valerius** would file the pier under two columns before dawn — *Marine restraint* and *Mechanicus yield dispute* — because truth on Aethelgard rarely arrived as a single line you could salute. It arrived as **parallel filing** you could only hold by stamping twice and refusing to merge.

Drusus closed his log with hands that did not shake yet, the motion of a man refusing his body the relief of drama. Specimen status went down as terminated by hostile third party; lessons as culture containing internal tribunal; packs repairing reputation faster than lives; Mechanicus appetite correlating with incident escalation — Strategium flags for a file that would outlive everyone on the pier.

He appended one private line he did not share on open *vox*: *Type A pupil response correlates to Type B enforcement behavior — possible command hierarchy or ritual coupling. Repeat capture required.*

The sentence was hope dressed as procedure. He knew it. He wrote it anyway.

## VII. Proportion — Rarest Mercy

Varro scrubbed blood off bone rails until his gauntlets steamed, working prayers into friction because he had learned long ago that rites which did not cost labor became gossip. That night he did not pray for victory. He prayed for proportion — the rarest mercy in a universe that loved extremes — and listened to the sea take the pier’s leavings the way confessions are taken when no priest remains: without comment, without forgiveness, only weight.

When the last lumen on the mezzanine cut out, Threx’s aug-eyes lingered on the empty cage one second longer than courtesy required, as if absence were a sample. Drusus lingered too, auspex dark, listening to rain resettle on mesh. Somewhere inland, Type **B** cadence marched to a rhythm the pier had not stopped — only interrupted — and the interruption would appear on someone’s ledger as profit lost.

**Kessler** found the boy’s skin straps abandoned near bone rail — not dropped in flight, **laid down** — as if the child had learned that some burdens could not travel landward without becoming spectacle. She did not pick them up. Picking up would have turned witness into **relic** Merit could clip to mesh tomorrow.

**Drusus** scraped a tissue sample from mesh where the hunter’s hide had kissed null-field — useless now, perhaps useful later — and sealed it under *terminated / hostile third party / repeat capture required*, because learning too late that your enemy had been **auditioning** you while you mistook patience for biology was debt the Chapter could not afford to pay twice.

**Varche** logged pier embarkation on Navy pane without romance — restraint schedule, not closure — because live alphabets died in courthouses admiralties could not sleep on while mortals paid for efficiency dressed as victory.

**Orwen** — not yet block capitals, still learning — watched the boy's straps near bone rail and wrote *child present; unstamped* in a margin nobody saluted, because testimony without adjectives was sometimes the only vaccine against officers who called the courthouse *closure*.

**Mira** passed landward wire with tally ink on her knuckles and did not smile. Her ledger had learned *witnessed* the way forges learned heat — useful, expensive, impossible to pretend had not happened when Type **B** cadence marched inland like soldier persistence the pier had interrupted, not stopped.

**Iria** led the water-bearer landward without ceremony — word by careful word, language rebuilt elsewhere — and untied no bell. At the stair-foot she stopped once and looked back at the empty cage — null-field still humming, mesh still beaded with rain — and untied no bell again. She had buried one after the bait kill. She would not bury another after a lesson the sea had already filed.

**Alaric** walked the landward stairs last, boots heavy with the particular gravity of a man who had won a fight and filed a defeat. He did not say *We hold.* Holding, tonight, meant carrying a child's sentence in your helm display until it became weight you could not delete — grit in a seal joint invisible until pressure found it, impossible to erase without lying to the mesh.

## VIII. Witnessed — Moving On

The pier kept humming. The crucible kept its specimen — dead now, disputed, **witnessed** — and moved on toward the next debt.

Drusus had watched alien patience and pupil geometry that tracked rank. Threx had logged restraint as data to invoice. The Chapter had learned a live alphabet could be erased by its own courthouse before ink learned grammar.

The boy’s sentence would ride in Alaric’s buffer like grit in a seal joint — invisible until pressure found it. **Kaell**’s auspex marriage would never arrive on the scheduled morning; **Furor** would choose him first at cord-weight’s firebase. Type **B** cadence answered the pier’s silence with **soldier persistence** inland — not withdrawal, punctuation without mercy — and **Kadmos** marked the rhythm on a slate beside Drusus’s *repeat capture required* until two lines rhymed without merging, then saved the null-field stub under *hostile capture; echo stub* and replayed harmonics until veins looked like rank tracking rank before inheritance had a name. Rhyme permitted in margins; oracle forbidden.

**Kessler** walked landward wire in one circuit — no looking back at the empty cage, no praise — because nostalgia and praise were both **theater**, and theater was how officers learned to call defeat *closure*. She counted mouths, hands, rebreathers instead; counting was the only vaccine that had never lied to her yet.

**Drusus** sealed tissue stub and Kaell correlation under Triumvirate stamp — *terminated / hostile third party; Sigma-Black sympathy rhyme; DO NOT MERGE with Type B cadence until repeat capture confirms* — sequence, not prophecy. Hope dressed as procedure. He knew it. He wrote it anyway.

**Kaell** would replay pupil geometry in firebase hololith until Furor chose him first — correlation without merge — because the pier had taught that some lessons arrived as **steam** before they arrived as doctrine.

**Echo** listened at landward wire for harmonics not yet **tide**-shaped — Triumvirate seal still **narrow** — filing null-field termination as sequence before cord-weight spent Versibar steam the hard way.

**Pol** kept monitor seed *sequence only* beside **Sera** splices until Furor stayed vocabulary arriving late on open channels.

**Santo** adjusted crate strap at landward wire and did not look at empty cage mesh. Looking would have turned specimen handoff into **spectacle**.

**Orwen** filed pier timestamps without adjectives — ranges, no metaphors — because facts laid like bricks survived longer than courthouse dressed as closure.

**Valerius** stamped embarkation toward **Cord Weight** twice before dawn — Marine continuity beside mortal witness count intact — because the next chapter would spend the cord in integers that opened ash.

**Threx**’s remote channel would file *tissue stub; yield dispute* beside *child witness; do not merge* until **Valerius** stamped *witness route only* and made parallel filing look like solvency long enough for cord-weight to spend Versibar steam the hard way.

**Varro** filed Threx restraint as data to invoice — seal attempted, not victory — and did not bless the empty cage. Blessing would have turned **witness** into package admiralties could sleep on.

Rain resettled on mesh. Null-field hummed its empty verdict. The sea took the pier’s leavings the way confessions are taken when no priest remains: without comment, without forgiveness, only weight.

Interest collecting teeth before receipts.

Null-field ache. Empty mesh. Boy’s sentence. Type B courthouse. Threx restraint logged as data to invoice. Sigma-Black rhyme still unverified on open record.

Cord-weight waiting. Versibar steam not yet named. Second Quarter Brief waiting in integers that would open ash.

Always owed.
