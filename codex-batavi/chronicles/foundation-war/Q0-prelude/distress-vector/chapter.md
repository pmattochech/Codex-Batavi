# The Crucible — *Distress Vector*

**Foundation War** · **Q0 Prelude** · *days after entombment*  
**Strategium anchor:** pre–CV y0 (sector commitment; **not** Aethelgard boots)  
**Filing layer:** pre–*Der Batav*; the Chapter is still filed as a tool and spoken of as a rumor.  
**Common name (fleet / auxilia):** *Grey Hounds* · **Formal header (Administratum):** *Slate Cohort — Hound Pattern*

**Geography lock:** **Insula Tenebrarum** · **Castra Vetera** / **Nine Phalanx** mesh — `[castra-vetera-galactic-lock.md](../../../../atlas-and-topography/castra-vetera-galactic-lock.md)`. **Witness Ford** outward filter; **Forge / Tempest / Crown** marsh channels. Retired in new prose: *Porta Nihili*, *Loken Passage*.

---

The sarcophagus bay was not loud. It did not allow itself to be loud. It held a constant machine-hum like a restrained animal, a sound so even and so patient that it made men careful with their breathing. The Grey Hounds — *the Chapter*, in its own mouth — had always lived aboard hulls more than they lived on soil. They knew the voices of deck-plates, the way a ship spoke through vibration, the way it forgave weight until it didn’t. But this hum had no forgiveness in it. It was a promise of function.

Alaric von Helis stood in the antechamber that served as a waiting room for immortality. His armor was iron-grey, the color of weather when the sun gives up. It made him look like a statue that had learned to move and refused to call it life.

He did not go in.

He could hear the cradle beyond the bulkhead — hydraulics asleep, seals awake, the long pressure of a machine designed to keep a man alive by admitting he was no longer a man. He could imagine the shape inside without seeing it: the coffin-body, the interface studs, the thin seam where the lid met the world and denied it entry. He could imagine the last breath in the moment before the machine took over, and the sound that breath must have made against metal.

Kadmos waited at his shoulder, silent as a folded map. Drusus and Varro were not present in the antechamber; they moved through the ship as if it were their own anatomy, and in truth it was. Drusus had entombed. Varro had judged. Kadmos had held the perimeter. Alaric had watched the procedure from the far side of glass because he had not trusted his hands to remain steady.

The door’s indicator rune blinked once — green, then amber, then green again — like a heart deciding whether it wanted to keep beating.

From the corridor behind them, a mortal deck-serf in void-grease and fresh fear hurried past carrying a slate box with a red seal. He stopped when he saw Alaric, froze the way people froze when they encountered weight, then bowed too quickly.

— Lord—

— Castellan—

The word *Chapter Master* did not come out. Not from the serf’s mouth. Not from anyone’s mouth these days without hesitation. Titles were supposed to be proof. Proof required archives. Archives had a habit of dying.

The serf swallowed and tried again, choosing the name that required no paper.

— Grey Hounds. Message for the Grey Hounds, sir.

Alaric watched the boy say it the way a crewman said the name of a tool he trusted in a storm. The affection in it was real. That was what made the next voice worse.

Valerius stepped into the antechamber with the calm of a man who had learned how to carry other people’s panic without spilling it. He wore no power armor, only the layered dignity of staff cloth and the hard geometry of a career spent in rooms where words killed more surely than blades. He held a data-slate in one hand and a physical dossier in the other, the dossier thick enough to be insulting in an age that worshiped redundancy only when it was official.

He did not look at the sarcophagus bay door. He looked at Alaric’s stance and read it the way a quartermaster read an empty magazine.

— They are filing him already — he said.

Alaric did not ask who *they* were. The Imperium had too many theys. Administratum. Navy. Priesthood. The endless middle strata that turned tragedy into a category so it could be shipped and stored.

Valerius held the slate up, and the header rune burned with bureaucratic certainty:

> **ASTARTES ASSET:** SLATE COHORT — HOUND PATTERN  
> **SUBJECT:** RADULF CASTRIN, COMMAND-CLASS, TERMINAL INJURY — STATUS: INTERNMENT  
> **NOTICE:** IDENTITY VERIFICATION PENDING

The phrase *identity verification pending* was a knife disguised as procedure. It implied that Radulf’s body might be the only thing about him that the Imperium would accept as real.

Kadmos’s jaw tightened.

— We are not an asset — he said.

Valerius’s eyes did not flicker.

— In their ledgers, we are — he replied. — Until we can prove otherwise.

Alaric felt the title in his own chest like a foreign object. Chapter Master. Castellan. Whatever they called him, the truth remained: he had inherited a pack with no pedigree scroll, no founding charter anyone could agree upon, and a badge on their plates that looked wolfish enough to invite rumor yet had no receipt anyone trusted. Hounds by name. Wolf by mark. A contradiction you could wear but not explain.

He stared at the line *identity verification pending* until it stopped being ink and became a corridor.

— Read me the annex — he said.

Valerius’s thumb tapped the slate. The tone that followed was not a voice; it was a playback — flat, time-stamped, the kind of record that pretended it was not testimony.

> **FILE:** SLATE COHORT — HOUND PATTERN / SARCOPHAGUS ANNEX / PRIORITY: IDENTITY-AUDIT  
> **LOCATION:** STRIKE CRUISER HOLD — BAY 3A  
> **SUBJECT:** RADULF CASTRIN (COMMAND-CLASS)  
> **CAUSE CHAIN:** PURSUIT OPERATION — PROVENANCE LEAD (“ORIGIN TRACE”) — COLLATERAL EVENT  
> **WITNESSES:** DRUSUS (APOTHECARION), VARRO (RECLUSIAM), KADMOS (STRATEGIUM), VALERIUS (STAFF), ALARIC VON HELIS (COMMAND)  
> **NOTE:** INTERNAL NAME-INVENTORY DISPUTED; OUTSIDE LEDGERS REQUIRE TEMPORARY TAG
>
> *…audio begins…*
>
> — You keep running after the paper — Radulf said.
>
> His voice was broken glass being forced to behave like a sentence.
>
> — You think if you find the name, you find the spine. You think if you find the father, you find the law. You think the Imperium will stop looking at you like an error.
>
> — We need the charter — Valerius said. — We need the proof.
>
> — Proof is a hungry god — Radulf answered, and coughed once as if the words had scraped his lungs raw. — It eats what you give it and asks for more.
>
> *…interface noise… pneumatic hiss…*
>
> — End this search. All of you.
>
> *…audio ends…*

When the playback died, the antechamber felt colder — not because the ship’s temperature had changed but because meaning had entered and refused to leave.

— End this search — Kadmos said quietly, as if repeating it would make it law. — That is an order.

Valerius did not nod. He did not shake his head.

— It is also a riddle — he said.

Alaric kept his gaze on the door. He could see Radulf’s absence as if it were a bulkhead that had been removed and left the vacuum behind. *End the search* could mean *stop digging*. It could mean *finish it so it stops digging into us*. A plain sentence that cut two ways was the kind of legacy war made.

He turned from the sarcophagus bay at last and walked toward the Strategium, each step a choice to keep moving while part of him wanted to kneel and remain in the antechamber forever as penance.

The Strategium was a room built to worship geometry. Hololiths floated in disciplined silence, systems rendered as wounds you could label. Officers moved like nerves around a spine. The wolf-mark banners hung in the corners, their presence an accusation: *you fly this sign, but you do not know why.* The mortal crew called it a wolf because mortals named silhouettes. The Administratum called it an unauthorized heraldry element pending verification. The Chapter called it nothing in speech and everything in posture.

**Kadmos** marked the nine wounds on a slate he did not show the hololith — geometry as insurance — because hunger dressed as legitimacy still required witness before throughput dressed it as charter packages admiralties could sleep on.

Drusus stood at a secondary hololith, gauntlets stained with the sort of red no scrubber could fully erase. His helm lenses angled toward Alaric as if to confirm the obvious: you are here; therefore we continue.

— Radulf’s internment buys time — Drusus said. He did not say *saves him*. Apothecaries did not waste words on sentiment when function was what kept you alive. — It does not buy legitimacy. The injury profile matches the pursuit chain. That will be used against us.

— Used how? — Kadmos asked.

Drusus’s answer arrived like a statistic.

— As proof that the Chapter cannot be trusted to police its own hungers.

Varro did not stand at the hololith. He stood near the vox arrays, cowled helm angled down like a blade poised over a throat. His silence was not absence. It was a tool he used to starve the wrong frequencies.

Valerius placed the physical dossier on the strategium table with care, as if the table might break.

— This is what remains of our name — he said. — And this is what does not.

He opened the dossier. Inside was not a story. It was a cemetery of stories: conflicting founding numbers, half-burned warrants, transit manifests that ended mid-sentence, lists of dead astropaths, pages stamped with *NULL* where a lineage should have been. Each time someone had tried to build the Chapter’s history into a single spine, the spine had been broken by someone else’s disaster.

Drusus watched the pages without reaching for them.

— State it cleanly, Alaric — he said. The Apothecary never said *please.* The word implied choice.

Valerius began to read, not as an orator but as a mortician.

> **SUBJECT:** SLATE COHORT — HOUND PATTERN / IDENTITY-INVENTORY LOSS  
> **SUMMARY:** repeated disasters erase provenance evidence, amplifying legitimacy hunger.
>
> 1. **Warp-leg loss:** charter-coffer ship *vanished* during translation; astropathic chain severed; no duplicates survived.
> 2. **Administratum destruction / redaction:** Founding registry replaced with **NULL**; tithe lineage scrubbed; “correlation pending” stamped over names.
> 3. **Mechanicus data loss / withholding:** vault sealed under treaty; access delayed under verification rites; then refiled as **lost to incident**.
> 4. **Internal catastrophe:** archive fire; slate purge under breach protocol; witness cadre killed; surviving copies judged “contaminated” and destroyed.
>
> **NOTE:** external offices treat discontinuity as evidence of defect, not evidence of harm.

Alaric listened as if the docket were a litany, because in the Imperium bureaucratic lists were often the only scripture that mattered. He could feel the hunger in the room tighten — officers leaning forward, not for comfort but for teeth. The Chapter’s legitimacy was a wound that never scabbed; each time it tried to heal, some new incident tore it open again, and the hunger to find the lost name became a virtue simply because it was active.

— That is why we hunt — Kadmos said. — Not for romance. For survival.

— For permission — Valerius corrected. — For a seal that keeps knives out of our ribs while we do the Imperium’s work.

Drusus’s helm dipped a fraction.

— Permission is not a medical term — he said. — It is a political one. Politics kills organisms as efficiently as toxins.

Varro finally spoke, voice slow and exact.

— A name can become a god — he said. — And gods demand sacrifice.

Alaric looked at the hololith. Nine wounds hung in the air like a constellation that did not want to be charted. Routes flickered. Hazard bands pulsed. The Crucible sat at the edge of mapped space with the kind of emptiness that tempted men to fill it with stories. *This is where our charter will be.* *This is where our father waits.* *This is where the wolf-mark will finally make sense.*

He had heard those sentences before. He had even believed them.

The Strategium door chimed. A vox-officer entered, pale, holding a slate like it contained a disease.

— Incoming packet — he said. — Distress-class. Older than our charts would allow. Routed through Mechanicus channels and Navy channels simultaneously. It requests… compliance.

— Read — Alaric ordered.

The officer’s throat worked.

— Address line — he said. — It’s addressed to… to us by filing name.

Kadmos’s head turned sharply.

Valerius took a step without thinking.

Drusus’s lenses narrowed.

Varro’s silence sharpened, as if he had tightened a strap around the throat of the room.

— The address line — Alaric repeated, slower.

The officer held the slate out as if it might bite.

> **TO:** SLATE COHORT — HOUND PATTERN (ASTARTES ASSET)  
> **FROM:** DISTRESS ORIGINATOR (UNVERIFIED)  
> **SUBJECT:** SAFE CORRIDOR RECOMMENDATION / RECOVERY REQUEST  
> **NOTE:** THANK YOU FOR YOUR COMPLIANCE

The words were polite. That was what made them obscene. Politeness implied familiarity. Familiarity implied eyes.

**Merit** — not yet named on roster, only a remembrancer's itch in Valerius's margin — would later learn recorders clipped to hololiths named *compliance* were debt Moon Hunt would spend in courthouse alphabets. Valerius killed the thought before the clip clicked: witness routes only.

Valerius's voice dropped, not from fear but from the instinct to speak quietly around a predator.

— That name isn’t public — he said. — Not that exact header. Not that stamp.

— Mechanicus will say it’s their channel leak — Kadmos said, already angry.

Drusus’s reply was immediate.

— It isn’t — he said. — The encryption markers are wrong. Not broken, wrong. Like an imitation of a key by someone who has watched keys being used.

Alaric watched Threx’s representative on a secondary channel — only a cog-sigil and a clipped vox tone, a mind behind a mask of etiquette.

— Castellan — the Mechanicus voice said. — We have received the same packet. We did not author it. We do not like the way it knows our procedures. We request joint review.

It was the first time in weeks that *joint* did not sound like a treaty word and instead sounded like a survival reflex.

Before Alaric could answer, the door chimed again — a different tone, the one the ship used for guests who carried warrants the Navy had already accepted.

The vox-officer looked at Alaric, then at the slate, then at the door, as if the universe had begun stacking impossible objects.

— Castellan — he said. — The Rogue Trader *Calico Mercy* is on final approach to our outer ring. She filed the same distress packet six hours ago from her own mast. She requests… audience. She says she holds an escort charter for our translation into the **Insula** theater.

Kadmos’s hand moved toward his sidearm without thinking, then stopped. Astartes did not draw on warrant-bearers in a Strategium unless the warrant was already bleeding.

Valerius’s mouth tightened.

— Insula — he repeated, tasting the word like unfamiliar wine. — Administratum have that subsector under provisional seal. **Castra Vetera** mesh. Fringe limes. She should not have a charter we have not seen.

— She has seen our filing name — Drusus said. — That is the more interesting pathology.

**Varro** listened at the vox arrays for harmonics that were not prayer — *Codex* death-skull geometry still, Executor jawline years down the annals — and filed the distress packet as proto-rite: seal attempted, not victory, because imitation keys were sometimes the only vaccine when void pretended it knew hull names.

The door opened.

**Maela Thornwake** did not enter like a pilgrim or a clerk. She entered like a woman who had made the void pay her in installments and intended to collect interest. She was not tall, but she wore height the way some people wore jewelry — as a negotiated lie everyone agreed to respect. Her coat was void-black cut with trader’s gold, the fabric stiff with embroidery that had survived more than one translation fire. A **Warrant of Trade** hung at her hip in a reliquary case thick enough to stop a stub-round; the seal on it was real enough that even Varro did not speak against it on sight.

Behind her came only two mortals: a **Navigator** boy with eyes too old for his face, hooded so the Third Eye did not scandalize the room, and a factor in ledger-gray who carried a coffin-slate instead of a weapon.

Thornwake stopped at the strategium’s honor line and bowed — not deep enough to be submission, not shallow enough to be insult.

— Castellan von Helis — she said. Her Gothic carried the clipped music of ships that had learned language from vox compression and loneliness. — I am **Maela Thornwake**, master under warrant of the *Calico Mercy*. I did not write your distress packet. I **received** it the way a sailor receives a wave that knows your hull’s name. I am here because the wave asked for **Grey Hounds**, and because I still have a route the Administratum will recognize long enough to spend.

Alaric did not offer a title back. Titles were currency; he would not spend his until he knew the rate.

— You knew our filing header — he said.

Thornwake’s smile was brief and without warmth.

— I knew a header someone wanted found — she said. — There is a difference. Your Radulf’s pursuit chain left footprints in places paper does not reach. I trade in footprints.

Kadmos stepped half a pace forward, armor whispering.

— You will speak his name with care — he said.

Thornwake’s eyes did not flinch from ceramite.

— I will speak it with accuracy — she replied. — He bought passage from me once, years ago, when your pack still pretended it was only a fleet. He asked for charts toward a **provenance echo** in the crucible theater. I sold him a braid I had walked once, before the ribbon turned mean. He did not return to pay the second half.

Valerius’s fingers tightened on the dossier.

— You are saying you led him into the injury chain — he said, flat.

— I am saying the void led him — Thornwake answered. — I am a trader, not a god. I log hazard. I do not command it.

Drusus’s helm turned toward the Navigator boy.

— Your house — Drusus said. — Name it.

The boy’s voice was thin.

— **House Keth-Marrow** — he said. — Bonded to Thornwake warrant. We do not hold **Insular** coffin ledgers. We hold… trader’s copies. Partial. Expensive.

— Expensive is how Navigators say *incomplete* — Drusus replied.

Thornwake lifted a hand, stopping an argument she had seen before.

— Castellan, you may have **Mechanicus** eyes and **Navy** guns and a room full of transhuman saints who hate owing mortals anything. I offer you what you actually need: a translation window into the **Insula Tenebrarum** approach, a berth clause the Lex Ignis fleet will recognize, and a distress originator that wants you alive long enough to arrive stupid. I do not offer safety. Safety is not sold in Nihilus.

She nodded to her factor. The factor stepped forward and placed the coffin-slate on the table beside Valerius’s dossier of erasures. Hololith light woke across its surface: star-lines, hazard bands, a narrow braid marked in amber — **PROVISIONAL: THORNWAKE ROUTE / NOT CHART-HOUSE SEALED**.

**Kadmos** read the amber braid without touching it — witness before throughput — because provisional charts were posture not packages and **Insula Tenebrarum** would tax both ledgers before **Castra Vetera** mesh earned a name the Chapter could salute without lying.

Varro’s silence broke into words, each one placed like a stone.

— Provisional — he said. — That is a confession.

Thornwake met the cowled helm without performance.

— That is commerce — she said. — The **Insular chart-houses** will not sell to you until you have bled on their doorstep. I sell you the approach **before** the doorstep breaks your knees. You may hate me later. Traders are accustomed to later.

Alaric felt Radulf’s sentence rise again in his mind. *End this search. All of you.* The room demanded he choose a reading. The distress packet sat on the slate with its polite thanks. The nine wounds pulsed on the hololith like mouths.

He looked at Thornwake — mortal, greedy, necessary.

— Why us — he asked. — Why not the Navy. Why not Threx alone.

Thornwake’s factor answered because Thornwake let him, the way captains let factors carry knives they did not want on their own belt.

— Because the packet names **Slate Cohort** — the factor said. — Because whoever wrote it wants a tool that hunts, not a bureaucracy that files. Because Rogue Traders survive by being early to hunger.

Threx’s cog-sigil flickered on the secondary channel.

— Castellan — the Mechanicus voice said. — We do not endorse Thornwake coffin data. We **will** observe her translation and compare ledgers. **Compliance is efficiency.**

Kadmos looked at Alaric, waiting for the word that would make anger into geometry.

Valerius looked at the amber braid on the slate and saw, already, the indictment a court would write if the Chapter returned hollow: *you followed a trader’s partial chart into a storm ribbon.*

Alaric held the room with his voice the way a wall held a tide — by being heavier than what pressed against it.

— We will go — he said.

— To end it? — Valerius asked, not as a challenge but as a plea for a definition.

— To end this search — Alaric replied. He let the ambiguity remain because war rarely allowed purity. — Either by finding the truth or by learning that the truth is a poison. We cannot keep bleeding ourselves for paper while the frontier sharpens its knives.

He turned to Thornwake.

— You will embark under **joint review** — he said. — Mechanicus auspex on your bridge. My Kadmos on your honor. Log every anomaly twice. If you sell us a route that kills mortals for profit, I will not need the Administratum to judge you.

Thornwake’s bow this time was almost respectful.

— Castellan — she said. — I sell routes that kill mortals because the void is priced that way. I will not pretend otherwise. But I will not waste **Grey Hounds** before I have been paid.

Drusus’s head dipped — approval disguised as clinical acceptance.

Varro’s silence became, briefly, shelter.

The vox-officer swallowed and waited for the final order.

Alaric did not say *We hold.* The word belonged to cordons not yet built, to firebases not yet named, to boys whose sentences still rode in futures the pier had not yet filed. Holding, tonight, was procedure disguised as motion — engines waking like teeth grinding in sleep. He did not dress it in hymn. He did not pretend it was destiny. He gave it the only dignity he trusted: procedure.

— Prepare translation — he said. — Accept Thornwake’s escort charter and the Mechanicus observer. Flag every warp leg under **CV annals** from the hour we commit. If the void is watching, we will not give it the luxury of a single ledger.

The officer moved. Orders became motion.
Somewhere deep in the sarcophagus bay, a machine kept Radulf alive by refusing to let him be whole. Alaric felt the shape of that refusal in his own new title and understood why the Chapter loved the hunt: because hunting felt like becoming whole, and wholeness felt like safety, and safety was the only prayer soldiers ever truly meant.

The distress packet sat on the slate with its polite thanks.

The Grey Hounds began to run — behind a Rogue Trader's amber braid, toward a crucible that did not yet have their name.

**Kadmos** would hold Thornwake's honor line on the outer ring — witness before throughput — because joint review was posture not package and **Castra Vetera** mesh would tax both ledgers before the Chapter earned a name it could salute without lying.

**Drusus** appended a private margin for translation burn — *Thornwake partial chart; treat as sequence only until attested; do not brief captains* — not prophecy, **sequence**, because briefing would have turned amber braid into **oracle** before Moon Hunt taught the pier that live alphabets could die in courthouses.

**Valerius** stamped the departure slate twice — Marine continuity beside mortal witness count intact — because parallel filing was sometimes the only mercy institutions could afford before integers opened ash.

**Echo** listened at translation burn for harmonics not yet **tide**-shaped — Triumvirate seal still **narrow** — filing amber braid as sequence before Moon Hunt taught pier that live alphabets could die in courthouses.

**Sera** tagged departure handshake *monitor only* until Pol seed starved merge columns and Mud Gospel earned attested injury at CV hour zero.

Interest collecting teeth before receipts.

Grey Hounds running. Amber braid. Crucible unnamed. Radulf refusing wholeness in sarcophagus bay.

Always owed.
