## I. Interest on a Debt Nobody Signed

The tombs did not need to **win** in the childish sense — flags, parades, clean triumph photos for archives nobody honest keeps; they needed only to **teach**, and the lesson arrived wearing ceramic hunger while the gulf’s earlier tightening — the micro-fractures, the wrong **turn** in sealed arithmetic — became **legible** as curriculum rather than accident.

**Contact**, the Strategium had filed it — neutral as a knife is neutral until it meets meat — and contact on tombs did not behave like a line advance; it behaved like **interest** accruing on a debt nobody remembered signing, paid first in attention, then in **skin**, then in whatever word you used when you could no longer pretend panic was temporary.

The **backward braid** was ugly: phased footprints had taught **intervals**; intervals now taught **flensing**; flensing taught mortals that reflection could lie and lying could become **hunger** for any story that stopped shaking.

Drusus’s deployment margin — *tremor scans may rhyme — record, do not sermonize* — did not prevent sermons; it prevented sermons from wearing **lab coats** and stealing Apothecarion bandwidth. Marines still felt **intervals** crawl under ceramite like cold insects; auxilia felt them as nausea without poetry.

**Valerius** stamped casualty columns **twice** — once for confirmed dead, once for minds gone while hearts still beat — because the Strategium’s appetite for single-line mercy would have collapsed both categories into one tidy lie.

**Echo** pressed at Triumvirate seals without earning names — permission still **narrow** — and **Varro** refused it spectacle: short binds, tight spacing, **Executor** wolf-skull present as infrastructure not theater. Where tombs timed pulses, silence had to stay **boring** on purpose; boredom was the only vaccine panic theology could not metabolize quickly.

Auxilia on the night watch learned to hear **intervals** in their teeth before auspex admitted rhythm — tomb harmonics climbing through cable-saints’ rosaries until rosaries sounded like ribs and ribs sounded like **invitations**. **Kessler** rotated sleep on odd hours because odd hours broke synchrony, and broken synchrony was sometimes the only vaccine a sergeant could administer without ink from Terra.

— If your hands start looking like someone else’s — she told a boy — you tell me before you teach your brother.

The boy nodded. Nodding was cheaper than understanding. Understanding would arrive with interest, priced in skin.

## II. Dress Yourself in What Remembers

Flaying was not battle rhythm. It was **contagion of meaning** — reflection lying, lying becoming hunger, hunger becoming theology improvised from cable-saints and rosary lamps that suddenly looked like ribs — until mortals collaborated in their own undressing because any story that stopped shaking felt like mercy.

**Flayed Ones** were not *troops*; they were **outbreak** — noble code unraveling into compulsive flensing, hierarchy dissolving into appetite dressed in metal skin, purpose reduced to a single tactile obsession: *Dress yourself in what remembers.*

They hit **human shanty stacks** on the gulf bridges — woven habitats of debt-labor and cable-saints’ shrines, infants sleeping beside tools older than their grandparents — like a moral argument made flesh: *If flesh is costume…* *…undress the world*.

Mortals died in sprays the auspex recorded as **thermal events**, while mortals who survived recorded it as **theology**, the **reading** split between instrument and witness the way **Drusus** hated because it meant panic had become **data** and **liturgy** at once, two languages contaminating each other until men could not tell whether they were obeying orders or performing rites.

Phase signatures cooled **after** contact the way they had in first-touch drills — **taxonomy**, not surprise — except here cooling smelled like butchers’ stalls and turned scopes into confessional booths.

Skitarii skirmishers learned fast that *first touch* pride tasted like copper when the touched thing wore **someone’s uncle**; **Threx** still filed salvage windows because filing was how Mechanicus kept hands from shaking visibly — **procedure** as corset — and hated that the Batavi file labeled his hunger **extraction** while their file labeled the same object **seal**.

**Iria**, when she moved through the wreckage with her tide-habit of looking for *flow*, stopped once beside a cradle that had become a bowl and did not speak; her silence was not shock but **inventory**, the kind of witness bridges respect when applause would be blasphemy.

**Sera** tested splices through smoke until color-code tape looked like wounds — red for dead routes, yellow for *maybe*, blue for *do not trust your joy* — and treated every reconnect as confession because **parallel filing** stopped meaning *redundancy* and started meaning **which brother you could still call back** when one line started singing tomb notes.

**Kadmos** quartered breaches with geometry instead of speeches: no deep pursuit through shanty warrens without rear seal; no *hero lanes* through cable-saints’ shrines just because optics loved a silhouette; **hold** translated into body bags carried *outward* before inward heroics spent more skin on vanity.

**Alaric** did not say *We hold* as litany on the breach net. He said *seal the stack* and *carry outward first* — hinge-voice doing mass-work while Drusus metered sedatives and Varro shortened binds until boredom returned as shield. Triumvirate differentiation held even here: syringe, stitch, wall — none pretending the tomb was polite.

A woman in a shanty stack — name later lost to ink bleed — held a rosary woven from cable and screamed without sound when a Flayed One’s shadow crossed her doorway. **Kessler** did not shoot the shadow. She shot the **hinge** that made the shadow possible — a support strut compromised, a gap that geometry had priced — and dragged the woman backward by the belt until the woman’s scream found air again.

— Not yet — Kessler said — and meant *not dead yet*, which was the only theology auxilia could afford on gulf bridges.

## III. Panic Theology — Foam at the Mouth of Reason

**Panic theology** bloomed overnight — not because the Emperor abandoned them (though some said it, because grief enjoys simple villains) but because terror is **creative** when *vox* fuzzes and tombs hum, faiths improvised from **cable-saints** and old superstitions about **mirrors** — anything that offered *rules* for why reflection could lie, why glass might show a face that wasn’t yours, why the bridge’s rosary lights could suddenly look like **ribs**.

Men who had survived **Vitreus** reached for the wrong lesson first — *doubt your eyes* — and discovered too late that doubt, unsealed, becomes **hunger** for *any* story that stops the shaking.

**Varro** would later call it *folk liturgy*; on the deck it was **foam at the mouth of reason**, and the Chapter filed it as **contagion of meaning** no bolt alone could quarantine.

**Merit’s** clerks tried to harvest *witness throughput* from screaming stacks until **Kessler** — teeth and paperwork — got the initiative refiled as **NON-COMPLIANT**; even **Threx** winced when **Valerius** explained that morale metrics built on flayed infant heat signatures would return as **fleet liability**, not yield.

Men who had survived **Tertius** flinched at **weight** wrongness; here weight felt *light* sometimes — wrong in the opposite direction — until the body read lightness as falling and reached for anchors, even if anchors were teeth and neighbor-skin.

**Drusus** did what he always did when the world behaved like a wound: he **documented**, not for comfort but for **containment**, mapping **contagious recursion** — not a plague in the mortal sense of coughs and boils but something uglier in practice, **cognitive contagion** carried through proximity to **tomb harmonics**, a pressure in the skull like tuning forks clamped on sanity, panic **mapping** onto neural loops until men began to *help* the flensing in their minds before metal touched them.

Auxilia broke faster against **fear** than bolters; that was the statistic Drusus hated most because it implied discipline was a veneer thinner than ceramite — and because it was **true** — so his conclusions stayed short while his appendices grew, **clinical work** holding the line where oratory would have been insult. He appended a frequency table beside a casualty column until the page looked like a hymn sheet written by an atheist who still feared choirs.

A man who had survived **Vitreus** smashed a viewport with his bare fist because the glass showed him smiling when he was not smiling. **Varro** did not comfort him. He laid ash along the frame and spoke three binds short enough to fit between heartbeats — not banishment, **weight** — until the man’s breathing slowed from performance back into **inventory**.

— Nomenclature is seal — Varro said — and your face is not a tomb yet.

The man wept without sound. Sound would have fed the contagion. Varro moved on, Executor wolf-skull catching lumen like a moon that refused warmth.

## IV. Second Front Inside My Apothecarion

Apothecarion interns learned to distinguish **Wolf’s Curse** drift from tomb harmonics without turning distinction into oracle — **rhyme** permitted; **prophecy** burned — until junior medicae stopped mistaking adrenaline for curse and started treating adrenaline as **data hygiene** under stress you could chart.

Medicae bins overflowed with men who flensed *air* — nails raking throats that wore no enemy claw — until sedative accounting became its own battle: enough to stop self-murder, not enough to make **combatants** into furniture; Drusus metered doses like artillery, cruel in the way water is cruel when rationed honestly.

**Threx** stood too close to a stack of remains arranged with Mechanicus reverence — obscene with ambition, eyes bright with the missionary gleam of someone who believes knowledge is worth any price *other people pay* — and gave orders dressed as suggestions: *Sample integrity. Quick extraction. Stasis veil. Minimal spiritual contamination — quantify later.*

A **Genetor** of his maniple reached — instruments singing their eager insect whisper — and **Alaric** moved like law, **hand** landing on the Genetor’s carapace — not a strike, not yet — **weight** enough to freeze servos: *protocol as violence*, the oldest dialect between Adeptus and Adeptus when both pretend they serve one Throne.

— You will **not** manufacture a second front inside my Apothecarion — Alaric said — quiet, final, the kind of quiet that makes bystanders discover they have been holding breath without noticing.

**Threx** filed the intervention as **Chapter barbarism** and, beneath a private heading he would not show allies, *Constraint noted*, friction partner speaking **extraction** where the Chapter spoke **seal**.

The irreversible fact was smaller than a battle and worse than a defeat: **Mechanicus ambition** had tried to monetize panic while panic was still **breathing**.

Bondsmen whispered **Dark Age of Technology** near samples until Varro crushed the word with ash again — **nomenclature is seal** — and the whisper retreated into **texture** where it could not recruit Skitarii ambition into opening a second appetite beside the first.

## V. Weight Until Weight Became the Only Sermon

**Santo** hauled wounded who clutched rosaries woven from cable and called them **saints**; he did not argue hagiography — argument would have fed the contagion — he simply moved weight until weight became the only sermon that still translated when words turned to foam.

**Mira Kell** stayed on tally, ink and blood on the same knuckles, **witness without applause**, because applause was how panic converted grief into currency; her ledger stayed ugly on purpose.

**Varro** walked **bridge spines** until the wind tried to steal syllables and failed, **Executor** wolf-skull catching stray lumen like a moon that refused warmth; he chanted **binds** — not to banish Necrons like cheap **theater** but to **stitch** the Immaterial’s eager tears where panic tore holes mortals could fall through — and his voice **tore**, not cracked — *tore*, as if each repetition cost thread from something that did not grow back on schedule.

Chaplain nets along those spines carried **parallel filing** too — duplicate binds at duplicate seams — not redundancy fetish; **insurance** against harmonics eating one corridor while brothers still breathed in the second.

Mortals pressed ears to gantries like children listening for whales and heard **intervals** instead — wrong lesson, almost useful — until Varro shortened litanies further until boredom returned as shield.

Soul-tax, the old poets would have called it; Varro had no poets left, only **duty** and the face he gave the galaxy so the galaxy would stop asking him to smile, **spiritual-weight** parallel to Drusus’s ledgers and Alaric’s hinge-law.

Where Drusus filed *cognitive contagion; ongoing*, Varro filed *bind integrity; failing at stress nodes* — two seals on the same wound, neither pretending the wound was polite.

**Threx** filed intervention as **Chapter barbarism** beside *Constraint noted* — friction partner speaking extraction where Chapter spoke seal — and **Valerius** stamped *witness route only* until friction looked like solvency when Mechanicus ambition tried to monetize panic while panic was still breathing.

## VI. Mercy Dressed as Atrocity

**Sergeant Kessler** found a man on his knees beside a coolant puddle, fingers hooked into his own cheek as if he could **unzip** mortality and step out cleaner.

She did not shout. She did not pray. She **shot** him — once, precise — before he finished teaching his children what *mercy* looked like when mercy wore claws, mercy dressed as atrocity, the taste swallowed and **lodged** because **mortal geometry** sometimes meant choosing which horror got filed under *necessary*.

Afterward she checked her hands the way armor-serfs check welds: not for tremor, for **truth** — because if your hands lie, your next shot becomes theater for the wrong audience.

Children stared — not accusation, **recording** — until auxilia folded tarp faster than explanations could arrive.

**Kessler** ordered silence not out of cruelty but because explanations became **folk liturgy** faster than bullets could sterilize.

**Valerius** tallied losses without flinching — columns aligned, assumptions disciplined, futures priced with the cold honesty logistics demands when love would kill more people than arithmetic — then later, **alone**, door sealed, lamp a narrow mercy, pressed his palms to his eyes as if checking they remained **his**.

Skin debt, the bondsmen had sung; the bridge eats debt; tonight the ledger ate **quiet**.

He wrote one line in his own hand — not for the Strategium, for himself — *We held the count*, and hated that the sentence felt like prayer because prayer was exactly what Drusus warned could become contagious.

A child who had **recorded** the shot — eyes too old, mouth still too young — asked Kessler whether the man on his knees had been *bad*. Kessler knelt to the child’s height, which cost her pride and paid it back in honesty.

— He was **sick** — she said — in a way bullets sometimes cure before words can.

The child filed *sick* beside *necessary* and would carry both words like stones in a pocket until a later chapter taught him stones could be **anchors** or **weapons**, depending who held the hand.

## VII. Romance Curdles — Saints of Panic

Night on the rosary decks did not cool the debt; it only made lamps look like confessionals — **Lex Ignis** clocks trading codes with Mechanicus wheels until synchronization pretended to be virtue.

**Valerius** hated that synchronization most when it worked, because working made catastrophe look *managed* until the next thermal event proved otherwise.

On a rosary lamp post, someone had nailed a child's drawing — stick bodies, X marks, arrows — beside a cable-saint shrine that was now a bowl. **Varro** did not tear it down. He laid ash along the paper’s edge until the drawing looked like weather rather than prophecy, because prophecy was how panic recruited volunteers for debts they could not repay.

Water points ran **taste** — recyclers sour with stress and ash — until men flinched from drinking and dehydrations added a second mortality column neither Drusus nor the tombs had to lift a finger to claim; Valerius posted **ROTATE** until the word looked like doctrine.

Here the romance **curdles** — if romance is ordeal made honest — the monster not only xenos but **what fear makes us willing to become**: collaborators in our own flensing, scholars of our neighbors’ skin, saints of panic, heroes who discover too late that courage without **seals** is only adrenaline spending credit on credit.

The gulf tightened, and somewhere a tomb timed a pulse none of them had permission to hear — they heard it anyway, **consequence** sealed in bone and *vox*-static until the next route choice came due, **Bridge Saints** already sharpening their names in the dark.

**Drusus** appended a closing margin for translation burn — *skin debt may present as Exanimus-adjacent tremor rhyme; treat as tomb-coupled false-positive unless sustained across three intervals* — not prophecy, **sequence**; the same discipline that had kept **Wolf’s Curse** from becoming theater in the medicae bins, translated forward for chaplain lanes where theology itself could infect — **record**, **meter**, **deny spectacle**.

**Valerius** stamped the embarkation slate to **Bridge Saints** twice: once for chaplain nets tightening, once for mortal lines still solvent, because the next chapter would charge both debts at interest — and interest, on bridges, always wore teeth before it wore receipts you could stamp twice without lying outright.
