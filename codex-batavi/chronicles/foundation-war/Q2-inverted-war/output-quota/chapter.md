## I. Hooks Bought Time — Quotas Spent It

Hooks — not bombs — had bought them time. Time, in Incus, translated into **quotas**: numbers that climbed because fear climbed faster, because ***Lex Ignis*** auditors smiled at throughput the way sharks smile at blood they pretend is statistics, because guild factors discovered *virtue* in overtime until overtime became **liturgy** and liturgy became a shift bell nobody could refuse without starving someone they loved.

The **backward braid** was honest and ugly: Cinder Nine’s nineteen dead had bought roofs; roofs bought **output**; output bought the hive’s second mouth — the one that ate stability while pretending to feed it. Instruction logged behind biometrics on a gallery floor now wore **posters** calling itself *labor unrest*, because Administratum loved posters and loved *simple*.

Tithe mathematics are theology with better stationery. When quotas rose, **wombs** rose too — not as poetry, as accounting: more bodies promised, more hands promised, more *output* promised to thrones that would never visit the halls where output was extracted. The Administratum called it *demographic optimism*. Drusus called it what it was on his private slate — **pressure on a biological line** — and locked the word behind a cipher because some truths made officers brave in the wrong direction.

In the shift-warrens, women learned to time contractions to break bells because a body that chose its own rhythm was a body that could be fined; in the manufactories, men learned to laugh at pain because laughter scanned as morale on overseer picts. Incus did not invent cruelty. It industrialized it until cruelty looked like *throughput* and throughput looked like virtue.

## II. Throughput Optimizations Beside Birth-Rate Charts

**Threx** posted throughput *optimizations* beside birth-rate charts as if the two were siblings who enjoyed each other’s company — numbers smiling at numbers while humans learned to hate the paper between them. Drusus watched the pairing from the mezzanine lip and felt the old itch from the Silica Field return: systems **coupled** without permission, learning each other’s rhythms until one began to mimic appetite convincingly. Quotas were not noise. Quotas were **steering**.

**Kessler** translated the poster math into profanity for auxilia who could not read Gothic fast enough to understand why their water rations had thinned; profanity, at least, did not pretend to be virtue.

— Someone upstream is counting your children as future shifts — she told them.

The sentence did not start a riot; it started a **silence** worse than riot, because silence meant people believed her.

Midwife halls doubled as **census temples**: white tile scrubbed until holiness looked like hygiene; votive candles illegal and present anyway — little treasons mortals committed against regulation to keep their minds *animal* enough to push, to breathe, to believe a child could be a blessing and not a ledger line. Steam hissed from sterilizers until the corridors sounded like something exhaling.

**Drusus** entered not as voyeur but as **actuary**: white apron over armor, hands bare below the elbow in a gesture that was half hygiene and half theater — *see, I touch your world with skin*. Consent for blood draws was extracted the way you extract teeth: quickly, with pretense of mercy, paperwork signed in names women could not read fast enough to flinch from. He did not explain why he needed third-shift samples *and* first-shift *and* swing — he never explained enough to comfort, only enough to comply, because comfort was a corridor he could not secure and compliance was a door he could lock.

A midwife watched him with eyes that knew how to hate without moving her mouth. Drusus nodded once, respect without friendship, and labeled vials in a hand steadier than his thoughts. Each vial clicked into its rack like a small verdict.

## III. Brood-Flutter — Who Conducts

Biometrics *in absentia* became obsession — not romance, pathology dressed as diligence. Cohort heart rates piped through *vox*-analog lines until the Apothecarion listening post sounded like a nest of sleeping giants breathing in unison. Footsteps synced across levels that should not share drumbeats; micro-tremors in line workers’ hands aligned shift to shift like **choir practice** without a conductor anyone could see.

Drusus slept in slices beside the speakers and woke with graphs burned into his retinas. He began to hate the graphs for being beautiful. Beauty, in data, was a predator’s camouflage.

One night he ran a null test — randomized shift labels, scrambled worker IDs — and the waveform *smiled* at him anyway, peaks aligning to false names as if the hive recognized the game and played along. He shut the test down before anyone else saw it. Some results were worse shared; they turned competent men into mystics, and mystics made mistakes that cost lives in straight lines.

On a hololith in a tent that stank of ash and solvent, **Kadmos** scoffed — armor still powdered with Incus grit — because scoffing was how warriors kept awe from becoming fear.

— A hive doesn’t *flutter* — he said.

— This one does — Drusus replied — and threw up waves stacked on waves, each peak a shift change, each trough a lie the machines told themselves about randomness — graphs that pulsed like a second dawn beneath the first.

Kadmos leaned closer despite himself. **Varro**, who had entered without announcement, stood at the tent’s edge as if edges were where truth lived. He leaned in at last, *Codex* death-skull reflecting data he pretended not to worship; light slid across bone enamel and made numbers look like scripture.

— If it is a choir — Varro said softly — **who conducts**.

Drusus had no answer worth ink. Silence, for him, was also data.

Varro took rites *into* the factories — not triumphal Ministorum theater, not processions that required clean streets, but **threaded discipline**: whispers against ancient pistons; binds laid on breath until breath became rhythm workers could not hear without obeying, even when they did not know they were obeying. Some flinched. Some prayed without knowing which god listened. Some hated him for making holiness smell like sweat — as if sweat were not always holy in places where bodies were priced by the hour and souls were discounted as *sentiment*.

Foreman’s whistles tried to swallow his voice; whistles lost.

**Mira Kell** watched from a gallery with eyes that counted everything except what she chose not to count. Her stillness was not neutrality; it was **storage**.

## IV. Valerius — Two Slates, One Truth

**Valerius** moved among foremen, separating *threat* from *grievance* the way engineers separate load from noise — dry love, useful love, the kind that kept men from being shot over misunderstandings that could have been solved with a better shift roster and a worse conscience. He carried two slates: one for the Castellan, one for himself. Only one of them ever told the full truth.

When ***Lex Ignis*** security pivoted, it did so with the confidence of men who believed paperwork could carve reality. Skitarii overwatch rotated a spotlight into a union hall like a stylus carving guilt into faces — every wrinkle a confession, every blink a ledger entry. Someone shouted *compliance* as if the word were a shield; someone else shouted a name that was not the Emperor’s; the hall became a lung inhaling rage.

Riot answered because riot was Incus’s immune system: messy, stupid, honest.

**Molten glass** flew — fast, bright, cutting — where some fool had tried to turn a bottle into a weapon and instead turned heat into a lesson. Words became bricks. Bricks became falling. The air turned to smoke that tasted like burned sugar and old vows. Skitarii advanced in lockstep, galvanic rifles low, optics hunting heat signatures the way creditors hunt signatures. A bottle arced; a knee dislocated with a wet pop; someone screamed for the Emperor and meant *mother*.

**Kessler** tried to wedge a cordon line between culted anger and factory kids who had come to watch because boredom kills slower than las but kills all the same. Her *vox* to Alaric was not poetry:

— Hall compromised — human density high — request suppressive posture not punitive.

The Castellan’s answer was two syllables — *Understood* — and arrived like a door closing.

**Merit** tried once to clip a recorder to hall hololith until **Valerius** stamped **NON-COMPLIANT** before riot thinned — witness routes only, no throughput dressed as union folk liturgy before injury was attested at molten glass lip.

**Mira** watched from gallery with eyes that counted everything except what she chose not to count — storage, not neutrality — because flutter was hive immune system and immune system was debt ring of teeth would price in air before flare geometries taught mass-as-verdict.

## V. You Will Not Become Cover

**Santo**’s militia block — boys drunk on duty and drama — tried **human shields** because holodramas taught them virtue looked like standing in front of las. Here, virtue looked like obedience to geometry that spared strangers, and geometry did not care about drama. Santo’s jaw set anyway; honor was a drug, and he was young enough to confuse dosage with destiny. He spread his arms as if width could bargain with bolters.

**Alaric** moved through smoke helmed, inevitable — not fast, *inexorable*. Mortals parted not from awe alone but from the animal certainty that mass was about to become law. One controlled blow — palm and sternum physics calibrated the way Drusus calibrated plungers — put militia pride down without snapping the boy’s spine.

Santo hit decking; breath left in a single ugly rush; honor crawled back slower than blood.

— You will not become *cover* — Alaric said — voice not shout, *law*. — You will become *men*.

Lip split; blood thin as confession. Shame hurt worse — always does, in romances where boys wish to be knights before they learn walls.

**Kessler** dragged two kids behind a column and cursed them safe with profanity sanctified by volume control, because sometimes the Emperor’s protection sounds like a sergeant telling you to keep your head down or she will personally end you and explain later.

**Mira** saw Santo fall from the gallery rail and did not run. Running would have made her **kin** visible in the wrong register. She stored the image instead — son, blood, Castellan’s palm — the way she stored everything that might become payroll or leverage or survival, depending on which bell rang next.

Behind a clinic curtain, separated from riot by plasteel and the thin courtesy of a medicae screen, a midwife counted contractions in rhythm that matched a **vox-chant** no station played — pattern older than guild charters, older than Incus’s factory saints. **Drusus** heard it through a bulkhead while his *vox* crackled with calls for cordons and his hands still smelled of iodine and fear.

The rhythm *fit*. That was the obscenity: not that it was alien, but that it **fit** — as if something had tuned human labor and human birth to the same metronome.

His stomach performed a marine’s forbidden thing: **flip**.

## VI. Biologic Harmonic — Triumvirate Only

He logged ***biologic harmonic***. He locked the file with a curse he did not voice — ***Brood-Flutter*** — and stared at his own gauntlets as if they had touched something that left residue ideas could carry. The name was ugly. Ugly was useful. Ugly meant he would not confuse it with **Wolf’s Curse**, with Versibar steam, with the hundred other catastrophes the Chapter already knew how to mourn incorrectly.

On the slate’s margin he wrote, smaller than regulations allowed: *cross-file Pier Nine instruction echo — speculative — Triumvirate only*, because cowardice dressed as prudence was still cowardice, and he was tired of being careful with the wrong things.

When the riot finally coughed itself empty, **Alaric** stood in the union hall’s wreckage beside a toppled lectern carved with old guild vows. Ash wrote gray fingerprints on his greaves; he did not wipe them.

**Kadmos** reported casualties in the flat voice of a man reciting what he wished were someone else’s memory.

**Varro** moved along the walls, fingers tracing gouges where thrown bricks had revealed older layers — paint on paint on propaganda — like strata in a sick earth.

— We bought time — Kadmos muttered.

— Time bought quotas — Alaric replied. — Quotas bought this.

He did not say *hooks not bombs* again; repetition would have turned wisdom into slogan, and slogans were how hives learned to lie back.

The **brood** whispered in human time — soft enough to be mistaken for wind through vents, insistent enough to be mistaken for *progress*. The Chapter — still blind to the full name of its foe — printed posters calling it ***labor unrest***. Administratum loved posters. Administratum loved *simple*. Love stories, the old romances taught, begin in **misrecognition**. Incus was falling in love with its own hunger and calling it **output**.

## VII. Parallel Columns — Triptychs Harder to Beautify

Triptychs were harder to beautify than singles — *declared output* beside *auxilia water* beside *midwife hours slept* — and guild factors raged because triptychs told the truth in three voices at once, which was how you prevented one mouth from choking two kinds of debt.

Alaric rescinded no quotas — rescission would have fed the hive’s other mouth, the one that ate stability — but he ordered **Valerius** to publish parallel columns anyway: *declared output* beside *auxilia water* beside *midwife hours slept*, because triptychs were harder to beautify than singles. Guild factors raged that the Chapter was *moralizing logistics*; Valerius answered with stamps, not arguments, and stamps were how you won the paper war until the night war arrived.

**Kadmos** doubled patrol cadence until brothers joked they were rehearsing ballet; jokes died when a patrol reported two manufactories’ break bells syncing to the second without *vox* coordination — **choir practice** again, wearing work clothes.

**Drusus** visited the listening post at third shift when the flutter climbed and the waveform pulsed like a second heartbeat the hive refused to admit was **shared**. He did not sermonize. He wrote *who conducts* in the margin and sealed it, because some questions were weapons if spoken aloud before you had earned the right to aim them.

**Iria** listened at a vent mouth until her ears rang and still could not split the notes cleanly; she told **Drusus** only:

— The hive is learning to sing in rounds.

Midwife halls had begun counting beside throughput charts — same cadence, different throat — and **Threx**’s birth-rate optimizations held hands with quota chant until incentive spoofing dressed as virtue. Drusus hated the rhyme because rhyme was not oracle yet; rhyme was **data hygiene** warning you the metronome was shared.

She watched his jaw tighten because *rounds* implied structure, and structure implied **instruction** again — the word that had followed them from mud to glass to ash like a blood type.

Rain — piped, recycled, flavored with rust — hissed against Cinder Nine’s hide. Somewhere a quota chant rose for the night shift, the same cadence as the midwife’s counting if you listened without wanting to.

**Varro** extinguished a candle a worker had left burning near a relay — too gently to be cruelty, too firmly to be kindness — and stepped back into ash-light, *Codex* death-skull catching lumen like a moon that had learned to judge.

A foreman tried to merge midwife hours with throughput on one slate until **Valerius** took the stylus and split the column without looking up.

— Stamp twice — Valerius said — or explain to the Castellan why thirst looks like productivity.

The foreman stamped twice. Incus was teaching the whole mesh the same lesson in different dialects.

**Mira**’s eyes followed Varro once, then returned to the floor, carrying whatever they carried without sharing.

**Santo** pressed a rag to his split lip and did not look at his mother, because looking would have broken something neither of them could afford to break yet.

A guild factor — silk under ash, smile polished for tribunals — tried to merge the parallel columns on one slate until Valerius took the stylus with the same gentleness he would later use on Navy liaisons who wanted one throat for two debts.

— Stamp twice — Valerius said — or explain to the Castellan why you wanted thirst to look like productivity.

The factor stamped twice. His smile did not return. Smiles, on Incus, were also quotas — and quotas could be audited.

## VIII. Flutter Continues Whether He Watches

Above them all, the hive’s vents sang their constant note — work, work, work — while something else listened beneath the note and smiled without a mouth.

Drusus stood alone in the listening post one minute longer than duty required, headphones still warm from his skull, waveform still pulsing on the screen like a second heartbeat the hive refused to admit was **shared**. He thought of Threx’s birth-rate charts holding hands with throughput optimizations — incentive spoofing dressed as virtue — and of Varro’s question with no answer worth ink: *who conducts*.

He closed the file. He did not delete it. Deletion would have been comfort, and comfort was a corridor he could not secure.

**Kadmos** walked the listening post corridor once with **Santo** at his shoulder — not mentorship, **inventory** — counting how auxilia bunched when the flutter climbed a half-tone. Bunching was how virtue almost killed the wrong people fast enough to choke; Incus was teaching the Castellan’s artillery mind to read **crowds** as terrain before Exanimus gave the lesson a name.

The flutter continued whether he watched or not.

**Drusus** sealed the Brood-Flutter file behind biometrics one last time that shift and appended *Ring ingress; expect environmental coupling; sequence only* — not prophecy, **debt** accruing interest in theaters that pretended they were unrelated.

**Santo** pressed a rag to his split lip and walked another patrol without looking at **Mira** — witness without applause traveling in both directions now, mother and son learning the same ugly grammar: *useful* the way forges are useful, *honest* the way ledgers are honest when they refuse to beautify grief.

**Varro** walked the gantry where quota chant had tried to rise and did not accuse the foreman who silenced it — accusation required evidence his office could not yet carry without breaking more boys than it saved. He dipped his *Codex* death-skull once — small bow to the dead child — and moved on, because some binds were too young to name and too urgent to skip.

**Drusus** stood over the quota hololith one last time and watched kin-lines pulse beside output integers — inheritance wearing production uniform — until **Iria**’s *Not yet* blinked beside a denial that was not cruelty but **schedule**. Rain on Cinder Nine tried to turn chant into weather; someone silenced it because silence was cheaper than explaining why a child was dead and still counted on the slate.

**Varro** filed the listening-post threshold as proto-rite — seal attempted, not victory — while **Threx**’s Skitarii swabbed chapel gallery under protest and logged *Compliance deferred* beside *Castellan denial*. Appetite deferred was not appetite denied; the crucible was learning to file hunger without calling it holy.

**Valerius** stamped the listening-post roster twice — Marine lane beside mortal lane — and watched a junior clerk try to merge them until **Kadmos**’s shadow fell across the slate without a word spoken. The clerk stamped twice. Incus was teaching the mesh the same lesson in different dialects: one throat for two debts was how convoys choked.

**Alaric** stood at Cinder Nine lip one minute after quota chant silenced and did not remove his helm — continuity, not spectacle — while kin-lines pulsed beside output integers and inheritance wore production uniform like a skin the Chapter could not cut without cutting children who had not yet chosen sides.
