## I. Ash Under Armor — Void Scrubbed Memory

Incus’s ash clung in greaves long after translation — packed into joint seals, worked into the creases of ceramite until it became a second pigment — then **void** scrubbed it away and replaced it with cold that laughed at memory. Cold did not negotiate; it simply removed the story of warmth until your body believed warmth had been a myth told by traitorous nerves.

The Chapter did not leave the hive behind so much as **carry** it under armor: **Brood-Flutter** graphs locked behind biometrics; **Santo**’s split lip a small red argument about boys and honor; **Mira**’s watching eyes a weight no crate manifest could list — cargo heavier than supplies — and the quota chant that still matched midwife counting if you listened without wanting to.

The **backward braid** was ugly: hooks had bought roofs; quotas had bought riots; riots had bought ***Brood-Flutter***; now void asked whether harmonics could **couple** across theaters the way Silica Field had coupled with quartz scream — same appetite wearing different uniforms.

Beyond the burn the new theater waited with the patience of something that had already eaten once and learned patience was cheaper than hunger.

## II. Logus Ring — Mouth That Chewed

The **Logus Ring** was a breaker-yard orbiting nothing worth naming aloud. **Kilometers** of hulls stripped and stacked until they resembled teeth worshipping void curved inward like a mouth that had swallowed fleets and **chewed** until only ribs remained — an anatomy lesson taught without textbooks, scale imposed on the eye until the eye stopped trusting distance.

Scrapper clans called it **Sanctum** when drunk and **Maw** when sober; they signed contracts in blood-finger and wax like penitents signing indulgences, then learned signatures meant **pressgang** and bodies reclassified as *auxilia voidcrew* because the Administratum loved verbs that turned people into **inventory**. The Ring did not care what you called it. The Ring chewed.

Pressgangs worked in **cycles** — recruit, bleed, replace — because void did not forgive empty bunks the way mud forgave empty boots. Scrappers signed for *twelve-week salvage apprenticeships* and learned on day four that *week* was a translation error between Gothic and hunger. Administratum factors stamped *auxilia voidcrew* on throats that still thought themselves free citizens of nowhere important.

**Valerius** copied those stamps into a private column labeled *debt transfer* until the column grew tall enough to shame a Navy liaison into admitting, off-record, that the Ring was not a yard but a **mouth** that ate paperwork and called the meal *efficiency*.

One scrapper — **Jorik**, throat stamped *auxilia voidcrew* in ink that had bled through skin — signed for twelve weeks and learned on day four that *week* was hunger’s translation error. He hammered texture into a cable sheath while whispering a work-song that rhymed, by accident, with quota chant from a hive he had never seen. **Drusus**, passing on a mag-boot patrol, logged the rhyme as *environmental coupling — unverified* and hated that his stylus wanted to write *choir* anyway.

Jorik did not know he had been filed. Filing was how empires loved the powerless: quietly, completely, without requiring gratitude.

Translation from Incus burn to Ring approach took long enough for *vox* to thin and for men to remember that *sky* was a word invented by planets. **Alaric** stood the strike cruiser’s oculus gallery once, watching the yard resolve from a glint to a jaw, and did not speak. Speech would have invited officers to mistake awe for fear. What he felt was not fear exactly; it was **scale** — the human mind’s old injury when it meets something too large to story-tell without lying.

Behind him, **Kadmos** muttered a range solution under his breath, not for anyone’s ears, habit of a man who turned discomfort into numbers because numbers could be held.

## III. Choir Without Saying Choir

The strike cruiser’s auspex painted the yard as **teeth** and then as **targets** — friendly wrecks tagged wrong until techs learned to distrust *friendly*, because friendly hulls could hide listening posts the same way Sallow Morrow’s gallery had hidden paired boots.

**Drusus** watched particulate scatter patterns climb in ugly sympathy with old **Brood-Flutter** harmonics and forced himself not to say *choir* aloud; *choir* was a word that turned competent sailors into mystics, and mystics mis-torqued seals. He logged the sympathy as *environmental coupling — unverified* and hated that *unverified* was the same cloak cowardice wore when it dressed as prudence.

**Kessler** — NCO patience burned down to a wick — found herself teaching boys who had never known sky except as rumor how to **breathe** in rebreather cadence without panic, because panic void-killed faster than las and faster than shame. She made them run the cycle until their throats learned obedience: inhale two, hold one, exhale two, *again*, until the mask stopped tasting like suffocation and started tasting like routine.

— Again — she said — and they hated her until hate became **habit**, and habit became *breathing*.

She did not tell them she still heard quota chants in her sleep, or that Incus had taught her to fear rhythms that matched without permission. She told them only what void required: *small motions, small panic, small selves — until the bulkhead says otherwise.*

Chaos spoke here in **wreck-rites** — not sermons in choirs, but grease-paint theology: plasma couplings daubed with engine oil like unguents; prayers etched into reactor shrouds until machine spirits wheezed **heresy** and obedience in the same cough; laughter that rang hollow as **tanks** because laughter was how humans kept their throats open when their minds wanted to close.

## IV. Varro — Burn What Listens Back

**Varro** walked scaffolds above the cult’s handiwork, *Codex* death-skull helm a fixed witness above grease and oath-marks, censers cold enough to dare anyone to mistake him for spectacle. He did not burn every shrine — *not yet* — because fire makes martyrs and martyrs make **routes**, and routes were what wreck-priests wanted most. He burned only what he could justify to that same bone-mask: idols that had learned to **listen** back — little listening boxes wired into scrap altars, auspex crumbs fed to fetishes until superstition wore cog-teeth.

When the flame took, scrappers cheered and Varro did not cheer with them. He watched smoke climb as if smoke were another congregation learning ascension.

A wreck-priest tried to **sell** Varro a *blessed auspex* wired to a child’s toy speaker — cheap miracle, expensive lie — and learned, without sermon, that the *Codex* skull could imply *no* with a tilt alone. The priest retreated into a rib-bay where shadows stacked like unpaid debts.

**Drusus** followed only far enough to take air samples — **swabs only**, the old treaty with Mechanicus hunger — and noted hydrocarbons consistent with *intentional* lubricant trails, not accident.

— Someone teaches routes here — he told Alaric on a channel too narrow for admiralties. — Not xenos proof. **Curriculum** proof.

Alaric answered with silence that meant *file it*, because filing was how you kept curriculum from becoming **dogma** before evidence paid tuition.

**Kessler** posted yard cordon with backs to backs — shoot the ground first — because wreck-rites were grease-paint theology and theology that listened back was debt war-by-ink would spend when Mechanicus factors arrived pretty as creditors at a deathbed.

## V. War by Ink — Truth Oxidizes

Mechanicus factors arrived on tenders pretty as creditors at a deathbed, hulls buffed until poverty looked like ceremony. They waved salvage charters stamped with cogs until paper looked like **law** and law looked like permission to strip a corpse while the family still wept in the next room.

**Kadmos** interdicted with boarding parties and the tired patience of a man denying cousins at a funeral — mag-boots kissing void-black plating, *vox*-channels polite as knives. In one boarding tube, a void-born serf vomited anyway; **Kessler** caught his shoulder and shoved him back into line with a profanity that doubled as instruction — *aim it sideways, you’re not a fountain* — because disgust was easier to manage than panic, and panic killed tubes faster than cultists.

On the tender’s bridge, servitors watched with chrome eyes that pretended not to judge. Kadmos judged anyway.

— **Profit** is not **truth** — he told a magos emissary who wore augmetics like jewelry.

The emissary smiled with lips that had forgotten how *not* to.

— Truth oxidizes — he answered, delighted by his own cleverness.

**Threx** — inevitable as rust — hovered in analysis mode on a secondary bridge feed, optics drinking hull geometry, voice stripped to the same appetite he had brought to Incus throughput. Compliance with charter maximized recovery timelines, he offered, as if timelines were mercy.

On the command deck **Alaric** let the argument play — **war by ink** — because sometimes ink bought minutes bolts could not, and minutes in a yard like this were measured in throats.

**Valerius** slid tallies under the Castellan’s gauntlet without drama: *days of air*, *hours of corridor*, *lies quartermasters tell so men sleep*. The friction partner’s language of stamps and yield ground against the Chapter’s language of held corridors and living bodies, two kinds of mathematics trying to share one mouth.

A Navy liaison — young, ambitious, still believing void was a career ladder — tried to merge the columns on one slate until Valerius took the stylus from his hand with the gentleness of a man disarming a child holding a grenade.

— Marines and mortals do not share a throat — Valerius said. — Stamp twice or explain to the Castellan why you wanted them to choke together.

The liaison stamped twice. His face learned something that would save lives later, when *later* arrived as a hull scream and a seam becoming a mouth.

**Alaric** watched from the Castellan’s perch without comment — hinge-voice saved for geometry, not pedagogy — while **Kadmos** posted rear fire solutions that assumed mortals would bunch where mortals always bunched: at airlocks, at water points, at any door that promised *inside* was still a word with meaning. The Ring taught that *inside* was negotiable. Negotiation was why Marines existed.

## VI. Teeth Inside Teeth — Micro-Charges

Micro-charges did not announce themselves with speeches. They spoke in a language too quiet for drama: a cough, a shiver, a seam deciding it had never been a seam, only a suggestion — then the seam became a mouth, and mouths were what the Ring had been teaching since Incus ash still lived in seal joints.

The blade that broke comedy was small — **micro-charges**, a chain meant to **shear** a troop carrier along a weld seam like cutting bread — because comedy in void war is the belief that armor makes you whole.

Marines inside became suddenly *biblical*: men in a split world, gravity arguing with faith, faith arguing with training, training doing what it could while the universe pretended physics was a moral failing. Someone laughed — wrong sound, too high — and someone else cursed him silent because laughter in a splitting hull was a door opening onto the void’s favorite joke: *you thought you were inside something*.

Red lamps spun; emergency foam coughed from ports and hardened into useless sculpture; a brother’s gauntlet tore through a rent and kissed vacuum for a heartbeat before seals slammed and the hand came back frostbitten and furious. Decompression screamed through corridors — a sound too human to be called wind — and the Batavi moved in tilted geometry, bulkheads become floors, floors become spite, handholds become theology.

They emerged from smoke and spinning lamps like saints climbing from a **sunk ship**, except saints rarely carried bolters and swear-words etched into their teeth.

Inside the torn seam, **Kadmos** found micro-charges nested like **teeth inside teeth** — not one betrayal line, a *pattern* suggesting someone had rehearsed hull failure the way Incus had rehearsed break bells. He photographed with his helm archive because photography was harder to edit than memory, then sealed the file under **Triumvirate** biometrics before Threx’s optics could treat images as salvage previews.

— If this was cult — he subvocalized to Drusus — it was cult with **blueprints**.

— If this was Mechanicus — Drusus began — and stopped, because finishing the sentence would have been a second mouth opening in a yard already crowded with mouths.

**Varro**’s *Codex* death-skull caught dying station light and turned children of scrappers into weepers who did not know why — only that *bone* had become a moon and moons meant judgment in stories older than the Ring.

The brother whose gauntlet kissed vacuum came to **Drusus** with frostbite painted in angry lace across knuckles. He did not ask for sympathy. He asked whether the hand would still **count** triggers clean.

— Count first — Drusus said — feel later.

That was Apothecarion romance: cruelty accurate enough to be kindness. The brother nodded once and returned to the torn seam to photograph teeth inside teeth, because some men learned to file horror before they learned to mourn it.

**Kessler** ran another breathing cycle with auxilia who had vomited in the boarding tube and were now ashamed of shame — the worst compound interest void collected.

— Again — she said — and meant *until your body believes you are still inside something*.

**Alaric** passed the torn seam without stopping — hinge duty, mass reserved for geometry — but his shadow fell across the brother photographing teeth inside teeth long enough for the man to understand: being seen was not **spectacle**; it was receipt. Receipt was how walls learned to hold without needing speeches.

## VII. Days — Romance’s Honest Love Poem

Void taught a second syllabus after teeth: **days of air** were not calendar days but hunger’s translation of *week* — Jorik had learned on day four, throat stamped *auxilia voidcrew*, that contracts were receipts the Ring cashed in breath. **Valerius** copied debt-transfer stamps until the column grew tall enough to shame Navy liaisons into admitting the yard was a **mouth** that ate paperwork and called the meal *efficiency*.

Scrapper children learned to sleep in corridors where ribs stacked like hymnals and vacuum kissed seams without warning. **Varro** did not burn their oil shrines yet. He noted coordinates for the day fire would be justified — seal law, not sentiment — because even small human stupidities could become **folk liturgy** if harmonics learned their lyrics.

Alaric did not speak encouragement; encouragement would have wasted breath that could be spent clearing. He **cleared** until clearing became place: a pocket of held vacuum, a moment where the Ring’s mouth **paused** mid-bite because something harder than appetite had lodged in its teeth.

After, the supply cord stretched until honest quartermasters admitted *we have **days*** — and in void war, days are the romance’s honest love poem: **short**, sincere, sufficient to keep marching until the next lie.

One quartermaster — face gray with fatigue, not ash — spread fingers on a tally slate as if touching the numbers could warm them.

— If we push another boarding — he said to Valerius, not to Alaric, because mortals learn which doors are safer — we eat reserves before we reach Ignis mesh.

Valerius did not promise comfort. He promised **paper**: signatures, stamps, a lie thin enough to march on. The irreversible read was plain in the tallies anyway: the yard had teeth, and teeth did not negotiate. Cult routes were broken or bought; Mechanicus charters were *adjusted* with signatures that would cost someone a career later, when later arrived.

**Threx** logged spoilage and opportunity in the same breath because Mechanicus grief wore numbers when it could not wear shame. Alaric listened to Threx’s channel hiss static that wanted to be triumph and settled for being a receipt — same sound as Pier Nine, different theater, same appetite dressed as procedure.

**Valerius** negotiated air the way chaplains negotiated souls — without promising heaven, only **sequence**: first pressure patches, first water, first wounded, first the living secured so tomorrow could not pretend yesterday had been rumor. When a Mechanicus factor tried to slide *sanctified recovery* language beside a casualty column, Valerius filed it parallel under *Mechanicus asserts salvage continuity* and forced initials, the same trick he had learned would keep blood off wax for another week — except wax did not exist here; only vacuum, which signed nothing and took everything.

In romances, rings of teeth mark the threshold where heroes learn the world is also a **mouth**. The Batavi learned — and filed the lesson beside others — **Unbent** meaning not *unharmed*, but *still clamped jaw when needed*.

When he whispered *not us* to a partner who had not asked, the partner thought it superstition. After the second mirror spiral, it would be **procedure**.
