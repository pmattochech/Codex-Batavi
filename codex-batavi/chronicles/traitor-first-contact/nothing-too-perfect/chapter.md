# [CHRONICLE] Nothing Too Perfect

**Where:** From contested **spire-stack** approaches into **Castra-Vetera** ministry / hab mesh (*Secunda* seat)  
**Who:** Alpha Legion cell(s) — names are lies (**Alpharius**); ivory **Severin Holt** (*Quinta* Silence — the scope lock); Legatus **Henric Kessler** + **II** *Secunda*; dual-path **Valens Ritter** (Silence + Shadows)

**Intel premise:** Hydra already carries Batavi scars (Ten Vexillas temper-split, Armin/*Quinta*). This war is not open slaughter — **logs, disguise, sleeper agents, invisible hunters**.

He was Alpharius.

That was the first truth he offered the dark after the ivory pauldron locked his scope, and it was a lie the way all useful truths are lies. The Hydra did not need a personal name to flee a gantry. He needed corridors that believed he was maintenance, a face that had always worked this shift, a packet already bleeding toward traitor margins.

Behind him the spire-stack still ticked with grey countdown — Quinta finishing World Eaters into silence. Ahead, Castra-Vetera’s ministry mesh opened like a throat that preferred paperwork to blood. Perfect. Alpha Legion loved throats that preferred paperwork.

— I am Alpharius — he told the first sleeper cell on a dead channel, because the joke was doctrine. — Continue the inserts. The greys have a pale shoulder that sees. Price that.

Elsewhere in the same war — not later, not earlier, the same war — Severin Holt of Quinta walked the stack’s cold gantries with an ivory right pauldron and a pattern already burning behind orange lenses.

He had not swept lucky. He had read a baseline: empty air that was too empty, a gantry that refused the small noises gantries make when men exist on them, a scope-glint that tried not to be a scope-glint. Hydra invisibility was a costume. Costumes had seams.

— Contact retained — Severin voxed on Silence lattice, clipped as a warrant. — Scope-hunter. Quinta ivory. Trail into Vetera mesh. Do not announce. Do not sermon. Hunt.

He moved the way ivory moved when the enemy wanted ghosts: not jump-pack theater, not saw-mass. Carbine compact. Auspex as confirmation, never as faith. Eyes on where other eyes went.

In the Central Bastion’s deposition vaults, Henric Kessler did not hunt with a scope.

He hunted with lists.

*Secunda*’s Legatus sat in artificer grey and bronze faceplate, crimson-and-white crest still as a magistrate’s warning, Deathwatch honor block silver-black on the right — a jurist who treated stray bolts as lawsuits against the Emperor. Before him: shift rosters, tithe-adjacent courier tickets, Arbites co-signed work bills, three sleepers who had been too helpful during the spire relief corridor’s civilian evacuation window.

— These three — Kessler said, voice precise Gothic, Lex already in the grammar — present as model citizens. Model citizens do not exist at this density without a director. Bring them. Interrogatory under joint warrant. No theater. No broken jaws before the file speaks.

*Secunda* brothers moved like assigned-target packets. Stalker barrels. Measured angles. Civilians in arc meant trigger fingers bound by clause, not mercy theater — mercy was a side effect of not inventing lawsuits.

The first sleeper smiled the correct smile.

Kessler watched the smile the way Silence watched gait.

— State your shift code — he said.

The sleeper stated it perfectly.

— State it again — Kessler said — as if you were tired.

The second recitation was still perfect. That was the fault. Tired men smear digits. Hydra men rehearse.

— Too perfect — an ivory pauldron at Kessler’s shoulder murmured, not Branimir’s voice, local Silence on *Secunda* leash. — Cut.

Restraints. Custody. Not a dark alley execution — a drag toward *Lex* that would consume publicly if the mesh allowed. Kessler filed the dissent that was not dissent: collateral zero; sleeper one; pattern debt opening.

— Next name on the insert list — Kessler ordered. — And pull every courier ticket that shares ink-chemistry with his badge. Faults leave residue. Residue is a map.

The one who called himself Alpharius — the scope one — wore a dock-inspector’s gait through a lower transit spine and almost believed it. Almost was death. He corrected a limp he did not have, then corrected the correction, and felt the old Hydra nausea: perfection stacking until it sang.

Severin Holt was already in the spine’s noise, ivory against grey, reading the song.

Not close. Not revealed. Parallel. The same way the scope had been parallel until it wasn’t.

— You looked at me — Severin’s vox was private, a needle into a channel the Hydra thought dead. — From empty air. Continue walking. I am pricing your exits.

The Alpharius almost answered with a name. He used the only one that was allowed.

— I am Alpharius.

— That is a lie — Severin said. — Keep it. Lies have gait too.

In the ducts above a ministry tram cut, another Alpharius — also Alpharius, also a lie — moved in true invisible: photo-absorbent, no pride-flare, Headhunter patience. He was not the scope-filer. He was the knife behind the knife, inserted to kill ivory if ivory came hunting logs.

He did not expect the brother with **red hands**.

Valens Ritter came on black right pauldron for this band — Shadows geometry under Silence price — stalker carbine short enough for ship-spine and hab tunnels, lightning claws seated flush until the timeline demanded they not be. Dual-path. Rare. The mesh folklore called him Silent Shadow when it wanted a story; the file called him a tool that cleared boards when only excess cleared them in time.

Ritter did not relish. He closed.

The duct-Alpharius offered a perfect maintenance cough. Ritter read rehearsal the way ivory read gait — predator timing under a maintenance mask. Claws deployed. Time-to-silence. The aftermath would offend Shadows peers who preferred deniable minimal signature. *Strategium* had asked for board-clear anyway.

One Hydra node ended without a speech.

Ritter voxed ivory-first, as dual-path law required on mixed lattices:

— Invisible band culled. Continue pricing. I rotate as needed.

Severin did not congratulate. Ivory did not congratulate. He adjusted the map: scope-Alpharius still live; sleeper list under Kessler; duct knife gone.

Kessler’s second interrogatory was a woman who had never been a woman in the way the badge claimed — or had been, once, before the Legion taught her to be a better version of herself. She recited ministry procedure with bronze-clean edges. Kessler set his long power spear across the table not as threat theater but as a measured line: the point rested where a guilty hand would eventually twitch.

— You will request counsel — Kessler said. — You will receive it. You will also answer which insert code salted the relief corridor’s volunteer slate. If you refuse, the Arbites will still eat. If you lie perfectly, my ivory will cut the lie. Choose the less stupid path.

She chose perfection again.

Silence cut it. Pattern debt. Custody. Kessler’s slate gained another fault-line that pointed, like all Hydra fault-lines, in three directions at once.

The scope-Alpharius tried to become a dead man in a crowd — the oldest trick. Severin hunted him through logs first: which face had entered the transit spine after the gantry; which aquila-badge had never sweat; which courier had left a packet for traitor margins with a seal too clean for wartime grease.

Kessler found the packet’s twin in a *Secunda* vault inbox labeled routine.

— Routine is a disguise — Kessler said to the vault air. — Open every routine that arrived in the same minute-window. I want the wrong ones.

*Secunda* measured fire did not mean slow minds. It meant the right names died or knelt in the right order while civilians kept walking. Stalker shots took two more inserts in a hab queue without mass-reactive sprawl. Grav support hummed once where a bridge wanted to panic and was not allowed to.

Dual war. Same minute.

Above, Hydra tried to hunt the hunters: a third Alpharius in Arbites-adjacent black, perfect badge, perfect sneer at wolf-echo greys. Ritter met him in a service throat wearing ivory for the approach — Silence-first — then black for the finish when the disguise broke into a knife. Red gauntlets. Flush claws. No appetite. Board clear.

— How many heads — the dying lie asked, curious despite the end.

Ritter’s answer was scarce as Castellan economy:

— Enough.

Severin cornered the scope-Alpharius where the ministry mesh met a rain-slick observation shelf — not a duel ground, a filing error in architecture. Ivory pauldron. Compact carbine. The Hydra had a needle-pistol and the old question burning hotter than plasma:

— How did you see me?

Severin Holt did not give him a sermon. He gave him doctrine the way a cuff gives a wrist its future:

— Nothing is too perfect. Empty air has a pattern. You erased too much.

The Alpharius laughed under a face that was everyone’s and no one’s.

— I am Alpharius.

— You are custody — Severin said, and the restraints bit with Arbites-ratified honesty. — The law will eat. Your packet already left. We will price that too.

Far below, Kessler closed an interrogatory slate with a jurist’s dry satisfaction — not joy, compliance. Sleepers named inserts. Inserts named dead drops. Dead drops named the observation shelf. *Secunda* and Silence had fought the same war as the Hydra: one side through disguise and invisible knives, the other through logs, gait, and measured fire.

On a sealed channel that would never admit a personal name, a remaining Hydra voice told the margins:

Cohors Batavorum — *Secunda* + ivory shoulders (pattern hunters; “nothing too perfect”).  
File-war Legatus: bronze face, Watch honor block, spear-line — **Henric Kessler** / **Secunda** (self-signed on warrant pict).  
Scope-lock ivory: **Severin Holt** (Quinta-marked lattice).  
Dual-path: ivory/black rotator, red hands, claws — culled two invisibles.  
Hydra names: irrelevant. All Alpharius. All lies.  
Assessment: do not only price their slaughter slices. Price their paperwork. It bites.

Severin marched his custody toward *Colmeia Justitia* light. Kessler revoked a range for a *Secunda* brother who had almost fired wide near a civilian arc — paperwork as anger. Ritter cleaned ichor from red gauntlets and filed Silence-first because the lattice said so.

The war did not end. Hydras do not end.

But for one night in Castra-Vetera, the invisible learned that empty air could be read — and the greys learned again that some enemies only die properly when the file and the duct close on the same tick.
