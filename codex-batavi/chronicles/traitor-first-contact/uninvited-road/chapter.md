Magister Vhorak of the Ashen Spiral had already decided the vault would open like a throat.

Otho knew that before the first Rubricae boot rang on the annex deck. He knew it the way he knew furnace bleed: not as prophecy, as pattern. The chart-house annex sat under ferrocrete and purity seals on the Witness Ford approach — not a cathedral, not a dream. A place where bonded Navigators kept dead roads in metal. The Immaterium above it was already wrong in a familiar way: too much story pressed into too little air.

Haruspices at Otho’s flanks did not gasp. They listened.

Passive radar. Coefficients. The rite’s hunger plotted as spike and resonance while Nullity’s cobalt markers moved into the chamber’s angles like men setting braces under a failing roof. Thin grey line mass held the outer approaches without ceremony — present, useful, not the point. The point was the cradle, the shard, and the weather Vhorak was trying to make holy.

The thing in the sealed cradle was coffin-dark, etched with routes that should not exist for the uninvited. To the Ashen Spiral it was a path-key. To Otho it was a receipt that killed people who treated geography like a hymn.

— Lift it — Vhorak told a living disciple, staff-light crawling Prospero-memory into the air. — The rite needs only the shard and silence from the corpse-Emperor’s dogs.

Silence did not arrive.

Nullity did.

Cobalt brothers planted hazard discipline into the vault — witch-mark interdiction, cognitive beacons, blank-adjacent pressure that made panic turn back on itself. Rubricae advanced in dustless perfection and found their weather thinning. A disciple’s syllable, meant to marry shard to Ribbon, found less air and died as unfinished sound. The annex did not become a temple. It became a kill-box that hated narrative.

Vhorak tasted the glove on his candle and smiled behind the horned grille — smile as insult.

— Dreamless Throne-dogs — he voxed to the Ashen Spiral. — Mute cattle. Dead Legion scrap that forgot how to sing. Kill the gelded cobalt. Take the key.

Otho opened the Grid.

Where the Ashen Spiral poured myth into the annex, his mind imposed geometry: fluid matrices, anomaly classes, corrupted code-strings where a lesser psyker would have seen gods. He did not shut limbic fire with faith-void theater. He relabeled input until the beast under every Batavi skull was under-fed on affect. Daemons and whispers — what few dared the Ford’s edge — became furnace telemetry: spikes, bleed, resonance bands. The aesthetic of horror was noise.

A Haruspex murmured a class-code. Two Nullity brothers shifted three degrees and cut a Rubricae joint as if the future had already been tallied. Another Haruspex fed Otho the rite’s friction variable: Vhorak was not only stealing. He was trying to *name* a road through Tenebrarum weather that preferred corpses to passengers.

— You cannot meter Prospero — Vhorak snarled across the vault. — That shard is a road. I will name it. I will walk it. Your Emperor’s silence ends here.

Otho adjusted Blindgate the way a tech-wright adjusts a failing seal — occlusion loops, command-code mantras, refusal to grant the attacker narrative purchase. The working Vhorak hurled to unmake will slid off like oil off glass and left the Magister with rage instead of triumph. Around them Nullity closed another lane. Grey line at the outer doors — mentioned only as mass — kept the theft from becoming a Hab chase. The focus stayed cobalt and cipher-helm: Nullity’s procedure, Haruspices’ quiet radar, Otho’s Grid like a cold dam.

— Name yourselves — Vhorak demanded, because nameless enemies offended a Magister’s need to file conquest.

Otho’s voice came through the cipher-grille flat, almost liturgical in its refusal of mysticism.

— No.

A Nullity sergeant put a disciple down mid-glyph. The cradle’s seals held. The shard remained ugly, logistical, unimpressed by Prospero’s ghosts. Vhorak understood, with first-contact ugliness, that these were not merely dreamless dogs. They were a Chapter that treated the Immaterium as an ocean of entropy to be metered — and his rite as a leak.

He reached for the shard anyway. Pride refuses audits.

Otho did not leap to duel like a Champion. He pointed — a small motion — and Nullity collapsed the last clean angle between Magister and cradle. Haruspices sang no song; they simply made the Ribbon’s opportunistic whispers thin around the theft. Vhorak’s staff met cobalt procedure and found that procedure could hate.

— That is not a god’s key — Otho said, sparse, clinical, bolt-shells and blank-pressure turning the annex into failed scripture. — It is a road that kills the uninvited. You will not take it. You will not name it. You will not dream it into a door.

The Ashen Spiral broke the way sorcerous certainty breaks: not all at once, but as each Rubricae lost the weather that made them feel inevitable. Living disciples dragged what still bled toward translation-scream smoke. Vhorak disengaged because Magisters who want a later rite do not die on the first unnamed Grid.

On the climb out he spat into the cabal net — not labels, insults, the kind of names a wounded pride files so tomorrow’s hatred has a handle:

— Mute cattle.  
— Abacus-gelded dogs.  
— Cobalt eunuchs who meter what they cannot sing.  
— Ledger-worms. Flat-souled scrap. Throne’s castrati.

Then the warning, uglier for being useful:

Do not steal roads from them.  
Steal from Chapters that still believe the warp is a story.  
These ones will classify your gods until your gods starve.

Behind him, in the annex, Otho did not celebrate. Celebration was affect the Grid did not need. He had Haruspices re-tag the anomaly weather until it went quiet. Nullity checked the cradle seals with the boredom of men who treated survival as maintenance. The Chief Librarian looked once at the coffin-dark shard — Witness Ford’s dead handwriting in metal — and filed the only victory that mattered.

The road remained a receipt.  
The rite remained a leak that had been closed.  
And the Ashen Spiral left with insults instead of a Chapter name — which, to Otho, was acceptable output.

They could call his brothers cattle and eunuchs and worms.  
As long as they did not take the Ford’s road home to dream it into murder.
