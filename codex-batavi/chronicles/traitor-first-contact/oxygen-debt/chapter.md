Lord Gholrax the Kind Ledger loved silos.

They were honest architecture: grain, chem, pressure, the slow faith of empires that believed hunger could be scheduled. On Khan-Bator’s agri-industrial stack the air already lied — fertilizer sweet, solvent sharp, oxygen rich enough to make fire remember shortcuts. A cultist had done Grandfather’s smallest mercy before the grey pattern-hunters caught him: a ritual in a maintenance throat, a door that should not open, a throat that did.

The portal unfurled like a boil deciding to be a mouth.

Plague Marines stepped through with the patience of weather. Beasts of Nurgle heaved after them — joyful, gelatinous, flies writing hymns in wet air. Death Guard sorcerers lifted rusted staves and began the soft song that turned lungs into gardens. Gholrax came last, Kind Ledger open in his mind: every world a column, every cough a tithe.

— Easy — he told his sons, voice like damp bells. — These greys are known now. Cage. Storm. Joyless knives. Editors who will not clap. Offer them rest. They work too hard.

Scar-slate said Cohors Batavorum. Versatile. Ugly. Off-mesh hunters. Gholrax filed them as patients who had not yet accepted the diagnosis.

The first grey answer was not a sermon.

It was a hatch that refused to open on plague-tempo, then opened on Batavi tempo: *Tertia* chem-sealed plate, Errant collars, flash-skirts, flamer and melta geometry already pricing the oxygen. Theron Brach’s lattice did not debate Grandfather. It treated the portal as a **lying-air event** — ambient O₂ tick audible in helm habits, subordinates waiting for the nod before sealed throats became fire lanes.

A Beast of Nurgle rolled into a grain-corridor laughing.

Promethium remembered every shortcut the cultist had taken. Controlled burn ate joy without poetry. The Beast’s laughter became fuel math. Plague Marines advanced through the char and found the fire was not hatred — it was **liability closure**.

— Rude — a Plague Marine chuckled, delighted. — They sterilize the gift.

Gholrax’s sorcerers widened the song. Flies thickened. Mortals in the lower silos began the soft ending — and then cobalt pauldrons cut the psychic weather like a blank invoice. **Nullity** did not argue theology. They made the plague-song find less purchase, forced Beasts to be meat and mass instead of parable, dragged warp-bright edges toward dull. A plague-sorcerer’s third blessing guttered mid-syllable as if the air had filed it under *denied*.

From the high catwalks and inverted approaches, another grey temper arrived: denser stride, gravitic boot debt, storm-shield mounts, Roman patience. **Orin Valestrand**’s *Quarta* did not chase the portal. They **held the crest** of the silo stack — meters and hours, seal integrity, withheld fire until mass spent itself on grav-locked angles. Inevitability met a wall that priced ankles and rotation tables. Plague tide climbed and found the climb had become debt.

Gholrax felt the first true irritation of the night: not pain — **accounting**.

— They refuse kindness — he murmured. — Very well. Be kinder.

Plague Marines pushed. Blight grenades. Endurance that should have shamed any Chapter into despair. Greys died — Batavi were not immortal — and still the lattice did not collapse into screaming purity. Bodies were dragged, hatches sealed, burns signed, Nullity rotated, Quarta held, Tertia burned what could not be held. No encore for sensation. No bargain for the cellar priest. Just work.

In a chem-throat slick with blessing and solvent, Gholrax met the thermal Legatus under orange lenses and a crest that refused to glitter. Flamers spoke. Type Ignis geometry — corrosive fault, chem-sealed haft — treated Nurgle’s generosity as a containment problem. Gholrax offered the soft smile his Legion wore like a gift.

Brach did not match it.

When he spoke, it was loud enough for a refinery and cold enough for a det-charge:

— Your gift is oxygen debt. We collect.

No pantheon. No Ancestor speech for the enemy’s comfort. One line, then fire that remembered shortcuts.

A Beast tried to embrace a Quarta shield-line and found grav-lock and melta making embrace into mass times acceleration. A plague-sorcerer forced a garden through a Nullity lane and watched the garden become incomplete — flies falling as if bored of miracle. Plague Marines learned what Iron Warriors and World Eaters had filed in other words: these greys would spend brothers, not meaning.

Gholrax laughed damply when his Kind Ledger showed red.

— Extract — he ordered at last, still fond, still wounded. — Leave them the silo. Leave them the smell. They will cough later. Everyone coughs later.

They pulled back through the portal’s shrinking mercy. The cultist was already a stain. The stack still burned in signed windows. Cobalt and chem-grey and grav-grey held the throat until the boil decided to be a scar instead of a mouth.

On the far side of rot, Lord Gholrax the Kind Ledger filed for the XIV and for any cousin who still believed Castra Vetera was only a wall that stayed home:

Cohors Batavorum — confirmed.  
Silo breach / Khan-Bator grammar. Cult door opened first; greys answered second.  
Tempers present: chem-thermal (*Tertia* / Brach — “oxygen debt”); grav-static hold (*Quarta*); cobalt blanking (Nullity) vs sorcerers / Beasts.  
They do not accept kindness. They price plague as liability. Fire without rapture. Hold without despair theater.  
Beasts burned to math. Song thinned.  
Scar-names: oxygen debtors / seal-flame dogs / kindless wall.  
Assessment: Grandfather’s soft toll still applies — later. Tonight they collected the invoice first. Do not assume their mesh only fights clean wars. They fight dirty air as doctrine.

Somewhere in the stack, Theron Brach waited for an auspex tick before the next hatch.

Orin Valestrand tapped heel seals twice on deck.

Nullity rotated to the next incomplete miracle.

The silos remained Imperial — scarred, stinking, held.
