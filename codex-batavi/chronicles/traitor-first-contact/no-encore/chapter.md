Lord Cacophonist Vael Syrinx had brought a song the glass should have loved.

Vitreus rose ahead in lens-forge glare and photonic galleries — light broken into industrious beauty, the kind of place Emperor’s Children invaded when they wanted the universe to admit it still had an aesthetic. Noise Marines opened the approach with sonic ecstasy that peeled paint from saint-murals and made mortal stevedores weep blood from the ears. The warband’s scar-slate already named Cohors Batavorum: cage-dogs, storm-slaughter, joyless flayers, ivory pattern-hunters. Versatile greys. Ugly greys. Syrinx had laughed at the filing. Ugliness was a spice. Versatility was a stage.

— Make them flinch — he told the file, voice layered through vox-grilles like perfume through a knife. — Make them clap. If they will not clap, make them scream. Same religion.

Lirion the Gilded Cut walked at the song’s edge, perfect as a theorem: blade angles rehearsed until boredom itself should have been impossible. He wanted a rival who understood beauty. He would accept a rival who at least watched.

What met them first was neither.

Gunmetal pauldrons. Storm shields and combat shields locked into a geometry that had no interest in being seen. Stasis Cohort — though Syrinx did not gift them that name — held the gallery throat the way a bulkhead holds pressure: assigned lanes, testudo patience, bolter packets that treated Noise as weather. No sway to the bass. No flinch to the scream-harmonics. No chase when a Noise Marine flourished for an encore that never came.

Curiosity lasted one beautiful minute.

Then boredom arrived like an insult.

— Again — Syrinx ordered, irritated at having to order what sensation should have forced. — Louder. Newer. Hurt them into attention.

The song widened. Flesh-vibrated. A lens-array spiderwebbed. Mortals in the far stacks died in pretty shapes. The shield-wall did not applaud. Brothers fell from grey fire without soundtrack. Bodies hit deck with the rude honesty of weight. Lirion cut a gap that should have become a duel and found only another shield angle closing as if his perfection were a draft to be sealed.

— They are deaf — a Noise Marine snarled, almost pleading.

— They are rude — Lirion corrected, gilded edge trembling with unused genius. — Look at me.

No one looked.

Irritation climbed the warband’s spine. Emperor’s Children could endure hatred. Hatred was attention. This was worse: **procedure**. The greys killed as if the Noise were an industrial hazard listed on a slate — vent, seal, continue. Syrinx felt the old III Legion nausea: an audience that refused to be an audience.

— Champion — he voxed, silk gone thin. — Take a name from them. Take a bow. Take anything.

Lirion went forward like a prayer that expected stained glass.

Industrial grey deeper in the gallery did not answer with theater. SEPTIMA plate — heat-filter brows, anti-glare panels, crimson trim that refused to sparkle — moved on Lucan’s lattice without speech. Plasma and las spoke in underburn windows. Bolters remained the boring backbone. A Legatus silhouette planted a long haft at a manifold node as if the fight were a containment problem: mono edge, clean plane, no chain-scream to compete with Noise. Ritual silence during overcharge. Not awe. Not insult speech. Just work.

Lirion offered him a perfect cut — the Gilded Cut’s thesis, the line that had ended better artists than this forge-dog.

The grey did not duel it.

He edited it.

Mono geometry met perfection as fault, not as compliment. Lirion’s flourish became an error in a vent window. Sensation found less air. The champion’s next beautiful syllable died as a severed motive, not as tragedy — as correction. No taunt. No name. No encore offered to the dying art.

Syrinx’s scream-song cracked into rage.

— Look at us — he howled through every caster, Lord Cacophonist finally begging for theology. — We are the III. We are perfection. You will watch—

The Stasis wall did not watch.

It held.

SEPTIMA opened another window. Photonic stress punished careless excess harder than any sermon. Noise Marines discovered that volume without purchase was only hull damage. Syrinx poured newer horrors into the gallery and received the same answer: shield angles, assigned targets, a Legatus who would not speak, would not bow, would not grant the mercy of rivalry.

Boredom had become irritation. Irritation became rage. Rage found nowhere to sit except extract codes.

— Out — Syrinx spat, silk burned off. — Out before they bore us to death.

They pulled back under covering Noise that sounded, traitorously, like someone screaming at a closed door. Lirion’s body remained in the gallery — a perfect corpse denied an audience — and the greys stepped over art the way men step over cable.

No grey voice followed them into the void.

On the extract, Vael Syrinx filed with hands that wanted to compose and could only account:

Cohors Batavorum — confirmed.  
SEPTIMA temper (VII filing match): glass / plasma / photonic editors. Gunmetal shield cohort — will not clap. Will not chase song. Will not duel for beauty.  
**Vael Syrinx** / Noise: ignored.  
**Lirion the Gilded Cut**: corrected, not rivaled. No speech from greys. No names gifted. No encore.  
Scar-names: joyless glass / wall that won’t clap / editors / boring dogs.  
Assessment: do not bring sensation here expecting converts. They treat perfection as vent math. They will not watch you. That is the wound.

Behind them, Vitreus kept making lenses.

The Wall did not bow.
