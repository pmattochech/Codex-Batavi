# [CHRONICLE] Wasted Talent

**Classification:** Novel chapter — Iron Warriors POV · Batavi already scar-known from prior traitor contacts · **Vexillatio org still unknown** until Bifronte’s self-name  
**When:** Present era — after Word Bearers / Red Corsairs / Black Legion (Haarken) / Thousand Sons (Ashen Spiral) first-contact scars have circulated  
**Where:** **Bifrons-Ferrum** — vault armory under grey siege guard (dual-world gulf fortifications)  
**Who:** **Warsmith Drogan Kalkas** (Iron Warriors siege warband); Iron Warriors breach cadre; Legatus **Gervas Bifronte** + **VI** *Sexta* (Siege Master / *Pater Murorum*) — identities spoken only when Bifronte reveals them

**Intel premise:** Kalkas’s warbook already carries foreign scars — cellar silence, red failure-measure, claim-denied gauge, mute meter-gelded cobalt. He expects the Batavi to fight **like those encounters** (quiet wall, anti-choir, gauge-refusal, cobalt classification). He does **not** expect them to **defend** like Imperial Fists **and** Iron Warriors in one breath, then **counterattack** the same way — a polished, symmetrical **Iron Cage** that forces the IV to remember **Barabas Dantioch**. Traitor margins still **do not** understand Batavi Vexillatio / Praetor structure until this fight.  
**Beat lock:** Bifronte self-names in full — **Gervas Bifronte**, Commander of **Sexta**, Father of Walls, proud Batavi of **Cohors Batavorum**, one of **Ten Vexillas**, one of **Ten Praetors of Castra Vetera** — then insults Kalkas as a **nameless wretch** / **wasted talent** from the **Man-Child of Olympia**. Warsmiths leave with rage, disgust, and a **theorized** filing of what a *vexilla* might be.

Warsmith Drogan Kalkas had the vault armory on his slate as a solved equation.

Bifrons-Ferrum’s understack smelled of coolant, cordite ghosts, and the patient dust of magazines that expected to outlive governments. Iron Warriors moved in calculated quiet — not Word Bearer song, not Corsair joy, not Herald proclamation, not Prospero dream. Breach frames. Melta schedules. Servo-harness math. Kalkas’s warband had paid for foreign intel: the grey Chapter now named in traitor margins as Cohors Batavorum — wall-dogs, cellar tenants, claim-deniers, mute cattle who metered gods. Useful warnings. Expected temper: industrial, silent, offensive to anyone who needed theater.

Organization? Still a blank. Scar-names spoke of greys who held and greys who killed and greys who refused claim and dream. Nobody had handed the IV a clean company table. Nobody had explained how the wall was cut into pieces that still moved as one.

He had come to crack their armory vault anyway. Iron within. Shells. Plasma cells. The kind of stockpile that made a Warsmith’s pride itch.

— Outer curtain in three — Kalkas voxed. — Expect the greys from the scars. Quiet wall. No song. No dream. No pirate greed. Cut them like the other Legions cut them. Do not lecture. Open the throat.

The first charges spoke.

The grey answer was wrong.

Not the wrongness of cellar silence or claim-denial or cobalt metering — those he had budgeted for. This was wrong as in **his own Legion’s reflection, polished until it hurt**. The vault greys did not merely hold. They **defended** like Imperial Fists and Iron Warriors in the same breath: stubborn geometry without sermon, redundant kill-math without Olympia’s open bitterness. Vigilance that built the strike while he thought they slept. Indomitor mass and storm-shield lanes that ate his first wave without shouting.

Then they **counterattacked** the same way.

Gate A and Gate B moved as one equation — twin commit so clean it felt stolen from the IV’s old notebooks and washed until the spite came off. Melta. Plant. Count. Mark. An anvil on a tempo that was not haste and not hesitation. Not a ragged Iron Cage of pride and punishment. A cage **symmetrical**, **polished**, almost beautiful in its cruelty to attackers who believed only Iron Warriors were allowed to be this honest.

Kalkas’s lip curled behind the grille until it hurt.

— They defend like us — a siege-sergeant spat, offended into honesty. — And like the Fists. Then they hit back the same. Same breath.

— That is not possible — another snarled. — That is theft. That is mockery.

It was worse than mockery. For a heartbeat Kalkas remembered a name the Legion preferred to bury under iron and hate: **Barabas Dantioch** — Warsmith excellence without the permission to rot. These greys were performing siege as if that old talent had been salvaged, cleaned, and spent on walls that still served the living. Competence without the IV’s bitterness. Hold without the Fists’ sermon. Breach and counterbreach without wasted pride.

That offended him more than any golden Throne.

— Push — Kalkas ordered, voice iron. — Break their cage. Prove the polish cracks.

They pushed.

Vault approaches became a meat ledger. Iron Warriors spent shells like doctrine. Grey Terminators and Gravis cages spent heat like monks who had already conquered the battlefield inside their own skulls before the first charge armed. Kalkas saw a Tartaros lord at the breach-heart — ornate iron halo, twin-pillar pauldron relief, polearm that planted like a mason’s proof — directing in staccato colors and counts, not speeches. Pre-breach the greys had been almost monastic. Breach minute they became zero-latency. The vault door did not hurry. Neither did they — until they did.

Kalkas hated recognizing the method.

He hated more that it worked.

A boarding wedge of his best tried to force a secondary throat. The greys let them see an opening, then closed Gate B on their ankles with the kind of redundant kill-math Warsmiths called sacred when they wrote it themselves. Iron Warriors died angry. Anger was allowed. Confusion was not. Confusion meant the enemy had rewritten the catechism.

— Warsmith — the sergeant voxed, raw. — This is not the greys from the other scars. This is an Iron Cage that got… clean. Symmetrical. It is—

— I know what it is — Kalkas cut, and hated that Dantioch’s ghost had already walked through his mind uninvited. — Open the vault or die trying. Do not name him. Do not name it.

Naming either would make it real.

The Tartaros lord advanced only when the equation required it — vigil-first, then sudden — and when the corridors finally brought Warsmith and grey lord into the same ferrocrete throat, Kalkas lifted his siege maul as if he could beat the insult back into orthodoxy.

The grey lord met him with a mason’s polearm made for vault war: hammer-dominant head, plant-ready haft, contempt for stupid haste. Sparks. Servo-scream. A storm bolter punctuating counts. Around them his brothers kept the vault armory sealed the way a forge keeps a furnace door sealed — not from fear, from ledger.

Kalkas snarled the old truth Iron Warriors used like a prayer.

— You stole our war—

The Tartaros cowl turned. Orange lenses. When the grey lord spoke, it was not sermon — construction truth, stamped so clean an enemy could not pretend he had misheard:

— I am Gervas Bifronte, Commander of Sexta, the Father of Walls — a proud Batavi and Astartes of the Cohors Batavorum Chapter, one of its Ten Vexillas that hold the lines, and one of the Ten Praetors of Castra Vetera.

Hammer-haft shifted a degree. The next line closed like a vault seal.

— And you — Bifronte said — are nothing more than a nameless wretch. Nothing more than wasted talent from the Man-Child of Olympia.

The insult landed harder than the maul.

Not *traitor*. Not *faithless*. **Nameless wretch.** **Wasted talent** — spoken by a mason who had just filed his own name, office, Chapter, and theater rank into the Warsmith’s ears like a ledger entry — as if Kalkas and his entire Legion were brilliant tools left to rust under a petulant demigod’s sulk, while these greys had kept the math and spent it on fortresses that still served the living.

Deep rage rose first. Then disgust — thick, industrial, personal. Not the clean hate Iron Warriors reserved for Fists. Something fouler: contempt returned with interest by wall-dogs who dared to name themselves while calling the IV **nothing**.

Kalkas howled without poetry and tried to kill the sentence by killing the speaker.

Bifronte did not howl back. He corrected. Plant. Hammer. A short bind that treated Warsmith fury as a thermal problem already solved before the first charge armed. His brothers sealed the angles so their commander did not need to become a whirlwind. Twin-gate. One count. The vault remained closed.

Iron Warriors understood then, with the ugly clarity of men who respect only results: the Batavi scars from other Legions had been true and still incomplete. Against this force — this **Sexta**, if the mason’s word was true — the greys were not only cellar-quiet or claim-denying. They were a siege Chapter that could offend the IV by building a polished cage — defend like Fists and Iron Warriors, counterattack the same — and by refusing to waste what the IV had turned into spite under the Man-Child’s shadow.

Kalkas disengaged when the armory throat became a meat-bill without a prize. Extract codes. Red on his own slate. His warband pulled back under covering fire that sounded, traitorously, a little like the greys’ own patience.

On the void-climb he filed where rage and disgust would keep the ink wet:

Cohors Batavorum — vault greys at Bifrons-Ferrum.  
Not the greys of the other scars alone.  
Defend like Fists + Iron Warriors. Counterattack the same.  
Polished Iron Cage. Symmetrical. Reminds of **Dantioch** — do not speak the name.  
Their mason named himself: **Gervas Bifronte**. Commander of **Sexta**. **Father of Walls**.  
Claims: proud Batavi; Astartes of Cohors Batavorum; **one of Ten Vexillas** that hold the lines; **one of Ten Praetors of Castra Vetera**.  
Theory (unconfirmed): *Vexilla* = holding formation / siege slice of the Chapter; ten such slices; each with a Praetor-grade commander for Castra Vetera mesh. Not *Codex* companies. File to traitor margins. Refine on next blood.  
He called us **nameless wretch**. **Wasted talent** from the **Man-Child of Olympia**.

He almost erased the theory lines. He did not. Warsmiths keep accurate ledgers even when the numbers hate them — and even when the filing feeds a disgust that will outlive the campaign.

Broadcast went out on sealed IV channels: better org-sketch than any prior scar had bought. Incomplete. Useful. Poisoned with the need to make the greys pay for the tone in which it had been taught.

Behind the sealed armory, Bifronte reset thermal checks and tether seals, already building the next strike while any remaining Iron Warriors might still think the vault slept. Father of Walls was not a cheer. It was a job description. He did not celebrate. Celebration was haste of the heart. The vault did not hurry.

Neither did he — until he did.
