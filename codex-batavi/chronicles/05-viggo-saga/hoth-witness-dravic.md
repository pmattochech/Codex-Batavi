# [CHRONICLE] HOTH-SECUNDUS — WITNESS DEPOSITION (CAEL DRAVIC)

**Classification:** *Reclusiam* sealed — Triumvirate access; *Decima* line need-to-know  
**When:** **~545.M42** (same engagement as [`viggo-saga.md`](viggo-saga.md))  
**Where:** Hoth-Secundus — melt-line redoubt / black-ice ravine  
**Who:** **Brother-Sergeant Cael Dravic** (*Decima*); receiving officer **Varro** (Judex)  
**Companion (Judex response):** [`varro-anima-speculum.md`](varro-anima-speculum.md)

---

## I. HOLD GEOMETRY

Captain Graile had the redoubt — stakes, overlapping fields, a melt-cutter lane that made the position **boring** to assault. That was the point. Boring kept brothers alive when the Castellan walked into weather that wanted men dead.

Alaric’s order was not shouted. It never was.

— **Hold here. I will clear the vector below.**

Graile answered on the command net with one word — *Acknowledged* — and cut his channel to the squad so they would not hear what Dravic was about to hear: the Castellan’s boots leaving the lip.

Dravic took the overwatch niche Graile assigned — thirty meters lateral, ice shelf shaved flat enough for a prone auspex. Scope discipline. No heroics. *Decima* doctrine: you watch the Wall so the Wall does not have to watch itself.

Then the ravine **changed color**.

---

## II. WHAT THE SCOPE SHOWED

Thermal first — not vid. The melt-cave under the lip went from cold purple to **furnace red** in one breath. Servo-scream carried up the fissure even through the storm. Dravic magnified.

He saw **industrial grey turn crimson** along joints and ribs — as if the armor were catching up to a curse it had not earned on the scrolls. The Castellan’s silhouette dropped to **four points of contact**. Not a Terminator advance. Not a man.

A **hunter**.

Genestealers moved in the frame — brood-kin, then shapes Dravic’s mind filed later as **Patriarch guard**: taller chitin, faster angles, the kind of xenos that did not die when line brothers breathed right. They came at the red geometry in a rush.

The red geometry **did not negotiate**.

Claws incandescent. No bolter discipline. No *We hold*. Only tearing — downward rake through a skull-case that could only be the Patriarch’s crown, then stillness so complete the scope’s audio pickup heard **ice settling**.

Dravic’s mouth went dry inside his helm. His hand found his gladius hilt without permission from his brain. Training said *Decima* does not flinch. Biology said **prey**.

He did not vox Graile. He could not make the words.

---

## III. RETURN

The red drained from the plates as suddenly as it had come — Dravic would swear to that under oath. The Castellan rose to full height on the lip of the fissure, grey and crimson pauldrons correct again, movements **too controlled** to be calm.

In his shadow, something small and white.

Alaric walked up the slope toward the redoubt vector — toward **Dravic’s** shelf. The Brother-Sergeant rolled onto his knees, scope forgotten, palm on gladius pommel, **half-draw tension** in the wrist and **stopped there**. Not drawn. Not lowered. Frozen in the only geometry that might keep him alive if the Wall had not come back.

The Castellan passed within two meters.

Dravic smelled ozone and spent heat. Saw the pup tucked against the boot — living, shivering, impossibly small. Saw Alaric’s helm turn one degree — amber lenses — as if the Castellan **registered** a brother and chose, by millimeters, not to misfile him as cave-meat.

Then the Lord of the Wall was gone downslope toward Graile’s position, and Dravic’s vox finally worked.

— **Captain. The Lord Castellan is returning. He is… he is not alone.**

Graile’s answer, flat:

— **Report to the Judex when we dock. Say everything. Omit nothing. Do not trade the story for comfort.**

---

## IV. VARRO’S CHAMBER (ABOARD *WRATH OF NOVIOMAGUS*)

Dravic stood in the cowled silence of *Reclusiam* annex steel — no iconography, no comfort. Varro did not sit. The Executor skull’s red lenses held steady.

— You did not draw — Varro said.

— No, my Lord Judex.

— You wanted to.

A pause that was not forgiveness.

— Yes.

— Good — Varro said. — Wanting is honest. Drawing would have been **geometry error**. Report.

Dravic reported. When he reached *red plates*, Varro’s gauntlets did not move, but the air pressure in the room **tightened** — the way it tightened in Hell Cells when beasts receded.

— The Patriarch’s guard — Dravic finished — were not enough. It ended fast.

— It ended **alone** — Varro said. — That is the hazard. Not the xenos. **Alone.**

He did not name what Dravic had seen. Not yet. That naming belonged to another file — and to a sight Varro would take up himself when the Castellan stood before him under lumen that did not lie.

— You will not speak of this in the mess — Varro said. — You will not teach brothers to fear the Wall. You will teach them to **hold the line** when he asks you to hold. That is *Decima* function. Dismissed.

Dravic left with his hand still remembering the hilt.
