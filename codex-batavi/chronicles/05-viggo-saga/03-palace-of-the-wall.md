# [CHRONICLE] PALACE OF THE WALL

**Classification:** Triumvirate sealed — disciplinary/spiritual intervention  
**When:** **~545.M42** (immediately after [`02-triumvirate-summons-breached.md`](02-triumvirate-summons-breached.md))  
**Where:** Noviomagus Prime — Castellan palace sector  
**Who:** **Varro**, **Drusus**, **Alaric von Helis**, on-duty *Decima* guards  
**Cross-references:** [`garmr-witness-dravic.md`](garmr-witness-dravic.md) · [`viggo-saga.md`](viggo-saga.md)

---

The palace was built to absorb noise. Even armored boots sounded smaller inside it, as if the structure itself refused to amplify men.

Decima guards lined the inner hall at regulation interval. Every visor followed Varro and Drusus as they crossed the threshold, then snapped forward again too quickly.

No one spoke.

At the far end, before the sealed strategium door, Alaric stood in full plate, helm on, hands empty. The white pup was not visible. The armor was industrial grey. The stillness was wrong.

Varro stopped five meters out.

— Castellan.

Alaric turned by degrees.

— Judex. Apothecary.

Drusus read the tone: controlled, exhausted, defensive.

Varro did not waste distance.

— You were summoned.

— I received the call.

— You did not report.

Alaric’s reply came low.

— I had containment priorities.

Varro took one step closer.

— You had command duty.

The air in the hall tightened. Two Decima marines shifted weight without moving their boots.

Alaric’s helm angled toward Drusus for an instant, then back to Varro.

— The line is intact. No friendly dead in my lane.

— This is not an after-action scorecard — Varro said. — This is chain integrity.

Drusus saw Alaric’s fingers flex once at his sides and stop.

— I chose what kept the most lives.

Varro’s voice did not rise.

— You chose to break formal summons immediately after a Red Wolf event.

Silence hit the corridor like impact glass.

Alaric answered through the helm vox, roughened at the edges.

— I did not lose the line.

— You nearly lost yourself.

The words landed harder than volume could have.

Drusus took one half-step forward, not between them, but into the same geometry.

— Alaric — he said. — This is intervention, not condemnation.

Alaric did not look at him.

— I am standing.

Varro cut in.

— Standing is minimum, not absolution.

A long breath crossed Alaric’s speakers, and when he spoke the defensive edge had become open irritation.

— Say what you came to say, Judex, or get out of my corridor.

Drusus felt the fracture profile change in real time. Shoulder tension rose. Hand-flex cadence increased. Vox grain roughened into pre-escalation rasp. He shifted his left thumb to the concealed signal stud in his sleeve — the one routed to Tyvar’s watch circuit if containment failed.

Varro saw the move and did not acknowledge it.

Alaric took one step forward.

— I held the line. I kept your chapter breathing while you filed phrases in a vault.

No one in Decima moved, but every visor was now fixed on the three of them.

Varro reached over his shoulder and drew the Crozius in one smooth line.

He did not raise it to strike.
He only armed it.

Sentence coils woke with a dry mechanical click, and red lightning crawled around the hammer-head in branching arcs that painted the corridor with blood-color flashes.

Alaric stopped.

Drusus watched memory hit him through posture alone: not fear of pain, but recognition. This was the same weapon that had put him down when words were no longer enough and force had to bring him back from the edge.

Varro stood there with the charged Crozius low at his side, unmoving, cowl still, lenses burning.

He did not cower before the challenge because this was his function: he was fear where fear was required, and he had never feared the Alpha when law was on his side.

Silence lasted six beats.

Then Varro spoke, each word cold and exact.

— Put yourself together, or the Black Cells will do that for you, mongrel.

The line hit the hall like impact ordnance. One Decima guard flinched before discipline caught him.

Alaric’s right hand twitched toward his hilt and stopped in the same motion.

Varro did not move the Crozius.
He did not need to.

When he spoke again, the steel remained in his voice, but something older entered underneath it.

— Before you answer me, remember who you are.

Red arcs crackled once more around the hammer head, then steadied.

— Remember what you achieved.
— Remember what you suffered.
— Remember what you built and held when no one believed it could be held.

The corridor remained absolutely still.

Varro took half a step closer, not enough to threaten distance, enough to force listening.

— You are not a mongrel.

Another beat.

— And most of all, you are not Valerius.

The name landed like a blade between armor joints.

Alaric’s shoulders dropped by millimeters, not surrender, not collapse — impact accepted.

Drusus eased pressure off the Tyvar stud but kept his hand near it.

When Alaric answered, the aggression had not vanished. It had been re-caged.

— I stabilized.

Varro answered immediately.

— At the edge. Because of a pup. Because a line sergeant held when fear said draw. Because chance gave you six clean minutes.

He lowered the Crozius by degrees, energy field still live.

— I am not here to bury you, Alaric. I am here because the Chapter cannot carry your silence as doctrine.

Alaric did not answer for four measured breaths.

— Understood.

Varro did not accept it immediately.

— Repeat in command terms.

— Formal summons takes priority over private containment unless line-collapse is active and declared. I will report on first call.

— Good.

Drusus stepped in now with his own closure.

— I want telemetry review and sleep-cycle audit. No argument.

Alaric’s answer was almost dry.

— You planned that before you walked in.

— Correct.

For the first time, something close to a human exhale passed through Alaric’s vox.

— Fine.

Varro turned toward the corridor.

— Decima, stand easy.

The guards did not relax much, but the strain left their shoulders by degrees.

Before leaving, Varro looked back once.

— This ends here for tonight.

He paused.

— It does not end in general.

Alaric gave one short nod.

Varro and Drusus walked out together. Behind them, the palace swallowed sound again, but this time the silence felt less like avoidance and more like work waiting to be done.
