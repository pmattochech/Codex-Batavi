# [CHRONICLE] SUMMONS BREACH

**Classification:** Triumvirate internal — command integrity incident  
**When:** **~055.M42** (immediately after [`01-varro-drusus-after-garmr.md`](01-varro-drusus-after-garmr.md))  
**Where:** Noviomagus Prime — Triumvirate chamber ring, then transit corridor to palace sector  
**Who:** **Varro**, **Drusus**, absent party **Alaric von Helis**  
**Cross-references:** [`varro-anima-speculum.md`](varro-anima-speculum.md)

---

The Triumvirate chamber had three stations and one central mark. Two stations were occupied. The third remained empty long enough for emptiness to become an argument.

Varro stood at his station, cowl fixed, hands still.

Drusus watched the chron strip on his slate tick through the final seconds of mandatory report window.

No heavy footfall in the outer hall.
No seal request.
No vox acknowledgment.

Only the chamber’s own machine hum, steady and indifferent.

Drusus broke the silence first.

— Attendance window closed.

Varro did not look away from the empty station.

— Read status.

— No transit incident. No medical lockdown. No armor disable signal. No command conflict higher than this call.

— Conclusion.

Drusus spoke it without softening.

— Absence by will.

Varro turned then, slow and exact.

— Send second call. Full seal. Record this one as unanswered.

Drusus sent it. The signal left the room with all the precision of a blade and came back with nothing attached.

No response.

The second window died the same clean death as the first.

Drusus did not set the slate down this time.

— Third contact? — he asked.

Varro gave one short nod.

— Send a servoskull. If the corridor is blocked, send a servitor behind it.

Drusus keyed both commands. In the upper corner of his display, pict-feed came alive: a servo-skull drifting through palace steel, lumen eye sweeping, vox rune broadcasting full Triumvirate seal. Five seconds behind it, a utility servitor advanced with command relay hardwired into its chest unit.

The feed reached the inner corridor bend and fractured into static.

For one heartbeat the image returned: floor plating, wall shadow, then a violent tilt as if the skull had been struck out of the air.

Both signals went black at once.

A single red rune replaced the feed:

**UNIT OFFLINE.**

Drusus watched the dead display without blinking.

— Contact asset terminated. Servitor terminated. No recovery ping.

Only then did he set the slate down.

— Formal summons breached.

There was no anger in Varro’s posture. Anger would have been easier to carry.

— Name the risk.

Drusus did not need to search for words.

— If a formal Triumvirate summons can be ignored by the Castellan after a Red Wolf event, line command reads ambiguity where there must be none.

Varro gave one short nod.

— Correct.

He stepped away from the station and toward the exit hatch.

Drusus remained where he was.

— We can issue censure first.

Varro stopped, back still to him.

— Censure is for distance. This is not distance.

Drusus moved to catch up.

— You intend immediate contact.

— I intend to place law where silence has started pretending to be law.

They walked the corridor in lockstep, white arm and black cowl crossing steel lit by low amber strips. Servitors on track rails paused as they passed, either by protocol or by fear.

At the first junction Drusus spoke again.

— If we press too hard, he may lock down further.

Varro did not slow.

— If we do not press now, lock down becomes office behavior.

— He is not our enemy.

Varro turned his head enough for one lens to catch Drusus fully.

— That is why we are walking to him instead of sending sentence teams.

Drusus held the look, then inclined his head.

— Understood.

They reached the palace ingress gate where Decima guard already stood with weapons locked to safe angle and shoulders too rigid to be at rest.

The lead guard sergeant saluted.

— My lords.

Varro returned the salute with minimal motion.

— Open.

The gate split on a central seam and exposed the long internal corridor where Alaric kept his private command silence.

Varro stepped through first.

Drusus followed half a pace behind, and for the first time in years he felt something close to fear — not of Varro himself, but of the shape this conversation might take once it reached the man both of them had come to keep from falling.
