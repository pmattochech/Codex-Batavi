## I. THE REEF

The reef-chain did not keep secrets. It kept **smell**.

Brack carried iron and burned incense from a hundred minor shrines the Administratum had never bothered to number — reed wicks, animal fat, the cheap resins poor worlds used when they could not afford temple grade. In the last week something else had joined the mix: a sweetness that was not floral, a copper that was not forge-work, a thin rot beneath both that made veteran nostrils tighten the way they tightened before a Furor ward in the Apothecarion corridors. Tide clerks on the gunboat blamed storm turnover. Navy pickets blamed smuggler scows. Auspex blamed nothing, which was how auspex usually behaved when the veil had been **touched** and did not yet want paperwork.

The inner reef was *Quinta* business in the only sense that mattered on Aethelgard: close enough to Needles memory that assault brothers filed it as **home water**, far enough from parade grounds that the lattice did not smooth every edge. Mangrove throats opened and closed with tide. Salt pans flashed white between stands of black wood. Feral shrine-stacks rose on reef stone older than the Chapter’s claim, their spirals not Batavi, not Imperial — geometry written by people who believed hunger could be **invited**.

*Strategium* had not sent a Legatus for this insert. It had sent an **investigation element** — modest kit, modest timetable, modest hope of closing a cult net before the net learned how to **seat** something worse than prayer.


The gunboat *Salt Ration* smelled of brine sealant and cold promethium — Navy austerity dressed as competence. The element mustered in the assault trunk while the reef slid past the armored slit in slow, grey layers: cloud over brack over the dull pinprick of cookfires on outer atolls where mortals still pretended the frontier was only poverty and not **theater**.

A **sergeant** stood at the center of the trunk with the briefing slate in one hand and no intention of handing it off. He had served three Needles assaults without earning a name worth putting in this account. His pauldrons carried *Quinta* grey and the scuff marks of a man who had stopped expecting ceremonies to explain why he was still alive. Behind him, six brothers young enough that their ceramite still looked **new** beside the veterans’ battered plate — post-neophyte, pre-barracks myth, hungry for a first reef war that would fit on a training scroll.

They had been told they were hunting a **cult**.

Warp-tainted murders along the inner reef. Bodies opened with enthusiasm that exceeded feral piety. Informant coordinates bleeding from a shrine-complex on the second tide’s azimuth. *Vermilion* adjacency on the classification line — not full Judex deployment, not *Alabarda* triumph — but enough red ink that the sergeant knew the slate could turn ugly without turning **loud**.

— **Line geometry** — he told the trunk, voice flat as tide tables. — **Bolt and blade. No jump packs. No pursuit into solo spacing. You see transhuman heat that is not us, you hold and vox. You do not improvise heroics for a clerk’s legend.**

The youngest shifted weight at the word *legend*. Barracks had already done its work.

On Noviomagus and in the assault bays of Aethelgard, *Quinta* youths traded stories about **Einar Dreadwolf** the way shore children traded storm warnings — half awe, half fear, none of it fit for official doctrine. The wolf who **never knelt**. The Lost brother under the Deathwolf’s jaws. A lieutenant who did not take neophytes because precedent might **copy** what should never be copied. Some said he had held Furor alone for a century before the ship broke him across something worse. Some said he screamed at the sky after kills because agony was the only honest language left. Veterans who had served beside him before Moraine-Nine did not correct the stories. Correction was cruelty. Silence was **melancholy** filed as discipline.

The sergeant had seen **Einar** once at distance on a reef convoy purge — red limbs, black pauldrons, cowl, **Varro** walking behind him like a shadow that weighed the same as a blade. He did not tell the youths that. Some truths were not for neophyte slates.

— **We are not hunting the Lost** — he said instead. — **We are hunting cultists who think the reef is tired. We close the net. We withdraw. Judex owns what Judex owns.**

No one in the trunk asked who owned the smell that had started **before** their gunboat burned fuel.



Insert came at low tide with engines dialed to **quiet** — the reef did not forgive heat signatures that advertised Astartes pride to feral shrines and the things that fed them. The ramp kissed mangrove mud that sucked at boots. Brack climbed the greaves immediately, cold and tasting of old blood. Auspex whined once, painted false heat on a stand of dead trees, then went obediently blank as if ashamed.

The sergeant ran the tally the way Needles had taught him: six youths, two veterans besides himself, bolt crates sealed, combat blades racked, gene-grace sacks **absent** because this was investigation, not recovery. No *Alabarda*. No banner. No Chaplain sermon. Only the Chapter’s grey moving through green dark with the discipline of men who understood that the first corpse would teach them whether the briefing had lied.

One youth — the youngest, still wearing a training cut on his knuckle guard that had not yet earned a story — breathed through his rebreather filter and whispered a question the sergeant pretended not to hear.

— **If the Lost is already out here — **

— **Then we hold our line** — the sergeant said. — **And we do not become a second problem.**

The trunk behind them sealed. *Salt Ration*’s engines faded up-channel. The reef closed around the element like a mouth that had been chewing on something for days and had not yet finished swallowing.

They moved inland on informant coordinates, boots finding traction where mortals would have sunk, vox disciplined to burst-only. Above them, birds stopped mid-call in pockets the sergeant marked and did not speak of. Brack went still in those pockets. The wind carried incense and copper and, underneath, the grammar of kills that had not been made by cultists alone — too clean, too finished, too **ahead** of the map.

They were not told the trail might already belong to the Chapter.

They would learn it on the first corpse, and again on the fourth, and finally on the salt pan where geometry stopped being Batavi and started being **precipitation**.

## II. THE TRAIL

The first corpse lay in a mangrove throat where the water went black at noon even when the sky was pale. Roots arched over the channel like the ribs of a creature that had drowned slowly. Flies had not found the body yet — another wrongness; reef death usually brought vermin before Astartes did. The sergeant noted it, said nothing, and gestured the element to **hold** the lip while he went down alone.

A cultist — not disputed. Robes cut open from sternum to pubis, fabric parted with a precision that mocked the cheap stitching mortals used for temple dress. Organs arranged on the root-mat with the pedantry of men who believed arrangement pleased something listening: heart to the east, liver split and mirrored, tongues laid in a crescent that pointed toward the inner reef. Warp residue clung to the stones like oil on glass, throwing back the auspex’s sweep in a thin shriek that died when Brother **Kaelen** — youngest on the insert — flinched and looked away.

The sergeant knelt. He did not touch the meat. He read **spacing** the way assault specialists read jump solutions: distance from the last shrine stub on the informant map, angle of entry, whether the killer had stood uphill or in the channel itself. Needles had taught *Quinta* the difference between zealot frenzy and **closure**. Frenzy spilled. Closure **filed**.

Too clean.

Too **finished**.

Cultists killed in passion left chaos — overturned bowls, torn charms, footprints in mud that lied about direction. This kill left **grammar** — a message written for readers who understood assault geometry. The sergeant did not say *Astartes*. He did not need to. He said, to the trunk vox when the channel cleared enough to carry:

— **First contact. Cultist only. Warp smear. No friendly heat.**

On *Salt Ration*, someone would file that and feel relief that was not yet earned.

---

The second corpse was two throat-lengths inland, hung from a reef-stone hook the cult had driven into a tree that should not have supported the weight. The third was in a salt pan shallow where brack had pooled red and then been **stirred** by tide — as if the killer had waited to see whether the water would drink what was offered. Each time, cultists only. Each time, opened. Each time, distortion that made the auspex whine and then go obediently blank, as if ashamed to testify.

The youths began to speak in the compressed dialect of men trying not to vomit inside helms.

— **Escalation** — one veteran muttered. — **They’re feeding something.**

— **Or something is feeding on them** — the sergeant answered, and the trunk went quiet.

He did not say *ahead of us*. He did not say *faster than our map*. Both were true in the arithmetic of spacing. The informant coordinates had described a cult net **scattered** across six mangrove fingers and two pan bowls. The bodies they found were not scattered. They were **strung** — each kill closer to the shrine-complex azimuth, each kill more recent by brack drying rate and the sourness of the warp smear. Something was walking the net in the same direction the element walked, metabolizing shrines faster than clerks could have drawn a combined battle plan.

The element moved anyway. Investigation geometry did not permit retreat because auspex was rude. It permitted **hold**, **vox**, and **continue** until a Legatus or Judex line said otherwise. No such line came. The reef kept its silence about who had already passed through.

---

By the fourth corpse the light had begun to think about second tide. Mud sucked at greaves. One youth’s filter clogged with incense ash from a stub shrine the cult had abandoned — candles melted into a single red slug, bone charms snapped as if someone had stepped on them in passing without bothering to hate them individually. The sergeant swept the stub, found no fresh blood, found **only** the direction of departure: inward, always inward, toward the spiral the informant had marked in yellow on the slate.

The fourth body lay at the edge of that inward line, opened the same way as the others, but with a difference the sergeant felt in his teeth before he saw it with his eyes. The arrangement was **shallower** — less pedantry, more speed. As if the killer had stopped lecturing to the warp and started **clearing**.

The sergeant circled the corpse once. On the far side, in dried brack that had held the shape of a boot while the tide was out, he found the print.

Transhuman scale. Heel plate scored in three short lines — traction claw doctrine, Batavi industrial, not Chaos trophy-work. Toe pressure deep enough to say the wearer had **run** here, then stood over the cultist long enough to finish the grammar anyway.

The sergeant looked up the inward path. Mangroves darker ahead. Birds silent in a pocket he could not yet see. No vox crackle of friendly challenge. No *Quinta* call sign.

He dropped to one knee over the print the way he had knelt over the meat — not to worship evidence, to **conceal** it. The youngest was three paces back, still turned half away from the fourth body’s arrangement. The sergeant covered the brack with his knee until the heel scoring vanished under ceramite, then rose as if he had only been checking greave seal.

— **Fourth contact** — he told the vox, voice unchanged. — **Same filing. Cultist only. Warp smear increasing. Advancing on complex azimuth.**

Brother **Kaelen** swallowed audibles the filter could not hide.

— **Sergeant** — he said — **the smear on this one. It’s not only cult residue. It’s — **

— **It’s above your pay grade** — the sergeant said, not cruel, **exact**. — **Hold line geometry. We do not pursue solo heat.**

He did not say *Batavi heat*. He did not say *traction claws*. He filed the print in memory only, the way Needles filed storm warnings: data you acted on without teaching children to read the sky too early.

Some truths were not for neophyte slates.

The element advanced. Behind them, something in the mangroves held still — not the gunboat, not the cult, not the birds. A pressure the youths would later call *daemon* because young mouths needed size. The sergeant would call it **precedence** and keep his hand off the youth’s chest plate until insertion math demanded it.

Eight more corpses lay between the fourth kill and the salt pan where the spiral waited — twelve cultists opened in all, if a man still wanted arithmetic. The sergeant would not count them aloud. Counting made slaughter look like progress. He checked the tide clock instead: second tide rising, coordinates growing warmer on the slate, the inward path narrowing toward a bowl of reef stone that had not yet shown its three dead Exalted or its lord.

The trail, by then, was no longer ambiguous.

It was **theirs**, and the Chapter had not sent the element to investigate it.

The element had been sent to **witness** what the trail already meant.

## III. PARALLEL VECTORS

While the investigation element still waded the mangrove fingers and filed corpses the sergeant would not count aloud, other movements had already begun on bearings that did not share a vox net. *Strategium* had drawn one mission on *Salt Ration*’s slate. The reef was running **three** without asking permission.

This was not a lone hunt. It was a **precipitation** — vectors converging because geometry demanded it, not because anyone had drawn a combined battle plan in the same room.

---

### Einar

**Einar Dreadwolf** did not travel with the investigation element. He had not traveled with anyone in the sense *Quinta* meant when it spoke of **lines** — not since induction onto **The Lost**, not since **Varro**’s name replaced a Legatus on every mission slate that mattered. **Steppenwolf** tallies still carried him on the detached registry; *Amissi* carried him under the Deathwolf’s jaws. The two filings did not argue. They described the same distance from pack warmth in different ink.

He had come ashore hours before *Salt Ration* kissed mud — gunboat insert was line geometry, slow and witness-safe. Einar’s insert had been **absence**: a lighter craft, a quieter ramp, a corridor of mangroves that learned his weight the way Needles shore had learned it when he was only **Furor** and pride still passed for stability.

Full **Mk X Tacticus** moved now with *Amissi* heraldry subdued under cowl — black pauldrons, red limbs, **Mk V** bowl dark. Dreadwolf Claws slept along the vambraces until the moment required extension. The high cowl hid the cable merge along jaw and nape; it did not hide the **bulk** of Exanimus hypertrophy or the way his stride refused parade spacing. The Lost did not announce themselves. The reef announced them anyway: birds stopping mid-call, brack going still, insects failing to restart in pockets he passed through.

At a stub shrine choked with reed wicks, he killed three cultists in the time it took a mortal priest to draw breath for a final syllable.

No sermon. No mercy line for the record. Dreadwolf Claws extended once, retracted flush, fingers free to finish what blades had opened. He left the bodies in **grammar** the sergeant’s element would misread — opened, arranged enough to warn the warp, not enough to waste seconds pedantry no longer deserved. Exanimus strength layered on a century of Furor-only discipline; **Butcher’s Nails** riding the mane under cloth, not steering yet, but **listening** for the frequency that lived at the bind-site ahead, a low itch behind the teeth that said the spiral was trying to **seat**.

He was not *Versibar*. The distinction mattered to *Medicinae* and mattered more to **Varro**. Einar could feel the Red Demon test the chain at the bars and find it holding — marine visage still fixing wolf and demon in the same grip, tripartite bind not yet a public failure. That hold cost **distance**. Distance was the only leash he still trusted when the lattice over-sharpened his wildness.

Each shrine he cleared was one fewer voice in the summoning choir. Each body he left was evidence. He did not vox the investigation element. They were line brothers. Their geometry would break if they stepped into his.

---

### Varro

**Varro** moved on a parallel bearing, lower in the mangroves, *Executor* cowl cutting brack without ripple. The Judex was not on *Salt Ration*’s manifest. He was not on the sergeant’s insert roster. He was on a watch interval **Varro** himself had defined when the cult net first showed warp heat in a pattern that meant binding, not piety.

He was not hunting the cult.

He was hunting **Einar**.

Distance measured in tree lines and silence, not meters. Eyes on ceramite that must not shed **Batavi blood** — friendly throats, friendly gene-grace, the catastrophic arithmetic of a Lost brother cutting through line brothers because the Red had lied about whose plate was whose. Ears tuned for the moment when corruption might **wear** the armor instead of the man: nail-song climbing past audit, liturgy bleeding into Brass cadence, the wolf no longer holding the demon but **feeding** it.

From a root hollow, Varro watched Einar clear the stub shrine. He watched the kills finish. He watched the claws retract. He did not intervene. Intervention was for **failure** or **bounded** verdict, not for competence that still made the Judex’s skin tighten.

Every kill Einar made was a datum.

Every silence between kills was a louder datum.

Varro’s crimson hands stayed still. Hurry was for men who still believed events could be surprised. The reef had not surprised Varro since Moraine-Nine taught him what **first Exanimus** looked like when it was forced across a man who had held Furor for a century without kneeling. He filed what he saw into *Vermilion* memory: *clearance ongoing; armor holds; no friendly blood; watch continues.*

Ahead, the bind-site frequency sharpened. Somewhere on another bearing, a gunboat element’s vox burst would crackle through the trees — *fourth contact, cultist only* — thin, honest, unaware. Varro did not answer it. The Judex answered only **convergence**.

---

### Armin

**Armin Sturmwahl** came from the seaward approach on a second vector, alone, jump pack absent, assault load stripped to what mud war required. He had not ridden *Salt Ration*. A *Quinta* cutter had dropped him on the outer reef with the same modest timetable and a different **ledger** in his hand.

His reason was not on any slate the sergeant could read.

**Caution** — the kind that was not fear of the World Eaters or cultists but fear of **necessity**: whether the slaughter ahead could still be folded back into duty without becoming appetite; whether the brother he had known before **Kzorn’s Maw** still lived inside the stride that had gone **wrong** after the ship. He did not call it love. *Quinta* did not award names to that filing. He called it **geometry** and followed it anyway.

Moraine-Nine lived in his thumb on the grav-comp test stud he did not need — habit, not mission. He remembered the salt pan. He remembered eleven names. He remembered **withholding** the cull stroke when **Einar** stood in red hands and said *We hold, as the Needles hold the cold tide* and the ridge line chose **mercy** because Varro’s eyes had measured **bounded** before the gladius finished its argument.

Armin could not order **Einar** now. *Amissi* belonged to Judex slate. A Legatus could not own the Lost wolf; he could only **approach** him on a bearing that might intersect before the bind seated something the Chapter would have to burn.

He crossed a cultist the sergeant’s element had not yet reached — fifth on the inward string, if a man still wanted arithmetic — and read the same grammar the sergeant had read on the fourth: speed over pedantry, closure over frenzy, spacing that was not cult work. Armin’s mouth tightened. He did not touch the body. He looked at the boot scuff in brack where a larger ceramite heel had paused and moved on, seaward no longer, **inward** like a man following a tide line only assault specialists trusted.

Vox crackled once from the line element’s channel — the sergeant’s flat report, unchanged, disciplined. Armin did not break net. He did not need to. The report told him the gunboat brothers were still alive and still blind to whose trail they walked. That blindness was policy. It was also **danger**.

— **Necessary** — Sturmwahl said to no receiver, to the brack, to the memory of a jump-qualified friendship that no longer shared pack rhythm. — **Or appetite. The ridge will tell me.**

He sealed his gorget and kept walking.

---

### Convergence

Three vectors. One bowl waiting inland.

**Einar** toward the spiral, listening to the bind.

**Varro** behind Einar, measuring corruption and blood.

**Armin** behind both in the only sense that mattered — not following Varro, not commanding Einar, closing on **brother** geometry before the epicenter answered whether the duel was still **theirs** to survive witnessing.

None of the three knew the gunboat element was walking into their wake.

None of the three were together.

The investigation element, eight corpses still ahead of them on the map, kept advancing because that was what line geometry did until the salt pan opened and the three dead Exalted showed their swollen plate and the duel inside the spiral taught every witness that precipitation had already become **liturgy without prayer**.

## IV. THE SPIRAL

The last eight corpses on the inward string did not teach the element anything the fourth had not already taught. Only **repetition** — cultists opened, warp smear thickening, brack drying slower as if the air itself were learning appetite. Brother **Kaelen** stopped looking at arrangements after the sixth. The veterans looked at **spacing** instead: who had moved faster, who had stopped arranging, who had begun to **clear** rather than lecture. The sergeant said nothing. He watched the tide clock and watched the yellow azimuth on the slate narrow until mangroves broke open and the world became sky and salt.

---

### The bowl

The shrine-complex opened in a pan bowl where ancient reef stone had been stacked into a spiral that should not have held weight. Mortal masons had not built this in any filing the Administratum would admit. The stones leaned inward like teeth around a tongue of white sand. At the center, heat shimmered — not sun, not promethium — **warp fever**, the visual lie of a rite trying to **seat** something in meat before the bowl decided whether to become incursion.

Smell changed on the lip.

Incense died. Brack died. What remained was copper and sweet rot and the machine-oil stink of chain teeth that had chewed ceramite recently and had not been cleaned.

The element halted on the treeline because assault doctrine said you did not walk into a bowl without reading the rim. The sergeant raised one fist. Hold.

— **Contact at complex** — he voxed, voice still flat. — **Warp heat central. Structural spiral. No friendly call sign.**

*Salt Ration* answered with static shaped like a question. No Legatus override. No Judex line. Policy still pretended this was cult closure.

---

### Three exalted

Three shapes lay outside the spiral’s inner ring, where the stones made their first full turn.

**Exalted Eightbound** — dead.

Even in death they offended proportion. Ceramite swollen until pauldrons looked like tumors. Chainblades — paired eviscerators on one, dual lacerators on another, a heavy chainglaive sunk into the sand beside the third — locked in rigor or still whispering one tooth at a time, as if the weapons had not been convinced the hands were gone. Faces behind open helms were not faces anymore: skin grafted to iconography, Khornate geometry written as **disease**.

They had died in **wrong geometry**.

One sprawled backward over a stone with legs at angles jump packs would not sign. One knelt as if in prayer except the prayer had been interrupted by claws from above. One lay in a spray arc that pointed toward the spiral center, ceramite gouged in three ragged channels that said the killer had come from heights assault manuals had no chapter for.

Not Batavi spacing. Not cult spacing. **Closure** at a scale the sergeant’s element was not cleared to produce.

Brother **Kaelen** made a sound the filter could not classify.

— **Emperor’s blood** — he whispered.

— **Hold line** — the sergeant said. — **That is not cult work.**

The veterans did not whisper. They exchanged one look and filed it where melancholy lived: something **Chapter-class** had already passed through here and spent itself on the demi-daemons before the true apex arrived. Three exalted meant three demi-daemons removed from the bind’s choir. Whatever still moved in the spiral center was what remained after that payment.

The youths saw slaughter and assumed cult retribution escalated beyond their briefing — heretics punished by heretics, warp eating warp, a morality simple enough for barracks.

The sergeant saw **precedence** and felt the cold in his shoulders deepen.

---

### The rim

The investigation element crested the bowl lip and stopped as one organism discovering it had walked into a cathedral **mid-liturgy**.

From the rim, the spiral was a throat. Sound came up from it — steel on steel, chain-teeth on ceramite, a wet machinery failing upward in pitch. Something too large for cultists and too fast for the dead exalted moved in the stones with a cadence that was **not** line abreast, not friction-rite spacing, not any geometry the sergeant could insert into without becoming a second problem.

Insertion was **impossible**.

Not cowardice. Mathematics. One wrong step and Batavi blood would join the brack for reasons that had nothing to do with cultists. The sergeant’s hand found Brother **Kaelen**’s chest plate and held him on the lip. The other youths did not breathe.

On the landward side of the bowl, a cowl moved through dead reed without ripple — **Varro**, lower than the lip, not yet between, **measuring**. On the seaward side, assault grey paused at the treeline — **Armin Sturmwahl**, gorget sealed, thumb on a grav stud he did not need, not descending to the line element, not challenging the spiral yet. Two vectors the youths could not see. One vector they could see only as **motion** inside the stones.

Red limbs flashed between reef teeth. Black pauldron. Dreadwolf Claw stutter, retract, stutter. A second shape warped taller than human allowance — lacerator scream, daemonic claw opening and closing, plate that looked like World Eaters iconography had become **skin disease**. The bind-site fever pulsed once, as if the bowl had inhaled.

The sergeant understood, too late for comfort and early enough for fear:

The trail they had followed was not the enemy’s.

It was **his**.

The cultists had been cleared on the way in. The exalted had been spent on the way in. What the element had been sent to **witness** was already happening at the spiral’s tongue — precipitation become visible, legend become **kinetic**, the Lost wolf who never knelt meeting the lord the cult had tried to seat.

Inside the spiral, the duel was already in motion.

The rim held. The sound did not.

## V. NOT BATAVI GEOMETRY

The spiral had been built for worship. It became a **kill-box** the moment **Einar** refused the enemy’s idea of floor.

---

### Lord of the bind

The **Slaughterbound** was what remained when three Exalted had already been paid into the altar: lord-of-bind rhetoric made flesh, the cult’s last attempt to **seat** apex hunger in meat before the bowl became incursion. Lacerator screaming on one arm — teeth that would not stop whirring even when the air was empty. Daemonic claw on the other opening and closing with a wet patience that mocked fingers. Plate warped until World Eaters iconography looked like **skin disease**, ceramite and flesh sharing the same swollen lie.

Traitor archives would later boast a **Bloodthirster** caged in the soul. *Vermilion* would file *possessed apex, bind rhetoric unconfirmed* and close the line. What mattered on the salt pan was simpler: the thing was **fast**, **heavy**, accustomed to killing in open Khornate geometry where everything died **facing forward** and died grateful to have seen the blade that took them.

**Einar** fought it the way brack fights stone — not by winning strength contests but by refusing the contest’s shape.

He was not *Versibar*. The rim could not have held that. What moved inside the stones was still **bounded** enough to audit — marine visage holding chains, wolf and demon in tripartite grip, Exanimus hypertrophy paid for in hours of cult clearance and three exalted already removed from the choir. The bind-site fever pulsed around them like a lung. Each pass of steel made the warp heat shiver. If the rite had fully seated, the bowl would have become someone else’s problem. It had not. **Einar** had arrived while the lord was still **becoming**.

---

### Wrong spacing

The Slaughterbound charged forward because forward was how Khorne counted.

**Einar** went vertical.

Traction claws bit reef stone at an angle assault manuals filed under emergency, not doctrine. Dreadwolf Claws extended in stutter bursts — knuckle blades flashing, retracting flush, free hand finishing what steel opened — and the Slaughterbound’s mass turned where its habit expected open sand. It found **lip** instead. Found **height**. Found a silhouette that was still Batavi in heraldry and was **not** Batavi in spacing.

**Not Batavi geometry.**

No *We hold* cadence in the footwork. No line abreast. No friction-rite distance for the youths on the rim to mirror. No pack synchrony. The wolf who never knelt fought as if the reef were Needles and the enemy were tide: cut, withdraw, cut from elevation the lord’s fused plate could not rotate to in time.

From the rim, the investigation element saw **color** before they saw technique — red limbs, black pauldron, cowl lost to motion, Mk V bowl flashing once when the head turned and the horizontal crimson visor stripe caught brack light like a second wound. Brother **Kaelen** tried to count exchanges and failed by the third. A veteran whispered numbers anyway, as if counting could tame what counting could not survive.

— **Do not step in** — the sergeant said, hand welded to the youth’s chest plate. — **Hold.**

Insertion was **impossible**. Not cowardice. Mathematics. One wrong step and Batavi blood would join the brack for reasons that had nothing to do with cultists. The sergeant had filed traction claws on the fourth corpse and understood, now, whose heel had made the print. Understanding did not grant permission to enter **that** spacing.

On the landward lip, **Varro** stood still enough to be stone. On the seaward treeline, **Armin** did not descend. Both men watched the same duel and measured different catastrophes.

---

### The exchange

Steel on steel. Chain-teeth on ceramite. A sound like wet machinery failing upward in pitch.

The Slaughterbound committed with the boredom of something that had never met a prey who **sidestepped upward** instead of back. Lacerator arc — full extension, ration-tin promise, Terminator arrogance translated into traitor flesh. The air whined where the blade passed.

**Einar** was not there when the weapon arrived.

He was on the spiral’s inner lip, claws through the join where possessed plate had fused **wrong**, body weight and Exanimus hypertrophy driving into a gap doctrine said should not exist. The Slaughterbound staggered. Recovery geometry opened for half a heartbeat — Khornate habit inviting finish work.

**Einar** did not grant it.

He withdrew upward again, not retreat, **re-angle**, Dreadwolf Claws stuttering along the lord’s shoulder pauldron until ceramite screamed and the lacerator’s return swing arrived late. The daemonic claw raked air that had been chest a moment before. Warp heat spat from the bind center as if the bowl objected to being denied its seating.

The lord roared without words — Brass frequency, not *Quinta* law.

**Einar** answered with silence and work.

Second pass. Third. The spiral stones took cuts meant for thighs. Sand drank lubricant from chain teeth. One of the dead Exalted’s eviscerators shivered in rigor as if jealous. On the rim, youths felt awe trying to arrive before their discipline had issued permission. The sergeant felt **tension** — correct, earned — the tension of a man watching Chapter-class closure he could not command and must not join.

The Slaughterbound began to **learn** — late, angry, still heavy enough to end the fight if one mistake granted it forward geometry again. It pressed into the spiral throat, shrinking the vertical options, forcing **face-forward** combat where its mass was scripture.

**Einar** gave it one face-forward instant and spent it.

Traction claws skidded on stone, found purchase where there should have been none, lifted his mass through a gap that was not a jump pack arc but was **Needles** all the same — shore assault habit surviving inside *Amissi* watch. Dreadwolf Claws punched once, retracted, punched again. The second strike was not flourish. It was **accounting**.

---

### Termination

The Slaughterbound’s bind rhetoric died in the body before the body died in the sand.

The kill was not banishment. It was **termination** — traitor lord down at the spiral’s tongue, possessed plate split along the wrong seam, lacerator still screaming because the weapon did not know the hand had gone. The daemonic claw opened, closed on nothing, opened again with a child’s empty patience.

Bind-site collapsed around the corpse like a fever breaking. Warp heat sucked inward. Incense smell did not return. Brack did not return. Only copper and cooling metal and the machine-oil stink of chain teeth slowing down.

**Einar** stood over the body.

For one heartbeat he was still a **marine visage** holding chains.

No skull taken. No trophy chain. No open offering for Brass to count. The reef kept the receipt the way the reef kept smell — private, honest, unfit for *Alabarda* cloth.

On the rim, the element did not cheer. *Quinta* did not cheer slaughter that had not been theirs. They breathed again because breathing had been withheld, not because victory had been shared.

The duel was over.

What came next would not be geometry.

---

## VI. THE SCREAM AND THE RIM

The next heartbeat after the chains held, **Einar** screamed at the sky.

---

### The scream

Not words. Not cadence. Not the *Quinta* gift line, not *We hold*, not anything the assault bays taught neophytes to file under **conduct**. What came out of him was rabid, upward, stripped of law — throat open to brack cloud and warp-thin air as if the body needed the reef to witness what the body had refused to kneel to.

**Nails** answered first in the mane under cowl — cable merge twitching along jaw line, dry hydraulic memory from a ship the youths had only heard about in legend. The **second beast** answered second, not *Versibar*, not Execratio terminal, but the Red frequency that lived inside tripartite bind and tested the bars when slaughter went long. Hours of cult clearance had fed it. Three Exalted dead had fed it. The Slaughterbound’s termination had fed it last. Victory did not soothe. Victory **invoiced**.

The sound crossed the salt pan and came back wrong off the spiral stones, multiplied into a choir of one voice. Birds that had been silent broke into flight too late, as if ashamed they had missed the cue. The bind-site shiver that had not quite become incursion **exhaled** and left metal cooling and sand ordinary again — ordinary except for the lord’s corpse and the demi-daemons and the Batavi in red limbs still standing over all of it.

**Einar** did not fall. He did not kneel. He screamed until the scream spent itself against the sky and what remained was breath and the wet shine on his vambraces and the tremor in hands that Dreadwolf Claws had left **free** and still lethal.

---

### Rim

On the rim, the investigation element held because holding was all line geometry had left.

Neophytes felt **awe** before discipline issued permission — the awe of men who had seen a legend become kinetic and could not yet name what they had seen. Later they would call the enemy a greater daemon because young mouths needed **size** to match the eyes. Later they would speak of the wolf who never knelt in eternal agony as if agony were the same as glory. Veterans would not correct them. Correction was cruelty. Melancholy was **discipline**.

Brother **Kaelen** shook once inside his helm and stopped shaking because stopping was what *Quinta* demanded. He had counted exchanges and failed. He had whispered *Emperor’s blood* at three Exalted dead. He would carry this pan in his sleep the way shore children carried storm warnings — without poetry, without relief.

The sergeant felt **tension** — correct, earned, the kind that outlived missions. Not fear of the cult. Not fear of the Slaughterbound. Fear of **precedence**: that the print he had hidden on the fourth corpse belonged to the man still breathing in the spiral center; that the trail had been Chapter work all along; that he had brought youths to witness what Judex owned and Legatus could not order. His hand stayed on **Kaelen**’s chest plate until the scream ended, as if contact could keep the line from breaking inward.

— **Hold** — he said again, though no one had moved. — **Hold.**

No one cheered. *Quinta* did not cheer slaughter that had not been theirs.

---

### The pan after

The Slaughterbound’s lacerator slowed, teeth still whirring, then stuttered to stop with the mechanical petulance of weapons that had lost their argument. The daemonic claw lay open on salt, palm up, as if waiting for a god to take receipt. Warp heat was gone. Only copper and cooling ceramite and the oil stink of chain teeth.

**Einar** stood over the lord one more moment — marine visage, chains, wolf and demon still in the same grip per the Judex files the sergeant would never read. Cowl back in place when the scream finished. Dreadwolf Claws retracted flush. He did not look at the rim. He did not look at the three Exalted. He looked at the seaward treeline once, landward reed once, as if measuring who had been close enough to **audit** without being close enough to **share**.

**Varro** began to close from the landward side, not running, not calling out. **Armin Sturmwahl** began to close from the sea, grav stud untouched, assault grey cutting the bowl lip at an angle that would intersect the spiral throat without crossing the line element. They had not traveled together. They had converged anyway. Precipitation was not finished. It had only finished **killing**.

The investigation element breathed because breathing had been withheld. Victory had not been shared. Witness had.

What came next would be words — Judex, Legatus, ops update, hunt resumed. What came next would not be geometry.

The reef waited for the men who owned those words to take their places between the Lost wolf and the line.

---

## VII. PRECIPITATION

**Varro** finished the landward walk first. **Armin Sturmwahl** finished the seaward walk a heartbeat later. They had not traveled together. They did not apologize for the simultaneity. They took positions between the Lost wolf and the line element with the practiced geometry of men who had been converging on the same fear from different compass points and had never agreed what the fear **named**.

---

### Between

From the rim, the picture was simple enough to break a neophyte’s sleep: Judex cowl, Legatus grey, red-limbed *Amissi* between them and the cult-slaughter the youths had mistaken for the enemy’s work. Behind that triangle, three Exalted dead and one Slaughterbound lord cooling in salt. Behind the triangle’s back, seven brothers who had been told to **hold** and were learning that holding was not the absence of violence but the discipline of **not becoming** violence’s second invoice.

**Varro** did not look at the Slaughterbound. He looked at **Einar**’s plate — gorget seam, pauldron seal, the places corruption wore when corruption won. **Armin** did not look at the three Exalted. He looked at **Einar**’s chest, where scream aftermath worked itself into silence one breath at a time.

They exchanged no greeting. They did not need to. The glance that passed between Judex and Legatus was enough: *necessity* on one face, *corruption* on the other, both filing the same man in different ledgers.

The sergeant felt the pan tilt toward law. His hand left **Kaelen**’s chest plate only when **Varro**’s cowl dipped once — audit in motion, not complete.

---

### Judex line

Varro’s voice was a nail driven through air that had just been filled with screaming.

— **No Batavi blood on the pan** — he said, to the sergeant, without looking at him. — **Hold your line.**

The sergeant held.

— **Aye** — he answered, because answer was required and because disobedience here would be performance, not courage.

Varro’s crimson hands did not move. His attention stayed on **Einar** — on the mandible line under cowl, on the cable bulk the cloth hid, on the question whether the Red had steered during termination or had only **tested** the chain and found it still marine. The Judex had shadowed this hunt for hours. He had watched cultists die in grammar the line element would follow. He had not intervened because intervention belonged to **failure**, and failure had not yet arrived.

— **Youth element** — Varro added, still not turning. — **File cult closure. Do not file my position. Do not file his stride.**

Brother **Kaelen** swallowed audibles again. The sergeant understood: the slate would lie by omission, and omission was sometimes how *Vermilion* protected neophytes from growing legends they were not cleared to carry correctly.

---

### Brother question

**Armin** stepped one pace closer to **Einar** than Varro’s geometry strictly required. Legatus privilege, or brother privilege, or the old jump-qualified habit of standing where you could catch a falling man if gravity decided to collect.

He did not look at the Slaughterbound corpse.

— **Was it necessary** — Sturmwahl asked.

Not pleading. Not accusation. A **question** filed where only two men could hear it — though the rim heard the shape of it anyway and would remember the pause more than the words.

**Einar**’s voice, when it came, was tide-flat.

— **The bind was seating. Three exalted already fed it. The lord was last.**

Armin held the geometry in his mouth a moment, tasting verdict before law gave it. Moraine-Nine lived behind his teeth: eleven names, withheld cull, a salute that had been **mercy** because Varro’s eyes had measured **bounded** before the gladius finished its argument. This pan was not Moraine-Nine. It was still the same brother in red hands, still the same question dressed in new armor.

— **You spent yourself on the way in.**

— **I spent cultists** — Einar said. — **They were not yours to find alive.**

The line element heard *cultists* and felt pieces slide into place with the ugly click of truth. The sergeant did not confirm. Confirmation was not his rank. He kept his face where Needles had taught him to keep it: tide-table flat, assault specialist neutral, a man who had hidden a traction print and would hide more before the day was done.

**Armin** looked at the three Exalted once, finally — not worship, not fear, **arithmetic**.

— **Could the line have closed the net without this?**

— **No** — Einar said. — **Your geometry is line geometry. The lord required mine.**

Not cruelty. Weather report.

---

### Verdict

Varro’s cowl dipped a second time — smaller motion, final inch of audit.

— **Armor holds** — the Judex said. — **Corruption did not wear the plate. Watch interval resumes.**

To the sergeant, that was the real terror: not that the wolf had failed, but that **failure had been measured** and found **bounded**. Terror with relief braided inside it, the way *Quinta* braided mercy and murder on Moraine-Nine and called the result **procedure**.

**Armin** did not contradict the Judex. He did not assent aloud either. He filed *necessary* and *bounded* in different pockets and would live with the weight between them the way Legatuses lived with any weight that did not kill them on the ridge line.

— **Varro** — Sturmwahl said, not looking at the cowl. — **If watch resumes, the line withdraws on my word.**

— **On your word** — Varro said. — **After my dip.**

The cowl dipped. *Bounded; proceed* — the motion the sergeant would later teach as law without teaching the story behind it.

---

### Ops update

**Einar** turned toward the spiral’s outer dark where subsidiary nodes still stank of incense and unfinished choir. Operations update, delivered the way other men delivered weather reports — no performance for the rim, no legend curated for neophytes who would mythologize anyway without his help.

— **Two subsidiary binds east on the chain. Reef coordinates follow on encrypted burst. Do not follow my vector. Your geometry is line geometry. You will die in mine.**

His wrist slate pulsed once. The sergeant’s did not receive the burst — clearance, distance, kindness. **Armin**’s slate chimed low; Legatus entitlement to the hunt’s continuation without entitlement to the hunt’s **stride**.

No appeal to the youths. **Kaelen** opened his mouth. **Varro**’s cowl shifted one degree. **Kaelen** closed it.

**Einar** looked at **Armin** once — not familial, not cold: **recognition** between men who had jumped the Needles and no longer shared a pack rhythm but still shared a count.

— **We hold, as the Needles hold the cold tide** — Einar said.

The cadence was not for the line. It was for the Legatus who had once needed to hear it on a salt pan where eleven names burned and one name did not.

**Varro** did not repeat the words. Judex did not borrow *Quinta* cadence unless liturgy required it. He only watched the wolf prepare to leave and measured, one last time, whether corruption would wear the armor when motion resumed.

It did not.

Precipitation had filed its verdict. What remained was **hunt**.

---

## VIII. HUNT

Then **Einar** ran.

---

### Hunt

Not retreat. **Hunt** — into mangroves, into wrong spacing, into the distance that had always steadied him before the lattice could over-sharpen his wildness again. **Steppenwolf** registry and *Amissi* watch did not contradict in motion: both were the same confession that pack warmth was not his stabilizer. The reef was.

Dreadwolf Claws flashed once in brack shadow — knuckle blades catching tide light, retracting flush — and were gone. Birds hesitated, then failed to sing. Brack went still in a pocket and stayed still, as if the water remembered who had passed and chose not to gossip.

**Varro** did not run after him. Judex did not chase wolves; Judex **measured** them and let distance do the work watch required. The cowl turned landward, following the frequency of motion without admitting pursuit. Watch interval resumed on the ledger the sergeant would never read.

**Armin** did not run either. Legatuses did not chase Lost brothers into spacing that had already refused line geometry. He stood one more heartbeat on the salt pan with the *Quinta* cadence still in his ears and the Moraine-Nine annex still in his thumb, then turned toward the rim where his authority **did** reach.

---

### Rim after

The sergeant exhaled. The sound was small and ugly and human enough that **Kaelen** jumped inside his helm — one spasm of relief, quickly buried.

— **Withdraw** — the sergeant said. — **On Legatus signal when it comes. Until then, hold lip.**

It came. **Armin** raised one fist. The element descended from the bowl in reverse order — youths first, veterans screening, no one looking back at the Slaughterbound lord longer than duty required. Gene-grace sacks still absent. Investigation geometry closing as **cult net collapsed at apex** on a slate that would omit names the way *Vermilion* preferred.

On *Salt Ration*, later, clerks would file cult closure and warp smear reduction. They would not file **precipitation**. They would not file three Exalted dead in wrong geometry. They would not file a Slaughterbound terminated without trophy. Neophytes would do that work anyway, badly and beautifully, in barracks where law was still young enough to want **size**.

---

### Legend register

The youths would speak of this for decades.

They would call the enemy a **greater daemon** because young mouths needed size to match what their eyes had seen. They would call **Einar** the wolf who never knelt, in eternal agony, as if agony were the same as glory. They would forget the cultists — or remember them only as props in a story about the Lost — because props were easier than arithmetic.

Veterans would not correct them. Correction was cruelty. Melancholy would sit in assault bays like tide tables: *he was ours before the ship; he is still ours; he is not yours to follow.*

Brother **Kaelen** would one day tell the story to recruits who had not been on the rim. He would get the boot print wrong. He would get the enemy’s rank wrong. He would get the **necessary** right, because the pause after *Was it necessary* had been louder than steel.

The sergeant would never mention the traction claw scoring he had hidden with his knee. He would file *witnessed bounded closure* in a pocket that was not the official slate and would teach **hold** on the next insert until the word meant what the pan had taught it: *do not become the second invoice.*

---

### What the reef kept

**Armin** was the last Batavi to leave the bowl lip. He looked once at the three Exalted, once at the lord, once at the spiral stones that had been worship and had become a kill-box. He did not pray. *Quinta* did not pray at sites Brass had touched. He sealed his gorget and walked seaward to a cutter that would take him back to a war he could command.

**Varro** had already vanished into mangrove shadow on his own bearing — not Einar’s bearing, never identical, parallel enough to keep corruption from wearing the armor when the next bind tried to seat.

The reef kept smell.

It kept, also, the memory of geometry that was **not** Batavi — red limbs on vertical stone, Dreadwolf Claw stutter, a scream that was not cadence, Judex and Legatus taking positions between wolf and line as if the Chapter were one body arguing with itself in public. It kept the image of a brother running away into work that had no parade and no end, because the hunt did not end when the lord fell. The hunt ended when the mesh ran out of nets or the wolf did.

Until then, precipitation was only the weather the frontier used when vectors collided without permission.

The tide, for once, had been denied its receipt on the cult’s terms.

Brass had been denied its trophy on the lord’s corpse.

The Lost wolf had walked back into distance — still **Unbent** in the only filing that mattered on a reef that did not read Administratum scrolls.

---

### Precipitation (oral register)

In *Quinta* mouths the pan would acquire a name slower than **Red Tide** and less fit for *Alabarda* cloth: **Precipitation** — the day the trail was already Chapter work, the day three Exalted died off-screen in wrong geometry, the day a Slaughterbound lord fell and a scream replaced liturgy. Barracks would compress it into one sentence for neophytes who needed a hook:

*He was already out there when we arrived. We held the rim. Varro measured. Sturmwahl asked. The wolf ran.*

Veterans would add nothing.

Silence was the other cadence the Needles taught.
