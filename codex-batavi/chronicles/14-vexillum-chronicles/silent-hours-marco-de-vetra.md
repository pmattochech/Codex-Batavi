## I. BAIT-MOON ARITHMETIC

Marco de Vetra was never a secret. It was a **promise** the *Strategium* wrote in plain language: *we will put the cry here*, *we will put the laser here*, *we will let the enemy know* — because a bait-moon that pretends to be subtle is **cheap** expendability instead of **expensive** delay.

**Cassian Vetra** stood where the charts said the first shell should land and accepted the arithmetic without flinching. His war was measured in **seconds** bought for traffic the Wall needed more than it needed him.

To the crews — human, tired, convinced holiness had abandoned the Threshold — he spoke in a cadence borrowed from **Varro**’s public rites. Not faith theater. **Signal discipline**: breath paced, sentences ending on downbeats so scared lungs could **sync**.

— **Let them spend shells on me.** He said it once per rotation, exactly once, because repetition would make it a slogan and slogans **echoed wrong** in hash. — **The shout is not mine — it is yours, behind me.**

## II. THE STORM IN THE WIRE

Maleventum-class EM hash does not care about courage. It cares about **noise**.

*Auspex* ghosted. *Vox* chewed syllables into static. The Shouter’s farm — acres of glass and metal pretending to be a single throat — flickered between **ready** and **liar** on status boards until technicians started laughing wrong.

Vetra kept **manual** redundancies hot — cables and click-codes the *Armourium* sneered at until the storm proved **elegant** machines could be gagged first.

Raiders came in cutout livery: human pirates, xenos kit where profit allowed, buyers somewhere off-scroll who wanted **arrays intact** — resale value, intel value, the obscene fantasy of **capture** without paying the **scrap** price.

*Nona* intended to **spoil** the merchandise.

---

## III. LAYERED DELAY

Suppression interlocked the alleys between array farms the way *Quarta* interlocked trenches — different gravity, same religion: **geometry** eats time.

Boltstorm and heavy bolter teams rotated on **ninety-minute** clocks so barrels cooled before thermal alarms became **false witnesses** under EM damp. Sergeants logged heat the way Apothecaries logged fever — not because the *Codex* demanded poetry, because **lies** in the wire killed relay integrity faster than bolter rounds.

Ruin held melta charges on coupling bays like a **threat** made physical: Vetra issued the **scorch** posture order **twice** on the net so no sergeant mistook hesitation for mercy — then authorized **one** preemptive burn, a bay sacrificed **clean** so no buyer handoff could claim **intact** hardware. The flash lit the farm’s underbelly; technicians screamed about budgets until the Legatus’s *vox* cut flat.

— **Budget this:** he said. — **Alive.**

---

## IV. ONE PULSE

When the storm peaked — when hash thickened until even manual lines **stuttered** — Vetra ordered a **single** Shouter pulse on minimal power.

Message body: **classified** in every register open to this chronicle. Existence: **not**.

Noviomagus acknowledged.

Somewhere in the mesh, a clock **reset** that mattered more than any local body count. **Ivar Malevent**, bleeding in other void theater that week, would later call it the same **invoice** in different ink: Threshold brothers paid in different ports for the same **wall**.

---

## V. THIRTY-ONE HOURS

The perimeter stayed **contested** for thirty-one hours — not continuous climax, **grinding** ownership: ten meters won, eight lost, five held because a dead brother’s shield still **angled** right on the deck.

Twenty-seven names went to slabs.

Array damage: **repairable**. Handoff to buyer: **denied**.

When silence finally returned to the wire, it was not peace. It was **absence** Vetra did not trust yet. He kept manual watch another six hours anyway — the flaw that burned through captains, the discipline that kept Marco de Vetra from becoming a **cheap** footnote.

---

## VI. FIELD FOUR

**IX** flies **Silent Hours** on the *Alabarda* not because the battle was loud, but because it was **not allowed** to fall **quiet** in the wrong mouth — proof that *Nona* wins in **receipt**, not kill ratio.

The shout survived. That is the only boast the cloth allows.
