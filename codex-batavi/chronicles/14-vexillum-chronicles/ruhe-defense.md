## I. GRAVITY AS LAW

They did not see the hive fleet. They saw the **shadow** — pressure on auspex, on *vox*, on the animal brain that still lived under ceramite. The Tyranid did not need to be understood to be **felt**: a wrongness in the shingle sky, as if the rock itself had learned to **want**.

**IV** *Quarta* had trained on Incus-Gravis until gravitic boots were not equipment but **habit**. On the trench spine, every step was a debit. The Legatus of the Fourth did not waste metaphors on the open channel.

— **Hourly boot check. Report seal integrity. If your ankles sing, you’re already in debt.**

Branimir — not yet *ivory*, not yet *cataloguer*, only a brother who had learned ammonia leaks on his father’s shift — pressed his tongue to the roof of his mouth and **listened** to his squad the way he listened to faulty recyclate: not for courage, for **deviation**.

The trench smelled of ozone, old blood, and crushed stone. Somewhere behind the line, hives waited for the spine to **hold** or **fail**.

---

## II. THE VEIL

*Auspex* lied politely first. Then it lied **badly**.

Ranges jumped. Friend-tags ghosted. The Shadow in the Warp was not a sermon; it was **static** in the machine and **pressure** behind the eyes — swarm-scale, the kind of veil that made sane men reach for triggers **early** because waiting felt like consent.

The Legatus forbade early.

— **Semi-auto only on crest. Missiles trade targets like clerks — I want receipts, not poetry. Storm shields on the choke. You do not chase glory downslope; you **anchor**.**

Branimir’s heavy bolt rifle spoke when told. Assignment, not volume. He watched Sergeant **Korval** — name logged because his auspex discipline became a *Strategium* footnote — refuse to mag-dump into a ripple of gaunts that **looked** like breakthrough. Korval killed three with three pulls, adjusted two meters left, killed two more. The rest of the ripple learned geometry the hard way.

Branimir noticed something else: Korval’s hands did not shake. Neither did his own.

He filed that under *wrong*, not *good*. Instinct before theory.

---

## III. TRYGON

The Trygon did not announce itself with theater. Stone **split**. The trench became a throat opening.

First brother in the hole died **fast** — mercy dressed as physics.

Second died **slow** under claws while his squad mate drove a melta lance through synapse flesh and paid for the angle with a shoulder crushed to **paste** — ceramite screaming, Branimir close enough to hear servos argue with bone.

The Legatus did not shout encouragement. He **adjusted** the line — two meters left, one fire team down, plug the hole with Ruin before the ripple became rout.

Branimir threw himself into the gap without hero language. His world narrowed to **muzzle** and **claw** and the obscene **heat** of xenos breath. He fired until his barrel sang wrong, changed mag by touch because eyes could not afford the sky, and when the Trygon’s tail whipped stone into shrapnel, his left clavicle — already scored in **047.M42** mining work — took **fresh** lace. Same geography of pain, new receipt: **metal** does not apologize.

When the beast finally **folded**, it did so like a machine deprived of command — not dead enough to be clean, dead enough to stop moving.

---

## IV. WHAT THE APOTHECARY WROTE

Drusus walked the trench after — not with field-medic theatrics, with **registrar** silence. He knelt beside brothers who could still speak and brothers who could not, and his voice on the forearm unit was the same temperature for both.

Branimir watched him pause at Korval — vitals stable, eyes **too** steady.

Drusus did not say *Ruhe*. The word was not yet doctrine the line could wear without breaking.

He logged **coefficients**: reaction temperatures **flat** where fear should spike; pupils **correct** where panic should dilate. Margin note, later quoted in closed *Medicinae* minutes: *“Veil-adjacent detachment — distinguish from combat shock before treatment.”*

Branimir did not understand the math. He understood **threat**: if the Apothecary studied you like a specimen, you were either **damaged** or **useful**.

He decided to be useful.

---

## V. THE ORBITAL WINDOW

When the first Navy lance struck beyond the cloud deck, the trench **jumped**. Dust and xenos ichor rose in one plume — sacred and obscene married in the same breath.

No one cheered.

Brothers who could stand **counted** ammunition and **checked** spine integrity for cracks that would shame the *Armourium* survey. The Legatus walked the line once, helm on, *vox* limited to **acknowledge** and **continue**.

Losses sat in the *Strategium* **acceptable** envelope — a phrase that tastes like ash beside a stack of ceramite slabs waiting for names. Branimir learned that day that **acceptable** was a number someone else signed.

---

## VI. AFTERMATH (THE COHORT’S DEBT)

Years later, when Silence Cohort instructors spoke of **pattern** without Shadow in the Warp, they would cite men who had learned vigilance because biology **failed** to scream when it should.

Branimir’s dossier would record **Noviomagus Secundus *Ruhe*** and the **collective** stillness of the Fourth’s cohort — chronicle-lawyers argue whether that title maps **exactly** to this Incus-Gravis line or to a sister action in the same crusade season. The Chapter does not care for cartographers of pain. It cares that **IV** *Quarta* **held**.

---

## VII. FIELD FOUR

*Strategium* certified **Ruhe** not as a poem about courage but as a **reference hold**: high gravity, Tyranid veil, static war — proof the Chapter could **not move** and still **win**.

The *Alabarda* remembers the line that did not break before the sky did.
