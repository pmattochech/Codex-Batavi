## I. TWO LOCKS, ONE THROAT

The traitors held the gulf like a pair of hands around a throat — not metaphor, **geometry**.

**Korvus-A**’s macro-vault thrust from the waterward cliff like a fist of adamantium: storm cannon commanding open water, sensor spine watching every approach a Navy boat could invent. **Korvus-B** hung from the skyhook tether across the gulf — inner **spine** packed with reactor bleed and scuttle rites wired to **consensus**: if A fell **alone**, B would **vote** to burn the tether and drown the hab between them in vacuum and fire.

Their plate carried **no** legion marks the *Strategium* would print on a tithe scroll. Their habits carried **siege** — overlapping fields, murder holes, the patient cruelty of men who had decided the *Codex* was a cookbook and they only liked one recipe.

**Gervas Bifronte** studied both faces on the hololith the way other men studied **one**. *Strategium* scribes had already coined his name from orders that never split the world cleanly: **Gate A / Gate B** as a **single** equation.

---

## II. THE VAULT DOES NOT HURRY

Pre-breach, his *vox* was slow as tide.

— **The vault does not hurry.** Pause long enough to let the word *vault* mean both forts. — **Neither do we — until we do.**

Siege division brothers checked storm shield seals. Aggressor cadres ran thermal tolerances on their plates until *Armourium* ticks went green. Terminators stood in the assault boats like statues that had learned to **breathe** on a schedule.

Across the gulf, Gravis Eradicator teams rappelled routes on Korvus-B’s ventral sill — **predators** under a bridge, waiting for the moment the bridge’s owner blinked.

Bifronte switched channels.

— **Breach minute. Staccato only. No poetry.**

---

## III. ONE MACHINE, TWO MOUTHS

The boats hit Korvus-A’s landings.

The sound was not two battles. It was **one machine** with two mouths — Terminator thunder and Aggressor drumfire chewing landing corbels while the storm cannon tried to **depress** fast enough to kill the waterline. Shells threw geysers into the gulf; *Sexta* did not **pause** for the spectacle. They **measured** it — splash height, reload cadence, the cannon’s dead zone when traverse lagged.

On B, the first bulkhead cutter kissed metal.

Bifronte counted on *vox* — not inspiration, **numbers**. His voice dropped into the cadence every *Sexta* sergeant had drilled until dreams used it:

— **Three. Two. One. Mark.**

The first melta touched Korvus-A’s vault seam **on the same second** the thermal lance bit Korvus-B’s spine bulkhead. *Auspex* logs later framed the signatures as **textbook** — two peaks, one tick, ninety seconds’ grace **spent** exactly as authorized.

Inside B, traitor hands leapt for scuttle rites.

Some hands **stopped**.

The public annex calls the rest **classified**: Nullity-adjacent interference; Ruin teams moving **faster** than heresy could finish a prayer; a *blank*-weighted corridor the enemy had not mapped because faith in scanners is a kind of **sin**. The Chapter does not print how the consensus **broke**. It prints **result**: tether **held**, scuttle **unarmed**, cannon **silent** when A’s vault guts spilled open and *Sexta* walked the inner ring like accountants with bolters.

---

## IV. THE GULF AFTER

When the firing stopped, ozone and **cooked blood** replaced salt spray on the tongue.

Forty-four names went to slabs — *Sexta*’s price for buying **both** gates in one breath. The memorial wall would not sort them by fort; the certification was **twin**, not **half**. Brothers who died on A’s steps shared eternity with brothers who fell from B’s sill, because *Friction Geometry* had demanded they die as **one** ledger line.

Bifronte walked the tether hab himself — slow again, post-breach monk tempo — because *Reclusiam* had warned him: **political** speed follows **civilian** memory, and memory needed to see grey plate **checking** airlocks instead of **claiming** thrones.

---

## V. FIELD FOUR

Siege culture in Bifrons-Ferrum is not love of walls. It is **hatred** of **asymmetry** left unanswered.

The *Alabarda* for **VI** remembers **simultaneity** — two surfaces, one clock — proof *Sexta* is not merely **heavy**, but **synchronized**: the twin-gate warden’s answer to a gulf that wanted to **choose** which sin the Imperium paid first.
