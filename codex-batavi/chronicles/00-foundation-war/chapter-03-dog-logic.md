# The Crucible Book I — *Dog Logic*

**Foundation War** · **Chapter 3 of 38** · *Dog Logic*  
**Strategium anchor:** CV y2  
**Theater:** Pole I — the Moon Hunt  
*Classification update (provisional): **Type A**—display predation; **Type B**—pack clearance / area denial. On-page names for hostile culture: **none**.*

---

The Chapter had changed its watch’s **heartbeat**—routes un-spooled, rally points reborn each night, vox discipline tightened until even prayer learned to whisper. Still Rhylan’s empty rack accused them each dawn; still the bolt shell stood upright on a pack no one had yet dared strip for parts, spiral mud drying to a memory of fingers too patient to be kind.

They told themselves *discipline* would answer **arrangement**.

Until CV y2, most still believed the enemy *hunted*—singular appetite, duelist’s sacrament, the kind of war knights recognize in mirrors.

**Dog logic** arrived with the rain: not one stalker composing a tableau, but a **pack** bred— or trained— for *clearance*.

---

The **Black Fan** spread west of the Needles’ rain-shadow: a mud flat glazed with oil-sheen from seeps the locals burned for night-fires, framed by mangrove roots that rose like **teeth** in triplicate—the romance of geography always hungry.

Major **Elyan Certo** drew his cordon across that fan as if straight lines could shame a planet into obedience.

Militarum cadre, “coordination liaison”—a title that meant *we own the bodies you spend*—Certo had a voice tuned on parade squares and a husband’s grave on a world whose name he would not speak aloud, not even to officers he liked. He believed mortals could be **geometry**. He believed flanking Astartes center was *honor*.

“Marines hold the spine,” he sent over a channel Alaric had not blessed and could not silence without snapping the treaty that kept three thousand auxilia from becoming three thousand liabilities overnight. “We flank. We **guide** your fire. We show the Segmentum we are not cargo.”

**Sergeant Kessler**—mud still bleaching her flak white in strange patterns—caught his elbow hard enough to hurt a man who did not wear plate.

“Sir,” she said, “the Needles say the Fan waits. Not like The Throat—*different*. Iria says—”

“I don’t pay tide-witches to think,” Certo snapped, and the sentence did what snapped steel does: it cut the air too clean for apology.

Iria, small against the mangrove line, lifted neither hand in omen nor plea. She only **looked** at Alaric once—helmed, immovable—then at mud, as if asking the world which hunger would win.

Alaric did not countermand Certo on an open channel. Wars of paper kill slower than wars of mud, but they kill all the same.

He switched to Triumvirate band. “Kadmos—center mass. Varro—bind panic when it comes. Drusus—**triage permission** for cordon breach; do not lecture me on *purity* if flesh blocks bolts.”

Drusus: “Acknowledged.”

Varro: “Silence will be weaponized.”

Kadmos, almost a snarl: “I hate politics.”

“Then love **range**,” Alaric said.

---

The enemy did not arrive as hunters **present** themselves—no shimmer of stalker pride, no arranged lesson left for theologians.

Faunal **flankers** hit first: low, slab-muscled shapes the auspex mis-filed for precious seconds as *fauna*—Aethelgard’s bitter joke—until they tore through kneecaps and Achilles vectors with the bored efficiency of things **handled** on a leash.

Then the **enforcers** rose from mud that should not have hidden mass: broad silhouettes, breach-weight, movements that owed nothing to honor and everything to *minutes saved*. They did not collect trophies. They did not line boots. They **removed** problems with the industrial contempt of soldiers answering an impatient officer a thousand years dead.

Overlapping fields of fire—**their** fire—danced with Marine reactions trained for *knightly* violence. Feints drew eyes; second waves existed to **stampede** mortals into angles where hesitation would kill allies as surely as blades.

Certo’s cordon became a **funnel** he had not built and could not close.

He died screaming a name that was not his husband’s—some virtue-title burned into Academy brass—while mud took his chest and something like laughter rode the oil-rain without using human throats.

Kessler lived because rage is buoyancy. She dragged a boy—sixteen, Administratum dreams still stuck in his teeth—when **Drusus** cleared the line in a vault obscene with economy: surgical chainblade kiss along a flanker spine mid-lunge, weight transfer flawless, anger present only as **precision** if you knew him well enough to look.

The boy’s throat stayed intact.

His eyes did not.

---

Afterward, on a promontory slick as confessing glass, Alaric watched Marines breathe like men who had expected applause and received **arithmetic**.

Kadmos swore softly—no parade oaths now—voice hollowed.

“We prepared for a knight,” he said. “They fed us a **siege animal**.”

Threx’s lander coughed heat at the treeline; red lenses advanced with samples hungry as marriage. Alaric turned his helm once—the gesture entire warning.

“Swabs,” Drusus spat toward the Genetor without turning. “**Blood on the Fan only.** Nothing labeled Batavi without my seal.”

Threx’s vox crackled amusement drowned in politesse. “Apothecary’s generosity is noted.”

Iria climbed until stone granted her height, cloak clattering.

“You met the **soldiers**,” she said—not loud, but carrying. “The **hunters** will think them *noisy*.”

Drusus cleaned his blade without ceremony. When he spoke, it was not comfort. It was **naming**—the first crude taxonomy that would later save them and shame them by turns.

“Event classification updating. Threat dichotomy: **Type A**—display predation. **Type B**—area denial pack tactics.”

Varro inclined his skull toward the dark where mangroves pretended to be innocent.

“Two gospels,” he said. “One war.”

Wind pulled oil-rain sideways like a veil.

Somewhere under the Fan, tide fingers searched pockets the living had vacated—**counting** what would wash up when mercy or mockery next chose to speak.

The Chapter called it victory on vox because **victory** is sometimes the lie that keeps throats un-slit until morning.

Alaric did not say *We hold.*

He watched the mud and learned **dog logic**: packs do not debate knightly things; they **finish**.
