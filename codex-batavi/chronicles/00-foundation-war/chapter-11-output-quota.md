# The Crucible Book II — *Output Quota*

**Foundation War** · **Chapter 11 of 38** · *Output Quota*  
**Strategium anchor:** CV y9–y10  
**Theater:** Pole II — Incus-Gravis Secundus  
*Strategium tag (wrong): **industrial unrest / cult-of-output**. Apothecarion scratch-tag: **cohort synchrony anomaly**—see sealed file *Brood-Flutter*.*

---

Hooks—not bombs—had bought them time. Time, in Incus, translated into **quotas**: numbers that climbed because fear climbed faster, because *Lex Ignis* auditors smiled at throughput, because guild factors discovered **virtue** in overtime until overtime became liturgy. Tithe mathematics are theology with better stationery. When quotas rose, **wombs** rose too.

**Midwife halls** doubled as census temples: white tile scrubbed until holiness looked like hygiene, votive candles illegal and present anyway—little treasons mortals committed against regulation to keep their minds *animal*. Drusus entered not as voyeur but as **actuary**. Consent for blood draws was extracted the way you extract teeth: quickly, with pretense of mercy, paperwork signed in names women could not read fast enough to flinch from. He did not explain why he needed third-shift samples *and* first-shift *and* swing—**he never explained enough to comfort**, only enough to comply. Biometrics **in absentia** became obsession: cohort heart rates piped through vox-analog lines; footsteps syncing across levels that should not share drumbeats; micro-tremors in line workers’ hands aligning shift to shift like choir practice. “A hive doesn’t **flutter**,” Kadmos scoffed over hololith, armor still powdered with Incus ash. “This one does,” Drusus replied, and threw up graphs that pulsed like a **second dawn** beneath the first. Varro leaned in—skull reflecting data he pretended not to worship. “If it is a choir,” he said, “who conducts.” Drusus had no answer worth ink.

Varro took rites *into* the factories—not triumphal Ministorum theater, but **threaded** discipline: whispers against ancient pistons, binds laid on breath until breath became **rhythm** workers could not hear without obeying. Some flinched. Some prayed without knowing which god listened. Some hated him for making holiness smell like **sweat**—as if sweat were not always holy in places where bodies were priced. **Mira Kell** watched from a gallery with eyes that counted **everything** except what she chose not to. **Valerius** moved among foremen, separating *threat* from *grievance* the way engineers separate load from noise—dry love, useful love.

When ***Lex Ignis*** **security** pivoted—Skitarii overwatch rotating a spotlight into a union hall like a stylus carving **guilt** into faces—riot answered. Molten glass: fast, bright, cutting. Words became bricks. Bricks became **falling**. Santo’s militia block—boys drunk on duty and drama—tried **human shields** because holodramas taught them virtue looked like standing in front of las. Here, virtue looked like **obedience** to geometry that spared strangers. Alaric moved through smoke helmed, **inevitable**. One controlled blow—palm/sternum physics calibrated the way Drusus calibrated plungers—put militia pride **down** without snapping the boy’s spine. Santo hit decking; breath left; honor crawled back slower. “You will not become *cover*,” Alaric said—voice not shout, *law*. “You will become **men**.” Lip split; blood thin as confession. Shame hurt worse—**always does**, in romances where boys wish to be knights before they learn walls. **Kessler** dragged two kids behind a column and cursed them **safe** with profanity sanctified by volume control.

Behind a clinic curtain, a midwife counted contractions in rhythm that matched a vox-chant **no station played**—pattern older than guild charters. Drusus heard it through a bulkhead and felt his stomach perform a marine’s forbidden thing: **flip**. He logged **biologic harmonic**. He locked the file with a curse he did not voice—*Brood-Flutter*—and stared at his own hands as if they’d touched something that left residue **ideas** could carry. The brood whispered in **human** time. The Chapter—still blind to the full name of its foe—printed posters calling it **labor unrest**. Administratum loved posters. Administratum loved *simple*.

Love stories, the old romances taught, begin in **misrecognition**. Incus was falling in love with its own hunger and calling it **output**.
