# The Crucible Book II — *Flare Geometries*

**Foundation War** · **Chapter 13 of 38** · *Flare Geometries*  
**Strategium anchor:** CV y11  
**Theater:** Pole VI — Ignis-Minor · **Drukhari** raid cadence  
*Imperial filing (surface): **xenos slave raid / thermal disaster**. Strategium truth: **hostage geometry** priced in Marine seconds.*

---

The Ring’s teeth had taught them mouths. **Ignis-Minor** taught them **flares**—solar violence dressed as industry, polite as a forge until it turned your shadow into **paper** and your flesh into rumor. Gravity here was honest only when it hurt. Thermal miners worked harnessed to cliffs that *flared* without warning—**solar belches** the Administratum called “scheduled venting” because liars love calendars. Silhouettes became memories between heartbeats.

The Drukhari did not arrive as invasion. They arrived as **geometry**. Raider hulls cut heat-shimmer into planes a mortal eye refused to stack correctly—too sharp, too **proud**—and boarding hooks fell like calipers measuring **fear**. Slave-spears **priced** bodies the way merchants price fish: quick appraisal, wet arithmetic, *minutes* of Marine hesitation bought cheap if you positioned mortals where hesitation would be **sin**. **Kessler’s** auxilia—boys she had taught to breathe—got caught in gantries because void training is not **thermal** training; panic wore new clothes here and fit just as fatally. She swore vox would hear her hatred later. On the ground, hatred was **useless**; placement was survival.

Hostage math followed with the beauty of a theorem written in screaming: if Alaric moved *now*, twelve died; if he waited *now*, twenty died *differently*; if Kadmos failed to find the **flicker**, everyone learned death’s favorite joke—*later*. **Captain Ossian**—name later struck from praise-scrolls because gratitude embarrassed men trained to despise gratitude—made a trade not out of courage and not out of cowardice, but out of arithmetic he could **live inside**. Half a company pushed into a kill-channel that bought **ninety seconds**. Ninety seconds for Kadmos to cage a webway echo with auspex teeth. “Clock it,” Alaric said—not louder than flare-roar; *final*. Kadmos **clocked** it. The universe, for once, pretended punctuality was mercy.

The raid did not break like a line breaks. It **withdrew**—aristocrats bored at a peasant show, laughter rattling vox-static into syllables that did not need translation: *Pretty prey.* Drusus treated burns that were also **lessons**: skin cooked to doctrine, nerve maps screaming data about *pain bandwidth* he would rather not possess. Varro bound the dying to **silence**—not cruelty; containment against despair’s contagion, against screaming that would teach the living to **jump** off cliffs next. **Valerius** went ash-pale under station lights and tallied **debt** in columns that made ships look possible and souls look **expensive** in the wrong currency. Alaric stood amid thermal reek and did not remove his helm. Helms were not modesty here; they were **continuity**.

On a data-slate buried under tactical overlays, a personnel flag blinked—easy to ignore, easier to **feel**. *Needles envoy requests return.* **Iria**. Alaric denied it without cruelty, because cruelty would have wasted time. *Not yet.* Some loves—in the old romances—are expressed by refusing to call someone into fire until fire is **honest** about its appetite.

Romances teach that love and slavery share a grammar until one learns **refusal**. The Chapter refused—messily, at cost—with bolt and triangulation and half a company’s grief pressed into ninety seconds like powder into a casing. Ignis flared again, indifferent. Men learned the flare did not **hate**. It simply **consumed**, which was worse.
