# The Crucible Book II — *Ring of Teeth*

**Foundation War** · **Chapter 12 of 38** · *Ring of Teeth*  
**Strategium anchor:** CV y10  
**Theater:** Pole V — Logus Ring (void breaker-yard)  
*Threat mix: **Chaos-adjacent wreck-cults** (visible); supply war vs **Mechanicus** salvage claims; *Lex Ignis* charter friction.*

---

Incus’s ash clung in greaves long after translation—then **void** scrubbed it away and replaced it with cold that laughed at memory. The Chapter did not leave the hive behind so much as **carry** it under armor: Brood-Flutter graphs locked; Santo’s split lip; Mira’s watching eyes—cargo that weighed more than crates. New theater: the **Logus Ring**.

The Ring was a breaker-yard orbiting nothing worth naming aloud—**kilometers** of hulls stripped and stacked until they resembled teeth worshipping void, curved inward like a mouth that had swallowed fleets and **chewed** until only ribs remained. Scrapper clans called it **Sanctum** when drunk and **Maw** when sober. They signed contracts in blood-finger and wax like penitents signing indulgences, then learned signatures meant **pressgang**: bodies reclassified as *auxilia voidcrew* because the Administratum loved verbs that turned people into **inventory**. **Kessler**—NCO patience burned down to a wick—found herself teaching boys who had never known sky except as rumor how to **breathe** in rebreather cadence without panic. Panic void-killed faster than las. “Again,” she said, and they hated her until hate became **habit**, and habit became *breathing*.

Chaos spoke here in **wreck-rites**—not sermons in choirs, but grease-paint theology: plasma couplings daubed with engine oil like unguents; prayers etched into reactor shrouds until machine spirits wheezed **heresy** and obedience in the same cough. Varro walked scaffolds where cult laughter rang hollow as **tanks**. He did not burn every shrine—*not yet*—because fire makes martyrs and martyrs make **routes**. He burned what he could justify to his own skull: idols that had learned to **listen** back.

Mechanicus factors arrived on tenders pretty as creditors at a deathbed, waving salvage charters stamped with cogs until paper looked like **law**. **Kadmos** interdicted with boarding parties and the tired patience of a man denying cousins at a funeral. “**Profit** is not **truth**,” he told a magos emissary. “Truth oxidizes,” the emissary replied—smiling with lips that had forgotten how *not* to. **Threx**—inevitable as rust—hovered in analysis mode, optics drinking hull geometry. “Compliance with charter maximizes recovery timelines,” he offered. Alaric, on the command deck, let the argument play—**war by ink**—because sometimes ink bought minutes bolts could not. **Valerius** slid tallies under the Castellan’s gauntlet: *days of air*, *hours of corridor*, *lies quartermasters tell so men sleep*.

The blade that broke comedy was small—**micro-charges**, a chain meant to **shear** a troop carrier along a weld seam like cutting bread. Marines inside became suddenly *biblical*: men in a split world, gravity arguing with faith. Decompression screamed through corridors. The Batavi moved in tilted geometry—bulkheads become floors, floors become spite—with the ugly grace of practice. They emerged from smoke and spinning lamps like saints climbing from a **sunk ship**, except saints rarely carried bolters and swear-words etched into their teeth. **Varro**’s skull caught dying station light and turned children of scrappers into weepers who did not know why—only that *bone* had become a moon. Alaric did not speak encouragement. He **cleared** until clearing became place—a pocket of held vacuum, a moment where the Ring’s mouth **paused** mid-bite.

After, supply cord stretched until honest quartermasters admitted: *We have **days**.* Days, in void war, are the romance’s honest love poem—**short**, sincere, sufficient to keep marching until the next lie.

In romances, rings of teeth mark the threshold where heroes learn the world is also a **mouth**. The Batavi learned—and filed the lesson beside others—**Unbent** meaning not *unharmed*, but *still clamped jaw when needed*.
