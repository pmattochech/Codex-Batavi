# The Crucible Book II — *Ring of Teeth*

**Foundation War** · **Chapter 12 of 38** · *Ring of Teeth*  
**Strategium anchor:** CV y10  
**Theater:** Pole V — Logus Ring (void breaker-yard)  
*Threat mix: **Chaos-adjacent wreck-cults** (visible); supply war vs **Mechanicus** salvage claims; *Lex Ignis* charter friction.*

---

Incus’s ash clung in greaves long after translation—packed into joint seals, worked into the creases of ceramite until it became a second pigment—then **void** scrubbed it away and replaced it with cold that laughed at memory. Cold did not negotiate; it simply removed the story of warmth until your body believed warmth had been a myth told by traitorous nerves. The Chapter did not leave the hive behind so much as **carry** it under armor: **Brood-Flutter** graphs locked behind biometrics; **Santo**’s split lip a small red argument about boys and honor; **Mira**’s watching eyes a weight no crate manifest could list—cargo heavier than supplies—and beyond the burn the new theater waited with the patience of something that had already eaten once and learned patience was cheaper than hunger.

The **Logus Ring** was a breaker-yard orbiting nothing worth naming aloud. **Kilometers** of hulls stripped and stacked until they resembled teeth worshipping void curved inward like a mouth that had swallowed fleets and **chewed** until only ribs remained—an anatomy lesson taught without textbooks, scale imposed on the eye until the eye stopped trusting distance. Scrapper clans called it **Sanctum** when drunk and **Maw** when sober; they signed contracts in blood-finger and wax like penitents signing indulgences, then learned signatures meant **pressgang** and bodies reclassified as *auxilia voidcrew* because the Administratum loved verbs that turned people into **inventory**. The Ring did not care what you called it. The Ring chewed.

Pressgangs worked in **cycles**—recruit, bleed, replace—because void did not forgive empty bunks the way mud forgave empty boots. Scrappers signed for “**twelve-week** salvage apprenticeships” and learned on day four that *week* was a translation error between Gothic and hunger. Administratum factors stamped *auxilia voidcrew* on throats that still thought themselves free citizens of nowhere important; **Valerius** copied those stamps into a private column labeled *debt transfer* until the column grew tall enough to shame a Navy liaison into admitting, off-record, that the Ring was not a yard but a **mouth** that ate paperwork and called the meal *efficiency*.

Translation from Incus burn to Ring approach took long enough for vox to thin and for men to remember that “sky” was a word invented by planets. **Alaric** stood the strike cruiser’s oculus gallery once, watching the yard resolve from a glint to a jaw, and did not speak. Speech would have invited officers to mistake awe for fear. What he felt was not fear exactly; it was **scale**—the human mind’s old injury when it meets something too large to story-tell without lying. Behind him, **Kadmos** muttered a range solution under his breath, not for anyone’s ears, habit of a man who turned discomfort into numbers because numbers could be held.

The strike cruiser’s auspex painted the yard as **teeth** and then as **targets**—friendly wrecks tagged wrong until techs learned to distrust “friendly,” because friendly hulls could hide listening posts the same way Sallow Morrow’s gallery had hidden paired boots. **Drusus** watched particulate scatter patterns climb in ugly sympathy with old **Brood-Flutter** harmonics and forced himself not to say *choir* aloud; *choir* was a word that turned competent sailors into mystics, and mystics mis-torqued seals. He logged the sympathy as *environmental coupling—unverified* and hated that *unverified* was the same cloak cowardice wore when it dressed as prudence.

**Kessler**—NCO patience burned down to a wick—found herself teaching boys who had never known sky except as rumor how to **breathe** in rebreather cadence without panic, because panic void-killed faster than las and faster than shame. She made them run the cycle until their throats learned obedience: inhale two, hold one, exhale two, *again*, until the mask stopped tasting like suffocation and started tasting like routine. “Again,” she said, and they hated her until hate became **habit**, and habit became *breathing*. She did not tell them she still heard quota chants in her sleep, or that Incus had taught her to fear rhythms that matched without permission. She told them only what void required: *small motions, small panic, small selves—until the bulkhead says otherwise.*

Chaos spoke here in **wreck-rites**—not sermons in choirs, but grease-paint theology: plasma couplings daubed with engine oil like unguents; prayers etched into reactor shrouds until machine spirits wheezed **heresy** and obedience in the same cough; laughter that rang hollow as **tanks** because laughter was how humans kept their throats open when their minds wanted to close. **Varro** walked scaffolds above the cult’s handiwork, *Codex* death-skull helm a fixed witness above grease and oath-marks, censers cold enough to dare anyone to mistake him for spectacle. He did not burn every shrine—*not yet*—because fire makes martyrs and martyrs make **routes**, and routes were what wreck-priests wanted most. He burned only what he could justify to that same bone-mask: idols that had learned to **listen** back—little listening boxes wired into scrap altars, auspex crumbs fed to fetishes until superstition wore cog-teeth. When the flame took, scrappers cheered and Varro did not cheer with them. He watched smoke climb as if smoke were another congregation learning ascension.

A wreck-priest tried to **sell** Varro a “blessed auspex” wired to a child’s toy speaker—cheap miracle, expensive lie—and learned, without sermon, that the *Codex* skull could imply *no* with a tilt alone. The priest retreated into a rib-bay where shadows stacked like unpaid debts. **Drusus** followed only far enough to take air samples—**swabs only**, the old treaty with Mechanicus hunger—and noted hydrocarbons consistent with *intentional* lubricant trails, not accident. “Someone teaches routes here,” he told **Alaric** on a channel too narrow for admiralties. “Not xenos proof. **Curriculum** proof.” Alaric answered with silence that meant *file it*, because filing was how you kept curriculum from becoming **dogma** before evidence paid tuition.

Mechanicus factors arrived on tenders pretty as creditors at a deathbed, hulls buffed until poverty looked like ceremony. They waved salvage charters stamped with cogs until paper looked like **law** and law looked like permission to strip a corpse while the family still wept in the next room. **Kadmos** interdicted with boarding parties and the tired patience of a man denying cousins at a funeral—mag-boots kissing void-black plating, vox-channels polite as knives. In one boarding tube, a void-born serf vomited anyway; **Kessler** caught his shoulder and shoved him back into line with a profanity that doubled as instruction—*aim it sideways, you’re not a fountain*—because disgust was easier to manage than panic, and panic killed tubes faster than cultists. On the tender’s bridge, servitors watched with chrome eyes that pretended not to judge. Kadmos judged anyway. “**Profit** is not **truth**,” he told a magos emissary who wore augmetics like jewelry.

The emissary smiled with lips that had forgotten how *not* to. “Truth oxidizes,” he answered, delighted by his own cleverness.

**Threx**—inevitable as rust—hovered in analysis mode on a secondary bridge feed, optics drinking hull geometry, voice stripped to the same appetite he had brought to Incus throughput. Compliance with charter maximized recovery timelines, he offered, as if timelines were mercy. On the command deck **Alaric** let the argument play—**war by ink**—because sometimes ink bought minutes bolts could not, and minutes in a yard like this were measured in throats. **Valerius** slid tallies under the Castellan’s gauntlet without drama: *days of air*, *hours of corridor*, *lies quartermasters tell so men sleep*. The friction partner’s language of stamps and yield ground against the Chapter’s language of held corridors and living bodies, two kinds of mathematics trying to share one mouth.

The blade that broke comedy was small—**micro-charges**, a chain meant to **shear** a troop carrier along a weld seam like cutting bread—because comedy in void war is the belief that armor makes you whole. The charges spoke in a language too quiet for drama: a cough, a shiver, a seam deciding it had never been a seam, only a suggestion. Then the seam became a mouth. Marines inside became suddenly *biblical*: men in a split world, gravity arguing with faith, faith arguing with training, training doing what it could while the universe pretended physics was a moral failing. Someone laughed—wrong sound, too high—and someone else cursed him silent because laughter in a splitting hull was a door opening onto the void’s favorite joke: *you thought you were inside something*. Red lamps spun; emergency foam coughed from ports and hardened into useless sculpture; a brother’s gauntlet tore through a rent and kissed vacuum for a heartbeat before seals slammed and the hand came back frostbitten and furious. Decompression screamed through corridors—a sound too human to be called wind—and the Batavi moved in tilted geometry, bulkheads become floors, floors become spite, handholds become theology. They emerged from smoke and spinning lamps like saints climbing from a **sunk ship**, except saints rarely carried bolters and swear-words etched into their teeth.

Inside the torn seam, **Kadmos** found micro-charges nested like **teeth inside teeth**—not one betrayal line, a *pattern* suggesting someone had rehearsed hull failure the way Incus had rehearsed break bells. He photographed with his helm archive because photography was harder to edit than memory, then sealed the file under **Triumvirate** biometrics before Threx’s optics could treat images as salvage previews. “If this was cult,” he subvocalized to Drusus, “it was cult with **blueprints**.” Drusus answered, “If this was Mechanicus,”—and stopped, because finishing the sentence would have been a second mouth opening in a yard already crowded with mouths.

**Varro**’s *Codex* death-skull caught dying station light and turned children of scrappers into weepers who did not know why—only that *bone* had become a moon and moons meant judgment in stories older than the Ring. Alaric did not speak encouragement; encouragement would have wasted breath that could be spent clearing. He **cleared** until clearing became place: a pocket of held vacuum, a moment where the Ring’s mouth **paused** mid-bite because something harder than appetite had lodged in its teeth.

After, the supply cord stretched until honest quartermasters admitted *we have **days***—and in void war, days are the romance’s honest love poem: **short**, sincere, sufficient to keep marching until the next lie. One quartermaster—face gray with fatigue, not ash—spread fingers on a tally slate as if touching the numbers could warm them. “If we push another boarding,” he said to Valerius, not to Alaric, because mortals learn which doors are safer, “we eat reserves before we reach Ignis mesh.” Valerius did not promise comfort. He promised **paper**: signatures, stamps, a lie thin enough to march on. The irreversible read was plain in the tallies anyway: the yard had teeth, and teeth did not negotiate. Cult routes were broken or bought; Mechanicus charters were “adjusted” with signatures that would cost someone a career later, when later arrived; **Threx** logged spoilage and opportunity in the same breath because Mechanicus grief wore numbers when it could not wear shame. Alaric listened to Threx’s channel hiss static that wanted to be triumph and settled for being a receipt—same sound as Pier Nine, different theater, same appetite dressed as procedure.

**Valerius** negotiated air the way chaplains negotiated souls—without promising heaven, only **sequence**: first pressure patches, first water, first wounded, first the living secured so tomorrow could not pretend yesterday had been rumor. When a Mechanicus factor tried to slide “**sanctified recovery**” language beside a casualty column, Valerius filed it parallel under *Mechanicus asserts salvage continuity* and forced initials, the same trick he had learned would keep blood off wax for another week—except wax did not exist here; only vacuum, which signed nothing and took everything.

In romances, rings of teeth mark the threshold where heroes learn the world is also a **mouth**. The Batavi learned—and filed the lesson beside others—**Unbent** meaning not *unharmed*, but *still clamped jaw when needed*. **Kessler** counted her auxilia breathers on a slate until counting became prayer. **Kadmos** stared into the Ring’s glitter until glitter became a map he did not want to memorize. **Valerius** closed his personal slate with a click too soft to be satisfaction.

Somewhere in the dark between stacked ribs, a scrapper clan marked a new shrine in oil and ash—small, stupid, human. Varro did not burn it. Not yet. He noted its coordinates for the day fire would be justified, and walked on, *Codex* skull steady, carrying Incus in his bones the way void carried cold: not as memory, as **weight**.

Hololith briefs for the next hop already named **Ignis-Minor**—thermal mines, harness lines, “scheduled venting”—and **Alaric** read the words the way he read wreckage: slowly, without performance. Sunlight as industry sounded like another mouth learning to chew in a register the Chapter had not yet learned to hear. He did not tell the fleet they were marching toward **flares**; he told them they were marching toward **minutes**, because minutes were the only truth void ever sold at honest price.
