## I. SHEPHERD’S HOOK

They took the squad on **Shepherd’s Hook** — a tide relay the annex later misspelled as *Shepherds Hook* in three different hands — when the moons above Moraine-Nine still only **whispered** brass.

**Einar** had held **Furor** for a century and worn it like Needles pride: flame-pool eyes, exhaustion filed as competence, *Axiom* obeyed because the shore had taught him that a Batavi who could not hold himself was a Batavi the tide deserved. He had never crossed into **Exanimus**. He believed — with the quiet arrogance of men who have never been wrong about their own stage — that he never would.

The incursion did not give him the chance to keep that belief.

Khorne-leaning bleed through the relay lattice was not a full breach — not yet — but enough to make corridors **honest** about violence: bulkheads sweating rust-colored condensation, vox carrying laughter that was not Ork and not human, auspex painting heat where no promethium burned. **World Eaters** came with the bleed — not a grand warband parade, a **butcher’s** detachment escorting a prison hulk whose registry the Batavi would later read as **Kzorn’s Maw**.

Twelve *Quinta* brothers. Names that would become sacks on *Brine Ledger*: **Stenmark, Torsten, Brand, Rurik, Voss, Wold, Yorik, Grimvard, Leif, Sigvard, Hakon** — and **Einar**, lieutenant, oldest in pride if not in rank.

They held eleven hours.

Then the Hook **failed** the way tide structures fail — not all at once, in **invoices**: a door that should have sealed; a kill-team channel that went thick; **Torsten**’s vox cutting mid-count. When the World Eaters entered the last junction, the squad still fought as *Quinta* — chainsword and bolt, vertical habits applied to horizontal hell — but capture was already written in the geometry. You could read it in the way **Brand**’s ammo tally stopped updating.

## II. CONSCIOUS NAILS

**Kzorn’s Maw** did not feel like a ship. It felt like a **throat** swallowing metal.

The World Eaters did not sermonize. They **worked** — forge-smell, blood-smell, the soft wet mechanics of implantation conducted with the boredom of men who had done this ten thousand times on enemies who stopped being arguments and became **material**.

They took gene-seed first. They took nerve. They took **time**.

They saved **Nails** for last on each body — and they kept the Batavi **awake**.

**Einar** heard **Stenmark** before he saw him.

Not a scream — Stenmark would not grant them that. A **grind** — skull plate under bone saw vibration, then the first hammer pulse of the nail driving home and the body jerking against restraints that were not for mortals. The sound traveled through the deck the way tide sound traveled through Needles pilings: you felt it in your teeth.

— **Hold** — **Stenmark** gasped — not to his captors. To the empty cage he still believed he owned. — **Hold — **

He did not finish the word.

What answered was not *Axiom*. What answered was **wolf** without Furor’s leash — a howl that made the bulkhead ring, then **Exanimus** violence tearing at straps, then nothing human enough to call **brother** and still too Batavi to call **enemy**.

**Einar** counted the heartbeats he could not help.

When the howl stopped, **Stenmark**’s eyes were open and **empty** of the marine who had held the Needles line.

**Einar** killed him with the first **Dreadwolf Claw** he had stolen from the ship — open-hand gauntlet, ugly traitor steel, not Batavi issue.

The World Eaters watched. Some laughed. None interfered. Interference was for men who still thought this was **conversion**. It was **consumption**, and consumption liked witnesses.

That was the first **gift**.

---

The days that followed were not measured in ship time. They were measured in **installations**.

**Torsten** — bolt afterward, because the nails had made prayer of his throat. **Brand** — opened chest, nails incomplete when the cage broke, **Einar** finishing what the ship started before Brass could taste the soul. **Rurik, Voss, Wold, Yorik, Grimvard, Leif, Sigvard, Hakon** — each with the same architecture: awake for the hammer, gone in the cage, mercy when the marine did not answer and the wolf **howled** through the visage.

**Einar** learned the auspex of mercy was not science. It was **listening** for the moment when your brother’s face stopped arguing with the wolf.

He did not weep. Weeping was a luxury Furor had burned out of him in the first hour. Pride had been the fuel; pride became **useless**; what replaced it was not gentleness — it was **duty** welded in the dark, the same cold weld the Triumvirate would later file as Type III in founders and here filed as **survival**.

The World Eaters called it obedience.

**Einar** called it **geometry**.

## III. LAST

They came for **Einar** when eleven gifts were already spent and the hulk’s galleries stank of Batavi ash and Khorne appetite.

He was not the youngest. He was **last** because the ship wanted the lieutenant to **see** the ledger completed before his own skull was opened.

Four World Eaters pinned him. A fifth — faceless behind butcher plate — drove the first nail while **Einar** remained conscious, because consciousness was the point.

Pain was not one thing.

It was **layers**:

The **skull** — white fire behind the eyes, vision splitting into brass edges and Needles grey.

The **Nails** — not Wolf’s Curse, not *Axiom* failure — **foreign rhythm** inserted into the meat, demanding march tempo his heart did not own.

The **wolf** — his own architecture, Furor’s cage suddenly **too small**, predator throwing itself against bars that had held a century because something else had entered the cell.

The **Red** — not a word he knew yet as theology — a second hunger riding the nail-song, Khorne bleed from the moons above, answering slaughter with **yes, more**.

**Einar** did not break the cage.

He **held chains**.

Later, Varro would file it as tripartite bind: marine visage fixing wolf and demon in the same grip. On the ship, it was only **teeth** clenched until ceramite groaned and the World Eaters leaned in, delighted, because delight was their liturgy.

They implanted the **cable loom** along the nape when the nails were seated — merge line for the mane that had not existed yet, foreign architecture waiting for the stage the ship would **force**.

**Einar** remained awake through all of it.

When they released him, he was not free.

He was **leashed**.

## IV. SLAUGHTER IN THE MAW

What came after was not Furor. Not yet Exanimus in the Chapter’s ledger — that would be Moraine-Nine’s invoice.

What came after was **throughput**.

The World Eaters had grown bored of their work. The bleed had grown **hungry**. The Batavi lieutenant with new nails and old pride burned to ash walked the galleries because staying still meant the Red would steer and he would not know whose hands were on the claws.

Ship steel on both wrists — **Dreadwolf Claws** taken from a World Eaters locker: open-hand lightning claws with blades that **extended from the knuckle couplers**, fingers still free beneath the field. Weapons of the enemy, geometry of the enemy, **honesty** of the enemy.

He killed the surgeons first.

Then the guards.

Then the traitor auxiliaries who had held the restraints.

He killed **World Eaters** who came back laughing because laughter was an invitation Brass understood. He killed in corridors where blood ran ankle-deep and the auspex of mercy no longer applied because there was nothing left in those rooms that wore a Batavi face and still **argued**.

Some later filings would call it escape. In the debrief shell he would call it **accounting** — *spent them before they spent me again*. On the ship it was only **work**.

The hulk did not fall because he won.

It fell because the moons’ bleed turned **sharp**, because engines died, because a prison ship with no pilot but panic met Moraine-Nine’s brack and **broke** open on the feral face, vomiting atmosphere and meat into salt pans where Orks already gathered.

**Einar** rode the crash in the center of the ship’s dying geometry, nails singing, cables twitching in a mane that **erupted** as the hull tore — first **Exanimus** forced through skin and law in one afternoon, jaw brutalized forward, fangs past the lip line, **Dreadwolf Claws** crackling at his wrists as metal screamed. Red hands not yet filed because the Chapter was not there to file them.

He walked out of the fire into Ork country and kept killing.

That slaughter continues in [red-tide/chapter.md](../red-tide/chapter.md) — **Epicenter** onward. The recovery element would call it **heat at the epicenter**. **Einar** had no name for it except **work unfinished**.
