## I. BODIES, NOT RESCUE

Moraine-Nine did not announce disasters. It **absorbed** them the way the Needles absorbed storm surge — brack swelling in the mangrove throats, tide tables amended by clerks who had learned not to waste panic on paper. The shepherd moon’s **feral** hemisphere was a ledger entry until something **heavy** fell through the upper brack and made the ledger lie.

The astropathic burst arrived thin and honest, stripped of choir harmonics: a **World Eaters** prison hulk had **lost** attitude over the feral face, punched atmo in a column of burning metal, and **broken** on salt pans where **Ork** scrap-villages already moved toward the smoke like vermin toward a wound.

No vox from the hull.
No Navy claim of loyal extraction.
No *Strategium* line that said *brothers living*.

Only coordinates, crash azimuth, and a casualty manifest still bleeding names into the relay.

**Armin Sturmwahl** stood in the assault bay while the message cooled on the slate. He read it once the way he read jump solutions — not for comfort, for **closure**. The words he did **not** find were the ones lesser commanders hunted: *survivor*, *beacon*, *hold until*. The words he **did** find were hull breach, Khorne-adjacent bleed on the moons above, and a squad roster attached to a prison ship that should not have existed in Aethelgard’s tide theater at all.

He heard **names** that might still be **carried home** if the tide and the greenskin were denied their share.

— **We recover the dead** — he told the bay. — **We burn them Batavi. We do not leave them for the tide or the greenskin.**

The Navy liaison on the hardline made the mistake of speaking hope because her contract required it.

— **Legatus, our picket reports heat at the epicenter. Single Astartes-class signature. If your men are down there — **

— **If my men are down there** — Sturmwahl said — **we bring back what can be named. We do not invent rescue because your picket is lonely.**

She went quiet. The bay did not.

Around him, QVINTA armor stood in recovery load — bolt pistols, chainaxes racked for close work, jump packs **absent** because this was gunboat insert and mud war, not descent glamour. Twelve brothers. Enough to fight if the wreck fought back. More than enough to **carry** what remained. Brother **Haldor** had served beside **Lieutenant Einar** on three Needles assaults; he did not ask whether Einar could be alive. He asked, in the flat tone assault specialists used when they already knew the answer:

— **Do we bag gene-grace at the wreck line or on the gunboat?**

— **At the line** — Sturmwahl said. — **Drusus wants clean geometry. No Ork teeth in the sacks.**

Haldor nodded once. Grief, if it was there, had been filed before the bay doors opened.

**Varro** came without argument.

He did not cross the deck like a man joining a mission. He crossed it like a **sentence** already written, cowl low, crimson hands black in the assault lumen, *Executor* skull profile turned away from the Navy liaison so she would not waste courtesy on a face that was not offered. The Judex did not travel for hope. He traveled for **what hope cost** when it lied — and for the moment, if it came, when a Legatus would need a witness who could tell **cull** from **mercy** without flinching.

Sturmwahl did not thank him. Thanks were for victories. This was **recovery**.

The wider theater still stank of what had **not** been contained cleanly. For eleven days the moons above Moraine-Nine had reported brass-echo fever — not a full incursion, enough to **thin** the veil and teach Khorne’s name to corridors that had only known Ork hunger before. *Strategium* had sealed the larger geometry. What lay on the feral hemisphere was **remainder**: escaped hull, escaped pain, escaped accounts. Sturmwahl had read the prison-ship annex in transit. World Eaters did not take Batavi to **hold** them. They took gene-seed, nerve, and time. They took men who could be turned into **arguments** against their own Chapter.

He closed the annex.

— **No speeches** — he told the bay. — **We are not here to win. We are here to close names.**

Varro’s voice came from the shadow beside the grav-rack, flat as a filed blade.

— **If one walks** — the Judex said — **you will want my eyes before your hand.**

Sturmwahl looked at him.

— **If one walks** — he answered — **I will want your verdict before my hand.**

No one in the bay mistook that for drama. It was **procedure**.

The gunboat *Brine Ledger* waited in the tide well with engines dialed to **quiet** — Moraine-Nine’s brack did not forgive heat signatures that advertised Astartes pride to feral Orks. Insert in forty minutes. Return before second tide surge. Burn rites on the deck if the mud refused to give up dry ground. The timetable was the kind Navy called **modest** and QVINTA called **honest**: enough time to recover bodies, not enough time to pretend the moon was safe.

Sturmwahl’s thumb found the grav-comp test stud on his jump harness anyway. Habit, not mission — packs stayed racked. Sub-audible hum rose through the bay floor, held, **passed**. Around him, QVINTA had learned that sound the way shore children learned storm horns: if the hum was wrong, the slate **delayed**. If the hum was wrong, you **did not** go, because gravity always collected and the Needles had taught the Chapter that the tide did not refund mistakes.

The hum held.

Sturmwahl sealed his gorget.

— **We go for the dead** — he said. — **Anything else is my error, not yours.**

The bay answered with ceramite and silence — the only kind of assent assault brothers trusted when the mission was already a funeral that had not yet found its bodies.

---

The transit trunk to *Brine Ledger* smelled of brine sealant and cold promethium — Navy austerity dressed as competence. Sturmwahl carried the casualty slate in his left hand and did not hand it off. Some commanders delegated names so they could pretend distance was professionalism. Sturmwahl had learned on Aethelgard that distance was how the tide took what you were not willing to **hold**.

The manifest column was short enough to be an insult.

**Lieutenant Einar.** Needles cadre. Assault line. Status: **unresolved**.

Below him, eleven QVINTA names already stamped **KIA / hulk capture** before the crash telemetry arrived: **Stenmark, Rurik, Torsten, Voss, Wold, Yorik, Grimvard, Leif, Sigvard, Brand, Hakon.** The astropath had not sent poetry. He had sent **accounting** that still expected the moon to argue.

The recovery element’s names were not on that slate. They were on the insert roster — **Haldor, Falken, Eskil, Orm, Knut** among the twelve — men still breathing, still able to carry what the annex had already written in ink.

Haldor walked behind him without looking at the slate.

— **Einar filed Furor-only through every audit I saw** — he said, quiet enough that only Sturmwahl and the shadow behind them would hear. — **If the ship broke him across Exanimus, it did it there. Not before.**

Sturmwahl did not answer *if*. He answered **procedure**.

— **Then Drusus will want the stage marked.**

Varro’s cowl shifted once — assent without comfort.

— **Stage is not soul** — the Judex said. — **You are going for bodies. Remember which ledger you are writing.**

The Navy liaison tried again on the trunk vox, nameless in the record, voice compressed by relay discipline.

— **Legatus, picket revises: the signature is active. Movement pattern consistent with melee engagement. If you wish, we can — **

— **We can burn fuel and arrive on time** — Sturmwahl said. — **Hold your picket on station. Do not interpret my dead for me.**

*Brine Ledger* cut atmosphere with the timid thrust profile Moraine-Nine demanded. Through the armored slit, brack cloud rolled in layers — grey over black over the dull copper of feral Ork cookfires already pinpricking the salt pans below. The moon did not look like a war. It looked like a **kitchen** left untended until something spoiled.

Brother **Falken** — youngest on the insert, still wearing the assault specialist’s crimson scuff from a training cut that had not yet earned a story — checked the gene-grace sacks for the third time.

— **Seven sacks** — he reported. — **Twelve brothers. Math is wrong on purpose.**

— **Math is right** — Sturmwahl said. — **We are not bringing home twelve.**

Falken swallowed that and kept checking seals.

**Orm** and **Knut** worked the chainaxe racks in silence — old Needles habit, steel kissed once per weapon as if the moon might judge the sound. **Eskil** ran auspex calibration against the hull’s interference whine; the rest stacked bolt crates with the bored precision of men who had already decided grief would be a luxury after insert. One veteran near the Judex bench watched Varro without watching him — the way QVINTA men did when they wanted to know whether the mission was punishment, penance, or law.

None of them said **rescue**.

The picket’s revised report arrived as a numeric sting on Sturmwahl’s wrist slate: one Astartes-class heat, continuous, epicenter locked to the hulk’s broken spine. No second loyal signature. No vox handshake. No beacon prayer.

Sturmwahl closed the slate.

He had known **Einar** a century in **Furor** — flame-pool eyes, pride that never asked for relief, competence filed as arrogance by brothers who did not like the Needles in a man’s mouth. If the heat signature was Einar, it was not the Einar who had taught Sturmwahl to count muzzle flashes on vertical assaults. It was whatever the ship had **made** in the days the manifest already called dead.

That was why the Legatus did not say *survivor* even to himself.

He said **name**.

— **We close Einar last** — he told the trunk. — **We close the annex names first. If the epicenter is still moving when we arrive, Haldor and Eskil take left approach. Falken stays on the gunboat with the sacks. Nobody chases heat into the hull without my word.**

Varro’s voice came from the aft bench, barely louder than the engine thrum.

— **And if the heat is your brother and not your brother?**

Sturmwahl looked at the brack closing over the view slit.

— **Then you will be there** — he said — **before my hand decides.**

The gunboat descended. Moraine-Nine’s tide smell entered through filters — salt, rot, sweet Ork smoke, and underneath it all the metal breath of a prison ship that had fallen burning and still had not finished **spending** what it had taken.

## II. THE HULK’S SHADOW

Moraine-Nine’s feral hemisphere was **Needles** logic made ugly — salt pans, black mangrove teeth, Ork scrap villages stinking of sweet rot. The prison hulk lay **open** like a ribcage refused by the sea: hull plates peeled back, corridors vomiting atmosphere crystals into brack wind.

Khorne’s work had already **touched** the moons above — not a full incursion, enough to **thin** the veil and make **Brass** answer when blood was spent in quantity. *Strategium* had contained the wider theater. This wreck was what **escaped** the account.

Sturmwahl’s auspex found **no** loyal vox inside the hull.

What it found was **heat** at the crash epicenter — one signature moving through hundreds of smaller ones, a **metronome** of kills too fast to be Ork alone.

---

*Brine Ledger* kissed mud on the feral shelf with the soft violence of a ship pretending it was not afraid of the tide. Ramp down. Salt crust cracked under boot ceramite. The air hit the filters in layers — brack, burned promethium, Ork stew, and something sweeter underneath that did not belong to xenos cooking alone.

**Brass**, Sturmwahl thought. Not a sermon. A **aftertaste**.

The first Orks came before the ramp finished hissing shut.

Not a Waaagh! — feral scum drawn by crash heat and the smell of Astartes gene-grace they could not name but knew how to **eat**. Six bodies on the left spit. **Eskil**’s auspex had called them three seconds early; **Haldor** and **Orm** met them with bolt pistols at mouth height, economical, no jump-pack glamour, just the QVINTA habit of spending ammunition like seconds on a landing clock.

— **Left approach clear** — Haldor voxed. — **No greenskin between us and the hull spine.**

Sturmwahl did not say *good*. He said:

— **Annex names first. Epicenter last.**

Varro descended last, black robe dragging brack mud without complaint. The Judex did not look at the Ork dead. He looked at the **hulk** the way mortals looked at gallows — not for horror, for **sequence**.

The prison ship’s shadow was worse up close than from orbit.

**Kzorn’s Maw** — translated registry, World Eaters kill-mark filed in butcher’s numerals — lay **open** along three kilometers of salt pan, hull plates peeled outward as if something inside had **exhaled** metal. Corridors were not corridors anymore. They were **throats** vomiting atmosphere crystals into brack wind, frost and blood sharing the same glitter when the tide light caught them wrong.

No Navy honor guard. No Mechanicus salvage tag. Only scrap-villages stirring at the forest edge, Ork silhouettes watching from mangrove teeth, waiting to see whether the grey giants would leave meat behind.

QVINTA moved in recovery geometry — two-man bounds, gene-grace sacks on **Falken**’s back at the gunboat line, the rest forward. Sturmwahl kept the slate clipped to his wrist. Each name he closed was a small act of war against the moon’s appetite.

They found **Brand** first.

Not whole. Not sacrilege the Orks had done — the hull had done worse before the greenskin ever arrived. Ceramite cracked along the chest as if something had tried to **open** him the way men opened reliquaries. Sturmwahl knelt long enough to confirm the pauldron stamp, long enough for Varro to see the neck sockets where implant work had been attempted and abandoned in haste.

— **Stamp it** — the Legatus said.

Haldor pressed the wax tally to the sack. His hands did not shake. That was not the same as steady.

Further in, the wreck began to **sound**.

Not vox. Not engines. **Rhythm** — chain weapons biting, screaming that had already ended but left echoes in bent metal, Ork laughter cut short in wet chunks. The metronome on Sturmwahl’s auspex matched it: one Astartes-class beat, steady, **industrial**.

**Torsten**’s name was on a corridor wall in blood that was not his — Batavi script smeared beside a World Eaters scrawl Sturmwahl did not bother to translate. The body beneath it was **Torsten**, yes, but the Legatus filed the scene, not the graffiti: mercy bolt through the helm, ceramite blackened from inside. No Nails on him. **Gone** before the ship could finish its argument.

— **Gift** — Varro said.

One word. Not approval. **Classification**.

Sturmwahl looked at him.

— **You are early** — he said.

— **I am on time** — the Judex answered. — **The ship is late.**

They burned nothing yet. QVINTA doctrine on Moraine-Nine was **carry first**, burn on deck where Drusus’s air was clean. The tide did not get smoke rights to the Chapter dead.

**Rurik** came next, then **Voss**, then **Wold** — each found in a different corridor mouth, each telling the same story in different postures: brothers who had stopped being **arguments** the enemy could use. Some bore the nail beds. Some bore only the **attempt**. Stenmark had died with his hands still gripping a chainaxe that was not Chapter issue — ship steel, World Eaters forge smell baked into the links.

**Yorik** lay under a collapsed brace, half-visible, as if the hull had tried to **hide** what it had done from the sky.

**Grimvard, Leif, Sigvard, Hakon** followed in the accounting order Sturmwahl had chosen — not the annex order, **his** order: close the names that would not argue before he walked into the name that might.

Orks tested the perimeter twice more. **Knut** dropped one with a chainaxe short stroke; the other fled into mangrove dark. No pursuit. Pursuit was how funerals became hunts.

By the time seven sacks were full, the brack wind had turned **warm** with spent blood from deeper in the wreck. The metronome had not slowed. If anything, it had **clarified** — fewer Ork signatures, fewer traitor remnants, one loyal heat still moving as if the moon were a single chamber and the epicenter were its heart.

Eskil’s voice came thin on the tight-beam.

— **Legatus. Epicenter line is three hundred meters through fused spine. Heat signature still active. No second loyal contact.**

Sturmwahl looked at the ridge of hull metal ahead — black against brack sky, edges glowing where crash fire had glazed ceramite. Somewhere beyond it, the prison ship’s deepest broken rib cage still **worked**.

He had not heard *Einar* in the rhythm.

He had heard **work**.

— **Haldor with me** — he said. — **Eskil on overwatch. Varro — **

— **I am with you** — the Judex said, already moving.

— **Falken holds the line** — Sturmwahl finished. — **Nobody crosses the ridge without my word.**

They climbed the fused spine in silence.

The QVINTA way on Aethelgard was to treat silence as **load-bearing** — not emptiness, structure. Sturmwahl felt the load increase with every meter: hull groaning, tide sucking at the pans below, the metronome ahead counting out a tempo that was not prayer and not rage but the terrible middle thing a brother became when the ship had already taken everything except the **body**.

At the ridge crest, the epicenter opened below.

The killing was still going on.

## III. EPICENTER

Below the crest, in the bowl of torn metal where the spine had folded inward, they saw it.

**Einar**.

No helm. No full plate — only chest, waist, and thigh ceramite, as if he had torn the rest away because it **slowed** the work. Sturmwahl had known him a **century** in **Furor** only — flame-pool eyes, pride that never asked for relief. What stood in the kill-bowl wore **Exanimus** for the first time: jaw brutalized forward, lower fangs past the lip line, mane erupted along the neck as if the ship had **pulled** the next stage through his skin. **Cables** and **implant loom** had **merged** into that new pelage, black against red, twitching when his shoulders turned. The stolen wrist-housing lightning claws from the ship — later purified as **Wolf Talons** — crackled at his gauntlet cuffs like the wrong kind of inheritance.

He killed **World Eaters** where they crawled from the wreck. He killed **Orks** when they lunged for the easy meat. Blood went past fresh **red hands** into **drench** — gauntlets, forearms, the stripped thighs shining wet. Fury was **uncollected**: he did not pause to see who watched. He was the most terrifying object on the moon because he was **still Batavi** in silhouette and **not** Versibar in law — and he had **not stopped**.

**Haldor** made a sound beside Sturmwahl — not words. Air forced through a closed throat.

— **Hold** — the Legatus said, without looking at him. — **Hold is mine to spend.**

On the bowl floor a World Eater dragged himself by one arm, trail bright behind him, mouth working sound that was not a battle cry anymore — only appetite failing upward. **Einar** turned. The claws **extended** and **bit**. The traitor’s body **separated** along a line the *Codex* did not teach and the Needles did not need to name: economy. Finish. Next.

The rhythm did not break.

Sturmwahl had trained beside that economy on vertical assaults when Einar was younger and still **proving** the shore in his mouth — counting muzzle flashes, counting tide windows, counting the exact moment pride became useful and the moment it became **hazard**. Never this rhythm. Never chain work as **breathing**.

Eskil’s tight-beam scratched once against Sturmwahl’s ear.

— **No second heat. Orks thinning. Epicenter stable on one mover.**

Stable.

The word was a Navy word. It meant *predictable*. It did not mean *safe*.

Varro stood a half-pace back from the crest line, cowl still, crimson hands loose at his sides — not weapon-ready, **witness-ready**. Sturmwahl had seen Chaplains pray in battle. Varro did not pray. Varro **listened** to something behind the face-plates of the universe, and what he listened for was not the Emperor’s comfort but the **shape** of a soul under strain.

— **Judex** — Sturmwahl said, low. — **Tell me what you see.**

A long beat. Crackle and wet separation. Ork squeal cut short. Brass wind.

— **I see a cage that almost broke** — Varro said. — **I see two beasts leashed by the same hands. I see not Versibar.**

Not Versibar was the only mercy the law could offer in advance.

Sturmwahl’s hand found his gladius hilt.

The geometry was **correct**. One monster fewer. Funeral rites spared. Brothers already **gone** — the annex had written them, the corridors had **proved** them, seven sacks already waiting on **Falken**’s line at the gunboat. If anything wearing Einar’s face was still here, it was a century of Furor **finished** in one afternoon: Nails, Khorne bleed, and duty welded wrong-shaped into the mane. **Cull** was how you paid the tide before the tide took the name and called it Ork meat or Brass trophy.

Haldor’s voice came harder now — discipline cracking along a seam Sturmwahl recognized because he had heard it in his own chest once, long ago, when the numbers said save the mission and the face said save the man.

— **Legatus — **

— **I know his face** — Sturmwahl said.

He did not say *I know his soul*. That was Varro’s ledger. He said **face** because faces were what Legatuses were allowed to mourn without heresy.

**Einar** killed again — an Ork who had not yet learned the kill-bowl was no longer its territory. The claws **extended**, fell, and the spray hit brack mud with the sound of tide hitting stone: familiar, obscene, **home**.

Sturmwahl took one step down the fused spine.

Varro did not stop him. The Judex did not bless the step. He **recorded** it — invisible, absolute.

The gladius cleared its sheath by a handspan. Enough for light to find the edge. Enough for **Haldor** to see what *correct* looked like on a Legatus who had promised the dead first and the living **last**.

— **If he turns** — Haldor said.

— **If he turns, my hand is faster than your grief** — Sturmwahl answered. — **If he does not turn, your grief is mine to carry.**

It was the cruelest kindness he had ever issued.

**Einar** killed the last Ork in the bowl — axe through spine, spray lifting in brack wind like a vulgar baptism. Green blood mixed with red until the pan looked like a wound the moon itself had made.

Then the rhythm **stopped**.

Not because the enemies were gone.

Because **Einar** chose stop.

He stood in the center of the kill geometry — chest heaving, cables twitching in the new mane, flame-pool eyes sliding toward the crest line for the first time as if sight were a **weight** that had to be lifted. For one instant Sturmwahl saw the old brother in the muscle before the stage — Furor exhaustion, pride burned to ash — and then the new jaw, the fangs, the blood mask, and the question that was not a question:

*Do you end me now?*

**Einar** let the claws go dark.

He flexed once, as if testing whether his hands still belonged to him.

The silence after was louder than the chains had been.

He brought his fists to his **clenched chest** — not surrender. **Salute**. He turned his blood-masked face toward **Armin** and **Varro** and held the posture the way the Needles held a storm line: rigid, shaking only where biology demanded. Behind Sturmwahl, Haldor exhaled like a man surfacing from brack.

— **We hold** — Einar said — **as the Needles hold the cold tide.**

The words were QVINTA assault cadence — Iron Protocol bent through Aethelgard’s coast, where **hold** meant you did not let the surge take the names on the seawall. Not Faith Protocol. Not a plea. An **answer** to the geometry of the moment: still Batavi, still bounded, still refusing to let the tide rewrite what he was.

Sturmwahl’s blade did not fall.

He sheathed it with a slow, deliberate sound — metal home, invoice paid in **restraint** instead of blood. The Legatus descended the last meters into the bowl. He did not embrace **Einar**. He did not praise him. He stopped at the distance assault doctrine prescribed between a living officer and a brother who had crossed a stage under Judex eyes.

— **You will come home** — Sturmwahl said. — **Not as rescue. As name.**

Varro’s cowl dipped once — not nod, **verdict filed**.

**Einar** looked at the Judex, then away, as if even gratitude would be a leash he could not afford. He did not explain. Explanation belonged to rooms with wax and witnesses — not to a kill-bowl where Brass still licked the air and every word might be eaten by the wind before it reached a ledger.

Sturmwahl did not demand it. He had heard the **cadence**. That was enough for the ridge.

— **Then we carry what remains** — he said. — **All of them. The shell comes after.**

**Einar** nodded once — the same economy he used on slaughter.

## IV. THEY WERE GONE

The debrief shell aboard *Brine Ledger* was too small for twelve meters of myth and still large enough to **hurt**. They had carried the last sacks up from the bowl in the same order Sturmwahl had closed them — annex complete, **Einar** last, walking under his own power between **Haldor** and **Orm** as if distance were a penance he accepted.

**Falken** had not asked questions at the ramp. He had only checked seals and looked once at **Einar**’s red hands the way novices looked at reliquaries they were not yet worthy to touch.

In the bowl, **Einar** had given the salute and **silence**. In the shell, with brack weather hammering the hull and the tide counting hours they did not have, Varro required the toll to be spoken where it could be **filed**.

**Einar** stood when the Judex entered. He did not sit. Sitting was for men who still believed their bodies were furniture.

His voice was flat tide.

— **They were gone.**

Not excuse. **Diagnosis** — the first time the sentence existed as law, not as breath on a battlefield.

— **I gave them the gift to die as Batavi before the enemy could bite at their souls.**

Sturmwahl had known the shape of it from seven corridors and eleven sacks. Hearing it named in the shell was worse anyway, because naming made mercy **repeatable** — and repeatability was how the Chapter survived what the Imperium would otherwise call murder.

The shell did not warm for the confession. Promethium heat from the deck plates below was Navy thrift, not comfort.

Varro listened the way he listened to battle — without encouraging the story to become **myth**. Sturmwahl stood opposite **Einar**, not blocking the Judex, not surrendering the room to him either. **Haldor** waited outside with **Orm** and the others, because some truths were not for every brother’s ears, only for the men who had to **sign** them.

— **Eleven** — Varro said. — **You claim eleven gifts.**

— **Eleven** — Einar answered. — **Plus those the hull took before I could reach them.**

— **Name the first.**

— **Stenmark.** No pause. — **He asked for it with his eyes. The ship had already opened his chest. Nails were next. I used what I could steal — the claws. Ship steel. He would have wanted the irony if irony still existed.**

Varro’s cowl did not move.

— **Torsten.**

— **Bolt. Clean. Before the scream became prayer.**

The list continued — **Brand, Rurik, Voss, Wold, Yorik, Grimvard, Leif, Sigvard, Hakon** — each delivered like a toll on a seawall, not like victory. Sturmwahl did not interrupt. He had read the annex. He had knelt beside the bodies. Hearing the names spoken in order was worse, because voice made them **recent** again.

When **Einar** finished, Varro asked the only question that mattered to law:

— **Did any still wear the cage when you killed them?**

A beat. Tide on the hull.

— **Two** — Einar said. — **Maybe three. The auspex of mercy is not science. The ship was loud. I killed when the wolf in them howled and the marine did not answer.**

— **And you?** — Varro said. — **Did you wear the cage when they nailed you?**

**Einar**’s cables twitched in the mane — involuntary, hated.

— **I was last** — he said. — **I was awake for all of it. I held the chains. I am still holding them.**

Drusus was not in the room — would come at Noviomagus with scalpels and shameless ledgers. Sturmwahl heard **pride** burned out and **duty** welded in, the same transmutation the Chapter had seen in founders, but **wrong-shaped**: foreign iron in the mane, a second hunger riding the wolf.

— **Frontier duty** — Varro said, not to Einar, to the air the record would occupy. — **Not heresy. Not absolution.**

— **File it** — Sturmwahl said. — **Under my seal. If the Confessor lattice argues, they argue with me.**

**Einar** looked at the Legatus then — flame-pool eyes steady in a face that was no longer the face Sturmwahl had teased after Needles landings.

— **If I fail** — he said to Armin, without looking at Varro — **you end me.**

Sturmwahl did not answer **yes**. He did not answer **no**. He answered like a Legatus who invoices gravity:

— **You will be watched.**

— **I know** — Einar said. — **That is why I saluted.**

Varro’s voice came from the dark hood line.

— **You will be watched by me. Not as punishment. As load-bearing.**

**Einar** flinched once — micro, there and gone — at the weight of that. He did not argue. Arguing with Varro was how men discovered they had already confessed.

The shell hatch opened on brack wind. **Haldor** stood in the corridor with eight other QVINTA silhouettes and the row of gene-grace sacks like a choir that would never sing. Beyond them, on the open aft deck where Navy rules allowed Astartes fire only under seal, **Falken** and **Eskil** had prepared the burn pans — promethium channels, tide shields, wax tallies ready for Drusus’s later quarrel.

Sturmwahl led the rites himself.

Not Chaplain liturgy — Legatus **closure**. Name by name from the annex, flame by flame, smoke bent sideways by Moraine-Nine’s wind until the brack smelled honest again. **Einar** stood at the rail through all eleven and did not look away. When the last sack’s seal melted and the smoke rose, a brother behind **Haldor** — **Knut**, young enough to still believe names were only names — whispered what the assault bay would repeat before anyone filed it in ink:

— **Dreadwolf.**

No one corrected him.

**Einar** heard it. He did not claim it. He did not refuse it.

Sturmwahl watched the tide take the ash and thought of Selene-Black years later, of Ork spires and jump fuel spent like coins — different war, same **invoice**. The moon had already earned a title in QVINTA mouths: **Red Tide**. The brother had earned one in theirs.

*Brine Ledger* lifted before second surge. The feral hemisphere shrank to a stain on the viewport. **Einar** sat in the farthest bench with cables still in his mane and **Varro** between him and the door, not as guard — as **hinge**.

No one spoke rescue on the return leg.

They spoke **hold**.

## V. AFTERMATH (REGISTER)

The formal filings arrived days later on Noviomagus relay, stripped of brack wind and Ork cookfires. What remained was geometry any *Strategium* clerk could audit — and one *Vermilion* line Varro would not let clerks copy.

Silent Vigil did not celebrate survivors. It **filed** them and returned them to work.

---

### The honor name

**Einar Dreadwolf** spread through **QVINTA** faster than ink.

It started on *Brine Ledger*’s aft deck — **Knut**’s whisper, the burn smoke, the way a name became convenient when the annex no longer fit in the mouth. By the time the gunboat made Aethelgard tide well, brothers who had not seen the bowl were already using it in checklists and jump briefings as if the word had always existed.

**Einar** did not adopt it in vox traffic. He did not forbid it either. He answered to **Lieutenant** on slates and to silence in the mess when the room tried to offer him space he did not want.

**Varro** answered to nothing.

In the *Reclusiam* annex, the Judex wrote one line in hand that did not circulate:

*In watch. Under the Deathwolf’s jaws. No soul-name. No precedent. No cult.*

Brothers could say **Dreadwolf**. Varro would not turn honor into liturgy. The distinction mattered because the Chapter had learned what happened when wolf-words became **theology** — Malakor was not a lesson you repeated for fashion.

---

### Medicinae

**Drusus** met **Einar** in white surgical light on Noviomagus transit and did not offer comfort.

Comfort was not a tool. Measurements were.

The Chief Apothecary’s helm hissed once — pneumatics, vapor, plate sliding sideways — and the room became a clinic instead of a myth. **Einar** sat where told. Cables along the mane twitched when the probe neared the merge line; he did not pull away. Pride had burned on Moraine-Nine. What remained was **duty** holding still for audit.

— **Stage crossed under foreign implant pressure** — Drusus said to the slate, not to the man. — **Exanimus onset forced. Furor-only history negated post–074.M42. Strand pressure elevated; second architecture parasitic, not sanctioned chimera expression.**

He glanced at **Varro**, who stood in the doorway like a nail driven through the frame.

— **Wolf-demon bind stable at debrief** — Drusus continued. — **Recommend perpetual Judex witness. Monthly cable trim. No auto-feed. Manual maintenance only.**

— **He eats** — Varro said.

— **He eats like a soldier** — Drusus answered. — **Not like a miracle.**

When the plates closed, **Einar** spoke once.

— **Will it get worse?**

Drusus did not lie with hope. He lied with **precision**.

— **It will get documented.**

### Judex watch — *Amissi* induction

No announcement. A slate addendum: **Einar** on **The Lost** (*Amissi*) roster — **first of The Lost** — under **Varro** direct authority; **Steppenwolf** detached registry **unchanged** on the tallies (**not** Legatus-owned closure cadre). **Varro** on every mission line; gene-seed on death per **Vermilion** protocol — harvest if clean, **destroy** if corrupt, **no** living tithe. Full armor refit (Assault Intercessor Mk X Tacticus, jump pack, half-helm, **Wolf Talons**, *Amissi* heraldry) filed post-debrief — see [amissi-the-lost.md](../../../personae-command-index/doctrine-and-organs/amissi-the-lost.md) · [steppenwolf-doctrine.md](../../../personae-command-index/doctrine-and-organs/steppenwolf-doctrine.md) §5.1.

The first live exercise was not Armageddon — not yet — but a traitor splinter bled off a reef convoy in the Aethelgard theater, heretics wearing stolen Aquila plate and the confidence of men who thought the frontier was too tired to judge them. **Einar** went in Assault Intercessor **Mk X Tacticus** — *Amissi* heraldry, jump pack for speed, half-helm locking nose and jaw while mane and **Nails** ran free, **Wolf Talons** engaged under a **red** field. **Haldor** and **Eskil** flanked at distance. **Varro** walked the center line behind **Einar** like a shadow that had learned to weigh the same ounces as a blade.

When the killing finished, **Einar** stood in the rinse of brack spray until the red on his vambraces stopped lying about freshness.

Then he turned toward the Legatus and waited.

Sturmwahl did not speak until Varro’s cowl dipped — **verdict: bounded**.

Only then did **Einar** say the cadence, quiet enough for steel, loud enough for law:

— **We hold, as the Needles hold the cold tide.**

Not to crowds. To the wall. To **Armin**. To the Judex who would file the words as **conduct**, not prayer.

### What clerks could not audit

**Tyvar**’s office sent one line through Custodes channels — not accusation, **timing**:

*If watch lapses, cull geometry must be pre-authorized, not debated at trigger.*

Sturmwahl answered in two words on the relay stub: *Already filed.*

In the assault bay, **Haldor** drilled closure cadence with recruits who did not know why the Legatus sometimes paused before saying *We hold* — a pause that measured the distance between **mercy** and **murder** in a single breath. **Falken** became careful around **Einar** the way men became careful around live melta. **Eskil** kept auspex charts that looked like tide tables and called anomalies **surge** until the slate agreed.

**Einar** kept mess-hall clearance. Not contempt for brothers — **focus**. When Varro’s watch interval was active, he ate where the Judex could see the mandible of discipline work: manual, industrial, unromantic. The Chapter honored him at distance. He preferred distance. Distance was the only leash he still trusted.

Once, a Confessor adjunct from QVINTA’s banner court asked whether the gift line should be taught as exemplum.

Varro’s answer arrived before Sturmwahl’s:

— **Teach the hold. Do not teach the ship.**

### Red Tide (oral register)

Moraine-Nine did not appear on public *Alabarda* cloth. It was too small for heraldic triumph and too ugly for Administratum poetry. In QVINTA mouths it became **Red Tide** — the brack pan where eleven names were burned and one name was **not** taken.

The tide, for once, had been denied its receipt.

Later, when **Gutgrind** fell on Selene-Black (**121.M42**), **Armin Sturmwahl**’s assault bay would run jump drills on a moon that **wanted** to be conquered. Brothers would laugh about scrap-spires and Ork kings because laughter was how QVINTA spent fear without spending discipline.

And in the hum-check silence before descent, veterans who had been on *Brine Ledger* would hear something under the grav-comp tone — not superstition, **memory**:

A chain rhythm stopping.
A gladius sheathing.
A voice saying *hold* as if the Needles themselves had learned to speak through a man’s chest.

**Speed** could not outrun that.

Neither could **Gutgrind**.

Moraine-Nine had not made **Einar Dreadwolf** a hero. It had made him a **geometry** the Chapter could use — watched, bounded, honored, feared — a brother you had known in **Furor**, met again in **first Exanimus**, and chosen, at the ridge line, not to kill **correctly**.

The wolf under the Deathwolf’s jaws still breathed.

The Chapter, for now, still held the chain.
