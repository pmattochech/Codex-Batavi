# THE BATTLE OF LUNA SIGMA: THE DAY THE WALL CRACKED

*(568.M42 — Noviomagus System, Luna Sigma)*

---

## I. THE SIGNAL (72 hours before impact)

The first to feel it was Librarian Henricus.

He was on watch at the **Warp post** — what the Batavi informally call *somewhere*, the gaze that goes beyond the physical plane — when **Haruspicium Batavorum** began to fail.

Not dramatically. **Haruspicium Batavorum** does not fail like a broken machine; it fails like a language losing meaning. Henricus was cataloging standard psychic traffic on the border — fragmented echoes of the Cicatrix Maledictum, threads of residual fear from the dead of the Great Heresy, the low steady pressure of the Immaterium against Blackstone Pylons — when the patterns stopped being patterns.

He forced the Geometric Grid. **Haruspicium Batavorum** imposes structure on chaos: hate becomes coefficient, horror becomes variable, hunger becomes frequency spike. He had cataloged Ork *Waaaghs!* as gusts of disordered data. He had cataloged Tyranid Shadow in the Warp as silence that swallows signal. He knew how to translate.

This time, the Grid touched something and the Grid **broke**.

Not the instruments — the mental structure. The geometry Henricus used to hold chaos far enough to analyze simply ceased to exist as a concept for 0.4 seconds. On his helm’s biometric logs, those 0.4 seconds appear as collapse of all metrics at once, followed by panicked reactivation.

The *Librarium* would later archive that class of failure as **Broken Haruspicium**: not a broken cogitator stack, but a broken *reading* — the instant **Haruspicium Batavorum** meets a phenomenon that unmakes the category system itself.

He bled from the nose inside the helm and transmitted a single word to Otho:

*"Apex."*

---

Otho heard and knew. In four decades as Chief Librarian he had classified dozens of threats in the Warp. He had felt the cold predation of a Lord of Change on his perimeter. He had touched, with the Grid’s tip, a Bloodthirster’s thirst in orbit. He knew how entities that size “know.”

*Apex* was not a classification in **Haruspicium Batavorum**. It was the word the Librarians had reserved, never needing to use it, for the event the protocol had no category for.

Otho went at once to Alaric’s Strategium.

He did not say “Daemon Primarch.” He said what the data said:

— There is something in the Warp that dissolves **Haruspicium Batavorum** by the mere fact of existing. No identifiable signal. No confirmed direction. Distance not measurable by available instruments. — A pause. — The data suggest vertical-axis scale. It is not a cataloged threat.

Alaric stood still for two seconds. For those who knew him, two seconds of total immobility meant the equation had changed order of magnitude.

— How long?

— Seventy-two hours, estimate. Could be less.

— Move Luna Sigma civilians to Noviomagus Tertius underground facilities. — Alaric turned to the vox. — *We hold.*

---

## II. THE BROKEN HARUSPICIUM (Addendum — *Librarium* perspective)

*Clinical log of Chief Librarian Otho — Cinnabar Archive — 568.M42*

What follows is the body of evidence behind the archive term **Broken Haruspicium** — from Henricus’s first fracture through the shutdown order.

In the 72 hours between Henricus’s warning and Angron’s manifestation on Luna Sigma, I monitored the forty-three **Batavian Haruspices** distributed across the Vexillationes at maximum alert. What follows is an honest report of what I observed in the Warp — and what the Warp did back.

The presence approaching emitted no signal. It emitted **normalized absence of signal**. Where I expected background noise — the constant echo of entropy, fractured psychic traffic on the border — there was growing suppression, as if the whole Warp spectrum began to recede from the source. An expanding aura vacuum.

Librarians trained to classify threats by pattern found no pattern. They found the negative of a pattern: the space where a pattern should exist and had been consumed. **Haruspicium Batavorum**, which turns the irrational into algorithm, has no category for the irrational that consumes the instrument of categorization itself.

**Biological effects were immediate and scaled nonlinearly:**

In the first 24 hours: six Librarians advanced from standard watch to initial Furor symptoms — sensitive canines, extreme irritability, sleep cycles interrupted by flight response. The Wolf’s Curse responds to what rational mind cannot process: when logic fails to categorize the threat, biology takes over with the only protocol it knows. The cornered animal prepares to flee before understanding the predator.

In the following 24 to 48 hours: three Librarians — including Henricus — showed spontaneous eye bleeding and incipient Exanimus symptoms. Not from combat. From **passive exposure** to something still days away on the physical plane. The Warp is not neutral to those who inhabit it professionally; when something of this scale crosses it, it vibrates back through every contact point.

In the final 24 hours before manifestation: I ordered all Librarians to close active Warp channels and operate exclusively on the physical plane. The decision was clinical, not strategic — I had felt enough to know keeping the Grid open at that point meant accepting permanent damage with no intelligence gain.

What I learned before closing: the presence had no directed intent. There was no strategic mind moving toward us with calculated goal. There was a *vector of destruction in motion*, the way a typhoon does not decide where it goes — it simply goes, and whatever lies in the path receives the full impact.

Angron did not come **to** Luna Sigma. Angron **arrived at** Luna Sigma.

The distinction matters.

---

## III. MANIFESTATION (Day of impact — Luna Sigma)

The World Eaters fleet left the Warp eight hours from Luna Sigma. There was no Loken Passage to guide them — they forced exit through the Cicatrix, leaving tears of corrupted reality Blackstone Pylons took hours to suture. The cost to them was irrelevant. World Eaters ships do not count own losses as an operational variable.

Alaric was on Luna Sigma with the First Vexillatio and two thousand line warriors. Tyvar, Shield-Captain of the Custodes, stood forty meters to his left — a golden statue who had not spoken a word since Otho’s warning. Drusus was in the rear, mobile *Apothecarion* positioned outside the first impact line.

The battle for outer perimeters lasted forty minutes. The World Eaters did not use tactics; they used mass and fury, and their fury was genuinely unlike any other Chaos force — no mutant corruption, no greed or intrigue. Only the absolute compulsion of the Massacre, carved into the Butcher’s Nails each had carried nailed in the skull since the Great Heresy.

The First Vexillatio held. *We hold.* The iron wall did not give way.

Then Angron descended.

---

There was no transport ship. He simply **was**, suddenly, in the middle of the battlefield, as if reality had decided to open an exception so he could exist there.

Three and a half meters was Alaric von Helis’s height — stabilized Execratio, Noviomagus’s anomaly. Angron had two and a half meters more. He was red where there was no other blood, and where there was other blood it was scarlet on scarlet. The wings were not wings — the physical manifestation of Khorne’s hate made flesh, structures that seemed at once bony and impossible, bending light around them. The Nails in the Daemon Primarch’s skull pulsed with sulfurous light.

The forty meters between Alaric and Tyvar collapsed into silence.

Alaric heard, on internal vox, Drusus:

— Telemetry of all Librarians in shutdown protocol. Axiom of Reason confirmed on all active channels. — A 0.3 second pause. — Castellan biometrics: amplified Axiom, no deviation to any Projection axis. Nominal for threat level.

*Nominal.* The most absurd word Drusus had spoken in the entire Silent Vigil.

Alaric felt no rage. He felt the Axiom of Reason expand to fill every centimeter of cognition — total cold, absolute prioritization, the world reduced to friction geometry. Angron was not an enemy to defeat. He was a variable to contain for the maximum calculable time.

He transmitted on vox:

— Continue evacuation. I hold his attention. — A silence. — *We hold.*

He walked toward Angron.

---

## IV. THE DAY THE WALL CRACKED

Angron’s first blow landed like a geological sentence — not a punch, but the inevitable conclusion that space should be vacated of Alaric’s existence. The Castellan interposed the *Stormbreaker*, the Storm Shield, pneumatic pistons driving adamantium stakes into Luna Sigma’s ferrocrete before impact.

The shield was destroyed. The stakes tore from the ground with blocks of fused ferrocrete. Alaric was thrown forty meters back, through an industrial storage facility, and stopped against a two-meter-thick containment wall.

The wall gave. Alaric did not.

He was on his feet before Angron crossed the wreckage, *Castra-Lupus* with cracks in thoracic reinforcement, three servomotors in the right arm fused. *Morkai’s Bite* was in his left hand.

What Drusus’s biometric logs captured at that moment was, in his later words, *“the most clinically disturbing metric of my entire career”*: Alaric von Helis, facing a Daemon Primarch who had just destroyed his shield with one blow, showed heart rate 62 beats per minute. Blood pressure within standard operational parameter. Adrenaline level: elevated, not critical.

The Axiom of Reason was working.

---

The fight lasted fifty-three minutes.

Full logs are *Vermilion* classified and will not be reproduced in this file. What may be said:

Alaric did not wound Angron. Not once, in fifty-three minutes of single combat. *Morkai’s Bite*, ice axe of a Chapter Master wielded like a work tool, struck the Daemon Primarch on seventeen documented occasions. On seventeen occasions the wound closed before the next breath’s impact could register.

What Alaric did was hold attention.

The geometry was this: while Angron focused on the only being on the field who had survived his first blow and kept advancing, the World Eaters around them lost spiritual leadership cohesion. Without Angron distributing Khorne’s presence across the field, they were only berserkers with Nails in the skull — lethal, but contained by the First Vexillatio.

Evacuation advanced.

Alaric was struck by Angron’s left wing in the thirty-first minute and lost the right arm below the elbow. *Castra-Lupus* ceramite shattered with bone. The arm, gauntlet, and Aethelgard Claws on it vanished under debris.

Drusus, on vox: — Castellan. Critical damage to right limb. Protocol of—

— *Silence.* — Alaric’s voice was as always. — *We hold.*

---

The Lupercal Anomaly was documented at the Battle of Luna Sigma in a way no theoretical analysis had anticipated.

Angron stopped.

Not long — perhaps two seconds, perhaps three. But he stopped. He looked at the gray-and-crimson iron colossus before him with one arm and still advancing. The pelts on *Castra-Lupus*’s shoulders — Viggo and the *Canis Batavorum* — projected the silhouette that had disturbed Blood Angels, Space Wolves, and traitors for centuries.

What was in Angron’s eyes in that moment, no living being on that field could interpret. Not tactical hesitation. Not fear. Something older — recognition between two organisms biology places in categories language cannot reach.

The Daemon Primarch of Khorne looked at the ghost of Horus Lupercal’s silhouette rising from a mutilated iron colossus advancing without right arm and without retreat, and for two seconds the universe went quiet.

Then Angron roared and the next impact was different — no longer the geological sentence of the first. It was something personal.

Alaric was caught by the final blow with the arm he had left interposed, using the elbow as last blocking point. *Castra-Lupus* imploded on the left side. He hit Luna Sigma’s ground with the full force of a Daemon Primarch behind the blow and stayed there.

Drusus’s sensors logged pulse present, minimal cognition, regeneration activity begun on lost arm and damaged internal organs.

The Lord-Castellan was alive.

---

The Grey Knights arrived forty minutes after Alaric fell.

Angron was banished. The process took eleven minutes and cost four Grey Knights. Details belong to the Ordo Malleus and are not in this file.

What is on record: when purification rites finished and Drusus’s team entered the field to recover Alaric, Shield-Captain Tyvar stood guard beside the broken *Castra-Lupus* chassis, spear in present-arms position — not combat, not execution. Honor.

Tyvar said nothing. He remained until Drusus and Apothecaries lifted the Castellan and carried him to the *Apothecarion*.

As the stretcher passed, the Custodes inclined his head two degrees.

For Tyvar, that was the equivalent of a speech.

---

## V. THE REGENERATIVE COMA (568.M42 — three months)

*Log of Chief Apothecary Drusus — Vermilion Archive*

The Castellan entered regenerative coma immediately after removal from the field. Catalogued damage: right arm lost below elbow, structural fracture of left hemithorax, temporary failure of four additional implanted organs, microfractures in forty-two percent of bones on the left side, multi-source internal bleeding.

Initial estimate was partial recovery in six months, with surgical reimplantation required for lost organs and provisional prosthesis for the limb.

The estimate was wrong.

In the third coma week, sensors registered regenerative activity at implanted-organ sites with no precedent in my archives. Tissue was not scarring around absences — it was *reconstructing*. Complex structures, with precise architecture of original Astartes organs, growing from basal tissue as if a master plan were engraved in the cell itself.

It took two days for me to accept what the data showed.

Alaric von Helis’s gene-seed, after five hundred fifty years of progressive integration with an organism in stabilized Execratio, had ceased to be a set of implants and had become the body’s **cellular master plan**. Execratio had not destroyed gene-seed; it had fused it to host basal DNA until the body no longer distinguished “original” from “installed” tissue. For Alaric’s biology, Astartes organs are native structures. And native structures regenerate.

On the ninetieth day the right arm had regrown to the wrist. Internal organs were functional. Microfractures had closed with bone density superior to pre-battle.

Alaric opened his eyes on the ninety-second day.

The first word was to Drusus, on watch beside the cot:

— How long?

— Ninety-two days.

Silence.

— Evacuation complete?

— Yes.

— Civilian casualties?

— Zero on Luna Sigma. Forty-two Batavi on the battlefield. Four Grey Knights in the banishment.

Alaric stared at the ceiling a moment.

— The arm?

— Grew back. — Drusus paused the type that means the full report is longer than the answer. — I will document the process. It is the Chapter’s most significant biological discovery since the Execratio of 031.

— Later. — Alaric sat slowly. — Where is Tyvar?

— Outside.

— Send him in.

Tyvar entered. Standard position — still, spear, auramite helm. Absolute Denial Protocol.

Alaric looked at him three seconds.

— You stayed on the field.

Tyvar did not answer.

— Thank you.

The Custodes inclined his head two degrees. Then left.

Drusus stared at the closed door. Then at Alaric.

— That is the maximum affection he can express — said Drusus, in the same tone he would use for synovial fluid viscosity.

— I know — said Alaric. — It is enough.
