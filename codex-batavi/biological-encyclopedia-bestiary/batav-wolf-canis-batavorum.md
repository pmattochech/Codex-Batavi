# Batav Wolf — *Canis Batavorum* (Aethelgard iconic apex)

**Scope:** Native **super-predator** of **Aethelgard Prime** — **wolf-shaped** (*canid* body plan), with **convergent** traits that mirror **big-cat** wetland ambush ecology elsewhere in the galaxy. **Not** Fenrisian **Thunderwolf** (*Canis fenrisianus*); **not** Chapter heraldic **Batav Wolf** charges except by analogy.

## Range and habitat

The Batav Wolf is **not** only a coastal animal. It occupies **two** productive theaters on Aethelgard:

**Shoreline biome** — **Needles** estuaries, **salt mud**, **intertidal** stone **Spires**, **brackish** mixing. Here it behaves as a **vertical** predator: tide as **delivery geometry**, rock as **launch**.

**Interior swamps** — **peat**, **slow river nets**, **drowned root** tangles and seasonal **sheet-flood** corridors between taiga toe and tidal rim (see geography **III. Swamps**). Here it behaves as a **cover** predator: reed shadow, peat hummock, half-submerged spoil from the **Age of Strife** — **stalk-and-ambush** along drowned channels and floating mats, using **murky water** as approach cover the same way open shoreline uses **noise** and **surf**.

**Excluded:** montane taiga apex guild and open snowfields — different chains.

**Macro-context:** Cyclopean tidal forcing still sets **pulse** everywhere brackish fingers reach inland; swamp hunts spike in **high-water** windows when prey **concentrates** against root walls and spoil geometry.

## Social structure

* **Group size:** Mixed-sex bands — typically **kin-clustered** — rarely exceed **ten** animals; **four to seven** is common where **prey density** and **territory** allow stable overlap.

* **Hunting:** **Almost always solitary.** A band may **share** range, **den**, and **scavenge rights**, but the **close** on large quarry is one animal’s work — fishers report **pairs** only in **teaching** juveniles or **desperation** winters.

* **Why bands at all:** **Territory marking**, **young rearing**, **mobbing** rare megafauna threats to the den — not **wolf-pack relay chase** tactics across open ground.

## Physical description (witness and specimen fragments)

**Form:** Unmistakably **wolf** — **canid** limb placement, **elongate** skull within a heavy build, **brush** tail, **digitigrade** stance. Convergence is **physiological and ecological**, not a cat borrowed into a wolf skin.

* **Overall silhouette:** **Stocky**, **deep-chested**, **low-slung** — reads shorter on the tape than the scales suggest: mass in **forequarter**, **neck**, and **jaw**, not in greyhound length. Built for **burst**, **drag**, and **ambush**, not endless **cursorial** pursuit across taiga steppe.

* **Head and bite:** Still **wolf-proportioned** — **long enough** muzzle to read as *Canis* — but **zygomatic** width and **masseter** architecture push **bite force** toward **big-predator** performance in brackish water (Terran analogues cite **jaguar-scale** mechanics in a **canid** frame). Coastal tales claim **obsidian-dark** claws and **tooth** wear — **silica** inclusion or mineral accretion — strong enough to **punch** plated hide.

* **Coat:** Base **charcoal**, **peat brown**, **brack green** — broken **marbling** and **ghost rosettes** (dock names: *weed-stain*, *mud rose*) that **break** outline in Needles dapple and swamp shade like **patterned wolf**, not imported cat pelage.

* **Limbs:** Forelimbs **thick**; paws **broad**, toes **splayed** — mud and peat. Partial **webbing** in estuary lines; swamp lines favor **lunge** from sucking organic soil.

* **Tail:** **Full** canid brush — **rudder** and **signal** — carried **low** when stalking, **level** when swimming a channel crossing.

* **Senses:** Forward **binocular** overlap for strike timing at **water surface**; ears **triangular canid** pinnae, often **half-cocked** or **laid** in brush to shave silhouette — still **wolf ears**, not rounded felid cups.

* **Scale:** Not the **largest** macropredator in the oceans — **efficiency** and **closure** in foul terrain define reputation.

## Predation ecology

**Hunting posture:** **Almost always one animal** on the kill — bands do not typically **relay** quarry like cursorial wolves of open prairie myth.

**Two dominant modes:**

**Spire / shore — “drop” hunt:** **Position** on exposed stone; **wait** for surge to deliver quarry; **short vertical leap** onto **young leviathans** feeding in estuary algae — kill at **rear cranial joint**; **drag** toward high stone (classic witness pattern in [der-batav-myth-foundation.md](../lexicon-and-litanies/der-batav-myth-foundation.md)).

**Swamp — “margin” hunt:** **Stalk** along drowned channels and peat islands; **ambush** at **neck** or skull from cover — fish, **mud lunger** analogues, smaller armored **channel** beasts, and occasionally **juvenile** leviathan-class migrants in **brackish** choke points. Uses **water** as **lane** and **sound cover**; crosses open bog only when **calendar** forces prey.

**Signature ethic (native metaphor):** Does not flee **chaos** — uses **tide**, **flood pulse**, and **terrain** as **weapons**.

**Competition:** Mid-chain sharks/rays at shore; swamp guild includes **hook** predators and **scavenger** swarms — Batav Wolf holds **niche** at **high effort / high reward** closure.

## Design genealogy (out-of-universe)

**Silhouette stays lupine.** **Ecological and evolutionary** parallels to the **South American jaguar** (*Panthera onca*) — especially **Brazilian** / **pantanal-like** wetland **stalking**, **stocky** power-to-weight, **marginal-water** hunting — inform **behavior** and **performance**, not a felid body map. **Social model:** small **canid**-style bands (cap **10**), **solitary** kills — patterned on wolf **flexible sociality** plus big-cat **ambush** economics.
