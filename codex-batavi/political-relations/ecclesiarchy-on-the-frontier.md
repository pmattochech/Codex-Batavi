## WIKI: ECCLESIARCHY ON THE FRONTIER (THE DOCTRINE OF EFFORT)

On Noviomagus Prime and the Nine Phalanx systems, the common citizen does not worship the Emperor through screaming fanaticism or flagellant processions in cathedrals. The frontier population bows its head and prays in silence before consuming rations, asking protection and stability, but true worship is done on foot with dirty hands.

For Castra Vetera’s people, labor is the supreme form of prayer. Silence is revered as absolute focus. If a citizen wishes to praise the Emperor, he does so by being strictly *human* and productive. The simple act of fixing a thermal boiler, baking bread that feeds the shifts, or training future laborers is seen as highest honor to the Golden Throne’s sacrifice.

Local doctrine holds there is no logic in begging miracles if the individual does not boot up to earn them. Penance for fault or sin is not paid with empty self-flagellation but with sweat: forgiveness is won by working double on the assembly line and delivering extra quotas to the war machine.

The figure of the idle priest, robed in silk and blind to physical suffering, was extirpated from the frontier. Local Ecclesiarchy works directly in the logistical hell of foundries and macro-forges. Bishops, priests, and deacons wear industrial coveralls under their stoles, often holding foreman and efficiency-auditor posts.

A sermon on Noviomagus never stops production. Litanies are intoned over *vox-casters* in exact rhythm with hydraulic presses, mixing prayer with that cycle’s tactical efficiency count. Many clerics actively operate torches, treating arc-weld glare as physical manifestation of divine light.

Communion administered by these priests carries absolute biological weight. Priests walk the forges carrying heavy backpack tanks of purified water and electrolytic paste, rehydrating exhausted laborers at their stations so no arm stops forging.

In Incus-Gravis Secundus’s underground hives, where absence of sunlight breeds lethal “White Plague” (severe vitamin D deficiency and psychological decay), Ecclesiarchy acts as the main medical arm. Clergy distributes essential supplement pills blessed as *“Hosts of the Sun,”* anchoring population health to devotion to the Throne.

The subsector’s spiritual leader is High Bishop Kaltos. A pragmatist and brute, he measures his diocese’s faith in tons of exported *adamantium*, holding an unbreakable alliance with Alaric von Helis founded on what he calls “Iron Zeal.”

That reality often collides with arriving missionaries, who see the frontier’s focused silence as heresy or disguised atheism. When outside prelates try to freeze forges to impose idle worship at high decibel, the Wall intervenes. Master Chaplain Varro often audits such figures, ruling that their hysterical scream wastes oxygen the boilers need.

Missionaries who insist on religious pride and arrogance are dispatched to lower levels or agricultural domes. Given shovels and torches, they are reeducated by effort. Those who survive forced labor grasp the frontier truth: the muscular exhaustion that keeps humanity alive in the dark is the purest hymn the universe can produce.
