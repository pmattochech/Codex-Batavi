# Geography — Aethelgard Prime (four biomes)

**Purpose:** Physical layout for prose and maps — **not** tactical grids.

## Overview

**Aethelgard Prime** sits in the Crucible’s **triple stellar pull** under **Tempestas-Major’s** magnetosphere and violet canopy. **Tides** run to **kilometer-scale** surge on the open shoal; civilization clusters where **law, ridge, and bridge** buy survival.

Four **major biomes** bracket habitability:

1. **Snowy mountains** — high spine and polar caps  
2. **Taiga** — cold needle-forest belts between uplands and low country  
3. **Swamps** — drowned basins, peat, slow rivers — buffer between forest and tidal plain  
4. **Shoreline** — **Needles** estuaries, **salt mud**, **falesia-style sea cliffs**, and **precipices** where land ends in knife drop to the tidal furnace  

---

## I. Snowy mountains

**Extent:** Summit ranges above the worst **cyclonic** lash from the giant; **polar** massifs where insolation is lowest and ice holds year-round.

**Character:** **Permanent snow**, **blue ice** fields scoured by **UV** under thin atmosphere patches, **avalanche geometry** locals learn like scripture. Rivers born here feed the taiga and, eventually, the swamps — **headwater shrines** and **bridge saint** legends often attach to **pass** and **col** work, not to the shoal.

**Human note:** High settlements are **rare** — logistics favor the belt below — but **PDF**, **auxilia**, and **Needles** guides treat peaks as **weather spine** and **relay** for long-range sightlines when **storm light** kills optics in the violet murk below.

---

## II. Taiga

**Extent:** Broad bands **below treeline**, rolling from mountain feet toward interior lowlands — **cold needle forest** (conifer analogues), thin soil, **black peat** starts here.

**Character:** Timber for **bridge guilds**, **charcoal**, **ridge cane** analogues; hunts for fur and **fiber** that fed the Age of Strife **bottle** trade alongside salt. Wind brings **reek** of brine when storms lift sea aerosol inland — taiga air tastes like **warning**.

**Naming:** Distinct from coastal **Needles** (thorn-estuary flora). Taiga needles are **ridge-timber**; shoreline **Needles** are **salt-root** — same planet, **forked** ecology.

---

## III. Swamps

**Extent:** **Depressions**, **morass**, and **drowned river nets** between taiga and the **intertidal** rim — freshwater bias upstream, **brackish** fingers where tides push **salt wedges** up sluggish channels.

**Character:** **Peat**, **mud lungers**, **rot** — Men of Iron-era **cuts** and **broken culverts** still surface as **geometry** in the slime. **River arbiters** hold court here as much as on open estuaries: **water-law** begins in **slow water** before it reaches **salt**.

**Risk:** Seasonal **flood** ties to distant melt and storm surge — **calendar** fights between **mountain thaw** and **ocean climb**.

**Iconic fauna:** *Canis Batavorum* (**Batav Wolf**) — **stalk** and **ambush** in peat, reed, and drowned-channel cover; overlaps **shore** populations that hunt the **Spire** margin. [batav-wolf-canis-batavorum.md](../../biological-encyclopedia-bestiary/batav-wolf-canis-batavorum.md).

---

## IV. Shoreline — Needles, mud, falesias, precipices

**Extent:** The **living edge** where **two-kilometer tides** redraw the map — **intertidal plains** of **salt mud**, **thorn-mangrove analogue** (**Needles**) along brackish channels, and — where ancient **fault** or **basalt** resists abrasion — **falesia-style** coasts: **sheer sea cliffs**, **bluffs**, and **precipices** mortals treat as **final argument** against carelessness.

**Needles:** Estuarine **blood-mangrove** texture — pneumatophores in **anoxic** mud, **fiber** economies, **tide bells** hung from gnarled limbs; **salt families** and **bridge spans** dominate **social geography** here.

**Iconic fauna (margin):** *Canis Batavorum* — the **Batav Wolf** — **Spire** “drop” hunts on the intertidal; same species **stalks** inland swamps (see **III**). [batav-wolf-canis-batavorum.md](../../biological-encyclopedia-bestiary/batav-wolf-canis-batavorum.md).

**Falesias / precipices:** Vertical faces **not** everywhere — pockets where rock holds while mud strips — **salvage** on **ebb**, **death** on misjudged **flood**. **Cable kin** metaphors bleed into **cliff logistics**: rope, **torque**, **span trial** initiation.

**Storm light:** Violet **canopy** from the giant; **High Gothic** signage fades — navigation by **ridge line**, **bell**, **reek**, **mud color**.

## Biome interaction (one glance)

| Biome | Dominant hazard | Primary export (mortal economy) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mountains | Cold, avalanche, UV | Ice-melt **timing**, stone, **pass rights** |
| Taiga | Fire risk (dry spells), predator trails | Timber, fiber, charcoal |
| Swamps | Disease, drowning, **brackish creep** | Peat fuel, slow-water **law** jurisdiction |
| Shoreline | **Tide**, cliff fall, **surge** | Salt, **sonar meat**, **Needles** harvest, bridge tolls |
