## 0. Preamble

### 0.1 Physical frame — tonnage as budget

**Bifrons-Ferrum** is one world that **broke and kept working**. After the stellar catastrophe that birthed **Ferrum-X**, the rocky body survived as **two hemispheres** — **Bifrons-Ferrum-I** and **Bifrons-Ferrum-II** — separated by a **~5,000 km vacuum gulf**. Colossal **adamantium cables** and **magnetic bridges** stitch the halves. Life is not “planet surface” in the Noviomagus sense; it is **span logistics under white-dwarf glare**, with arsenal districts and sealed **stasis tombs** where sleeping iron is treated as **civic inventory**, not rumor entertainment.

**Gravity / climate:** Local gravity sits near Imperial norm on habitable crust bands; the gulf’s **void wind** and cable **resonance** define weather more than rain. Ferrum-X’s **white-blue glare** and X-ray wash make open-span work a radiation ledger. Citizens speak of **hum weather** — when cable tone rises, every dock knows a tension event is coming. Slow death is **microfracture of trust and steel alike**: rust on tether housings, hearing loss from lifelong hum, cancers from bad span shifts, and the quiet knowledge that a cut cable deletes two cities’ futures at once.

**Scale:** Twin capitals (one per half), cable cities hung on mid-spans, arsenal stacks, tomb approaches, and dual-senate halls that only become law when they **reconcile on the neutral span**. Population is smaller than Noviomagus’s stacked billions and denser than Aethelgard’s mud belts — a **siege-civilization** packed into vaults, forges, and bridge guilds.

**What citizens think in:** **Tonnage**, **tension**, **Gate A / Gate B receipts**, **half-stamps**, **hum schedules**, **coffin weight vs quota**. Provincial feel: High Gothic law arrives; local identity stays **twin-kingdom** and **inventory-first**. The Throne is honored when **both halves** remain on the same ledger.

**Budget metaphor (lock):** **Tonnage.** A prayer that cannot be weighed is suspect. A funeral that cannot be booked against quota is unfinished. A wedding that does not **span** is incomplete theater.

### 0.2 Fork

**Not Noviomagus:** No Black Rain elevator religion, no single forge-stack capital ego. Throughput still matters, but the holy number is **balanced load across the gulf**, not filter-hour alone.

**Not Aethelgard:** Bridges exist, but here they are **void cables**, not mud spans. Tide silence becomes **hum silence** — do not shout a lie into the gulf.

**Not Incus weight-cult or Vitreus lens bureaucracy:** Bifrons is **twin-kingdom Dacian mood** — gorge as **altar**, death as **asset on inventory**, dual senates that must kiss seals mid-span or admit the world is already cut.

**Porta Nihili accent:** The Emperor is paid in labor that **keeps both halves named on one Throne receipt**. God keeps **inventory of both faces**. Outsiders misread: they see “death cult” or “Mechanicus vault theater.” Locals see **honest accounting of what must not fall**.

**What outsiders get wrong:** That Dreadnought tombs are a tourist crypt or clinical hospital. Mortals meet them as **civic shadow** — sealed approaches, changed hum, prestige of proximity, fear of waking rumors — never as Apothecarion taxonomy.

### 0.3 Sorting border (class police)

- **Primary sorter:** **Span pass / cable right** — which cars, tether cars, and mid-span districts a body may enter without escort.
- **Secondary sorter:** **Half-stamp + tomb/arsenal proximity** — I-face or II-face civil plaque; how close one’s district sits to sealed stasis approaches or munitions spine.
- **Felt before a clerk speaks:** Cable hum in the bones; which half’s cant your vowels carry; whether your coat smells of **span grease** or **tomb dust**; whether you look at **both horizons** when you swear.

#### Exposure / rights / meaning

| Stratum | Span / cable exposure | Rights felt | Meaning |
|---------|----------------------|-------------|---------|
| **Rabble** | Outer tether labor, gulf-edge scrap, cable-underside reclaim | Service climbs; no mid-span housing; half-stamp often forged or borrowed | Bodies that keep tension honest so others may pretend the world is one |
| **Common** | Bridge-guild shifts, arsenal floor, dual-dock clerks | Shift cars; reconciled half-stamps; tomb-approach service corridors (not vault doors) | Spine of tonnage theology; can marry across halves with guild witness |

#### Worked intersections (fiction borders)

1. **Neutral span market** — all three strata meet; rabble sell grease and luck charms; common stamp cargo; upper buy silence and reconciled seals.  
2. **Cable hum spike** — priority lock; stratum learned in who is ordered off the car first.  
3. **Tomb approach dusk** — common wardens and upper patrons; rabble turned at the dust line unless reclaim-contracted.  
4. **Gate A / Gate B dual dock** — clerk reads both half-stamps before the magnet engages; one missing face = sin smell.  
5. **Funeral weigh-bay** — coffin tonnage vs quota; class shows in whether the deficit is **paid**, **borrowed**, or **hidden**.

**Border fiction tells:** grease-black nails → guild plaque → dual-face key worn without looking. Insult: *one-face oath*. Mercy: a spare span-hour or a witness to reconcile your seal.

### 0.5 Local mortal sin & Legatus read

- **Local mortal sin — asymmetric seal:** Logging, blessing, or marrying a receipt to **only one half** while the gulf load, Gate pair, or funeral tonnage remains **unreconciled**. Enforced by dual-dock clerks, bridge-guild boards, Arbites on span riots, and Ministorum stamp booths that refuse single-face holiness.  
  - *Rabble color:* Borrowed stamp used on one dock only; cutting scrap from a cable without logging the other end’s tension change.  
  - *Common color:* Filing Gate A clean and Gate B “tomorrow”; weigh-bay deficit hidden on the quieter half.  
  - *Upper color:* Senate vote sealed on one face before neutral-span reconciliation; patronage that buys a single-face audit.  
- **Legatus public read:** Mortals say ***Pater Murorum*** — Father of Walls — when they mean the iron that makes both gates one equation. Guard billets and span gossip also say **Twin-Gate Warden**. Surface only: patience before breach, hatred of one-face haste; **not** siege doctrine, Valerian sealed detail, or gene-seed talk.

## 1. Military (mortal)

Bifrons-Ferrum’s mortal force culture is **corridor and cable geometry**. The PDF is trained to treat the dual world as one kill-channel mesh: hold Gate A and Gate B as a **single problem**, never as rival theaters. Riot doctrine assumes **span choke** — if the neutral market panics, both capitals starve. Arbites posture is heavy on the spans and light in outer reclaim; they care about **asymmetric seal** and cable sabotage more than tavern blasphemy.

When the Wall is late, **rabble die first** on outer tethers and gulf-edge scrap rings; **common** die second in arsenal corridors and dock locks; **upper** die last behind dual-face keys — unless a cable fails, in which case class becomes a rumor the void does not honor.

**Shared Sweat (surface):** Auxilia and Guard train on the same docks as bridge guilds; sweat is shared on tension drills and munitions lifts. Chapter brothers are **weather** — schedules that clear a span, silence that means move — not confessors. Mortals do not recite Friction Geometry; they learn *don’t split the gates* as folk safety.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Levy fodder, gulf pickets, bait for deserter sweeps, human counterweights on emergency tension crews. Desertion toward the gulf’s dark is a death sentence by physics before Arbites. Pride is thin and bitter: *we held the outer loop*.
- **Common:** Career PDF in arsenal corridors; bridge-guild militia with legal kill rights on saboteurs; Shared Sweat cadres who know Batavi silence as respect geometry. Draft seasons empty dock shifts; returning veterans wear **hum scars** (hearing loss) as service marks.
- **Upper:** Officer houses with dual-face commissions; senate war-clerks who authorize span lockdown; Span Houses that billet Guard liaisons without owning Chapter keys. Military honor is **reconciled command** — an order stamped on both halves.

**Worked examples**

1. **Span riot (Cage memory folklore):** Crowd tries to cut a “cursed” cable after a heretic-siege anniversary; PDF fires into the market while Arbites seal both Gate docks; common guilds form human chains to keep tension crews working.  
2. **Asymmetric draft scandal:** One half fills levy quotas; the other buys exemptions; dual-senate collapses until neutral-span reconciliation — classic **asymmetric seal** in uniform.  
3. **Shared Sweat munitions lift:** Guard and guild raise a siege shell crate under cable hum; no speeches; when a Batavi shadow passes, work continues — pride without claim.  
4. **Tomb-approach lock:** Mortal wardens turn civilians when Chapter precinct seals; rumor of “sleeping iron” rises; Arbites fine anyone selling waking charms as heretek theater (surface — not clinical Dreadnought law).

## 2. Culture

Amenities are **scarce, bought, or faked**. Leisure is cable-side dice, dual-dock drinking halls with windows on both horizons, and **span festivals** when tension stays green for a full shift cycle. Aesthetic: industrial grey, twin-pillar motifs, coffins displayed with **weight plaques**, wedding banners that literally hang across mid-cable. Public shame is **one-face display** — a feast held only on I while II starves. Public success is a **reconciled seal** posted where both crowds can see.

Non-Mass rites: **split vow** on mid-cable; **iron echo** (speak a name into the gulf — custom says it returns if the speaker’s intent is clean); **span child** naming when birth coincides with bridge repair; **torque wreaths** for dead engineers; **funeral weigh** as gallows liturgy.

**By class — include Sound**

- **Rabble:** Amenities = shared grease pot, stolen filter time near dock heat, songs that mock both senates. Leisure = illegal underside climbs (romance and corpse). Shame = dying without a weight line for the funeral bay. **Sound:** clipped, dark, *both ends or none*; humor that names death as cargo.  
- **Common:** Guild halls with dual clocks (I-time / II-time); arsenal canteen hymns timed to press cycles; marriage feasts that must host guests from both halves or be called asymmetric. **Sound:** exhausted loyalty, *hum-closed face*, soft contempt for idle thunder; pride in clean dual receipts.  
- **Upper:** Neutral-span galleries, private magnetic cars, patronage of tomb-approach chapels (civic shadow), auctions of reconciled cargo rights. Span Houses host quiet Shared Sweat tables. **Sound:** cooler High Gothic; span cant underneath; Span Houses quieter than auction piety.

## 3. Society

Rank is **half-stamp + guild board + senate shadow**. Humiliation is being forced to wear a **single-face plaque** in the neutral market. Street law prefers bridge-guild boards and dual-dock clerks before Administratum appeal. Governance is **dual senates** that are legally incomplete until they meet on the **neutral span** and press a joint slate. Representation for rabble is nearly nil except as tension-labor votes sold by guilds. Rationing follows cable priority: span crews eat first when hum spikes. Tax-as-wound: tithe that hits only one half is felt as **civic betrayal** — the wound is asymmetry, not the Throne’s hunger alone.

Marriage/contract: **span marriage** (vow mid-cable); **dual-face contract** (property logged on both halves); divorce filed as **load misalignment**. Death geography: memorial walls on both capitals; Wall-name rumors from Chapter war; **tomb shadow** districts where proximity is prestige and dread — not *Reclusiam* crypt law.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Street law is tether-brother oaths; tax is labor seizure; marriage is often unstamped and later “reconciled” for a fee; forgotten until a cable needs a body.  
- **Common:** Guild boards are real government; dual-stamp marriage is respectability; funeral weigh keeps name on inventory; representation through guild seats on span councils.  
- **Upper:** Dual-senate blood; tax exemptions traded as patronage; children betrothed across halves to prevent political one-face drift; Span Houses mediate without claiming peak crown.

**Friction matrix**

| Pressure | Rabble | Common | Upper |
|----------|--------|--------|-------|
| Who is “forgotten” | Always — both ledgers | By Terra; not by guild | By Terra; refuse either half’s omission |
| Holy object | Tether loop / climb knife | Dual half-stamp / plaque | Reconciliation slate / dual-face key |
| Ministorum | Suspect thunder / useful water | Stamp booth > cathedral | Patronage + dual chapel optics |
| Batavi presence | Fear / dark pride / weather | Shared Sweat respect | Necessary shadow; want influence without being owned |

## 4. Religion

Official creed remains Ecclesiarchy-baptized. Local accent is **inventory piety**: God loves the hand that keeps **both faces** on the Throne’s receipt. Priests wear industrial stoles on cable shifts; bishops measure faith in **reconciled tonnage**. Holidays that stop work: **Load Festival** (carry across mid-span), **Hum Silence** (listen-only watches when cable tone climbs), **Twin Gate Mass** (brief, dual-dock, never freezes arsenals for idle scream). Texts in practice: dual ledgers blessed as scripture-adjacent; High Gothic collects stiffly. Religious punishment: double shift on the underused half; public wearing of a single-face plaque; rare heresy trials for selling “wake the iron” charms near tomb approaches.

**Stamp booth vs chapel:** When holiness needs a receipt, the **dual stamp booth** beats the cathedral. Chapel wins for funerals and span marriages — still with weigh-masters present.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Prayer is grip and shared grease; priests who bring water and radiation paste are holy; thunder without tools is enemy.  
- **Common:** Faith of work as dual receipt; gear prayer becomes **three knuckle taps on both Gate posts**; sin is asymmetric seal.  
- **Upper:** Correct dual devotion as status mask; fund tomb-approach chapels; fear missionaries who freeze one half’s production for spectacle.

**Contrast only:** Batavi honor **Ancestor-Sovereign / Throne-weight** — not Ministorum God-Emperor worship. Shared words (*Emperor*, *Throne*, *seal*) ≠ shared creed. Mortals still use chapel language; the feel is **inventory-first**. Do not collapse Batavi silence into Ecclesiarchy ecstasy.

## 5. Economy

**World function / export:** Arsenal throughput for the Crucible and mesh — munitions, siege fittings, cable/tether hardware, stasis-adjacent sustainment logistics (mortal side: crates, seals, coolant, escorts). Twin forges and dual docks make Bifrons a **siege pantry**. Vault prestige draws pilgrimage coin and patronage, but the true export is **weight that kills on schedule**.

Work runs on **hum schedules**, Gate pair cycles, and senate reconciliation calendars. Tithe payment: dual-face manifests pressed on the neutral span; failure smell is **one half’s docks quiet while the other screams overtime** — asymmetric seal as fiscal stench. Exemptions: Span Houses and senate blood buy partial relief; bridge guilds buy none for tension crews (bodies are the premium). Real markets: grease, span-hours, dual stamps, funeral-weight credits. Ledger fiction: “balanced load” claims when one cable is already dying.

Wealth means **dual-face keys**, reconciled cargo rights, and tomb-approach patronage — not elevator cars or grain silos.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Paid in grease-time and ration chits; sell scrap and luck; wealth is a working stamp and a brother who holds your tether.  
- **Common:** Guild wages; overtime when Cage anniversary drills empty docks; wealth is plaque + clean dual receipt + marriage across halves.  
- **Upper:** Inventory ownership; tithe brokerage; wealth is the power to reconcile or withhold a senate slate.

**Failure smell (lock detail):** Rotting coolant on one dock only; coffins stacked on II while I’s weigh-bay runs empty; a Gate A seal bright and Gate B slate blank — civilians name it before Arbites arrive.

## 6. Education

Literacy on Bifrons is **load literacy** first: reading dual manifests, tension marks, Gate colors, and funeral weight tables. Who learns: common guild apprentices and upper house tutors. Who is kept dark: rabble climbers taught only oral tether law — enough to die usefully, not enough to forge senate ink (though forgery schools exist in underside rumor).

**Tracks:**

- **Bridge guild** — tension math, void climb, dual-dock ritual, saboteur recognition.  
- **Arsenal house** — munitions handling, friend–foe discrimination drills for mortal crews (full phrasing — no opaque abbreviations), siege-crate seals.  
- **Chapel / stamp** — High Gothic collects, dual blessing litany, heresy of waking charms.  
- **Administratum** — dual-face registry, tithe reconciliation, senate minutes.  
- **Tomb warden civic school** — approach etiquette, civic shadow law, what **not** to claim about sleeping iron (surface prestige, not clinical dump).

Curriculum-as-virtue: a child who can reconcile a practice slate is “adult”; a child who swears one-face is punished. Cost: years of hearing damage, patronage debts, bodies lost on apprentice climbs. Literacy *does* this: lets you argue a funeral weight, refuse an asymmetric draft, or sell Span House soft power.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Oral only; underside schools teach forgery and climb; formal letters rare.  
- **Common:** Guild schools mandatory; chapel literacy secondary; dual-clock arithmetic holy.  
- **Upper:** Tutors on both halves; High Gothic polish; Span House children drilled in both cants before senate shadows.
