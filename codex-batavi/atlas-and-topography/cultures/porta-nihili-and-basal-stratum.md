## 1. Noviomagus as provincial capital

**Noviomagus Prime** is **not** Holy Terra — but it is the closest thing the subsector has to a **provincial capital** in the ancient sense: **Imperial forms arrive intact** (Administratum registers, Ministorum chapels where the soot allows, High Gothic in law), while **local identity** remains **forge-first**. Citizens think in **shifts**, **district stamps**, **yield**, and **rain schedules**. The Throne is honored when **the line holds** and the **tithe seal** reads clean.

Elsewhere in Castra Vetera, worlds are **more** “barbaricum”: fewer cathedrals, more **oath**, **guild**, **tide**, **mine**, **signal**. Noviomagus is where **Imperial default** and **frontier substrata** negotiate in open daylight.

## 2. The faith of work (Porta Nihili basal creed)

Across Porta Nihili jurisdictions, **orthodox devotion** is expressed **through action**, not eloquence first.

* **The Emperor is paid in labor.** A completed shift, a logged manifest, a child registered to quota, a fuse replaced before the hive browns out — these are **prayers with receipts**.
* **Speech is cheap; seals are holy.** Open *vox* sermons matter less than **the tally-clerk’s stamp** and **the forge priest’s blessing on a torque wrench**. Ecstatic preachers who cannot **audit a cargo slate** are treated as **entertainment** or **suspect**.
* **Silence is offering** when the network is sacred: **relay silence**, **listen-only watches**, **mute archives** — spiritual disciplines born from **Men of Iron** aftermath and **cognito-hazard** discipline, then generalized into mortal habit.

This is **not** Mechanicus replacement theology. Mortals remain **Ecclesiarchy-baptized** in record. The **accent** is frontier: God loves **the useful hand**.

## 3. Historical stratum — Age of Strife bottle and fracture

**Shared legend (pre–Imperial reunification, pre–Batavi crucible):** For long centuries the Castra Vetera mesh behaved as a **closed trade bottle** — warp storms ate the outside; **inside**, worlds traded, married, and warred like **cousins**. That produced a **basal creole** of **calendars**, **debt words**, **storm names**, and **feast rails** still recognizable planet to planet.

**Fracture:** **Men of Iron** pressure during and after the Foundation epoch did not erase culture; it **throttled routes**. Cross-pollination dropped from **many-to-many** to **safe corridors** and **neighbor-only** lanes. Worlds stopped being mirrors and became **survival forks** — same ingredients, different **taboo geometry**.

Every local culture file below assumes: **two registers** — **high Imperial** (what scribes file) and **basal regional** (what workers say when the seal is already pressed).
