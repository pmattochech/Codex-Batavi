## 0. Preamble

### 0.1 Physical frame — rust as budget

**Noviomagus Prime** sits at **1.12G**, crust hollowed and ribbed with adamantium and ferrocrete, under permanent soot and acid **Black Rain**. Pale light from **Noviomagus-Alpha** reaches the stacks only as sick filter-glow through macro-forge dust. ~**120 billion** souls live in stacked hives dedicated to arms, armor, and shameless throughput. *Castrum Ferrum* hangs above as orbital citadel and hangar spine; the wet shafts, mid-hive bands, and forge lip are the mortal day.

Life is **rust management**: filters, gutters, reclaim vats, rain schedules, and the constant arithmetic of what corrodes first. Citizens do not think in “freedom” or “career.” They think in **shifts**, **district stamps**, **yield**, **filter grade**, and **which elevator car** a body may board without looking. The Throne is honored when **the line holds** and the **tithe seal** reads clean.

**Provincial capital feel:** Imperial forms arrive intact — Administratum registers, Ministorum chapels where soot allows, High Gothic in law — while local identity stays **forge-first**. Noviomagus is the closest thing the subsector has to a provincial capital in the ancient sense: not Holy Terra, but the place where Imperial default and frontier substrata negotiate in open daylight. Elsewhere in Castra Vetera the worlds lean more *barbaricum*; here the wall was built by urban *limitanei* who still live under it.

**Slow death:** Black Rain eats lungs, seals, and plaques. Unfiltered hours steal names. Open-rain dead are weather reports before they are funerals. The budget metaphor is **rust** — every virtue is something that still works after corrosion; every sin is something that slipped the ledger while the metal failed.

### 0.2 Fork

**Not Castra-Vetera Prime:** not autumn ecumenopolis judgment culture, not tithe nobility as primary weather.  
**Not Khan-Bator Prime:** not starch plains, silo-cities, or caloric oath as the holy object.  
**Not Aethelgard / Crucible tide-worlds:** not surge calendars or estuary law.  
**Not neighbor forge myths of “spiky barbarians”:** Noviomagus is **provincial Roman** energy — High Gothic law, local forge cant, receipt holiness.

**Porta Nihili accent line:** Faith of work expressed as **throughput theology**. Saints are forge masters whose dies never wandered. Sin is **unlogged scrap** and **missed torque**. Chapels exist; workers often prefer **the stamp booth** — *“God saw the seal.”*

**What outsiders misread:** Magnates as hive-kings who own the Chapter; Shared Sweat as kinship with Astartes; Solis soft power as Vexilla rank; Corbec as feast governor; rain stoicism as simple piety rather than **filter arithmetic**. Outsiders also invent Founding gossip and progenitor laundry lists — the open Imperial record on Noviomagus does **not** ratify those claims, and mortal street talk that does is rumor, not seal.

### 0.3 Sorting border (class police)

- **Primary sorter:** **Black Rain** exposure and **filter grade** (cracked rag → guild filter → house cartridge).  
- **Secondary sorter:** **Elevator rights** and **civil ID plaque** windows (denied / shift / house key / Solis bridge pass).  
- **Felt before a clerk speaks:** Canopy vs open gutter; which car a body boards without looking; whether the rain smell is chronic, managed, or bought-distant.

| Stratum | Black Rain exposure | Elevator rights | Meaning |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Rabble** | High / chronic | Denied / service-only | Filter-hours as currency; stairs and illegal climbs; mid-stack catch = beating or reclaim labor; open-rain dead = name erased |
| **Common** | Managed | Shift rights (plaque + window) | Canopies, shift tunnels; rain schedule as moral calendar; wrong car = not *seal-clean*; windows traded like dowries |
| **Upper** | Bought distance | House keys / private cars | Sealed galleries; Solis band still *knows* the smell; magnates pretend otherwise; bridge passes for lower-upper civil + Chapter-adjacent floors without owning the shaft |

**Worked intersections (fiction border tells):**

1. **Wet shaft** — rabble and common meet under failure weather; upper almost never.  
2. **Dry gallery after shift bell** — common up; rabble turned; upper already inside.  
3. **Storm lock** — priority only; stratum learned in the wait, not in a sermon.  
4. **Solis pattern** — covered routes + bridge passes; soft power without peak elevator ownership.

**Civil ID plaque** at the gate is liturgy. **Guest ink** is patronage currency. Chapter precincts = **parallel vertical** (not “upper class”). Lower-upper houses are the mortals most used to that parallel without claiming it. Shared Sweat crises briefly flatten rain class; hierarchy returns with the next dry gallery.

### 0.4 Fiction strata lock

**Labels:** **Rabble** · **Common** · **Upper** *(locked — not Administratum legal castes)*  
**Bridge band:** **Solis family / Solis-tier households** — **lower upper class**. Forge-cant rooms and Chapter-adjacent civil rooms without peak elevator ownership. Soft power = Shared Sweat / kitchen continuity and quiet *face-hold*. Still upper, not mid-common. **Do not delete this lock.**

#### Rabble

- **Who:** Undercroft reclaim crews, gutter runners, unstamped scrap-sorters, rain-blind beggars between elevator rights, Black-Rain lungers selling filter time by the hour.  
- **Mood premises map (Hymn, author-side):** Duty without audience = survival *is* the shift — lowest drains held so stacks above do not drown in rust. Forgotten, not faithless = **pre-forgotten** — never on the ledger worth remembering; oath is don’t steal a brother’s filter; don’t leave a body in open rain. Weathered service = faith is hands that still move. Loss as weather = missing people are weather reports; grief short, then back to the vat.  
- **Sound:** Short sentences, dark humor, pipe-cant. Proto–*We hold* as “we don’t leave the pipe.” Questions answered with verbs, not theology.  
- **Holy object:** Filter, knife, shared scrap, the dry patch under a brother’s canopy.  
- **How “forgotten” works:** Always. The Imperium does not file them; districts remember only while the drain still needs a hand.

#### Common

- **Who:** Shift guilds, middle-stack district dynasties (e.g. **Guild of the Lower Gutter**, **Third Shift Concord**), stamp clerks, forge hands with **civil ID plaques** — spine of throughput theology.  
- **Mood premises map:** Duty without audience = the seal *is* the prayer; Wall above must not starve for shells. Forgotten, not faithless = Nihilus-edge known; baptize and stamp in **forge cant**, not cathedral ecstasy. Weathered service = virtue = logged torque, on-time shift, rain schedule; sin = unlogged scrap, empty speech. Loss as weather = convoy silence and Wall-name rumors filed next to rust.  
- **Sound:** Exhausted loyalty. *Rain-closed face.* Soft contempt for loud missionaries; quiet respect for Shared Sweat without speeches. Longer sentences than rabble, but still receipt-shaped.  
- **Holy object:** Seal, plaque, shift bell, rain schedule slate.  
- **How “forgotten” works:** By the Imperium’s far offices, not by the district. Local memory is the guild ledger and the gear-prayer circle.

#### Upper

- **Who:** Stack-elevator magnates, forge-house elders, Administratum liaison blood, Ministorum chapter-house patrons, **lower-upper bridge houses** (Solis lock above).  
- **Mood premises map:** Duty without audience = audience is **local** (Chapter shadow, tithe, rival houses); form keeps the forge legal. Forgotten, not faithless = status anxiety — over-invest in seals and “correct” devotion so outsiders cannot call them fringe. Weathered service = feed the machine; charity as rationed usefulness; fear useless piety as much as heresy. Loss as weather = losses named in marble and ledgers — still treated as **cost**.  
- **Sound:** Cooler sentences; forge-cant under High Gothic. Peak magnates auction piety in careful doses. Lower-upper / Solis: quieter than magnates; less auctioned piety; *face-hold* instead of thunder.  
- **Holy object:** Chapel + clean audit + elevator key (Solis: window stamp + bridge pass + kitchen continuity).  
- **How “forgotten” works:** By Terra; refuse to be forgotten by Noviomagus. Names carved; audits kept; guest ink as immortality lite.

### 0.5 Local mortal sin & Legatus read

- **Local mortal sin:** **Unlogged scrap** — material, torque, or yield that left the seal. Enforcement: stamp booths, forge-priests, district guild courts, Administratum window clerks; Arbites when diversion scales to civic threat. **Class-colored versions:**  
  - *Rabble:* stealing a brother’s filter-hour or dumping reclaim off-manifest so a drain “doesn’t count.”  
  - *Common:* missed torque, empty speech at the booth, wrong-car boarding filed as not *seal-clean*.  
  - *Upper:* loud devotion that fails audit; tithe theater that hides diversion; guest ink sold as if it were a seal.  
  **Paired civic wound:** **missed torque** — the same sin family when the hand fails the machine rather than the ledger.  
- **Legatus public read:** Mortals call Corbec Ardens the **Warden Gauge** — forge governor of ammo clocks and embarkation tallies; *“Corbec keeps the clocks honest.”* Surface only: not feast king, not hive-king Castellan, not a sermon. Thin Valerian gossip may say the first seat broke and the Wall still holds; sealed doctrine stays sealed.

## 1. Military (mortal)

Noviomagus mortal force is organized around **rain geometry**, **forge-lip billets**, and **Shared Sweat** with the Wall — not around parade culture. PDF and career militia hold canopies, storm locks, and reclaim corridors. Imperial Guard draft and billet houses feed the wider mesh when tithe and war demand bodies. Arbites posture is **ledger violence**: riot control at wet shafts, elevator fraud, and scrap diversion that threatens civic order. When the Wall is late, **rabble rain pickets and levy fodder die first**; common shift militia die next in the ditch; upper officer houses file the cost and argue priority.

Chapter contact surfaces (Phase A only): Shared Sweat musters at forge-lip PDF canopy barracks; parallel elevator keys visible but not claimable; dark pride and fear by class. Full *Prima* doctrine, obligation lattice, and calibration war-culture live in Phase B — not here.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Levy fodder, rain pickets, wet-shaft meat when Ork scrap or underhive riot climbs the gutters. No career path worth a plaque. Survival doctrine: hold the pipe, don’t leave a body in open rain, don’t expect a sermon when the canopy fails. Friend–foe discrimination on the street is guild color, filter grade, and who shared a pipe watch — not auspex catechism.  
- **Common:** Career PDF / district militia, Shared Sweat pride without kinship claim, stamp-supply partners to forge-lip billets. Third Shift Concord middle-stack bands feel *Prima* line-replacement tempo as ammo queues and acceptance trials pressing their rain schedule — surface friction, not billet ownership. Ork hate is daily civic weather; xenos “specialists” stay alien.  
- **Upper:** Officer houses, Solis staff who open civil rooms without owning the shaft, patronage of PDF canopies as *face-hold*. Peak magnates want influence over Guard draft lists without being owned by the Chapter shadow. Lower-upper: closest civilian soft door to Shared Sweat / kitchen continuity — still mortal, still not Vexilla rank.

**Worked examples**

- **Draft window after storm lock:** Priority cars leave first; rabble wait in acid mist; common plaques buy the second wave; upper already dry. Stratum is the lesson.  
- **Wet-shaft riot:** Arbites seal elevators; forge-priests freeze scrap manifests; Shared Sweat may flatten class for one ditch — hierarchy returns when the gallery dries.  
- **Forge-lip Shared Sweat muster (Third Shift Concord band):** PDF canopy barracks + stamp booths within one rain-schedule district of the forge lip. Peak magnate cars do not share that ditch. Mortals who sweat beside brothers without a sermon read the Wall correctly; those who claim kinship misread it.

## 2. Culture

Amenities are scarce, bought, fake, or forbidden by rain and plaque. Aesthetic is **honest corrosion** (*black tooth* as seniority badge), *rain-closed face* stoicism, and gear-belt friction habits. Public shame is open-rain failure, wrong-car boarding, and **unlogged scrap**. Public success is a clean seal, a traded elevator window, a guest-ink invitation, and surviving a Shared Sweat ditch without speech-making. Everyday rites that are **not** Mass: *pipe watch*, *gear prayer* (three knuckle taps on a duty belt), *window stamp*, filter-hour trades, dry-gallery shift bells.

Leisure: reclaim-song circles under illegal canopies (rabble); guild halls with thin starch and thicker gossip (common); chapel patronage dinners and sealed-gallery concerts that must still pass audit smell (upper). Forbidden leisure: ecstatic missionary theater that cannot audit a cargo slate; loud devotion that fails the booth; scrap diversion parties dressed as “reclaim festivals.”

**By class — include Sound**

- **Rabble:** Amenities = dry patch, shared filter, knife that still cuts reclaim. Aesthetic = patched rubber, rain scars, *black tooth* earned young. Leisure = pipe songs, dark jokes, filter-hour gambling. **Sound:** Short. Dry. “We don’t leave the pipe.” Humor lands on death and rust; long piety is mocked mid-sentence.  
- **Common:** Amenities = plaque, canopy route, shift hall, stamp-booth queue rights. Aesthetic = *rain-closed face*, duty-belt polish, guild colors under soot. Leisure = gear-prayer circles, sealed starch rations, watching missionaries fail the booth. **Sound:** Exhausted loyalty; receipt-shaped clauses; soft contempt for empty thunder; quiet respect for Shared Sweat. Forge cant first, High Gothic only at the window.  
- **Upper:** Amenities = sealed galleries, private cars, chapel lintels, audit clerks on retainer. Aesthetic = cooler iron, house seals, *face-hold*. Leisure = patronage Mass, rationed charity as usefulness theater, kitchen continuity in Solis band. **Sound:** Cool High Gothic over forge cant. Magnates measure silence like yield. Solis / lower-upper: quieter than peak; less auctioned piety; sentences that leave room for the Wall without naming ownership.

## 3. Society

Rank is felt as **filter → plaque → car**, then as guild and house. Humiliation is rain exposure, wrong elevator, guest ink revoked, and being filed as **unlogged**. Street law is district dynasty first (Lower Gutter, Third Shift Concord), then Administratum window, then Arbites when blood or diversion scales. Representation is guild concord and house liaison — not democracy; the forge does not vote, it stamps. Rationing follows rain schedule and plaque grade. Tax-as-wound is the tithe seal and the elevator window fee — paid in yield, years, and children registered to quota.

Marriage / contract norms that define class: **window trades** as dowry among common; house alliance seals among upper; rabble **pipe oaths** (don’t steal filter; don’t leave body) with little paper. Death / name civic geography: open-rain dead = erased; Wall-name rumors filed next to rust; marble and ledger memorials for upper; *Prima* capital crypt and Chapter vaults are **not** populi property — mortals may hear “wolf’s floor” as forbidden gallery rumor only.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** No plaque worth a clerk. Street law = pipe watch and reclaim boss. Tax wound = filter-hours stolen by rain and levy. Forgotten always.  
- **Common:** District dynasties as civic spine. Plaque cult (birth iron). Rain schedule as moral calendar. Tax wound = tithe skim that empties the shift hall.  
- **Upper:** Elevator magnates and forge-house elders as local “senate” of rust. Solis bridge band translates Chapter-adjacent civil rooms without claiming wolf rank. Tax wound = audit fear and status anxiety dressed as devotion.

**Friction matrix**

| Pressure | Rabble | Common | Upper |
|----------|--------|--------|-------|
| Who is “forgotten” | Always | By the Imperium, not by the district | By Terra; refuse to be by Noviomagus |
| Holy object | Filter, knife, shared scrap | Seal, plaque, shift bell | Chapel + clean audit + elevator key |
| Ministorum | Useless / dangerous | Wrong accent, sometimes useful | Patronage tool + status mask |
| Batavi presence | Watch / fear / dark pride | Shared Sweat respect | Necessary shadow; want influence without being owned |

## 4. Religion

**Official creed:** Ecclesiarchy-baptized on record — God-Emperor language in chapels and law.  
**Local accent (Porta Nihili / throughput theology):** The Emperor is paid in labor. Completed shift, logged manifest, child registered to quota, fuse replaced before brownout — prayers with receipts. Speech is cheap; seals are holy. Ecstatic preachers who cannot audit a cargo slate = entertainment or suspect. Silence can be offering (relay silence, listen-only watches) — Men of Iron hangover as habit. Saints are forge masters whose dies never wandered.

**Priests vs laity:** Stamp-booth forge-priests and window clerks often outrank cathedral voices in daily holiness. Rain-side confessors serve rabble when they bring filters, not thunder. District chapels compete with seal booths; Shared Sweat priests rise when empty thunder falls.

**Holidays that stop work:** Rain-schedule feast rails tied to storm locks and shift-concord founding days; forge-master saint days when dies are re-blessed; tithe-seal confirmation weeks (work slows for audit, not for ecstasy). Idle cathedral time that ignores rain schedule reads as theft from the line.

**Texts in practice:** Manifests, torque logs, plaque registries, baptismal rolls that must match window stamps. High Gothic collects exist; forge cant carries the moral load.

**Religious punishment:** Unlogged scrap as civic-religious wound; public booth refusal; canopy exile; Arbites when diversion becomes heresy-of-tithe. Flagellant fashion imported from Castra-Vetera is mocked unless it carries a seal.

**Stamp booth vs chapel:** When holiness needs a receipt, the booth wins — *“God saw the seal.”* Chapel wins for baptism records, patronage display, and upper *face-hold*.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Pipe oaths over Mass. Confessors useful if they bring filter-hours. Sin = leaving a body in open Black Rain; stealing a brother’s filter.  
- **Common:** Gear prayer + booth orthodoxy. Baptize in forge cant. Sin = **unlogged scrap**, missed torque, empty speech.  
- **Upper:** Chapel patronage + clean audit as twin sacraments. Loud devotion that fails audit is taboo. Solis: *window stamp* piety — quieter, bridge-shaped.

**Contrast only:** Batavi honor **Ancestor-Sovereign / Throne-weight** — oath and Wall geometry — **not** Ministorum God-Emperor worship. Shared words (“seal,” “hold,” “duty”) ≠ shared creed. Population still uses chapel language; the **feel** is work-first. Do not collapse mortal throughput theology into Chapter *Axiom* or *Reclusiam* doctrine.

## 5. Economy

**World function / export:** Civilized forge capital of the geartrain — arms, armor, line-replacement parts, ammo tempo for the mesh. *Castrum Ferrum* and Steel Belt salvage feed reclaim. Horreum-Secundus and wet-forge periphery bodies inherit the same receipt culture with local deltas (Phase C stubs — not this file’s ownership).

**How work runs:** Shift guilds, district dynasties, forge-priest stamp cycles, Administratum windows, rain-scheduled throughput. Yield is moral and fiscal. Corbec’s public shadow is **ammo clocks** and embarkation tallies — mortals feel queues, not sealed calibration doctrine.

**Tithe payment and failure smell:** Tithe seal must read clean. Failure smell is **unlogged scrap**, missed torque, empty manifests, wrong-car fraud, and starch/ration diversion dressed as reclaim. When the seal fails, canopies thin, draft widens, and Arbites walk the wet shafts.

**Exemptions:** Guest ink and house keys buy distance from rain, not from tithe truth. Chapter parallel precincts are not civilian exemption theater. Solis bridge passes open rooms — they do not erase yield debt.

**Real markets vs ledger fiction:** Filter-hours, elevator windows, scrap reclaim, and starch trades are real. Magnate charity ledgers and some chapel patronage books are fiction that must still smell like a seal. “Wealth” under this sky = **dry air**, **clean audit**, **car without looking**, and **bodies still on the plaque**.

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Currency = filter-hours, reclaim knife-work, illegal canopy rents. Tithe wound arrives as levy and stolen dry time.  
- **Common:** Currency = plaque windows, shift yield, guild starch. Tithe wound = skim that empties the hall; virtue = seal-clean manifest.  
- **Upper:** Currency = elevator keys, audit clerks, patronage Mass, guest ink. Tithe wound = status death if the seal fails in public. Solis: soft power markets (kitchen continuity, bridge passes) without peak shaft ownership.

## 6. Education

Literacy on Noviomagus is not enlightenment — it is **window survival**. Who learns is who must stamp, audit, or speak High Gothic without losing forge cant. Who is kept dark is who only needs to hold a pipe and fear open rain. Curriculum-as-virtue: torque, manifest, rain schedule, plaque law, and “do not speak empty.” Cost is paid in years of shift, patronage debts, lung scarring in reclaim apprenticeships, and children registered early to quota tracks.

**Tracks**

- **Guild / shift schools:** Common spine — plaque letters, torque hymns, booth etiquette.  
- **House tutors / lower-upper kitchens:** Solis-band civil manners, guest-ink protocol, quiet High Gothic.  
- **Chapel schools:** Baptismal literacy and Ministorum collects — useful when they respect the booth; mocked when they do not.  
- **Administratum clerk tracts:** Window law, elevator registries, tithe arithmetic — upper and ambitious common.  
- **Reclaim apprenticeships:** Rabble track — filter grades, pipe watch, scrap sorting by smell; literacy optional until a forged window is attempted (then crime).

**By class**

- **Rabble:** Kept dark by design. Learning = which drain kills, which canopy steals, how to sell a filter-hour without dying. A child who reads too well is suspected of window forgery. Virtue = hands that still move.  
- **Common:** Guild schooling as moral spine. Literacy *does* plaque renewal, shift bidding, and seal-clean speech at the booth. Cost = childhood shifts and lung debt. Ambition track = Administratum window apprenticeship.  
- **Upper:** House tutors + chapel + audit academies. Literacy *does* patronage, tithe theater, and *face-hold*. Solis band teaches bridge manners — how to enter Chapter-adjacent civil rooms without claiming wolf rank. Peak magnates buy clerks so their own mouths stay cool.

**Worked example:** Third Shift Concord apprentice day — morning torque drill, noon booth observation, night rain-schedule catechism. A missionary guest who cannot read a cargo slate is dismissed before the gear prayer.
