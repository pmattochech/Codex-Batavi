# Contingency Axiom Lupus — sealed anti-inversion doctrine

*Vermilion file. Distribution by access matrix only.*

This doctrine defines chapter continuity behavior if command inversion risk is detected in the Castellan command band.

---

## 1. Bilateral anti-corruption premise

- **Primary countermeasure:** Alaric + Triumvirate preserve chapter loyalty against corruption drift.
- **Secondary countermeasure:** the chapter preserves Imperial loyalty if Alaric inverts.
- This is continuity doctrine, not punishment doctrine.

---

## 2. Scope and legal frame

- Applies only under confirmed inversion-risk thresholds.
- Covers chain integrity, command firewall behavior, and terminal response authority.
- Declares full non-lethal capture as operationally infeasible in terminal Alaric inversion state.
- Authorizes lethal cull only at terminal trigger tier.

---

## 3. Operational state distinction

## A. Red Wolf state

- Still Alaric cognition, with savage dominance and degraded command discrimination.
- Can remain recoverable under intervention and reintegration.
- Requires rapid chain correction and escalation readiness.

## B. Versibar command amplification risk

- Alpha-frequency capacity creates mass-coercion hazard over corrupted assets.
- Catastrophic profile: one wolf in command directing the pack against the Imperium.
- Treated as chapter-level extinction-grade risk if coupled with anti-Imperial command behavior.

---

## 4. Trigger tiers

## Tier I — Fracture Signal

- repeated formal summons breach
- private doctrine replacing formal chain behavior
- sustained post-event suppression with rising latency

Response:

- Triumvirate intervention
- readiness shadowing by designated executors
- chain audit and reporting lock enforcement

## Tier II — Crisis Activation

- persistent Red Wolf pressure with failing reintegration
- command outputs conflicting with chapter strategic law
- alpha-frequency clustering around hostile vectors

Response:

- command firewall activation
- segmented authority routing
- specialized cohort readiness elevation

## Tier III — Inversion Confirmation

- explicit anti-Imperial command issuance
- coercive control of corrupted assets
- lethal refusal of lawful containment

Response:

- cull authorization
- terminal anti-Alpha operation

---

## 5. Custody and architecture

- Initiation recognition: Varro + Drusus.
- Redundancy advocacy layer: Corbec Ardens + Armin Sturmwahl.
- Limited-access executors: Kadmos + Otho.
- Strategic readiness oversight: Tyvar.
- Elder intervention layer: Radulf.
- Request-level sovereign disclosure: Guilliman.

No single-office dependency is permitted.

---

## 6. Alaric disclosure boundary

- Alaric is informed that the protocol exists and why it exists.
- Alaric is not given full matrix internals, kill-route geometry, or executor choreography.
- Boundary logic: full detail disclosure is a known adaptation risk under inversion.

---

## 7. Doctrine constraints

- Protect Alaric while preparing to kill him if duty requires.
- Prevent repetition of Valerius-pattern corruption in inverse polarity.
- Keep chain-over-person law without narrative simplification of any officer.
- Maintain strict separation between care and permissiveness.

---

## 8. Legatus Ring and Tier A substitution

**Legatus Ring** (standing council, Noviomagus outer ring): Legati **I–IX** resolve operational consensus on Tier I–II readiness and ratify Tier A signatory assignment before black-chamber wax. **Radulf Castrin** holds witness weight without vote. ***Decima*** does not hold Ring seats — Triumvirate authority applies directly.

**Recorded dissent** (e.g. Tier III optics) is **Vermilion** audit liability — not veto at Trigger Group C.
