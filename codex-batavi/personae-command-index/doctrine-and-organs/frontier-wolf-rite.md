## I. Definition and scope

The *Frontier Wolf Rite* is the funerary protocol applied exclusively to Batavi who reach Execratio stage (**Versibar**) — whether by ritual execution foreseen in the Exanimus Testament (*Death of the Wolf*), or by transformation on the battlefield without prior Testament, or with prison Testament when containment fails.

It is not a mourning rite. It is **clinical and spiritual recognition that the machine has stopped** — and administrative separation between what the warrior was and what biology made him.

The distinction is fundamental: the Batavi does not die as monster. The Rite exists so death is recorded under the right name, not the beast’s name.

---

## II. The three phases of the rite

### Phase 1 — Diagnosis (Chief Apothecary Drusus)

The Chief Apothecary or designated representative approaches the body and performs clinical death confirmation. The protocol is verbal, on open *vox* for all present:

> *“The biological furnace collapsed. Execratio mutation devoured the code. Progenoid glands were incinerated by the beast’s cellular rewrite. There is no harvest. [Name]’s blood ends here. The equation hit zero.”*

**On absence of harvest:** Execratio destroys progenoid glands — seeds of Astartes genetic continuity. There is no gene-seed to recover. The Versibar is absolute extinction: that specific Batavi DNA line will not pass to future neophytes. That is why the Apothecary keeps gauntlets empty at the funeral — no harvest flask, no continuity promise.

**Machine oil:** Drusus anoints the shroud not with sacred oils or incense but with purified machine oil — the same lubricant that keeps the Vexillatio’s weapons running. *“Let iron return to earth. Let flesh return to the forge.”*

---

### Phase 2 — *Conclamatio* (Chief Chaplain Varro)

Varro performs the rite of the dead. With *Crozius Arcanum* vertical, he strikes ferrocrete base in three distinct blows, separated by silence, each rising in intensity. Each blow calls the warrior by name — not the beast’s name, but the name he carried before.

> **First blow:** *“[Batavi name] of the [Vexillatio].”*  
> **Second blow:** *“[Batavi name], Shield of Noviomagus.”*  
> **Third blow:** *“[Batavi name], Son of the Wall.”*

The silence after the third blow is ratification. If the warrior does not answer the call — and never does — Varro lowers the *Crozius* and declares:

> *“He does not answer the call. The watch is ended. Service is complete.”*

The triad of titles is invariant: Vexillatio (unit), Shield (function), Son of the Wall (identity). They are called in ascending weight — soldier to function to belonging — because the rite summons the person, not the rank.

---

### Phase 3 — Iron pyre

The Batavi funeral pyre is not built from sacred wood or draped with flags or scrolls. It is raised from war materials: twisted plasteel beams, empty heavy ammo crates, solid promethium blocks. The forge consumes what the forge made.

**The shroud:** The body — in bestial Execratio form, which does not revert with death — is covered by heavy ballistic canvas, bound tight with anchor chains. The procedure is not to hide shame of animal shape. It is to return to the Batavi, in death, the containment and dignity **Wolf’s Curse** stole in his last seconds alive. He is buried contained, as he lived.

**Ignition:** Varro throws the ignition cylinder. Solid promethium burns in orange flame and dense black smoke.

**Closing protocol:** With the pyre lit, the Triumvirate recites in unison:

> *“The Emperor dictates, we comply.”*

It is the same opening protocol of every campaign and close of every treaty. Its presence here is intentional: the Batavi’s service began with that phrase and ends with it. Obedience was not broken by biology — it was fulfilled until biology made it impossible.

---

### Addendum — The name in the foundation

After the pyre cools, the Batavi warrior’s name is carved into the ferrocrete footings of the nearest facility or settlement — not a separate monument, not a headstone, but the load-bearing structure of the city itself.

The warrior stops being the wall’s guard. He becomes the Wall.

The vigil does not end with death. It changes nature.

## III. The chain of names — Alaric’s plaques

### The plaque system (inseparable pair)

Every citizen of Noviomagus frontier hives receives at birth a **civil ID plaque** — rough iron rectangle with civil name, home district, and registry number. The “seal of humanity,” issued by local *Administratum*. Material: untreated iron, prone to oxidation, therefore heavy with physical history.

Every *Cohors Batavorum* Astartes receives at elevation a **service plaque** — grey ceramite aquila with Batavi name chosen at initiation, home Vexillatio, and year of elevation. Material: grey-iron ceramite, aquila in low relief.

The two plaques form an inseparable pair — full identity of a Batavi. A man who was citizen before warrior. For Versibars, that pair weighs more: it is all that remains of who they were before biology decided for them.

| Plaque | Informal name | Material | Content |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Root plaque** | Cradle plaque | Rough iron | Civil name, district, civil registry no. |
| **Iron plaque** | Line plaque | Ceramite | Batavi name, Vexillatio, year of elevation |

### The chain

The practice began during the Foundation War without formal doctrine — Alaric took the plaques from the first brother he executed and never stopped. The cord grew as the war grew and marines fell.

Alaric removes plaques from each Versibar he executes — by Testament ritual or on the battlefield — and binds them to a heavy steel cord wound in multiple turns on the right pauldron of *Castra-Lupus*, under Viggo’s white pelt. The pelt’s weight presses most metal against ceramite, silencing most plaques. Only those on the lower edge stay exposed to motion — they make the characteristic clink when Alaric walks: punctual, metallic, discreet.

Chapter veterans recognize that sound. Neophytes learn it in the first month of formation. When Alaric walks Noviomagus corridors and metal hits metal in that rhythm, every nearby Batavi knows what is being carried — and how much has already been lost.

### Current count (580.M42)

**743 pairs** — 1,486 individual plaques. Roughly 97 kg of iron and ceramite pressed under Viggo’s pelt.

Volume splits across two periods with radically different rates:

* **Foundation War (015–045.M42):** ~650 pairs. Most founding marines died as Versibar over thirty years of conflict. Chimeric gene-seed was new and untested; *Axiom of Reason* did not yet exist as doctrine; Wolf’s Curse was incomprehensible at first. Execratio rate was catastrophic. Those who survived — minds intact through decades of war without doctrinal tools — became first Legati and founding elite.
* **Post-foundation (045–580.M42):** ~93 pairs. With *Axiom of Reason* formalized, *Das Erbe Codex* cataloged by Drusus, and Exanimus Testament mandatory, rate dropped sharply. Over 535 years with a 5,000-marine Chapter, one Versibar every 5–6 years is the average of functional doctrine under continuous war.

The difference between the two periods is not only numeric. It is the physical-weight record of the cost of learning to be what Batavi are.

Haldor is the most recent pair — added after Incus-Gravis. The root plaque carries the name he had before Noviomagus. The iron plaque carries the name the Chapter gave him.

None of the 1,486 plaques bears Alaric’s name. He does not perform this rite for himself. There is a lone plaque on the cord — no pair, inserted among Foundation War pairs — that Drusus added without explanation after the 031.M42 Execratio. It is the only piece on the cord that does not follow the pair system. Alaric never asked what is carved on it. Drusus never explained.

## IV. Protocol variations

**Case A — Death of the Wolf (Testament fulfilled):** Full rite. Testament is read before *Conclamatio* — Varro states the choice the warrior made while still human. That is the rite’s most important point: death was chosen by a functional mind, not imposed by biology.

**Case B — Execratio in the field (no Testament or failed prison Testament):** Full rite, no Testament reading. *Conclamatio* keeps three calls. Absence of formal choice does not change the farewell’s shape — only removes Varro’s first line.

**Case C — Prison Versibar enters *Adversarii at Portas* and does not survive:** Rite on remains if recoverable. If body cannot be recovered from compromised sector, *Conclamatio* is performed with empty shroud — what Varro calls *“the absent rite.”* Plaques were already on Alaric’s cord from imprisonment; no second addition.

**Case D — Prison Versibar survives *Adversarii at Portas* and returns to cell:** Plaques stay on chain. Alaric does not return them while the warrior lives. The only case where a pair of plaques hangs on the cord while the owner still exists.
