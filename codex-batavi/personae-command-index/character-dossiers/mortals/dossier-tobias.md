# \[WIKI\] DOSSIER: TOBIAS

**Title:** Chief Servant of the Logistical Tier (posthumous) / Martyr of labor.  
**Other titles:** Master of Keys, Adamant Archivist, Eyes of the Fortress.  
**Archetype:** Bureaucratic efficiency / silent loyalty **Status:** Deceased (radiation accident in forges, 570.M42).  
**Age at death:** ~55–60 Terran years

## 1\. PSYCHOLOGICAL AND OPERATIONAL PROFILE

Tobias embodied servile dignity. He was not a lobotomized slave (servitor) but a free man of Noviomagus Prime who dedicated his life to running the *Cohors Batavorum*.

* **Memory of the Wall:** While Alaric planned war, Tobias managed consequences. He knew exactly how many liters of promethium remained, how many bolter rounds were spent, and radiation levels in service sub-levels.  
* **Fearful fidelity:** He felt respect bordering on dread for Alaric von Helis. Tobias was one of the few who saw the Castellan without his wolf helm and survived to keep the secret of his lord’s exhaustion. He did not serve from fear of punishment but from deep belief that, without the Castellan, Noviomagus would fall into oblivion. His sacrifice at ~60, holding a radioactive blast door open to save workers, cemented his name in Chapter history.

## 2\. CYBERNETICS AND PHYSIOLOGY

Like most high-ranking citizens on a forge world, Tobias was more machine than man, but his augments were functional, not martial.

* **Optical aug:** His right eye was replaced with a multi-lens auspex able to read barcodes, thermal signatures, and analyze structural integrity of metal bulkheads.  
* **Neural data interface:** Connectors at wrists and temple let him “plug” directly into fortress logistics terminals to process inventories at superhuman speed.  
* **Silent step:** Feet replaced with low-noise hydraulic prostheses so he could walk iron corridors without the usual metallic echo, emerging from shadows whenever the Castellan or Elara needed a report.

## 3\. RITUAL AND LOGISTICS ROLE

Tobias oversaw all funerary and transition rites in the civil and service sector.

* **Master of the bier:** Tobias brought the grav-bier for Viggo and coordinated logistics of transporting the wolf’s bones to Fenris. He treated death with the same precision as an ore shipment: respect, without paralyzing sentiment.  
* **Viggo’s talisman:** He received an amulet carved from the wolf’s bones, forged to channel **vigilance and stability**. For Tobias, the amulet was a reminder that even the smallest parts of a machine (or fortress) matter to the whole.

## 4\. THE SERVANT’S ARSENAL

Tobias avoided combat but was not helpless.

* **Electroshock cane:** He carried a heavy brass cane hiding a pulse generator, used mainly to discipline malfunctioning servitors or drive off metal rats in sub-levels.  
* **Command data-slate:** His main “weapon” was a reinforced data panel that let him lock doors, reroute power, and trigger ventilation systems, turning the fortress itself into a trap for intruders.

## 5\. RELATIONS

*The mason among giants.*

Tobias was the only unmodified mortal all three Triumvirate members treated as peer without ever saying so. He never held military rank, was never summoned to war council, and never asked for anything beyond supplies for his workers. Perhaps exactly for that, he was the only one they listened to when the human voice had to be heard.

* **Alaric von Helis:** The Chapter’s most improbable relationship. Tobias was the first civilian who treated Alaric as someone who needed a chair — not a throne. The night Alaric appeared in the kitchen with newly found Elara, Tobias simply dragged the room’s only reinforced chair and said: *“Sit, sir. You look like you carried the world on your back today.”* The Castellan sat. Tobias did not step back. From that improbable balance grew one of the few functional friendships Alaric kept through the Silent Vigil. Tobias never asked about war; Alaric never had to explain its weight.
* **Martha:** Two-decade marriage built on the same principle as Batavi doctrine: function before form. Tobias built walls and furniture; Martha heated what was inside. They rarely needed many words to coordinate logistics of a kitchen frequented by three-meter giants.
* **Elara Solis:** Tobias was the substitute father who taught Elara to laugh. While Alaric taught friction geometry and Varro taught duty’s weight, Tobias taught how to knead bread, fix a kitchen knife, and why walls exist to protect families who dine inside them. The wooden sword Elara wielded to defend Alaric from an Imperial regent lord was carved by Tobias in a workshop afternoon. For Elara, losing Tobias was losing the human half of the world.
* **Triumvirate (collective):** Drusus monitored Tobias’s health metrics as often as line Batavi’s — without telling the patient. Varro never spoke to Tobias beyond protocol but bowed his head on each departure, a gesture Chapter veterans read as functional respect. Tobias treated all three with the same disarming ease: Varro as “Uncle Skull”; Drusus as “the one who doesn’t smile but isn’t rude”; Alaric simply as “milord” — and sometimes “sir,” on hard days, in a tone that made the difference between the two titles perfectly clear.

## 6\. DEATH AND LEGACY

*570.M42 — radiation accident in Noviomagus Prime lower forges.*

The failure was catastrophic and without glory: an aging plasma reactor on sub-level forty-two went thermal collapse during a night shift. Evacuation protocols activated. Tobias, inspecting the adjacent corridor, accessed the safety blast doors’ manual control panel to secure exit for twelve workers trapped in the inner sector. The doors were old; the electric mechanism had fused with heat. He held manual lock with his right arm while workers passed one by one.

When Drusus’s rescue team arrived, they found Tobias seated against the panel, skin carbonized by radiation, thumb still on the lever. He died holding the door open for others.

Alaric stood guard by the casket three days and three nights, armed and armored, without eating or sleeping. It was not ordered; it happened. When Arbites asked which protocol to log for the vigil, Varro answered: *“Astartes captain’s military honor. Log it exactly so.”*

Chief Servant was never filled by one person again. After Tobias’s death, duties split among four servants, each covering a fraction of what he did alone. None of the four approached the whole.

Tobias’s eldest son inherited his aptitude and today works in sync with Kadmos’s tech-acolytes, treating fortress piping as arteries of a living organism. The newborn at the funeral became the youngest registered child — and grew up never knowing the man whose name is on the forge work roster.
